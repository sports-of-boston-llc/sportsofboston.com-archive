How to Build the Next Celtics Dynasty

By @BrianWill157 in Celtics, Featured, Opinion ⋅ June 5, 2015 at 12:00pm ⋅ Post a comment

In the 21st century, Boston is a city that has become accustom to winning. Since 2000, its major sports teams (Patriots, Celtics, Red Sox and Bruins) have combined to win nine championships. The Celtics though, despite being the one of the two most accomplished and winningest NBA teams in history, have only contributed one. On top of this, after losing three future Hall of Famers over the course of two years, it seemed like the team was going to be closer to the lottery than the Finals for the foreseeable future.

It seems to me though, that a Championship contender is not that far off. With a great coach in Brad Stevens, a top general manager in Danny Ainge, a young, cheap backcourt locked up for the next three years and loads of cap space coming, this is a team that could be hoisting the Larry Oâ€™Brien Trophy sooner than most would think. I am going to take a look at the current roster, free agents and the upcoming draft to determine the likelihood that this team could contend in a weak Eastern Conference next season.

Luigi Datome, SF (Restricted)

Jonas Jerebko, PF (Unrestricted)

Phil Pressey, PG (Restricted)

It sounds possible that the only player of this group that will be in a Celtics uniform next year will be Jae Crowder. Bass is going to be a bit pricier than Ainge would like and is not much of a priority. Datome and Pressey can be replaced by the four draft picks coming this year, although they could bring Pressey back for the minimum. Resigning Crowder should not be overly difficult. His struggles shooting will scare some teams off but his toughness and versatility make him a good fit for Stevens and this team. The Celtics have a young backcourt rotation in Avery Bradley, Marcus Smart and Isaiah Thomas locked up through the 2017 season and they will all combine to make less than $20 million in each of those years. That means, Danny Ainge can focus on drafting, trading and signing to shore up the front court. As it stands now, with Crowder back next year, he and Evan Turner will share small forward duties. I am guessing Ainge will package a pick with Gerald Wallace between now and next yearâ€™s trade deadline to get his $10.1 million expiring contract off the books, sooner rather than later. The big guy rotation is going to be similar to this past season with a combination of Jared Sullinger, Tyler Zeller and Kelly Olynyk sharing the paint. That front court is not NBA playoffs ready.

All three of these guys are quality bench players, but having two of them in at all times is not going to win enough games to make a contender. Jae Crowder played a career high 30 percent of his minutes at the power forward position this season and that number will be around the same next year. Even with that being the case, the lack of a real NBA center is the biggest hole on this teamâ€™s roster. Zeller may develop into a sufficient starter but for now he needs a bit of work on both ends. Ainge is going to need to spend money on either a four or a five and find a complementary piece to fit in at the other position. Thankfully, there are a lot of options out there.

The first option, and most exciting, is a combo including Cavaliers free agent, Kevin Love. It would take some juggling to work the cap situation but having Brandon Bass and Gerald Wallace of the books alone will put them close. Finding a good complement to play center is the next step. The obvious fit next to Love would be a rim-protecting, hard playing, seven-footer they could get on the cheap. They wonâ€™t be able to pay Love and of one the top guys such as Marc Gasol, DeAndre Jordan or Roy Hibbert but there are still a lot of cheaper options out there.

Robin Lopez would be a great fit but if Lamacus Aldridge stays in Portland, he will likely stay as well. However, if they could afford him and he is on the market, Lopez could fit in nicely doing to dirty work while Love stretches defenses along the perimeter. It is a long shot to get both but would be a great defensive rebounding pair, give Stevens a nice new toy on offense and push the current three big men to the bench for top of the line depth along the front court. This situation is along exactly the same in regards to Omer Asik (although his play in the playoffs was a bit precarious) and Timofey Mozgoz who has a cheap team option that Cleveland would be insane not to pick up.

The next tier consists of guys like Kosta Koufos and Brandan Wright. Koufos played well in the playoffs, is a force on defense and is young and cheap. Brandan Wright is my personal favorite. Itâ€™s expected that he will remain in Phoenix and his brief stint in Boston this season did not go very well but if he were to somehow return, I really like the fit. His versatility is extremely underrated. In 2013-14, perhaps his best season as a pro, Wright played 96 percent of his time at the center position and at Phoenix this season played at small forward almost 40 percent of the time. If Stevens has more time to work with him, Wright could turn into a great pick and roll threat and small-ball five. He also would cost less than the other options.

After Koufos and Wright the options for starting centers get a little thin. There are career backups such as Alexis Ajina and Nazr Mohammed. Or aging veterans like Samuel Delambert and Kendrick Perkins. Of course the other option is to keep Kelly Olynyk or Tyler Zeller in the starting rotation next to a star power forward like Kevin Love for another year which is not a bad plan either. It would also give them a chance to prove their worth as starting centers.

If Ainge isnâ€™t able to land Love or one of the other big name, big price free agent front court guys, there are other options. Some of them being: fill holes with one or two midlevel bigs and throw a max at a top wing player in the next couple years (Jimmy Butler, Kevin Durant); take the boat load of assets you have (two first round picks this year, young/cheap players, future picks) and throw them at Sacramento for DeMarcus Cousins; save your cap space for the time being, draft well this year and keep playing the asset swap game until something big comes along.

Unfortunately, for those of us who like big splashes in the NBA, I think the most likely is the last option. Top level swings are always in the highest demand and there has been little buzz about any of them coming to Boston. In addition, that would push Crowder back to the bench or into a stretch-four spot which might not be the best fit. DeMarcus Cousins is frightening to a lot of teams. His on court antics would not mesh well with Brad Stevens or the organization and banking the entire future of the franchise on time might be too big of a risk, especially considering the cost. His potential on defense is much better than the result he has shown and given his age, length and strengthÂ he should be able to progress to at least an average defender by next year. Lineups that would feature Avery Bradley, Marcus Smart, Harris, Crowder and Tyler Zeller could devastate opposing offenses with the ability to switch and swarm at four positions while having a decent rim protector with quick-ish feet guarding the paint. On offense, assuming Crowder improves his shooting, that is a team that can perfectly run Stevens offense and create a Poor-Man’s-Warriors flow.

The problem with signing both Crowder and Harris (or someone similar to Harris) to long term contracts is that is limits options for the future. Even with Harris and a new starting big man, this is not a title contender. If Ainge were to go after a top tier star in the next couple years it would take a lot of roster movement and if he fails, could create a perennial six seed that is not a contender and also is not getting top picks. Going after another wing, in addition to Crowder could put this team into purgatory for the next few years and increases the likelihood that someone gets a big contract, doesn’t live up to expectations and ends up as the next Gerald Wallace.

With a backcourt rotation set and depth across the roster, this is a team only one or two big moves away from contention.
