NBA Draft 2015: Who Do the Celtics Have Their Sights Set On?

By hnichols in Celtics, Featured, Opinion, Top Story ⋅ June 1, 2015 at 9:48pm ⋅ Post a comment

The 2015 NBA draft is fast approaching, with the lottery the focus of the conversation, the Celtics are looking to scratch up some talent to go with the youthful squad already in place. Everyone knows the Celtics have multiple picks, that they have, in a sense, ‘blown it up’ and are rebuilding, but how many picks do they have and where do they fall? The boys in green have the numbers 16, 28, 33, and 45 picks. The C’s had no ping pong balls in the draft lottery because, unlike the New York Knicks who tanked to get the fourth pick, and the Philadelphia 76ers who are just plain bad and landed the third pick in the draft, they played with pride and gave our great Boston sports fans something to cheer about.

The C’s squeaked into the playoffs as a seven seed to face the, now finals-bound, Cleveland Cavaliers. Although the Celtics were swept out of the playoffs, it wasn’t for lack of effort and intensity. Now they have the opportunity to add more pieces to they already hungry squad. Before we talk about this year’s draft, lets take a look at first-rounders the Celts have drafted in recent years.

  • 2014 – #6 Marcus Smart, #17 James Young
  • 2013 – #16 Lucus Nogueira (Ultimately #13 Kelly Olynyk)
  • 2012 – #21 Jared Sullinger, #22 Fab Melo
  • 2011 – #25 Marshon Brooks
  • 2010 – #19 Avery Bradley

Many of these players are now becoming key guys in the Celtics rotation. With the acquisition of ‘the new microwave’ Isaiah Thomas from Phoenix, the upward trajectory of Avery Bradley, the emergence of Marcus Smart, and the promise of James Young we have some talented guard play. I would be doing a disservice if I didn’t mention the stellar play of Evan Turner, the number two selection of the Sixers in 2010. Between Sullinger, Olynyk and Jae Crowder, there is no shortage of in-between size guys with a lot of heart.

Now, what do the Celtics need to move them to the next echelon in the watered-down eastern conference? It seems wise to make a run at a big man. Beyond that, they could really use a scoring forward who can operate from the perimeter. Ultimately the Celtics need TALENT to get them over the hump. Whoever they draft will play hard and either have, or learn to have, a will to win. Brad Stevens already has a new book of matches just for lighting a fire under guys. This leaves Danny Ainge right where Boston fans would like to see him, in the driver’s seat with picks, young players and a chance to improve this team.

Beyond the Lottery

Obviously Karl-Anthony Towns and Jahlil Okafor will go with the first two picks; there go the best two big men in the draft. On the upside, the division-rival Knicks aren’t getting either of them. The likely third pick is D’Angelo Russell, who would be a great fit for the need of wing scoring and shooting ability, he shouldn’t get past the Sixers. We can also keep in mind that they 76ers have a tendency to draft players who don’t play or pan out in the NBA, however, if he slips he won’t get far.

Let’s get down to business with the Celtics first pick in the 2015 draft at number 16. What is available for the C’s this far down the board? It seems that many analysts are tossing out the names of 6’9” power forward Kevon Looney from UCLA and 6’11” power forward Bobby Portis from Arkansas. These may be viable options for the Celtics in efforts to fill in the front line size. If truly after a big man, Portis may be the preferred pick here with a few inches and 20-30 pounds on Looney.

If 6’10” power forward Trey Lyles out of Kentucky were to slip this far he may also be able to add to the front line size of the Celtics although it seems workouts will be key for all the Kentucky kids due to the fact that everyone had to share minutes. Limited minutes is what happens when John Calipari is able to recruit an entire McDonald’s All-American team. Another option could be to draft Sam Dekker, at 6’9” he has a guard skill set with solid handles, ability to slash and score and a shooters touch. Coming from Wisconsin and a Bo Ryan offense he would be able to fit in and learn a system but also pick his spots to create. Dekker would fill the need for a bigger wing scorer and would surely benefit from daily battles with the tough defense of Jae Crowder in practice.

Kelly Oubre from Kansas may be available at sixteen as well, he is a long 6’7” small forward, with a 7’2” wingspan, again would bring some size and also, hopefully, some scoring ability from the wing. Now that we have covered the options at 16, it would be un-Danny Ainge-like not to imagine if we could do better. So the question is should the Celtics try to move up in the draft? Knowing what is needed the process has to be evaluating what is available, and also weighing what Boston would have to give up in picks and young talent to do so. Who to move is a difficult question. With the exception of Marcus Smart, because of his youth, Evan Turner and Isaiah Thomas there isn’t a lot of potential star power to give up. That question may be best left to Danny Ainge, what can be done is evaluate the potential a little higher up the board.

Moving On Up, Best Available

A lot of discussion has been made regarding the possibility of making a play for Willie Cauley-Stein, the 7’1” center out of Kentucky. There is no question of his size, reach, and athletic ability; these are things that cannot be taught. What has come into question with Cauley-Stein are things like work ethic, mental preparation and coachability. Calipari didn’t seem to have any issues with him this season, although when you make an undefeated run to the Final Four it’s hard for anyone to be too upset along the ride. If the Celtics have the opportunity to interview this young talent and favorably view his workouts he may be worth going after. With the Kings already having Demarcus Cousins, Cauley-Stein may be available at picks seven or eight.

Another enticing pick further up the board is Myles Turner the 6’11” power forward from Texas, who is likely to be available in the 8-10 area. This may seem like a second place prize if unable to land Cauley-Stein, but he no slouch and certainly has a lot of upside. A player that, unfortunately for the C’s, no one is sleeping on is Justice Winslow, the explosive 6’6” small forward from Duke. He is projected to go as high the sixth pick on ESPN’s big board top 100. Winslow helped lead Duke to a national championship this year as a college freshman alongside Jahlil Okafor and Tyus Jones. His ability to slash to the bucket with his dominant left hand was second to none in the collegiate ranks. He is able to knock down shots from outside, play tenacious defense, at times seemingly coming from out of no where, and his best feature is his transition offense. With a head of steam, and thunderous left-handed jams this guy has flashes of a familiar bearded man from Houston.

The Countdown has Begun

In just a few short weeks, the fun begins. On June 25th Boston sports fans will see if Danny Ainge and the Celtics front office will play it straight and try to get value at their assigned picks, or make moves to go up and get a potential big fish in this year’s draft. This type of move could be necessary in efforts to land a star free agent, whether it be this year or the coming seasons. If the C’s stay the course and take a player at sixteen we will still have a lot of reason to keep watching as we move toward picks 28, 33 and 45. The trend of rebuilding will continue in the coming years with the Celtics having a first round pick from Dallas next year in 2016 and also a first round pick from Minnesota (as long as the pick is outside the top 12, otherwise becomes two 2nd round picks). Tune-in as the draft will start on June 25th at 7pm from the Barclays Center in New York, NY.
