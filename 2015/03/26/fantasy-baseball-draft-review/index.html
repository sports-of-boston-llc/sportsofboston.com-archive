Fantasy Baseball Draft Review

By Tim Wirzburger in Fantasy, MLB, Opinion, Top Story ⋅ March 26, 2015 at 11:25am ⋅ Post a comment

It’s getting closer and closer to opening day, and if you’re like me then you’ve already got fantasy baseball on your mind. Many of you may have even done your drafts by now. You may even have some money on the line.

Personally, I’m in a 10-team dynasty league with friends that’s extremely competitive. Every guy in the league knows his stuff, and they’re all very active on the waiver wire and trading block. It’s also really interesting to see how people run their team in a dynasty league like this as opposed to a single-season league. And let me tell you- the draft is VERY different.

I wanted to show you my team and draft results and get into why I went with each pick, where the draft was going, and maybe give you some tips and insight as you approach your own fantasy seasons. First, let’s take a look at my keepers from last year. The way it works is, you get to keep up to 9 players and they take the a draft pick based on their respective rankings. So the free agent pool at draft looks very different.

Here is my team and the draft picks they slotted into:(1) Adam Jones, OF
(3) Evan Longoria, 3B
(4) Madison Bumgarner, SP
(11) Masahiro Tanaka, SP
(15) Brian Dozier, 2B
(16) Mookie Betts, 2B/OF
(19) Michael Pineda, SP
(20) Gregory Polanco, OF
(23) Noah Syndegaard, SP

Some injury risk but really high upside. I like the 5-category production from Jones, and Bumgarner is obviously an ace. Tanaka makes me nervous, but Pineda is someone who can pitch at ace level when healthy. Now let’s get into the draft.

R2: Jon Lester
I had the fourth overall pick. Tulo went first, then Jose Fernandez. I was worried Lester would be off the board, but Adrian Gonzalez was grabbed instead. In Lester I have a verified ace who’s now pitching in the NL and has a up and coming team behind him. He’s very valuable and could easily put up an ERA in the 2’s and 225 Ks.

R5: David Ortiz
I wanted to be sure to grab a power-hitting first baseman and there weren’t many left. I think Ortiz has plenty left in the tank and now he’s hitting in the middle of a star-studded line-up. I see another 25 homers and 100 RBIs in his future.

R6: Jorge Soler
I try not to take OF too early, especially when I already had several in my keepers, but Soler’s potential makes me drool and in a keeper league he’s a stud to own. I couldn’t decide between him and Christian Yelich, but when Yelich went just a few picks before him the choice was easy.

R6: Gio Gonzalez
I had 3 picks in round 6 (benefit of being out of playoff hunt last year). Gio is a solid pitcher who should strike out 200 guys and has the benefit of pitching behind Strasburg and Scherzer. He’s a solid SP2 and I have him as a 3 or even potenially 4/5. Great value here

R6: Yoenis Cespedes
Another outfielder, I know. But his power upside is just so great and how that he’s hitting with Miguel Cabrera and Victor Martinez in a contract year… Yoenis could be a monster this season.

R7: Greg Holland
I also don’t like taking closers early, but I liked him more than really most of the remaining players and it never hurts when you have an elite closer fall into your lap. I won’t take another closer for a while, but it’s good to get one out of the way.

R8: Anibal Sanchez
Very similar to Gio, he’s just a solid guy who will put in lots of strikeouts and get wins on a good team. He may not be the stud he was in 2013, but I don’t need him to be. Just keep doing your thing, Anibal.

R10: Andrew Cashner
Love the upside of Cashner. He’s a hard-throwing kid with tremendous upside and he pitches in the cavernous Petco Park. If he can stay healthy, there’s no telling what he can’t do. It also helps that the Padres made major strides in improving their offense this offseason.

R10: Jake Odorizzi
Like Cashner, he’s a hard throwing kid. This is a pitching dominant league and I didn’t want to make the mistake of not having potential studs up and down. What I like about him is, besides the fact this is a keeper league, he pitches for the Rays and they always seem to have young kids throwing like aces every year. This year its Odorizzi.

R11: Victor Martinez
VMart had a monster season last year. He’s obviously getting old and has has health questions, but that’s why he lasted so long in the draft. But he has a good chance of putting up stud-like numbers again and has great lineup protection. Between him and Ortiz, one will be a beast this year and maybe both. Thank god for the UTIL spot.

R14: David Robertson
This is the second time a closer just sort of fell into my lap. Robertson is an elite closer and now I really have two of the top five closers. Closers are a volatile investment but pay off when they work out.

R15: Yan Gomes
I always wait on catchers and at this point I was one of the few guys that didn’t have a catcher yet. I like Gomes a lot this year, being a young hitter who swings freely and is a big part of the Indians’ offense. He’ll be able to put up some solid production with potentially high upside.

R19: Starlin Castro
I couldn’t believe he fell this far but I was happy to grab him when I did. I had planned on waiting to the end of the draft to grab a shortstop who can contribute in steals and runs, but in Castro I have an elite SS who can help in 3-5 categories and will hit at the top of the emerging Cub’s lineup. Love this pick.

R21: Jesse Hahn
With my last pick of the draft, I picked up Hahn who could become an SP3 in the A’s rotation. Not a major risk if it doesn’t work out, but it could complement my staff nicely if it does.

Summary

I really like this team. It’s got a good balance of proven players and high upside, which is exactly what you want in a dynasty league. Guys like Pineda, Polanco, Castro, Soler, and Cashner could all turn into elite players this year. Love this team and don’t see any glaring weaknesses.

C- Gomes
1B- Ortiz, Martinez
2B-Dozier, Betts
3B- LongoriaSS- Castro
OF- Jones, Cespedes, Polanco, Soler
SP- Bumgarner, Lester, Tanaka, Gonzalez, Sanchez, Cashner, Odorizzi, Pineda, Hahn, Syndegaard
RP- Holland, Robertson
