All Three Patriots’ Divisional Rivals Make Huge Impact With Free Agency and Trades

By John in Featured, Opinion, Patriots ⋅ March 9, 2015 at 9:42pm ⋅ Post a comment

The Patriots captured that elusive fourth championship, which did all it could to elude New England again, but New England still reaffirmed their spot as a dominant team in the NFL, and certainly over the AFC East. Long considered pretenders to New England fighting for second place in the division, the Bills, Dolphins and Jets are getting really sick of playing second fiddle, and all three are hoping major moves just recently made major moves hoping to gain a leg up on the might Patriots.

We all know by know how coach Rex Ryan was fired by the Jets and wound up in Buffalo, but in the past week, all three of the aforementioned teams have made significant moves, either by trade or by free agency, in an effort to bolster their roster to better compete. The question now becomes, “How will these moves affect the Patriots and division as a whole?” It’s a question that can’t be fully answered until free agency and the draft finish up, and a freak injury is always a possibility in the NFL, but for now, let’s take a quick look at the moves, which I have ranked, as I see them, from least worrying to most worrying relative to New England’s divisional dominance:

#3 – Ndamukong Suh to Miami from Detroit

Once the new league years begins, Ndamukong Suh will sign a free agent deal with Miami worth $114 million over six years ($19 million annually) including $60 million in guaranteed money. This will undoubtedly give Miami a significant presence, obviously hoping to stop Tom Brady and New England’s offense foremost.

Sure, every quarterback Miami faces, Brady included, should get roughed up a bit more than they’re used to, but as far as New England goes, they’ve faced Suh in Detroit before and still done fairly well. No one directs an offense like Brady, and he is very good in getting the ball out in time, or taking the sack if needed.

Suh, on the other hand, has built up quite the reputation as a dirty player. Fortunately, it’s gotten noticeably better from his early days, but when he gets going, it gets tough to keep that sheer intensity in check. Either way, Brady should be able to keep an eye on Suh, and won’t let him by too easily.

#2 Brandon Marshall to New York from Chicago

The Jets acquired Marshall from the Bears for a 5th-round draft pick. He has three years left on his deal, but only one with guaranteed money, so it represents a low-risk, high-reward situation for the Jets with someone of Marshall’s talent.

Of course, the main problem here is that no receiver or corps is worth much without a quarterback that can get them the ball. Michael Vick isn’t expected to factor into the Jets’ plans, and Geno Smith still isn’t widely considered to be a solid option. Former Patriots Brian Hoyer and Ryan Mallet are thought to be interesting targets (imagine Tom Brady versus a former backup of his twice a year). Less likely situations are Jay Cutler (Chicago isn’t rumored to be too happy, and this would reunite him immediately with Marshall) or Nick Foles (Philadelphia recently resigned Mark Sanchez), if New York gets desperate enough.

The Patriots declined the massive option (as we know they would) on Darrelle Revis, which allows him to test the free agent market. Resigning him would be an excellent way to counter Marshall, but just in case, New England has other competent options to frustrate Marshall and whoever his quarterback ends of being.

#1 LeSean McCoy to Buffalo from Philadelphia

In a real big trade, Philadelphia sent their star running back to Buffalo in exchange for young and dangerous linebacker Kiko Alonso. Buffalo plans to replace CJ Spiller with McCoy, who only rushed for five touchdowns last season, but still pounded the rock for 1319 yards, his second-best season total ever. Buffalo also has issues at quarterback, which hurts their receiving ability, but adding McCoy gives them plenty of credibility rushing the ball, which will give them time to work on the passing game and take pressure off their quarterback situation.

The reason this should create nervousness in New England is the uncertain future of Big Vince Wilfork, who anchored the middle of New England’s line, eating up multiple offensive linemen himself when he wasn’t making big plays and causing huge amounts of havoc for opposing offenses. The Patriots declined his option, making him a free agent. While New England could resign him, the big man is certain to create plenty of interest in free agency. Having a man that size who is that good to oppose McCoy would do wonders.

In the event Wilfork leaves, the Patriots do have plenty of names on the roster they could juggle around; we saw how that philosophy eventually worked on the offensive line last season. However, no matter who would sub into Big Vince’s role, there will never be another Vince Wilfork, and any Patriots fan should feel a lot better should he still be roaming New England’s line in 2015.
