Mookie Betts Emerging for Red Sox

By Brendan Gallagher in Featured, Opinion, Red Sox, Top Story ⋅ July 12, 2015 at 9:46pm ⋅ Post a comment

Despite their position at the bottom of the American League East, the Red Sox have gone on a recent run that has left them in position to contend for the division title in the second half of the season. Much of the credit for that run has gone, deservedly so, to players like Brock Holt, Xander Bogaerts, and Eduardo Rodriguez, but one other young star has received much less fanfare.

Despite relatively little public recognition, Mookie Betts has been the Red Sox’ best player in 2015, and has undoubtedly contributed a great deal to the Red Sox’ chance, however small, to make a legitimate run at their division. For the first time in a very long time, the Red Sox’ MVP is not an established star, but a young player on the rise.

Like most of the team, Mookie Betts got off to a pretty rough start to 2015. There were all kinds of issues; he was dropped from the top of the batting order, and many fans were wondering why the team refused to include Betts in a deal for Phillies ace Cole Hamels. Betts then went on a tear that has seen him climb back up to the first two spots in the order (depending on where Holt hits), while becoming a favorite of Red Sox pitchers for his work in centerfield. Those early season doubters have been silenced, as Betts has been the Red Sox’ best player in 2015.

Aside from the aforementioned hiccup to start the season, Betts has been an offensive force for the Red Sox. Aside from a slight dip in walk rate, just about every offensive metric highlights Betts’s dominance atop the Red Sox lineup: .278/.330/.467 triple-slash, .343 wOBA, 116 wRC+, .189 ISO. He has even contributed 13 stolen bases in 2015, adding a much-needed speed element that the rest of the lineup sorely lacks. Power, speed, and on-base ability; Betts has been doing it all at the plate in 2015.

But wait, there’s more! In addition to being an offensive force, Betts has been a game changer with the leather as well. In about half a season’s worth of innings in Centerfield, Betts has already saved the Red Sox 10 runs, in addition to his 6.3 UZR/150. And to give him proper credit, consider that Betts had never played a game in the outfield until the start of last season in Pawtucket. Betts was drafted and developed almost entirely as a second baseman, and even finished off the 2014 season at the keystone following an injury to Dustin Pedroia. And after all of that, Betts has become one of the Red Sox best defenders, but also one of the best outfielders in the entire league. Just ask Jacoby Ellsbury about Betts’s defense.

The time has come to give credit where it is due, and Mookie Betts has stepped into the spotlight for the Red Sox in 2015. Holt and Bogaerts have gotten all of the credit and recognition for All-Star spots in the American League, but Betts deserves a spot just as much as either of those two players. He has been a force on offense, defense, and the base paths in 2015, and just so happens to lead all Red Sox players in WAR (3.0). The Red Sox are in good shape for the future because they have very good young players up the middle of the diamond that are developing into stars and carrying the rest of the team. Players like Mookie Betts.
