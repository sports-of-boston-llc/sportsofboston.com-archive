Notes and Observations Week 14: Patriots Take Control of The AFC, Defeat Texans 27-6

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ December 16, 2015 at 7:47am ⋅ Post a comment

After losing two games in a row you knew there was no way the Patriots were going to lose Sunday against the Texans. With Belichick and Tom Brady involved the Patriots haven’t lost more than two games in a row since 2002. Offensively the team is still banged up but the return of Rob Gronkowski made an immediate impact. Julian Edelman returned to the practice field much earlier than anyone expected, granted it was light work but still a positive sight nonetheless. Both of those only added to the fact that the Bengals lost the game and Andy Dalton for potentially the rest of the season and the Broncos blew a 12-0 lead at home against the Raiders. The Patriots took the opening and now control their own destiny.

– Sunday night was the most balanced offensive game plan that they’ve used all year. Tom Brady finished with 30 pass attempts while the team as a whole ran the ball 31 times. They finally decided to stick with the run and it paid dividends. LeGarrette Blount looked to be having his most productive game of the season rushing 10 times for 53 yards. Unfortunately his day and season were ended early. Brandon Bolden showed a good burst for his first extended look of the season finishing with 51 yards on 16 carries. James White started to make more of an impact in the passing game gaining 27 yards on a screen pass. It’s a shame that one of the best rushing games for the year is marred by the loss of Blount.

– Even though the Patriots weren’t using Blount a ton losing him for the year hurts. We all saw the show he put on down the stretch last year. Blount is the type of running back who is built for December and January when defenses are worn out. They signed former Broncos running back Montee Ball. While he has shown flashes in the past there’s definitely a reason he has been a free agent all year. At this point it doesn’t hurt to give him a shot and hope that he ups his game to showcase himself. I’d expect to see a lot more Bolden and White than Ball until he shows he deserves more attention.

– Luckily Brady is finally getting his receiving weapons back. Danny Amendola looks fully recovered and Gronk looked fine moving around on that knee, even with the brace. Edelman is still weeks away and I wouldn’t hate it if they rest him until the playoffs. A broken bone in his foot is not something to rush back into. We all remember what happened when Gronk came back after breaking his forearm. Brady looked more comfortable with Brandon LaFell and KeShawn Martin this week with Martin catching the team’s first touchdown. That fade to Gronk one-on-one with a linebacker in the end zone is exactly why he’s such a difference maker on offense. Brady can put it up and not worry about who’s coming down with it.

– I get that what Cam Newton is doing in Carolina is incredibly impressive but my *unbiased* vote would be for Brady. He completed 73% of his passes for 226 yards and two touchdowns. He’s 38 years old and he’s had to adjust to 10 different starting offensive line combinations, a middle of the road rushing attack, and injuries to his four best offensive weapons. With all of that he’s having one of the best seasons of his already tremendous career. Look across the country to see what’s happened with Peyton Manning for more proof of just how impressive Brady’s season is.

– All the credit in the world needs to go to Marcus Cannon. Most of the game he was tasked with stopping JJ Watt with help from Tre Jackson and others. However there were multiple instances where Cannon was asked to take Watt one-on-one and he did a fantastic job. Minus the one late hit he had on Brady, Watt was kept silent. The game plan, as we knew, was don’t let JJ Watt and DeAndre Hopkins beat you and the Patriots executed it to perfection. Thanks in large part to Marcus Cannon.

– As long as they never let KeShawn Martin return another punt he’s going to help this team in a major way. He has shown that he is on the same page as Brady and has his trust. Martin ran a sharp cut back route to find himself wide open for his touchdown catch. If he keeps the drops to a minimum he could be a real boost in the postseason.

– For the short bursts we’re seeing James White, there’s already some positive signs. He isn’t as shifty as Dion Lewis was but there are few guys in the league who are. In the passing and running game he’s shown good vision as evidenced on his 23 yard screen pass and the rushing touchdown. The draw run he scored on was caused by the fact that the Texans immediately thought it was a pass since he was in the game. Now that there’s film other defenses will have to account for him opening up the offense in the red zone.

– The Houston Texans don’t have the most explosive offense in the league, obviously. But you have to be satisfied with that defensive showing. As stated above the game plan was to stop DeAndre Hopkins and they did that perfectly. On defense they’ve switched their philosophy with lining up Malcolm Butler on a team’s number one receiver. Instead they use Logan Ryan on them with help over the top, usually Devin McCourty and Duron Harmon. That game plan kept Demaryius Thomas and DeAndre Hopkins quiet and they’re two of the best receivers in the league. Meanwhile that frees up Butler, who has proven to be one of the better cornerbacks in the league, to match up with a team’s number two. As he showed Sunday, minus the one bad read on the 49 yard catch from Nate Washington, he didn’t allow another completed pass. The Super Bowl wasn’t a fluke, Butler can flat out ball.

– Jabaal Sheard easily had his best game of the season. He had two strip sacks, one was recovered by Malcolm Brown which led to an easy score in a few plays. Sheard was constantly in the backfield finishing with four total tackles and one was for loss. While Chandler Jones had a quiet day he was opening things up for Rob Ninkovich and Akiem Hicks who totaled three sacks, one and two respectively. The loss of Dominique Easley is a minor blow as they have the depth. It’s tough for him because he was having a great sophomore season.

– Over the past few weeks we’ve started to see some flashes of the old Jerod Mayo and Sunday was his best game of the season. He finished with eight total tackles, seven solo, and one for loss. The best part of his game was the quick burst we grew so accustomed to. On many occasions he was right at the line of scrimmage to blow up a play. While physically he was lagging this season he’s still one of the smartest linebackers in the league. Now that it looks like he’s getting a bit of his athleticism back the defense is going to keep improving.

– After two tough losses and dropping from the number one seed to the number three in the AFC, the Patriots are right back where they were two weeks ago. The next three games are winnable on paper but as we are well aware anything can happen. Ultimately the Patriots control their own destiny and with Belichick and Brady running things there’s no reason to doubt they’ll have the number one seed at the end of week 17.
