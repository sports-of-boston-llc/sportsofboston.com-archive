Notes and Observations, Week 8: Patriots Virtually Clinch AFC East in 36-7 Win Over Dolphins

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ November 2, 2015 at 10:16pm ⋅ Post a comment

One of the best things about being a Patriots fan is having this luxury of knowing you’re going to the playoffs every year. Even more than that, you’re probably going to watch your team in the AFC Championship just like the past four years. In only week eight, the Patriots have basically wrapped up the AFC East. After emasculating the Dolphins on Thursday night they now have wins against each of their AFC East opponents. What’s more impressive is that score could have been a lot worse. The offense left a lot of first half points on the field as the defense was shutting the Dolphins down. That’s really nitpicking though as 36 points is nothing to bat an eye at.

– I genuinely hope everyone watching is really appreciating what Tom Brady is doing this season. What he’s doing at 38 years old is nothing short of amazing. On Thursday he went 26-38 for 356 yards, with four touchdowns and no interceptions. That brings his totals on the year to 2,410 yards with 20 touchdowns and only one interception that came off a bobbled pass from Julian Edelman. He’s on pace to throw over 5,500 yards, about 47 touchdowns, and maybe have three interceptions. He’s more focused than he’s ever been and it was never more apparent than on one play in the fourth quarter. With the game already in hand with a score of 29-7, Brady took a sack after missing a read. He then slammed the ball and his fist in frustration. Even though the game was already over Brady wasn’t satisfied. He wants to leave no points on the field. Tom Brady wants to win this season more than he wants anything in the world and I don’t think there’s anyone out there who can slow him down.Â 

– It was great having Dion Lewis back. He makes such an incredible difference when he’s out there. They don’t score on that first drive unless he converts that 3rd and 16 screen pass. His spin move that he dropped on the Koa Misi was out of this world. We all saw his ridiculous touchdown against Dallas but each week that he’s out there he makes a new play that makes me say “how did he do that?” Lewis finished the game with 11 total touches (five rushing, six receiving) for 112 total yards and a key 16 yard touchdown catch where he went untouched into the end zone. He provides such a different element that the defense always has to account for. Replacing Shane Vereen proved to be a lot easier than expected.

– When Julian Edelman and Rob Gronkowski both find the end zone you know it’s been a good day for the offense. On Gronk’s touchdown he ran right through an arm tackle and took it 47 yards for the score. Brandon LaFell had a huge block that sent Gronk the rest of the way. Edelman made up for the drops last week with two touchdowns of his own to go along with seven catches for 81 yards. The Dolphins didn’t have an answer for Brady’s two favorite targets all game and Gronk notched another game over 100 yards receiving with 113.

– All these weapons on offense I can’t see many teams coming up with a way to slow it down. Even LeGarrette Blount finished with a sneaky 72 yards on 17 carries. I’ve been one of the people who has hoped to see more Blount but when it all boils down they keep winning and putting up 30+ points while they hardly use him. By the time the winter months come around they’re going to have a very fresh Blount to start taking on worn out defenses. Belichick stashing Blount on the bench for the first part of the season might be the best thing he’s done all year.

– Another week goes by with people questioning the offensive line. Again they only suited up six offensive lineman and things looked a little worrisome with Tre Jackson went down late with a knee injury. Jackson’s MRI came back negative which is always a great sign. They only allowed two sacks all game to another defensive line who was said to be a problem. Ryan Wendell is back and has been playing well. Sebastian Vollmer has adjusted well to playing left tackle. Cameron Fleming has played solid as he came off the practice squad a couple weeks ago. Bryan Stork will be coming back and will be more depth along that interior line. It looks like Shaq Mason and Marcus Cannon are only a week or two away from returning as well. The bottom line is no matter who is on that line they are playing their hardest football on every snap. They are very well coached and a ton of credit needs to go to Dave DeGuglielmo.

– Has this defensive front seven attracted the attention of the rest of the NFL yet? All season I’ve been saying they will be what makes the defense go and lately they’ve been turning it to 11. Even without Jabaal Sheard the past two weeks they are constantly pressuring quarterbacks and stopping the run. Lamar Miller has been going off the past couple weeks and the Patriots held him to 15 yards. Allowing only 15 yards rushing is impressive no matter who the opponent is. And this wasn’t a situation like the Patriots against the Jets where they flat out just didn’t try to run. The Dolphins were actually trying to run before the score started to go the way it did.

– The defensive line has been outstanding. They use a nice rotation system where everyone is staying fresh. Even rookie Geno Grissom saw action and was able to cause pressure with the rest of them. Chandler Jones jumped into first place in total sacks as he added two on Thursday. In total the team had five sacks with Ninkovich, Hightower, and McCourty having one each. Everyone is getting playing time and keeping each other fresh. Rookie Malcolm Brown has been improving week by week and is turning out to be a very suitable Vince Wilfork replacement.

– Credit goes to second year player Dominique Easley as well. He sniffed out a screen pass in the back field and has been causing pressure through the interior of the line the past few games. He looks to be as healthy as he’s been since he’s been in the league and adds a pass rush element in the middle of the line.

– The Rutgers’ boys had a great night. McCourty had the sack while Duron Harmon and Logan Ryan both picked off their third passes of the season. Harmon and Ryan lead the team with three interceptions each. They did allow 300 yards through the air but they still aren’t looking very out of position. When they allow catches the cornerback or safety in coverage is typically right there to make the tackle. That’s evidenced by the fact that Ryan, Malcolm Butler, and Justin Coleman were one, two and three in tackles for the team with seven, five and five respectively. Their secondary will never be what it was last year but it just has to be manageable for the defense to work. As long as they improve week after week they’ll be just fine.

– With the trade deadline being Tuesday it will be interesting to see if the Patriots acquire anyone. The Browns have been said to be sellers but no one has mentioned if Joe Haden is available. My unlikely pipe dream is that the Patriots trade some picks for Haden. It probably won’t happen but it’s fun to imagine Haden donning the colors. It wouldn’t surprise me if they add some depth on the offensive line with the injuries at the position.

– Belichick usually treats the first eight games like his extended pre-season to see what kind of team he has. Well that team is one that is 7-0 and looks as if they haven’t even begun to peak.
