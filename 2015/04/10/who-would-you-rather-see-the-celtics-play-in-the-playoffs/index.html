Who Would You Rather See the Celtics Play in the Playoffs?

By Geoff in Celtics, Featured, Opinion, Top Story ⋅ April 10, 2015 at 12:06pm ⋅ Post a comment

After their win against the Pistons, the Celtics keep inching towards a playoff spot. The Green currently sit in seventh place in the East with four games to go, by virtue of holding the tiebreaker over Brooklyn. Their magic number to clinch a playoff spot is four (any combination of Boston wins and Indiana/Miami losses). There is still a small chance that the Celtics could get up as high as the six seed, but will most likely finish as either seventh or eighth in the Eastern Conference. Therefore that leaves three teams as possible first round playoff opponents: Atlanta, Cleveland or Toronto.

Possible Match-Up: #1 Atlanta Hawks vs #8 Boston Celtics

Role reversal from the 2008 playoffs! Back then the Celtics had the number one seed and the Hawks had just clawed and scratched their way into the playoffs with a 37-45 record (a possible record for this year’s Celtics), and guess what? The Hawks were able to push the eventual champion Celtics all the way to seven games in an exciting first round. Now, I’m not saying that just because the tables are turned that Boston could do that this year. That series in 2008 was a bit of an odd one with the Celtics unable to perform on the road in the playoffs. Every game in Boston was completely one sided where the games in Atlanta were close match-ups that the soon-to-be champs just couldn’t close out. The Celtics are 1-2 against the Hawks this year, but if they met in the playoffs it would be a completely different Celtics team.

In their first match-up the Hawks defeated the Celtics 109-105 in Atlanta on 12/2. The Celtics starting five that night: Jarred Sullinger, Jeff Green, Tyler Zeller, Rajon Rondo, and Avery Bradley. Boston had just come off a brutal stretch with four straight losees to Memphis, Portland, Chicago, and San Antonio. The gameÂ was stereotypical of the Green/Rondo led Celtics, a large lead at halftime that would eventually disappear (mostly in the third quarter) and result in a loss. The Celtics led 64-50 at half time only to come up short handed. In act two, Atlanta had a bit of easier time defeating Boston 105-91 on the road, and they were without Al Horford and Kyle Korver. The Celtics starting lineup that night was a bit closer to the product we see now: Jared Sullinger, Jae Crowder, Tyler Zeller, Avery Bradley, and Evan Turner. Green and Rondo were gone, but this was before the C’s had turned the corner and Atlanta man handled Boston the whole game. Finally, in their most recent match-up, the Celtics pulled the upset of the season defeating Atlanta 89-88 in the final game before the All Star break. The win was highlighted by Evan Turner’s buzzer beater jumper, andÂ this was the beginning of Boston’s run of success, especially in fourth quarters. They might not have had their current roster, and started the game with aÂ third group fiveÂ includingÂ Sullinger, Bradley, Turner, and now Marcus Smart, and Brandon Bass to face the Hawks. However, this game came the night before the All Star break where strange things always seem to happen. Good teams are eagerly looking towards the break and bad teams usually find a way to exploit that. This was a trap game for Atlanta, and the Celtics successfully sprung the trap.

The final game of these two team’s regular season match-ups will be the one Celtics fans will look to the most if these two face off in round one of the postseason. On top of the confidence Boston got by defeating the Hawks in their last match-up, Atlanta does not have the star power that most contenders have for the post season. They might have had four All Stars on their team this year, but not one of them is someone to point to and say he will lead this team on a deep playoff run. If there’s one team in the East that could be upset, it’s Atlanta, even if it’s not the Celtics pulling the upset.

Possible Match-up : #2 Cleveland Cavaliers vs #7 Boston Celtics

No, no, no! This is the one match-up any Celtics fan should be smart enough to realize Boston will not be able to keep up in. Two words: LeBron James. When James decided to return to Cleveland with the weight off his individual shoulders of winning a championship, he made it a mission to end the drought in his home town. Cleveland is my personal pick to make it out of the East and into the finals and I don’t see any team possibly challenging them in the conference. However, for the sake of argument here’s how the Celtics fared against the Cavaliers this season and keep in mind, they still have two games to go against them.

Way back on November 14, the Celtics almost did the impossible. They nearly defeated Cleveland, but thanks to old friend Rajon Rondo, the Cavs walked out of Boston with a 122-121 victory. Similar to the first match-up with Atlanta, this game had the one constant of the Rondo/Green Celtics, a late lead that could not be held onto. Entering the fourth quarter, the Celtics led 101-84. Yes, that’s correct, 101 points in just three quarters against the LeBrons, I mean Cavs. Then it happened again, the fourth quarter came and the Celtics were outscored 38-20 culminating in a last second play drawn up for Rondo and he didn’t even get a shot in the air. I happened to be at that game, and the whole time I was on the Orange Line riding home, all anyone could say was “How can you not at least get a shot off there?” This game again had another early version of the Celtics starting five: Sullinger, Green, Rondo, Bradley, and Kelly Olynyk.

The second round between these two is the main reason I want the Celtics to be as far away from the Cavs as possible. Cleveland absolutely demolished Boston 110-79 on 3/3, and this game did feature the Celtics roster as its currently constituted. Only Brandon Bass and Isaiah Thomas reached double figures in scoring with 15 and 11 points each, while all five Cavs starters and two bench players were able to do so. The Celtics never had a chance in this second game and while a playoff series might not exactly look like the box score of this second game, it still makes me want Boston to either finish sixth or eighth. With two more games against Cleveland in the regular season we should get a better indication of how Boston will match-up with the Cavs should they meet in the postseason. It doesn’t appear that James has any interest in resting before the playoffs, at least according the ESPN reporter Dave McMenamin.

Possible Match-Up: #3 Toronto Raptors vs #6 Boston Celtics

This one is a long shot, but still a possibility. The Celtics would pretty much haveÂ to win out and the current sixth seeded Bucks would probably have to lose out, but it’s not impossible. It would actually be a lot of fun if the last game of the season, which happens to be between these two teams, decided their final playoff seeds. Just for fun here’s the rest of Milwaukee’s schedule: 4/10 at New York, 4/12 vs Brooklyn, 4/13 at Philadelphia, and 4/15 vs Boston. That’s a much easier schedule than Boston’s two games against Cleveland and final match up with Toronto. Speaking of Toronto, what would a playoff match-up look like between the Raptors and Celtics?

Just like Atlanta, the Celtics have a 1-2 record against Canada’s finest NBA team, and it came down to a buzzer beater in over time on a crazy play drawn up by Brad Stevens. The other two match-ups Toronto took with a 110-107 win in Boston on 11/5, and a 109-96 in Toronto on 1/10. Just like the other two teams, neither one of these games saw the Celtics current roster and the one game Boston defeated Toronto, the Celtics played them close. However, the Raptors were without star point guard, Kyle Lowry. Lowry is the Raptors second leading scorer and best ball handler and Toronto hopes to have him back for the playoffs, but there is no timetable for his return at the moment. He would add a completely different dynamic in a playoff match-up that the Celtics may not see in their final regular season gameÂ against the Raptors this season.

My Pick

In the end, I would have to pick the Hawks as the team I would most like to see the Celtics play. That’s taking into account the realistic position of where they might finish in the standings, and the fact that I don’t think Boston will get out of the first round no matter who they play. Cleveland is far too good for Boston to even compete with and they might even get swept. Toronto does not seem a likely match-up given the rest of both Boston and current six seed Milwaukee’s schedule, and Atlanta is the only team left. The Hawks have been referred to at times this year as the baby Spurs, mostly because head coach Mike Budenholzer was a former assistant to Gregg Popovich, but they play like the Spurs too. Â A well balanced team that has worked their success through the draft and doesn’t have any highlight-reel superstars. This will definitely favor the Hawks in the early rounds of the playoffs, and the Celtics might have some success match-up wise, but Atlanta should be able to move on.

The Celtics still have to get into the playoffs first, but it’s fun to now move from talking about will they make it to who would you rather see them play. So I ask you, who would you like to see the Celtics play in round one?
