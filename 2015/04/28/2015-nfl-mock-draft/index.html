2015 NFL Mock Draft

By Jon Lyons (@TheRealJonLyons) in Featured, NFL, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ April 28, 2015 at 8:00am ⋅ Post a comment
  1. Tampa Bay Buccaneers- Jameis Winston, QB (FLORIDA STATE)

The Buccaneers need a franchise quarterback and it appears as though Winston will be the choice for them. Although I personally believe Marcus Mariota will be the better quarterback of the two, Winston seems to be the favorite Tampa Bay. He is talented and led Florida State to a National Championship in 2013. He has some serious off the field questions and his on the field play was lacking in some areas in 2014 (see first halves vs BC, Georgia Tech, and Miami) but if the Bucaneers can work with these issues, Winston could end up being a franchise player.

  1. Tennessee Titans- Marcus Mariota, QB (OREGON)

This pick could change if the Titans pull off the much talked about trade for San Diego’s Phillip Rivers, but unless that scenario occurs Mariota will likely be the pick here. There is some skepticism about his NFL ability due to the offense he played in at Oregon, but he has shown an ability to make every NFL throw and has an impeccable off the field record.

  1. Jacksonville Jaguars- Leanoard Williams, DT (USC)

The Jaguars are a team that looks headed in the right direction under Coach Gus Bradley, but this draft could be a turning point for them. If they can select an elite player in the first round, it will be a great building block for their team moving forward. Due to their lack of overall talent, they can go for best player available at number three.

  1. Oakland Raiders- Vic Beasley, DE/OLB (CLEMSON)

If the Jaguars don’t take Williams, it is likely the Raiders will. If he is unavailable, Beasley is a force who could be a great asset to the edge of Oakland’s front seven. The AFC West has Peyton Manning and Phillip Rivers (for now) so getting to the quarterback is critical. Pairing Beasley with last year’s first rounder Khalil Mack would make for a scary young duo.

  1. Washington Redskins- Amari Cooper, WR (ALABAMA)

Despite having DeSean Jackson and Pierre Garcon, the Redskins could go wide receiver here. They could also go defensive tackle or defensive back, but adding Cooper would give the Redskins starting quarterback (whoever that may end up being) a great weapon. Points will be important in an offensive heavy NFC East ad Cooper will help Washington score more of them.

  1. New York Jets- Brandon Scherff, OL (IOWA)

If Mariota falls it is likely the Jets take him at six. If not, Scherff would be a good selection for them. They have upgraded their secondary, running backs, and wide receiver corps in free agency and already have one of the better defensive lines in football. Offensive line is a need for them and will be even more important with suspect quarterback play.

 

  1. Chicago Bears- Dante Fowler, DE/OLB (FLORIDA)

 

The Bears need help everywhere on defense and Fowler could fit nicely for them. There is a possibility they go wide receiver with Brandon Marshall gone, but their front seven is the biggest need. They had trouble stopping everyone in 2014 and Fowler could provide the improved pass rush their defense needs.

 

  1. Atlanta Falcons- Shane Ray, DE/OLB (MISSOURI)

 

The Falcons had some bad injury luck in 2014 but that did not hide some glaring defensive deficiencies. Desmond Trufant is one of the best young corners in the NFL, but he can only do so much when no one is stopping the run or getting to the quarterback.

 

  1. New York Giants- Erick Flowers, OT (MIAMI)

 

The Giants had great difficulty consistently protecting Eli Manning last season. Flowers could play tackle or move inside to shore up the Giants interior line, giving Manning more time to find Odell Beckham Jr and Shane Vereen. A defensive back would not be surprising here, either.

 

  1. Louis Rams- Andrus Peat, OT (Stanford)

 

An already stout Rams defense got even better this offseason with the signing of Nick Fairley. Their offense has a young playmaker in Tre Mason, but their quarterback, wide receiver, and offensive line positions are all question marks. Since a quarterback of the future probably won’t be available at pick ten, the offensive line is the next biggest need. Greg Robinson has been excellent for St. Louis, but the absence of Jake Long makes this pick all the more important.

 

  1. Minnesota Vikings- Trae Waynes, CB (MICHIGAN STATE)

 

If Adrian Peterson is traded away, Todd Gurley could be picked here. Otherwise, Trae Waynes could be the corner that solidifies the young Minnesota secondary. Wide receiver could be an option here, but the recent acquisition of Mike Wallace makes the need at corner greater.

 

  1. Cleveland Browns- Danny Shelton, DT (WASHINGTON)

 

The Browns have two first round picks and hope to hit on both of them unlike last year. Shelton will solidify the Cleveland defensive line and help a unit that was solid last year continue to grow.

 

  1. New Orleans Saints- Alvin Dupree, DE/OLB (KENTUCKY)

Dupree is an explosive player who can make all those around him better. The Saints have struggled on defense and have invested heavily in the secondary over the past two seasons. An investment in the front seven would go a long way in building a strong, complete defense,

  1. Miami Dolphins- DeVante Parker, WR (LOUISVILLE)

Ryan Tannehill could use a legitimate weapon to get the ball to. Mike Wallace never fully panned out in Miami and the Dolphins haven’t had a true number one receiver since Brandon Marshall left. Parker could be the top target that Tannehill longs for.

  1. San Francisco 49ers- Kevin Johnson, CB (WAKE FOREST)

The 49ers lost both of their starting corners this offseason to free agency, so there is a big hole at that position for them. Linebacker is an option due to the retirements of Chris Borland and Patrick Willis along with NaVarro Bowman’s uncertain health.

  1. Houston Texans- Nelson Agholor, WR (USC)

 

Losing Andre Johnson created a void at wide receiver for Houston. With uncertainty at quarterback, the Texans could use a solid weapon in the passing game.

 

  1. San Diego Chargers- La’el Collins, OL (LSU)

 

Whether the Chargers choose to trade or keep Rivers, protecting their quarterback is an area that needs to be improved. Collins has the versatility to play several positions and could be an immediate starter. Todd Gurley could also be an option here.

 

  1. Kansas City Chiefs- J. Humphries, OL (FLORIDA)

 

The Chiefs had offensive line issues throughout 2014 and Andy Reid has never been shy about drafting lineman. Humphries will help bolster the Kansas City line and make things easier on All-Pro back Jamaal Charles.

  1. Cleveland Browns- Todd Gurley, RB (Georgia)

 

Since the Browns can address their defensive need with their first pick, they have the opportunity to land a truly difference making back with this pick. Gurley is a special talent and if not for injury issues could go in the top five. If the he is there at 19, it should be an easy decision for Cleveland. Even though they have several young backs, Gurley is on another level.

 

  1. Philadelphia Eagles- Dorial Green-Beckham, WR (OKLAHOMA)

 

Losing both DeSean Jackson and Jeremy Maclin in the last year has created a major void for the Eagles. Chip Kelly is an excellent offensive coach, but you need talented players to will. Beckham would be the first wide-out taken if not for character questions. Kelly is not afraid to bring a player with question marks into his locker room and Beckham could be a star in Philadelphia.

 

  1. Cincinnati Bengals- Arik Armstead, DT (OREGON)

 

Geno Atkins was an excellent selection for the Bengals out of Georgia, but injury issues have limited his effectiveness the past two seasons. If he were to comeback fully healthy, pairing him with Armstead would make for a dominant interior. If not, Armstead is a good insurance policy to have.

 

  1. Pittsburgh Steelers- Landon Collins, S (ALABAMA)

 

Troy Polamalu probably should have retired several seasons ago, but now that he is gone the Steelers will need to add another safety. Collins is not the exact same type of player as Polamalu, but he would be an instant talent infusion in a secondary that has been disappointing for too long.

 

  1. Detroit Lions- Eddie Goldman, DT (FLORIDA STATE)

 

Losing both Ndamukong Suh and Nick Failrey is a major blow for the Lions defense. It is likely they will take multiple defensive tackles in this draft, with Goldman being the first.

 

  1. Arizona Cardinals- Shaq Thompson, LB (WASHINGTON)

 

Darnell Docket’s departure left a hole in the front seven that Thompson hopefully can fill. Todd Bowles being gone also means that Arizona’s defense will not be as s tout as in 2014, but drafting an athlete like Thompson will help.

 

  1. Carolina Panthers- Jake Fisher, OL (OREGON)

 

The Panthers have a major need at offensive line and will most likely take whoever they feel is the best offensive lineman available. That could be Fisher or Cameron Erving.

 

 

  1. Baltimore Ravens- Breshad Perriman, WR (CENTRAL FLORIDA)

 

Torrey Smith left in free agency and Steve Smith does not have many years left to help the Ravens passing game. A running back could be an option, but if the Ravens feel Justin Forsett will repeat his 2014 performance, then wide receiver will likely be the move here.

 

  1. Dallas Cowboys- Ronald Darby, CB (FLORIDA STATE)

 

The Cowboys reached for Morris Claiborne several years ago and that appears to be a disappointment. Dallas has done an excellent job drafting offensive talent in recent years and hope to have similar success on the defensive side. Darby would be a good start.

 

  1. Denver Broncos- Jake Fisher, OL (OREGON)

 

Peyton Manning’s health will be a question mark for the remainder of his career, which puts protecting him at an even higher position on the priority list. Defensive line could be a target here as well, but keeping Manning upright as much as possible should be Denver’s chief concern.

 

  1. Indianapolis Colts- Byron Jones, CB (UCONN)

 

The last two seasons have seen the Colts get run over by New England in the playoffs. Defensive line and secondary are a priority for Indianapolis and they could go either here. It is likely to be a defensive heavy draft for the Colts.

 

  1. Green Bay Packers- Eric Kendricks, ILB (UCLA)

 

Inside linebacker is a spot that the Packers desperately need to upgrade. Clay Matthews was forced to play inside at times last year but he is at his best rushing from the outside. Kendricks could be an immediate starter for the Packers.

 

  1. New Orleans Saints- Jordan Phillips, NT (OKLAHOMA)

 

The Saints received Seattle’s pick as part of the Jimmy Graham trade. They could continue their front seven investment by taking the best defensive tackle available in Phillips.

 

  1. New England Patriots- Cameron Erving, OL (FLORIDA STATE)

 

The defending champs struggles mightily through the first four weeks of the season and that was largely due to offensive line issues, specifically on the interior. Dan Connolly is still a free agent but even if he comes back, New England could use an upgrade at guard. The Patriots could also choose a corner or defensive tackles here, but Erving falls he is too good to pass up. Expect two of the Patriots first three picks to be defensive line and offensive line with a corner also in the mix.
