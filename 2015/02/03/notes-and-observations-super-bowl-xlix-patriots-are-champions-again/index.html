Notes and Observations, Super Bowl XLIX: Patriots are Champions Again!

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ February 3, 2015 at 10:00am ⋅ Post a comment

Super Bowl XLIX lived up to the hype and then some. Sunday night saw the match-up of the two best teams in the NFL go down to the wire to decide the champion. Every snap counted and every second felt like an eternity, which is exactly how the Super Bowl is supposed to be. As expected, it was nothing like last season which saw the Seahawks embarrass Peyton Manning and best offense in NFL history by the score of 43-8. This game was different because the Patriots are simply better than the rest of the NFL. They have the best owner, the best coach, and now without a shadow of doubt they have the greatest quarterback in the history of the NFL, Tom Brady. Despite all that it still took an unknown rookie making an improbable play to win the game. That is exactly why they are the best team in the NFL.

– Malcolm Butler is now a New England legend. Even if he never made a good play the rest of his career, no one will ever forget his name. You can blame the play call all you went, and yes it was an awful call, but Malcolm still needed to make the play. He read the route perfectly and timed his break precisely leading to the game-saving interception. On the Seahawks previous drive he helped break up the Jermaine Kearse drop on third down which could have sealed the game for Seattle. Of course we can’t forget that on the play before he defended as good as he possibly could have and Kearse still made that ridiculous catch. It was fitting that Butler was the one to make the clutch play after yet another miraculous Super Bowl catch. Butler looked great in the preseason as a rookie and ended up working his way onto the active roster consistently by out-practicing Alfonzo Dennard. Once Dennard went on IR there was no more question that Butler was their number four corner. Thankfully their confidence in Butler paid the highest dividend imaginable, the Lombardi trophy.

– Let me just say if Butler didn’t pick it off and the Seahawks won after yet another improbable, inexplicable, miraculous reception I don’t think I could have handled it. I guess the new tradition with Super Bowls the Patriots are in is the opposing team will have one of those as they drive late trying to take the lead. This time the defense was immensely better leading to the clutch play.

– The debate on who is the greatest quarterback of all time is now officially over. I thought it was already over but of course I may be a little biased. Now it is impossible to argue anyone other than Tom Brady. Yes he threw two interceptions but this Seahawks defense was arguably one of the best of all time. Instead of focusing on those two interceptions focus on the fact that Brady went 37-50 for 328 yards and four touchdowns. Those four touchdowns were thrown to four different receivers (Gronk, LaFell, Amendola, Edelman). As a team they only had 57 rushing yards meaning the Seahawks knew Brady had to pass. Even with everyone on that hard-hitting defense keying in on the pass, Brady was still able to shred them for his fourth Lombardi trophy and his third Super Bowl MVP. One year earlierÂ Peyton Manning, with literally the greatest offense in NFL history at his disposal, choked in the bright lights of the big game. Meanwhile Tom Brady with a bunch of nobodies, and finally a healthy Rob Gronkowski, dug down deep and led another game-winning, fourth quarter drive against the best defense in the league. If someone asks you “who would you take as your quarterback if you needed to win the big game?” and you don’t say “Tom Brady,” then you’re just wrong.

– Of course a ton of credit has to be given to the offensive line. There were really only two occasions where Brady saw significant pressure. That was the sack by Bruce IrvinÂ and when Michael Bennett rushed the pass that was intercepted by Jeremy Lane. They carried eight offensive lineman for the game to protect against any setbacks to Bryan Stork, but it wasn’t necessary as he played the whole game. The extra linemen did help though as the Patriots utilized Cameron Flemming as an eligible receiver frequently. The key to beating the Seahawks was always keeping Brady upright to allow him to read the defense and find the open man. Nate Solder, Dan Connolly, Bryan Stork, Ryan Wendell, and Sebastian Vollmer did exactly that. They received a ton of criticism this season, deservedly so at times, but they came together and had a tremendous year. As a whole they allowed 26 sacks this year which was down from the 40 they allowed last season. Keep in mind long-time offensive line coach Dante Scarnecchia retired after lastÂ season and Logan Mankins was traded in pre-season. The new offensive line coach Dave DeGuglielmo did a tremendous job coaching this group and they are a huge reason for Lombardi #4.

– What’s not to love about Julian Edelman? You could very easily make the case for him winning the Super Bowl MVP as he caught 9 passes for 109 yards and the go-ahead touchdown late in the fourth quarter. Everyone was wondering how the Patriots were ever going to replace Wes Welker when he left before the start of last season. Fast forward to Sunday night and not only did Edelman play a key role in winning the Super Bowl, but there is a very good chance Welker retires. Those who know Edelman all say he is one of the hardest working players on the team and it shows every single play. Like Welker, he has become Brady’s favorite target, but unlike Welker he didn’t drop the game winning catch in the Super Bowl. I’d say the Patriots made the right call.

– Also, I’m 99% sure Edelman was concussed after that 3rd and 14 conversion. He took a huge hit and out of instinct got up and crookedly ran for a few extra yards. Good thing he was able to shake the cobwebs loose and snag the game winning touchdown. I’m sure his head will be fine, nothing that a little smoothie can’t fix.

– A healthy Rob Gronkowski was a sight to see in the Super Bowl. I’ll never understand how teams still put a linebacker on him in one-on-one coverage. Once again Brady saw it before the snap and took advantage of it for a touchdown. Gronk made a couple of big first down catches down the stretch as well which helped set up the game winning score. After the countless injuries and surgeries he had his doubters who thought he could never be what he was. Gronk had a tremendous year, won the comeback player of the year award, and now has his first Super Bowl victory.

– Danny Amendola made some key plays and had a big touchdown that brought them within three points. I still don’t think he comes back next season unless he takes a big pay cut. If he does take the pay cut then I would love to have him back. He is definitely a valuable receiver, he’s just not worth what he’s being paid. Regardless he has made some big plays this season and was one of the reasons they were able to win it all.

– Vince Wilfork was the only other player other than Brady who had a Super Bowl ring. He nearly had his career ended last season when he tore his achilles tendon. For someone his size that is an incredibly difficult injury to rebound from but Vince made sure he was ready. Wilfork didn’t miss a game this year and was on the field for the majority of the defensive snaps. Chris Berman mentioned on Countdown that he wouldn’t be surprised if Vince hung up his cleats after this year. I think it’s definitely a possibility especially after winning it all, but I still think Wilfork has a lot left to offer and isn’t ready to call it a career just yet.

– Sunday morning reports leaked that the Bills and the Jets are going to be interested in Darrelle Revis if he becomes a free agent. Aside from the touchdown to Doug Baldwin made possible by the pick by the ref, Revis shut Baldwin down. That was his only catch of the game. Belichick knows how valuable Revis is and even if it takes the Patriots paying Revis the $20 million option, I think they do it. Revis makes the defense what it is and I see him being back for at least one more year.

– As a whole, the defense did a good job of containing Wilson. They utilized Jamie Collins and Rob Ninkovich as a spy the majority of the game and it worked. They did record three sacks, one each from Revis, Ninkovich, and Chandler Jones. There were a couple of big plays allowed to Chris Matthews and of course the outrageous Kearse catch. Those plays were covered well, the offense just made the play. Throughout the game the defense had a good scheme and the executed it well. The last four offensive drives for the Seahawks went punt, punt, punt, and the interception. With the game on the line the defense held strong and made the big play when they needed it most, just like they did all season long.

– The way this game went is exactly why it’s such a shame the media made it all about deflated footballs. I’ve said it before that this game’s story-line wrote itself and was more than enough to drum up media stories. Yet they constantly talked about an issue that the majority of the NFL players and coaches, past and present, said wasn’t a big deal. Watching all the pre-game coverage was sickening. Everyone had the best things to say about the Seahawks but when it came to the Patriots it was all about how their legacy will be tainted. Give me a break. There is nothing tainted about this legacy. Now all the geniuses crying “you haven’t won since spygate!” can kindly shut their mouths. Spygate was an overblown issue just like deflategate but neither of those have any impact on the tremendous legacy of Bill Belichick, Tom Brady, and the New England Patriots. The Patriots have consistently been one of the best teams in the NFL for the past 14 years because of superior coaching, a focus on building a complete team, preparation, and precise execution on game day. Air pressure in footballs and cameras focused on the opposition’s sideline have nothing to do with the fact that the Patriots are just flat out better than the rest of the league. Deal with it.

– I’ll never understand how you could be on the one, with the best running back in the league and you call for a pass. I also have no complaints about that play call at all. Maybe that will be enough to make Marshawn Lynch want out and then he can join the Patriots since he and Gronk are such good friends. A guy can dream.

– Sunday night’s Super Bowl victory was even better than 2001. This win showed that Brady is still competing at the highest level of his career even 10 years after his last Super Bowl win. It cements the legacy of Belichick and Brady as the greatest coach/quarterback combination in NFL history. As I’ve already stated it firmly cements Brady as the greatest quarterback of all time and now makes an even stronger case for Belichick as the greatest coach of all time. What this team has been able to do in the salary cap era is simply amazing and I’m just thankful I’ve been able to witness this run.

– This closes the book on yet another season and what a ride it was. As always I’ve had a ton of fun writing these weekly articles and I am very grateful that Sports of Boston provides me the platform to express my views. Thank you so much to any and all of you who have read these. I would write if no one read them but it’s awesome knowing there’s an audience. Whether you agree or disagree with my views I appreciate all of you taking time out of your day to read, it truly means a lot to me.

– We’re onto the parade!
