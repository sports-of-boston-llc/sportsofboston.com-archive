Danny the Magician: Ainge Making Celtics Disappear in Flurry of Trades

By Nick Bohlen in Celtics, Featured, Opinion ⋅ January 21, 2015 at 7:30am ⋅ Post a comment

And then there was one.

That’s how many players Danny Ainge will have kept in spite of all his flurry of in-season trades. (With plenty left to come, I’m sure.) One player out of five trades, including four in one week.

Just call him Danny the Magician. He makes newly acquired Celtics just…disappear.

The first trade of this year was the outlier. In a three-way trade with the Brooklyn Nets and Cleveland Cavaliers (maybe facilitating LeBron’s return to a .500 Cleveland team wasn’t such a bad move after all?), Ainge landed Tyler Zeller and Marcus Thornton, two key rotation players for the Celtics. Not bad.

(The Celtics, of course, also landed a draft pick in the deal, as in every other trade this year it seems, but I’m just going to focus on the players acquired for the purposes of this piece. The Celtics have a lot of picks, enough to land Ainge on an episode of Hoarders, and let’s just leave it at that.)

After that? Clearance sale. Everything must go!

Will Bynum was acquired from the Detroit Pistons only to be waived. We hardly knew ye!

In a separate trade with the Cavaliers, the Celtics acquired four players in exchange for Keith Bogans’s non-guaranteed contract. As expected and according to plan, three were immediately waived to create roster spots. The fourth, Dwight Powell, would be included with Rajon Rondo in the blockbuster trade with the Dallas Mavericks.

That trade, of course, netted the Celtics Brandan Wright (the token “centerpiece” of what the Mavericks sent back), Jae Crowder and Jameer Nelson.

The centerpiece, Wright, couldn’t find, talk or buy his way onto the court in his short stint with the Celtics, despite being among the league leaders in PER and shooting over 70% from the field. That’s probably because Ainge was preparing to trade him, which he did to the Suns for a first round pick. So much for the centerpiece.

Jae Crowder is the lone ranger who has managed to stick around and seems like he might actually do so. With Jeff Green gone (more on that in a minute), you’d think Crowder would be good size on the wing for the Celtics, but he is a restricted free agent after this year. As we’re learning, you never know with Ainge. Clearly.

Nelson was never really a fit because of the Celtics’ backcourt depth and his player option for next season. So he was out the door to the Denver Nuggets in exchange for old friend Nate Robinson.

Robinson would seem to fit the same profile as Nelson — a veteran with no on a team prioritizing minutes to give young wing players NBA experience. But so many memories! So much nostalgia! And that’s all it will be, as Ainge turned right around and bought out Robinson’s contract, leaving Donkey free to join a more successful team of his choosing. Such a shame.

And then there was the Green trade. Ainge cared so little about getting anyone in return that Tayshaun Prince, who the Celtics wanted for his expiring contract, played for the Memphis Grizzlies after the trade agreement had been reached. He is joining the Celtics for their West Coast swing, but will speaking with Ainge about his future. Considering he’s a veteran player on an expiring contract, it’s hard to imagine anything except a trade or a buyout agreement of his own in the near future.

Austin Rivers, the other non-pick portion of the deal, was shipped to L.A. as part of a three-team deal, forming the NBA’s first father-son coach-player duo. The return? A second-rounder (duh), Chris Douglas-Roberts (to be waived, double duh), and Shavlik Randolph from the Phoenix Suns (somehow he seems a safe bet to remain with Boston for the time being, if only as insurance for when Ainge inevitably unloads other front court players).

In fact, neither Robinson nor Rivers ever actually made it to Boston, but were traded or bought out before even joining the roster. So I suppose technically Danny the Magician didn’t even make them disappear if they never were around to begin with? (Side note: can you really say that Rivers played against his former team on MLK Day?)

For those of you keeping score at home, Ainge has acquired 15 players from other teams in the past year, and kept all of…five. That’s counting Prince, who may be on his way back out shortly, and Randolph, who is likely around simply so Ainge can make more Celtics players disappear.

(Heck, Ainge has enough draft picks over the next two years to draft an entire roster of rookies, if he were insane. Or Sixers GM Sam Hinkie.)

If there’s one thing we can be sure of, it’s that Danny isn’t done dealing. A lot of players on the roster are trade candidates (if Rondo and Green are gone, who isn’t?), and the only question remains are which ones will be dealt. I’m going on record right now saying Brandon Bass ends up elsewhere by the February trade deadline, if only because he used to play for the Orlando Magic, and that would just fit so perfectly with Danny’s disappearing act, wouldn’t it? Thornton is certainly an option, as is Evan Turner on a team-friendly contract.

As long as Ainge is getting picks and/or players with no salary committed beyond this year, anything is possible (s/o KG).
