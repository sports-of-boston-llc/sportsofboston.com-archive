The National Media is to Blame for Deflategate Outrage

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ January 24, 2015 at 10:49am ⋅ Post a comment

Only in America would every major national news station cover the press conference of a man discussing the inflation levels of some footballs. All this outrage has been drummed up by the media, which is not shocking in the least. Thanks to the 24-hour “news” cycle we get to listen to so-called experts speculate for hours without an ounce of actual evidence. Yet without any evidence, other than the fact 11 out of 12 Patriots’ footballs came in under-inflated, they are already to convict Bill Belichick and Tom Brady of this heinous crime against humanity. Last time I checked in America it’s innocent until proven guilty…Unless of course you’re the New England Patriots.

Let’s take it back to where it all started. Indianapolis sports writer Bob Kravitz was the one to break the news that a source tipped him off about the Patriots using under-inflated footballs during the AFC Championship. The tweet turned out to be true, and the NFL was in fact investigating the Patriots footballs. So far so good, we have a reporter make a claim that gets backed up by facts. That, unfortunately, is where the credibility ended.

Bob Kravitz then went on to tweet that “if Bob Kraft is a true man of integrity he will take it out of the league’s hands and fire Belichick.” Just like that Kravitz threw away all his credibility.

Just to clarify this particular sports journalist, without a shred of physical evidence as to who or what was the reason behind the balls being deflated, calls for one of the greatest coaches of all time to be fired for something he may not have had any part in. Thank you Bob Kravitz for starting the media avalanche of stupidity. No matter how this all turns out just know that you were the one who caused the first snowball to roll.

Since the story broke both Bill Belichick and Tom Brady held individual press conferences where they addressed the deflated bal—-footballs. Both have denied altering the balls in any way as well as having any knowledge of who or what did alter them. Whether you believe them or not isn’t the point, but with what we have been presented so far it is impossible to say who is responsible for this. But that doesn’t stop the media members like Jerome Bettis from calling the Patriots “felons.”

Adding more fuel to the case against the mainstream media is the fact that Mark Brunell was nearly brought to tears over this. A grown man who played in the NFL was nearly weeping on live television because some footballs came in two pounds per square inch under regulation. Are you serious Mark? Yemen is being overthrown by rebels and you have the audacity to sit in your cushy ESPN studio and nearly weep over air pressure in footballs. Brunell should be fired for this.

It wasn’t until Friday afternoon where the NFL finally released their statement about the investigation. One of the most startling pieces of information was that they’ve interviewed 40 people in regards to the issue, yet Tom Brady hasn’t been one of them. Which leads me to believe the NFL doesn’t really care about resolving this issue if they choose to not interview one of the main “culprits.” In the statement they stress that the investigation is ongoing and they will not make any judgments until it concludes.

On top of all of that D’Qwell Jackson, the Colts linebacker who intercepted Brady and was said to notified the officials of the air pressure issue, has come out and said none of that is true. He said he didn’t tip anyone off and just wanted the ball as a souvenir. To add to that the Ravens, who also were said to have tipped off the Colts about the footballs, said they didn’t notice anything either.

Even with the NFL saying they haven’t made any judgments, the Ravens and Colts saying they didn’t notice anything, the media has no problem putting all the blame on Brady and Belichick. We have reporters like Albert Breer saying it’s about a “pattern of behavior.”Â Of course by pattern he can only be talking about Spygate since every single Patriots detractor loves to throw that buzzword around anytime there’s a chance of controversy. How exactly is one event of wrongdoing a pattern? Have they done anything since? Have they done anything before? No they haven’t so by definition that is not a pattern. That is a one-off occurrence that the anti-Patriots crowd will never let die.

As you have seen by these examples, and if you’ve listened to sports radio or put it on any news outlet the past week, you can see how the media has blown this beyond out of proportion. NFL players and coaches, past and present have chimed in saying they can’t tell the difference in a football that’s two pounds PSI lower than regulation. Yet the media can’t fathom how Brady didn’t know the difference and can’t accept him saying he can’t tell as fact. Even the fact that the under-inflated balls were taken out at halftime and replaced with regulation balls, leading to improved play from Brady isn’t enough for these sharks.

The media has gone from reporting on facts to just outrageous speculation. Now we have Sal Paolantonio saying Brady only did the press conference to help him sell Uggs to women. Wait, what? First off Brady’s a spokesman for men’s Uggs, so contrary to your statement that men don’t buy Uggs, Sal, it appears that they in fact do. Just know that Sal makes more money than you or I will ever make, and he gets to spout outlandish statements as fact without an ounce of evidence backing them up.

So where does this leave us? Well we’re basically where we started. The NFL is still investigating and until that is finished we don’t know the actual truth behind any of this. Which is exactly why the media should not be speculating as much as they have been. Unfortunately in a world dominated by being the first to break the story, we’re stuck listening to a bunch of ignorant people spew their opinions and label them as facts.

Throughout all of this the most unfortunate detail is this potentially classic Super Bowl match-up is being brushed aside over two pounds PSI of air pressure. The story for this Super Bowl writes itself. You have the old guard of Tom Brady, Bill Belichick and the New England Patriots going up against the potential new dynasty of Russell Wilson, Pete Carroll and the Seattle Seahawks. You have the last team to repeat as Super Bowl champions going up against the new team that’s trying to accomplish that same feat. This is the match-up real football fans want to see and all the media can do is talk about air pressure in some footballs. Even worse than all that is no one is surprised at just how awful the media has been throughout.

If there is concrete evidence proving the Patriots did this and they knew about it, then by all means fine them, take away a draft pick, whatever the punishment may be. Until then everyone needs to relax and actually start talking about the Super Bowl.
