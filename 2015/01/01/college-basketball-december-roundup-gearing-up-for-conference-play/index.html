College Basketball December Roundup: Gearing Up for Conference Play

By Nick García in College, Featured, Top Story ⋅ January 1, 2015 at 7:14pm ⋅ Post a comment

Harvard

Record: 8–3 (2nd in Ivy League)

The Crimson got off to a good start in December, extending its win streak to six games with double-digit wins over Northeastern, Vermont and Boston University. The wheels came off, however, when Harvard visited #6 Virginia on December 21, a 76–27 dismantling that could not have been much uglier. Ten days later, the Crimson lost by 10 at Arizona State, but rebounded for a 13-point win over Grand Canyon to close out 2014. As the calendar turns, Harvard is once again in a tight battle with Yale for Ivy League supremacy. The Bulldogs currently have a half-game advantage but the Crimson has three games in hand.

Harvard ranks 32nd nationally in points allowed and 10th in blocks, but could be suffering from a lack of offensive options. The entire gameplan runs through shooting guard Wesley Saunders, who is averaging a team-high 17.7 points (9.1 more than any teammate) and 2.3 steals, and is second on the team in rebounds (6.1) and assists (4.1) per game. He has also been shooting the lights out at 52 percent from the field and 52.9 percent from beyond the arc.

Crimson January schedule: 01/05 vs. St. Rose (Div. II), 01/10 @ Dartmouth (6–6), 01/14 @ Boston College (7–4), 01/20 @ Bryant (3–6), 01/24 vs. Dartmouth (6–6), 01/30 @ Princeton (5–9), 01/31 @ Pennsylvania (3–7)

Northeastern

Record: 8–4 (2nd in Colonial Athletic Association)

After a surprising 5–1 start, Northeastern alternated wins and losses in December, including going 1–1 against Cal Poly and Santa Clara in the Cable Car Classic, one of the last early-season tournaments of the year. The Huskies are getting used to playing close games, with their biggest loss coming at Harvard by 14 points and their biggest margin of victory being 12 over Western Michigan. Northeastern is just half a game behind Hofstra in the Colonial standings.

The Huskies have gotten the job done by sharing the wealth. Forward Scott Eatherton leads the team with 15.5 points, 7.2 rebounds and 1.5 blocks per game. Shooting guard David Walker is adding 11.2 ppg and forward Quincy Ford 10.4 along with one steal per contest.

Huskies January schedule: 01/03 @ Delaware (1–10), 01/05 vs. UNC Wilmington (5–6), 01/08 @ James Madison (7–6), 01/10 @ Towson (7–6), 01/14 vs. Hofstra (9–4), 01/17 vs. College of Charleston (5–8), 01/19 vs. Detroit (6–8), 01/22 vs. James Madison (7–6), 01/24 @ William & Mary (6–5), 01/28 @ Drexel (2–9), 01/31 vs. Elon (8–5)

Boston College

Record: 7–4 (12th in Atlantic Coast Conference)

The Eagles had a very solid month of December, winning four straight over Marist, Providence, Main and Binghamton before taking a hard-fought four-point loss to USC. BC bounced back with an easy 23-point drubbing of UMass Lowell to end the year. Of course, the Eagles are still a long way from competing in the ACC, one of the toughest conferences in the nation, which has among its ranks #2 Duke, #3 Virginia, #5 Louisville, #14 Notre Dame and #19 North Carolina. Still a 7–4 record at this point is about as good as could be hoped.

Small forward Aaron Brown leads Boston College with 16.1 points per game, while guard Olivier Hanlan is adding 15.5, four boards and a team-high 4.2 assists and 2.3 steals.

Eagles January schedule: 01/03 @ #2 Duke (12–0), 01/06 vs. Pittsburgh (10–3), 01/10 @ Miami (FL) (10–3), 01/14 vs. Harvard (8–3), 01/17 vs. #3 Virginia (12–0), 01/20 @ Syracuse (9–4), 01/25 @ Georgia Tech (9–3), 01/28 vs. #5 Louisville (12–1), 01/31 @ Clemson (8–4)

Massachusetts

Record 7–6 (T-9th in Atlantic 10 Conference)

One year after a breakout 2013–14 season, UMass is struggling mightily so far in 2014–15. The Minutemen went 2–4 in December, with 20-point drubbings at the hands of LSU and Providence and an overtime loss at BYU. They did beat Canisius handily, 75–58, and will try to build on their year-ending five-point win over Iona. UMass is 3.5 games behind Dayton in the A-10, a conference that is not looking nearly as formidable as it has in recent years.

The Minutemen are putting up some good individual numbers. Forward Maxie Esho leads all scorers with 13.9 points per game to go with 6.5 rebounds. Center Cady Lalanne is averaging 13.1 points and a very solid 9.2 boards and 2.2 blocks. Guard Derrick Gordon is adding 12.1 points and 2.2 steals, and his backcourt mate Trey Davis is pitching in with 10.9 points and 3.8 assists of his own.

Minutemen January schedule: 01/03 vs. St. Bonaventure (7–4), 01/07 @ La Salle (8–5), 01/11 @ George Mason (5–7), 01/14 vs. Davidson (9–2), 01/17 vs. Rhode Island (8–3), 01/21 @ Saint Joseph’s (6–5), 01/29 vs. Dayton (10–2), 01/31 @ Saint Louis (8–5)

UMass Lowell

Record: 6–6 (T-3rd in America East Conference)

The month started well for the River Hawks, as they beat N.J.I.T. 71–67 to extend their winning streak to six games. Since then, however, UMass Lowell has lost four straight by double figures, to Cornell, Dartmouth, Duquesne and Boston College. Still, 6–6 is the third-best record in the weak America East, just one game worse than Stony Brook’s 8–6.

Guard Jahad Thomas is leading the team in scoring (14.8), rebounding (5.4) and field goal percentage (57.1).

River Hawks January schedule: 01/02 vs. Binghamton (1–14), 01/05 @ Brown (7–7), 01/07 @ UMBC (2–10), 01/10 vs. Albany (5–7), 01/13 @ Maine (1–11), 01/17 @ Hartford (7–6), 01/19 @ New Hampshire (6–6), 01/22 vs. Stony Brook (8–6), 01/25 vs. Vermont (5–7), 01/31 @ Binghamton (1–14)

Holy Cross

Record : 0–1 (5–6 overall, 9th in Patriot League)

The Patriot League was one of the first to begin its conference play. Boston University and Holy Cross faced off at the Hart Center, with the Terriers coming away with a 75–72 overtime win. That extended the Crusaders’ skid to three games and ended a 2–5 month in which they beat only Albany and N.J.I.T. Four of the five losses came by double digits.

Holy Cross has displayed good defense and ranks eighth nationally in steals per game and 41st in blocks, but it hasn’t been enough to get wins, largely due to a low-scoring offense. The team’s leading scorer, forward Malcolm Miller, is averaging just 12.7 ppg, along with 1.6 bpg and 1.5 spg. Point guard Justin Burrell leads the Crusaders with 4.1 assists and 2.1 steals to go with his 11 points.

Crusaders January schedule: 01/03 @ American (7–6), 01/07 @ Colgate (4–10), 01/10 vs. Bucknell (6–8), 01/14 vs. Army (9–3), 01/17 @ Lehigh (6–6), 01/21 vs. Lafayette (9–3), 01/24 vs. Navy (5–8), 01/28 @ Loyola (MD) (4–8)

Boston University

Record: 1–0 (5–7 overall, 3rd in Patriot League)

BU went 4–3 in December, starting with a 12-point win over Binghamton and ending with the OT victory at Holy Cross. That win made the Terriers one of five 1–0 teams in Patriot League play, where Lafayette and Bucknell have better overall records.

BU’s offense runs entirely through guard Cedric Hankerson, who is averaging 18.8 points, a full nine more than the next-highest scorer, along with five boards, 2.6 assists and 1.8 steals.

Terriers January schedule: 01/03 vs. Lehigh (6–6), 01/07 @ Navy (5–8), 01/10 vs. Lafayette (9–3), 01/14 @ Colgate (4–10), 01/18 @ Loyola (MD) (4–8), 01/21 vs. American (7–6), 01/24 vs. Bucknell (6–8), 01/29 @ Army (9–3), 01/31 2 Lehigh (6–6)
