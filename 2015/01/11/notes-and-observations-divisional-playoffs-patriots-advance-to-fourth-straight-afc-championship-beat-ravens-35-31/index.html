Notes and Observations Divisional Playoffs: Patriots Advance to Fourth Straight AFC Championship, Beat Ravens 35-31

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ January 11, 2015 at 7:19pm ⋅ Post a comment

What an incredible game we all witnessed Saturday night. From start to finish, as a fan, you didn’t have a second to catch your breath. Every snap mattered and in the end it was a couple key plays that turned the tide of the game. The Ravens have been one of the few teams that has been able to consistently challenge the Patriots and you knew things were going to be no different Saturday night. Joe Flacco has become a monster in the postseason, and their defense has always had the ability to disrupt Tom Brady. While Flacco did show up to play, with the exception of his two interceptions, the pass rush was almost non-existent. Brady had time which allowed him to have a terrific game, including a classic Tom Brady game-winning, fourth quarter drive.Â 

– This is the type of game that all the purists who claim “you need to run the football to win in January,” absolutely hate. The Patriots had no running game at all. As a team they had a total of 14 yards on 13 carries. Brady’s touchdown scramble in the first was the most impressive run they had all game, and it didn’t matter. The Ravens strength is in their front seven. Their run defense is terrific, but where they are weak is their secondary. With an incredible game from the Patriots offensive line, Brady was able to pick apart the weak Ravens secondary.

– Tom Brady turned in a classic performance on Saturday night. After his lackluster performance in the 2012 AFC Championship against the Ravens you just know he wanted this more than anyone and it showed. He was fired up from the player intros until the very end. With the entire defense knowing he was dropping back to pass he shredded them to the tune of 33-50 for 367 yards and three touchdowns. As mentioned above he also ran for the first touchdown of the game which was an early indication that Brady wasn’t messing around. His go-ahead touchdown pass to Brandon LaFell in the fourth quarter was a thing of beauty. He released the ball when LaFell was at the 18 and he caught it right in his “bread basket” at the two. LaFell was covered well but Brady put the ball in the perfect location and that was the ball game.
– As great as he was, he wasn’t perfect. The second and last sack of the game for the Ravens came with about nine minutes left in the second quarter. That sack pushed the Patriots out of field goal range. Brady knows he can’t take that sack there and it cost them points. Then of course there is the awful interception at the end of the first half. He had Gronk with a step on the defense, but instead of lofting it he threw a line drive which Daryl Smith intercepted. Flacco then led them for a last second touchdown to Owen Daniels. Instead of the Patriots being up 21-14 with the ball at half, it was the Ravens with that score advantage.
– You knew that somewhere in that offensive playbook there was an option pass designed for Julian Edelman. I’ve always wondered why they never tried it because of his college quarterback background. The answer was they were saving it for the right moment. Saturday night was the perfect time to unleash that play. They were desperate and needed something to bring that momentum back. The execution was perfect. Gronk had the block on the edge to allow Edelman the time. It also helped the defense bit on a screen to Edelman which allowed Danny Amendola to coast pass them. Of course there was the GEM of a pass from Edelman. He threw a perfect spiral that Amendola was able to catch in stride. That play tied the game at 28-28 and stole all the momentum.
– Credit where credit is due to Danny Amendola. He has been criticized his entire time here, myself included, but he made two huge plays which helped win the game. There was of course the touchdown catch from Edelman, but he was responsible for their second touchdown of the game. Amendola caught the short out from Brady, broke a tackle, and then dove over the pylon for the score. In the playoffs you need big plays from guys who don’t always make them, and that’s exactly what Amendola did on Saturday night.
– Having a healthy Rob Gronkowski in the playoffs is such a difference maker. It’s shocking that the Ravens didn’t try getting more physical with him off the line of scrimmage because he was tearing them up. On his long grab up the seam where the world saw his butt, for those keeping score that’s two Gronk-butt sightings this season, he was untouched until his pants were halfway down his butt. His touchdown catch was another case of Brady looking at him, calling out the quick slant inside, and then hitting him. Brady told the defense he’s passing to Gronk and there was absolutely nothing they could do about it. That’s when you know you’re dominant.
– I really hope Bryan Stork isn’t injured. He’s been a rock of a center all season which is very impressive for a rookie. That being said, even with him out of the game the offensive line was impressive. They only allowed two sacks the whole game which were on back-to-back plays in the second quarter. Brady was rushed only a hand full of times and that was because of the strong offensive line play. With Stork out Ryan Wendell moved to center and Josh Kline played right guard. Wendell’s past center experience allowed him to step in and not miss a beat. Kline had struggled at left guard the last two games of the season, but at right guard he played very well. Considering the Ravens knew the Patriots were going to pass all game, there was no pass rush at all. Great performance from the line.
– Defensively the Patriots had a bit of a letdown to say the least. They just didn’t look sharp for the majority of the game. Darrelle Revis was beat early on the Steve Smith touchdown, and had two uncharacteristic penalties. Still a “bad game” for Revis is still better than 99% of cornerbacks in the league “good games.” After the Smith touchdown he wasn’t heard from much at all the rest of the game. Revis’ pass interference call late was a bit of a ticky-tack call, but we’ve all come to expect spotty officiating.
– Jamie Collins did have a very strong game though. He led the team in tackles with 11 and narrowly missed a couple of interceptions. He continues to show the ability to be around the ball and has turned out to be a very sound tackler. It’s hard not to be excited for the future when watching Collins and Dont’a Hightower.
– I’m not terribly worried about the defense at all. Yes they were pretty flat but the Ravens, especially Joe Flacco, are like an entirely different team in the playoffs. Flacco was nearly perfect early on starting the game completing his first eight passes. They’re a mentally tough team who can make big plays. The big thing is the defense stopped them when they had to at the end. More important than that is the defense made the big plays when they had to.
– The Rutgers trio of Devin McCourty, Duron Harmon, and Logan Ryan all played a major role in the win. McCourty had the first interception which unfortunately didn’t lead to any Patriots points, but it did keep points away from the Ravens. Logan Ryan had to come in when Brandon Browner went out with an injury and he played well. He didn’t make any interceptions or anything out of the ordinary, but he covered his assignment as well as you could have hoped. Ryan playing well is a great sign since he struggled towards the end of the season. Last but certainly not least Duron Harmon had the interception at the end of the game which sealed it. Ryan was covering Torrey Smith with Harmon over the top. Ryan led Smith towards the sideline and Flacco’s throw was more towards the numbers. Harmon read it and made the play. McCourty topped it off with the tip of the hail Mary at the end.
– John Harbaugh complaining about being outsmarted by Bill Belichick is exactly why I love Belichick so much. He just knows so much more about football from countless hours of film study that when he pulls a rare but perfectly legal play out it just puts his opponent’s mind into a pretzel. It’s laughable that Harbaugh said with confidence that the league would review the play and make adjustments. Well John the league came out today and said the play is legal. Stop crying and take the loss like a man.
– Terrell Suggs was a virtual non-factor. I think it’s funny how the Ravens never say the names of their opponents because they’re apparently in fifth grade. Well on Saturday nobody said Suggs’ name.
– As stressful as that game was I’d much rather have it gone the way it did than a blowout. The defense struggled, they had no running game, and were down by 14 points on two separate occasions. Despite all that they showed incredible resiliency and overcame both deficits. Brady looked like the comeback kid of his early career and the defense made the big plays when they had to. The Ravens are as tough as they come and always bring it to the Patriots. They pushed them to the brink but Brady and the gang would not go quietly into the dark night. Who would want to go up against these guys?
– We’re onto the AFC Championship for the fourth straight year!
