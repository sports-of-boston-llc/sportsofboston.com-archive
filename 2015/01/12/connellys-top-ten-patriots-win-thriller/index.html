Connelly’s Top Ten: Patriots win THRILLER

By Michael in Featured, Now!, Top Story ⋅ January 12, 2015 at 6:43am ⋅ Post a comment

1. PATRIOTS ADVANCE TO AFC CHAMPIONSHIP:

MISC

* I haven’t been that nervous viewing a sporting event since Game 4, 2004 – Red Sox vs. Yankees extra innings

* Second greatest Patriot game behind the Snow Bowl

* The Blue hoodie has always been a dangerous choice – 7-4 record

* I can’t believe the Patriots made that insane mistake of mis calculating the clock at the end of the game – I would have the punter Allen take a safety and run around the end zone and then kicked off – I know it would have made it a field goal game (would have been the biggest coaching blunder in Boston sports history, see #6)

* Goddell shouldn’t be wearing a hat with a B on it at a Baltimore – Pats game

* Matter of time before Gronk’s pants get pulled down from behind on national TV and his second career gets a kick start

* Brady might be one of the toughest players to ever play in New England

* Patriots 16-4 at home in the playoffs

* Boy are we spoiled! NINTH conference championship since 2002

* Patriots get us at least another week closer to Spring Training – what a vital member of our community

* Any other coach that ran Vereen ineligible receiver would have been called a genius because they were creative within the rules – but everyone wants to hint to the spirit of cheating

OFFENSE

* Proof positive the double pass validates the power of creativity. Â Great call on Edelman pass and caught a break with Baltimore in corner blitz BUT why hasn’t he thrown 15 times? – see Andy Johnson.

* If they lost, Brady would have been killed for his terrible interception at the end of the half

* NO runs in the second half

* Knew the Patriots were in trouble when newly signed Bolden ran out of bounds on the first play from scrimmage – disgraceful!

* Three-and-out on the first drive of both halves

* No screens, end-arounds (I hope McDaniels gets hired)

* Middle of the offensive line horrendous again – Vollmer and Solder were superb – never knew Dumervil was on the field and hardly saw Suggs

* Missed Jonas Gray getting to the hole fast

* Good catch by Amendola and effort to the end zone on first TD

* Hooman – four catches, wow – he had three all year!

* Gotta get points off that interception

* I’m not kidding, Brady is moving so well – I would put a quarterback draw into the game plan for down inside the ten-yard line

* Big first down by Edleman on winning drive, stretching ball forward on sideline

DEFENSE

* Horseshi* game plan of not bringing pressure – 1,000 times its been said – gotta bring pressure on these guys – Flacco just sitting back there

* Arrington – great game

* I can’t believe that the defense wasn’t ready for the play-action bootleg – they must have got 100 yards on that play and it was WIDE open

* Wilfork was getting blown off the line

* Ravens ran right at Chandler Jones – he did nothing all game

* When I was writing notes in the third quarter – this was what the title to the Top Ten was going to be – “Revis Island Makes Patriots Castaways” – Took a terrible time to have the worst game of his career

KRAFT SIGHTINGS

* 4:44 of the first quarter – he had a quiet game

OFFICIALS

* Interference on Revis but not on player covering Gronk on the first drive of second half

* Never seen the QB get hit so much after releasing the ball and once pile driven without a penalty

* Penalty for a guy yelling on a punt return but not for kneeing Brady to the ground

* No doubt Patriots alerted the officials to that substitution play before the game

BROADCAST

* Collinsworth was awesome at seeing the game and articulating it

SPECIAL TEAMS

* Twice Patriots punt while inside Baltimore 40 and got net 20 yards on each

2. AROUND THE NFL:

SEATTLE – CAROLINA – The only reason I loved this game is the thought – At every showcase/camp and on every recruiters clip board and every draft board – Russell Wilson didn’t have the measurables that Cam Newton had and thus was cast aside. But that game showed the power of someone with smarts and works on his craft as opposed to a spoiled over-rated athlete who throws off his back foot and who puts more thought into his touchdown dance than his footwork

GREEN BAY – DALLAS – If that was the Patriots that got screwed by that “lack of control” on Dez Bryant’s catch and then leap to the end zone, they would be talking about it for fifteen years just like the tuck rule nonsense

DENVER – INDY – Manning nine times one and out in the playoffs / 11-13 career record / Welker is an afterthought – one catch / 2-3 record for Denver in the playoffs, which is not as good as Tebow’s 1-1 / It now comes down to Gronk and not letting Andrew Luck beat you with his legs

REX RYAN – Bad news for Patriots with the foot man going to Buffalo – he has Bill’s number and already great defensive talent up there

OFFICIAL CHALLENGES – From Fox – Patriots and Atlanta the only teams not to win a challenge this year

INCIDENTAL CONTACT – If you’re getting beat by a receiver, why not try to get your feet tangled – they don’t call that

8. Old School:

In honor of John Harbaugh not knowing the rulebook:

In my book Rebound, I have a chapter called “Read the Rules!” That’s what Auerbach yelled at the New York Knicks owner Ned Irish at the 1953 draft after he complained about Auerbach selecting National Champion Kentucky’s top three players Frank Ramsey / Cliff Hagan / Lou Tsiorpoulous using the draft eligible loop hole (same he used to get Bird). Ramsey and Hagan became Hall of Famers, with Hagan used to trade for Bill Russell
