Connelly’s Top Ten: Patriots Haters Unite for Deflate-Gate

By Michael in Featured, Now!, Top Story, Uncategorized ⋅ January 23, 2015 at 7:43am ⋅ Post a comment

1. Deflate-gate:

* I would bet at least 50% of the quarterbacks do something to the football and our just happy that they didn’t get caught

* I think the Colts know it’s a common practice but were trying gamesmanship to bother Brady (like the Red Sox checking Albert Belle’s bat)

* Paul Perillo wondered if this why the Patriots were running reverses and throwing bombs at the end of the game because they were trying to give snitches stitches.

* It was a cold handshake between coaches

* Ironically, if John Haubaugh was whining about the footballs – Bill Belichick gave the key reference to get him the job at Baltimore (Baltimore should be the last team complaining about integrity with their accomplices to murder statue, illegal trade between brothers, woman beaters (that’s plural), five arrests in 2014)

* The officials couldn’t feel the difference so there is no way the intercepting linebacker recognized the difference. Colts were waiting for a turnover to get a ball so they could affirm the impression they had after their November 16th game in Indy. Thus two weeks later during Patriots – Packers game, Phil Simms and Jim Nantz spoke about Aaron Rodgers’ preference for ball pressure. This struck me as a strange subject but now it retrospect air pressure must have been topical in NFL circles because of the Colts accusations and therefore the subject came up during the pre-game meeting between CBS and Rodgers

* Obviously Belichick only threw Brady under the bus after Brady, Kraft, PR firm and lawyers talked it over with the coach on strategy

* Troy Aikman is a supposed neutral national broadcaster calling for a worse penalty than the Saints who were trying to maim people should get him censure

3.  Some cheating I found in old Globe articles and Sports Illustrated:

* Denver Broncos Super Bowl teams were fined and sanctioned after significant violations of salary cap allowing them to keep players leading to their 1997 and 1998 Super Bowl

* Leigh Montville wrote an article in 1977 before the Super Bowl: Gene Upshaw: “what’s cheating. You only cheat if you get caught.” Alan Page: “On a percentage basis, I’d say 100% of all pro players cheat sometimes.”

* Will McDonough 9/11/78: Redskins accused of having the Patriots playbook leading to their upset victory

* Bart Starr was fined for using the injured reserve improperly

* 49ers were found to have abused the salary cap rules with Steve Young allowing the player to remain on payroll during Super Bowl seasons

* Chuck Noll was caught using shoulder pads in a practice – no contact was allowed – it was called Shoulder Gate

* Don Shula playing with game films

* Redskins in 1987 hiding positive drug tests including three for Dexter Manley on their way to Super Bowl win

* Ron Borges in 1999 wrote an article on kickers microwaving footballs in follow up to Nick Lowery’s attack of a ball boy for not warming his football

* 1977 Raiders accused of having Pittsburgh’s game plan leaked by bitter assistant coach
