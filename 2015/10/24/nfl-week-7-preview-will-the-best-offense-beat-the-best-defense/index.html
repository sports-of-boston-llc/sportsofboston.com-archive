NFL Week 7 Preview: Will the Best Offense Beat the Best Defense?

By Marius in Patriots ⋅ October 24, 2015 at 11:19am ⋅ Post a comment

NFL Week 7 is beginning, and only 10 teams hold winning records – five of them are still unbeaten. The Green Bay Packers, Cincinnati Bengals and Denver Broncos are the three 6-0 teams, but all of them will take a bye week while New England Patriots and Carolina Panthers will battle against tough opponents to try and keep the winning streak.

At the top of the most power rankings you can find the reigning champions – the Patriots. Will they hold their ground against the impressive New York Jets defense? The Panthers are set for an interesting game, as they will host unpredictable Philadelphia Eagles.

Week 7 started with the NFC West showdown in the Bay Area. San Francisco 49ers hosted a game against Seattle Seahawks. For the last three years, one of these two teams represented the NFC in the Super Bowl, but the times have changed as both teams came into the week 7 with a negative 2-4 record. Even during an away game, the Seahawks lived up to the expectations and shut the 49ers’ offense down to a single field goal in a 20-3 victory.

However, the best action of Week 7 is yet to come during the Sunday matches:

New York Jets (4-1) @ New England Patriots (5-0)

The Super Bowl champion New England Patriots look unstoppable with the perfect 5-0 start. Their offence is best in the league with an average of 36.6 points per game, and quarterback Tom Brady has the best passer rating (118,4).

On the other hand, a few NFL experts have pointed out that the Patriots had a relatively easy schedule so far. Out of the five teams that gave in to the Patriots, only their Week 1 opponents, the Pittsburgh Steelers, now have a winning record. The upcoming match against the AFC East eternal rivals, the New York Jets, will be the best test for the Patriots.

The Jets made a lot of changes after the last disastrous season (4-12) and it is already clear that it’s paying off. While the former Jets’ head coach Rex Ryan is still struggling to get to a winning streak with his new team Buffalo Bills, the Jets are 4-1 under the lead of the former Arizona Cardinals’ defensive coordinator Todd Bowles.

The 15.0 points per game allowed average means that the Jets have the best defense in the NFL. They also have one of the best offensive lines in the league as they did an impressive job while defending the quarterback Ryan Fitzpatrick who has only been sacked twice this season – the least in the NFL.

In the NFL, the best defense usually wins against the best offence but this time it is hard to believe it will be the case as the overall Patriots’ performance is a lot more impressive than the Jets’. It will be really hard for the guests from New York to stop Brady’s army.

Prediction and pick: TonyBet is expecting a Patriots’ victory with a nine-point difference but we aren’t sure that the home team will be this dominant. Yes, Patriots are the clear favorites to win but the game may end with a much closer result.

New Orleans Saints (2-4) @ Indianapolis Colts (3-3)

These two teams have two things in common: an elite quarterback and a disastrous start of the season.

The Colts were often described as Super Bowl contenders this preseason, but the team lost its first two matches. They were pretty close to a third loss, but quarterback Andrew Luck created a miraculous comeback in the fourth quarter against the Tennessee Titans, achieving the first victory and the 1-2 record.

It looked like the Colts’ season was doomed as Luck wasn’t able to play in the next two games, but the veteran quarterback Matt Hasselbeck replaced him, which resulted in winning both games. Luck came back last week and showed a solid performance (312 passing yards and 3TD) but it was not enough to win against the Patriots on their own turf. Still, with the 3-3 record, the Colts are on the top of the AFC South division.

New Orleans’ story is relatively similar. While they weren’t considered as favorites during the preseason, no one knew what to expect out of all the NFC South teams. The Saints also lost their first two games and star quarterback Drew Brees missed Week 3’s matchup against the Carolina Panthers because of a shoulder injury.

The team’s second-string quarterback Luke McCown wasn’t able to replace Brees properly and the team fell to 0-3. Brees came back during Week 4, getting the team’s first victory against Dallas Cowboys. The Saints fell down to 1-4 when they lost their Week 5 match on the road against Philadelphia Eagles, but then made a loud statement when they held their ground against the 5-0 divisional rivals Atlanta Falcons, beating them at 31-21.

Drew Brees threw over 300 yards in 4 of 5 games he played this season and during the last three matches, he threw five touchdowns with only one interception. The outcome of this week’s game will highly depend on the quarterback’s performance. A Luck and Brees duel is a guarantee of an interesting game.

Prediction and pick: TonyBet prediction is Colts’ victory by 4-5 points and we support that guess. The Saints have bounced back after the rough start but they are a team who plays better at home. Now they have to battle the Colts in Indianapolis and it may be a mission impossible type of situation.

Dallas Cowboys (2-3) @ New York Giants (3-3)

This one is another classical NFC East opponents’ rivalry. The teams already faced each other in the season opener in Dallas, when the home team won 27-26 after some bad time management by the Giants quarterback Eli Manning. However, it was an expensive victory for the Cowboys as they’ve lost their star wide receiver Dez Bryant.

They won Week 2 match against Philadelphia Eagles but, the quarterback Tony Romo fractured his collarbone and his replacement Brandon Weeden didn’t look well at all, resulting in three lost matches. The Cowboys had a bye last week and they signed former Buffalo Bills’ quarterback Matt Cassel who will start in the match against Giants.

Meanwhile the Giants are leading this division (together with the Eagles) but the Cowboys are just one win behind. The Giants were leading in both of the first two games but ended up losing because of poor time management and started with a 0-2 record. The team made up with three wins in a row but last week they were crushed by the Eagles 27-7 in Philadelphia and now in this very important divisional matchup they are seeking victory.

Prediction and pick: TonyBet prediction is Giants’ victory by 3-4 points and it seems about right. Giants are still without their talented wideout Victor Cruz but it cannot be compared to the Cowboys loses due to injuries so the home team must clinch the victory.

Philadelphia Eagles (3-3) @ Carolina Panthers (5-0)

Carolina Panthers are mentioned as a potential Super Bowl team. Even after the 4-0 start, there were a lot of doubts and before the Week 6 matchup on the road against the Seattle Seahawks, they were considered underdogs, but the Panthers proved everyone wrong when they beat the Seahawks in an impressive fourth-quarter comeback.

The unquestionable team leader is quarterback Cam Newton, who sometimes seems unstoppable. Since 2011, Newton has rushed for 36 touchdowns – he is third in the league, only behind the star running backs Marshawn Lynch and Adrian Peterson. Newton alone can compensate for the lack of talent on the Panthers’ offense.

On the other hand, the Philadelphia Eagles are no push-overs. The team is led by the head coach Chip Kelly, who is considered as one of NFL’s masterminds and innovators. The Eagles struggled at the start of season with the 1-3 record after four weeks, but during the last two weeks we saw a different team.

They achieved two unquestionable victories in a row against New Orleans Saints (39-17) and New York Giants (27-7) and the Eagles have already proved that they can win on the road against tough opponents, when in the Week 3’s match they beat the league’s best at defending, the New York Jets (who are now 4-1).

Prediction and pick: TonyBet prediction is Panthers’ victory by 3-4 points. It’s pretty hard to predict the outcome of this match but technically, the home team’s chances to win are better.

Other Week 7 matches:

Buffalo Bills (3-3) @ Jacksonville Jaguars (1-5) – There are rumors that in this decade one NFL team will relocate to London and it will most likely be the Jaguars, who are playing in the UK capital every year and have a strong support among the locals. The Jaguars and the Bills matchup in the legendary Wembley Stadium will start this NFL Sunday. Despite the support, the Jaguars’ chances against the stronger Bills are pretty bleak.

Oakland Raiders (2-3) @ San Diego Chargers (2-4) – It’s an AFC West battle for the second place in the division. Last week, the Chargers lost an away match at Green Bay despite quarterback Phillip Rivers throwing for 502 yards, 2TD and no interceptions. The Raiders are young and talented, but the lack of experience may consequentially result in a loss.

Tampa Bay Buccaneers (2-3) @ Washington Redskins (2-4) – No. 1 pick Jameis Winston is struggling, but the team found a way to the victory last week against Jacksonville Jaguars. Still, this week they are facing solid Redskins’ defense and it will be hard to expect a positive outcome.

Atlanta Falcons (5-1) @ Tennessee Titans (1-4) – Last week, the Falcons dropped from the undefeated teams list with an unexpected loss to the divisional rivals New Orleans Saints. The Birds are angry and they are simply a better team than the Titans. It would be a miracle if rookie quarterback Marcus Mariota would save Titans from their fifth loss.

Minnesota Vikings (3-2) @ Detroit Lions (1-5) – After five weeks, the Lions were the only team without any victories (0-5), but not anymore. An overtime victory at home against divisional rival Chicago was exactly what this team needed. They have talent on both sides of the ball and they must prove it this Sunday once again at home against other NFC North team Minnesota Vikings. The Vikings are 3-2, but their weakness is one-sided offense based heavily on the running game.

Pittsburgh Steelers (4-2) @ Kansas City Chiefs (1-5) – In Week 3, Steelers lost their starting quarterback Ben Roethlisberger and last week his replacement QB Michael Vick also got injured, so the offense will be directed by the third stringer QB Landry Jones. He did surprisingly well in relief of Vick, and some are saying that Vick’s injury was a positive thing for the team.

There is a chance that Roethlisberger will be back already this weekend, but his status remains unclear.

The Kansas City Chiefs lost their star running back Jamaal Charles to the season-ending injury and they are struggling in every component of the game. It doesn’t matter with which QB will play, as the Steelers should clinch the victory in the Arrowhead Stadium in any case.

Cleveland Browns (2-4) @ St. Louis Rams (2-3) – While Browns sophomore quarterback Johnny Manziel is in the newspapers because of the fight with his girlfriend, the starting quarterback Josh McCown have proven that he is the man. He already threw for 1,416 yards and 8TD with only 3 interceptions this season. Still, it will be a hard task for the Browns to win on the road against St. Louis Rams’ solid defense.

Houston Texans (2-4) @ Miami Dolphins (2-3) – After a Week 4 loss in London, the Miami Dolphins changed their head coach, had some rest in the bye week and last week they rebounded with a solid victory against Tennessee Titans (38-10). No one doubts the talent on their squad but this week’s match against the struggling Texans will show if they still can rebound this season.

Baltimore Ravens (1-5) @ Arizona Cardinals (4-2) – Week 7 will end with the Monday Night Football matchup in Phoenix, where the Arizona Cardinals will host the Baltimore Ravens. The Cardinals are constantly mentioned as one of the best teams this season, while the Ravens are clear outsiders. In the battle of the birds this time the Ravens stand no chance.
