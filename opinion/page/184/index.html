Heated Rivalry: Top Five Patriots-Jets Games

October 7, 2011 at 3:30pm in Featured, Opinion, Patriots
zach writes
One comment

Since they each entered the upstart American Football League in 1960 the New England Patriots and New York Jets have been rivals, just from a location standpoint.

To put things into perspective, when both teams began they were called the Boston Patriots and the New York Titans respectively. But while the two teams represented cities that generally disliked each other, mainly from their baseball rivalry, there was no real animosity between them until 1997.

This was when legendary coach Bill Parcells, fresh off a Super Bowl appearance with the Patriots, defected to the New York Jets. From then on rivalry has developed into one of the most heated in the NFL.

2011-2012 NHL Expert Picks

October 6, 2011 at 8:06pm in Bruins, Featured, NHL, Now!, Opinion
John writes
Add a comment

The NHL season is upon us already, and the Bruins are ready to start their title defense. And Winnipeg is back in the NHL after Atlanta relocated. They’ll be in the Southeast this year until the NHL decides on how to realign the league. So it’ll be interesting to see how the new travel routes affect all the teams.

With the new season here, us here at Sports of Boston have brought you our selections for said season in the latest NHL Pick ‘Em. No New York writers are joining us this time, probably because they’re boycotting the champions from Boston. The Bruins and Capitals are popular picks, but the dangerous Canucks still have the most votes of confidence. Unlike the new Winnipeg Jets and the cellar dwelling Oilers, mind you.

In this competition, each panelist gets one point for correctly guessing a team to make the playoffs, or two points for successfully guessing a division winner. Guessing the President’s Trophy Winner for the NHL’s best team is worth two points. As for the fun part, correctly guessing the #1 seed in each conference exactly is worth eight points, then seven points for the #2 seed and so on. The tiebreaker is guessing which team will be the NHL’s worst, then second worst, then third.
