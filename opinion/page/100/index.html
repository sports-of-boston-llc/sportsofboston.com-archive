2013 MLB Playoffs Expert Picks: World Series

October 23, 2013 at 7:06pm in Featured, MLB, Now!, Opinion, Red Sox, Top Story
John writes
Add a comment

The World Series is finally here, and the Boston Red Sox look to consummate their worst-to-first turnaround against perennial contender St Louis. Coming off great pitching performances and clutch hitting, these two teams look to make this series one for the ages no matter who wins.

While most of our panelists don’t think pitching will be MVP-worthy in this series, it does look to be a long and fun series. With the end of the season near, would anyone expect any less?

For this final series, we’re introducing a new scoring category; experts will be guessing how many home runs will be hit in the final game by both sides combined. And in addition, ALL POINTS ARE DOUBLED!!! That means picking the winner of a series is worth four points, and guessing how many games the series will last and the MVP of the series is worth two point. Ditto for the combined HR total in the final game.

In the event of a tie, we’ll also be guessing the combined walks-plus-hits-per-innings-pitched of LCS MVPs Michael Wacha and Koji Uehara over the course of the World Series. Their walks and hits given up over the series will be pooled, as will their innings pitched, and the former sum will be divided by the latter.

Oh By The Way: John Farrell Should Win AL Manager of the Year

October 14, 2013 at 6:03pm in Featured, Opinion, Red Sox, Top Story
Brian Roach writes
Add a comment

Forget his success thus far in the playoffs. John Farrell has a huge case for AL Manager of the Year. This is thanks to a worst-to-first regular season finish for the Boston Red Sox after winning the AL East in 2013 with a 97-65 record. This was the best record in the American League after the Red Sox finished with the 69-93 record under Bobby Valentine last year.

The one thing Farrell got in 2013 was a commitment from almost all 40 players that could be used all season long that Valentine did not get during his one-year tenure with the club. Valentine did not get much respect from the starting pitching or the likes of Dustin Pedroia, David Ortiz and even Kevin Youkilis disapproved of what the former Red Sox manager was trying to do with the younger guys on the squad. Guys like Will Middlebrooks and Felix Doubront were trying to find their way on to the team and showed exactly how deep the farm system was for the Red Sox, but Valentine could not get much out of these guys.
