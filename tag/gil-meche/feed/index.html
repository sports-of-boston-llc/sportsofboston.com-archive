The Boston Red Sox finished off their month of May in suitable fashion. On New Hampshire Day at Fenway Park, the Red Sox beat the Kansas City Royals 8-1 to salvage a split of the series heading into tomorrow’s day off. After a much-needed sweep of the Tampa Bay Rays, the Sox came into this four-game series with the Royals confident and collected. They have been steadily getting healthier (with the exception of the bizarre on-goings of Jacoby Ellsbury and Josh Beckett), and climbing the standings in the very competitive A.L. East.

The first two losses of this series were somewhat overshadowed in this city by the Celtics finally overcoming the Orlando Magic to head back to the finals, but this was a series the Boston could not overlook, as the Royals are right at the top of the league in team batting average. After the most impressive pitching match-up of the weekend lived up to it’s hype on Saturday night, with Clay Buchholz one-upping Zach Greinke, the Sox entered Sunday needing a split to end this impressive month of May on a high note. And they got it.

Making the move from center to left, the speedy Jacoby Ellsbury gives the Red Sox a tremendous amount of range in away ballparks (left field in Fenway isn’t very big) — certainly a lot more than Jason Bay ever did. The one issue Ellsbury seems to have this season is his decision-making, a problem that has plagued him at times in the past. In the first inning on Sunday, Ellsbury failed to call off shortstop Bill Hall on a catchable ball, leading to an error and eventually a run for the Royals.

Later on in the ninth, Ellsbury failed to call off Adrian Beltre on a tough play in foul territory, and paid the price when the two collided. Beltre’s knee appeared to slam into Ellsbury’s chest, and the left fielder was forced to the leave the game. Details of his injury are not known, but it didn’t look pretty. After the game, Terry Francona said Ellsbury was getting X-rays on the left side of his rib cage and was “in pain.”

As for the game, the Red Sox used 12 hits and eight runs in support of Clay Buchholz, who lasted 5.0 innings and allowed three runs (two earned) to earn his first win of the year. The bullpen coughed up three more runs on the afternoon, but the Sox held on to beat K.C. 8-6.(...)

John Smoltz pitched five strong innings to record his first win in a Red Sox uniform and Kevin Youkilis belted two home runs as the Sox punished Kansas City’s pitching for a 15-9 win.

The game was all Boston from the start, as the offense scored nine runs in the first four innings. Youkilis, Jason Varitek and David Ortiz all homered while Smoltz dazzled, letting up one run on four hits in five innings while striking out seven. The game got so out of hand at one point that commentator Don Orsillo spent several minutes comparing Dennis Eckersley to Bee Gees member Barry Gibb.

Not so fast. Smoltz was pulled after throwing 97 pitches in five innings and Justin Masterson was called on to preserve the eight-run lead. Easy task right? Well, Masterson let the Royals back in the game by letting five straight batters reach base. In the end, he surrendered five earned runs in only 1/3 of an inning. After the Royals tacked on another run in the top of the seventh, bringing the score to 9-7, the Red Sox bats woke up again and they scored three runs in both the seventh and eighth innings. Takashi Saito pitched a perfect ninth inning in the pouring rain to closeout what became a crazy offensive battle thanks to sloppy defense and live bats.(...)
