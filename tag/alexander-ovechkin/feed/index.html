If you love hockey like I do, and you love fantasy sports like I do, Friday was the night where your worlds collided for an event of sportsgasmic proportions.

I’m talking about the NHL’s newest creation, aimed to liven up their annual All Star festivities. Last night, the NHL held its first All Star Fantasy Draft.

Earlier this month, 42 All Stars were chosen, both by fan votes and by the NHL’s Hockey Operations Dept. From those 42 players, two captains were named… Detroit Red Wings veteran defenseman Niklas Lidstrom and Carolina Hurricanes team scoring leader, Eric Staal. These two captains, along with the help of their assigned alternate captains (Lidstrom has been paired with forwards Patrick Kane of Chicago and Martin St. Louis of the Lightning, while Staal worked with Vancouver’s Ryan Kesler and the Caps’ blueliner Mike Green), went back and forth, choosing up the teams that will skate against each other in Sunday’s All Star Game.

They finished the 2009-2010 season with at least 20 more points than any other East squad, tallying 54 wins against just 15 regulation losses. They carry a 50-goal scorer, a 40-goal scorer, a 30-goal scorer, and four 20-goal scorers as part of the NHL’s best offense (316 total goals).

The Bruins, who feature the NHL’s worst offense, faced the daunting task of playing the Capitals in Washington twice in the season’s final week. Needing to rack up points and wins to ensure a playoff spot, the Bruins did just that. They suffered an overtime loss to the Caps last Monday, and countered with a 4-3 overtime win of their own against the same Caps on Sunday.

In collecting three points over the Caps this week, the Bruins moved themselves out of the eighth spot and into the sixth spot in the Eastern Conference playoffs. Therefore by beating the Capitals, the Bruins avoid facing them in the first round of the playoffs (and likely face an early exit).
