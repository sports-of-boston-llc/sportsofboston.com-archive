He managed a young rookie pitcher named Roger Clemens in his final season. That year, Clemens was 9-4 with a 4.32 ERA at the age of 21.

One of the most colorful players of this generation, Jose Lima, was found dead at his home in Los Angeles on Sunday morning. He was 37 years old.

His wife, Dorca Astacio, told ESPN.com that Lima died of a heart attack.

“Jose was complaining while sleeping and I just thought he was having a nightmare,” Astacio told ESPNdeportes.com. “I called the paramedics, but they couldn’t help him.”

Famous for the catchphrase “Lima Time,” Lima was 89-102 with a 5.26 ERA in 13 major league seasons with the Dodgers, Astros, Tigers, Royals, and Mets. He had his lone All-Star appearance in 1999 with the Astros, when he won a career-high 21 games with a 3.58 ERA.(...)

]]> https://sportsofboston.com/2010/05/23/jose-lima-dead-at-37/feed/ 0 Dom DiMaggio Dies at 92 https://sports-of-boston-llc.github.io/sportsofboston.com-archive/2009/05/08/dom-dimaggio-dies-at-92/ https://sportsofboston.com/2009/05/08/dom-dimaggio-dies-at-92/#respond Fri, 08 May 2009 10:36:42 +0000 http://www.sportsofboston.com/?p=21945 Former Red Sox great Dom DiMaggio died in his home in Marion, Mass. at around 1 a.m. Friday morning. DiMaggio, the brother of former Yankees legend Joe DiMaggio, was 92.

Dom was a seven-time all-star outfielder with the Red Sox. He hit .298 with a .383 OBP and 87 HRs in 11 seasons in the big leagues, all with the Red Sox. He scored 100 runs six times in his career, and played on the 1946 squad that nearly won the World Series.

He holds the Red Sox record with a 34-game hitting streak in 1948.

Dom DiMaggio is survived by his wife, three children, and six grandchildren.

]]> https://sportsofboston.com/2009/05/08/dom-dimaggio-dies-at-92/feed/ 0 Former Patriots Punter Dies https://sports-of-boston-llc.github.io/sportsofboston.com-archive/2008/04/29/former-patriots-punter-dies/ https://sportsofboston.com/2008/04/29/former-patriots-punter-dies/#comments Tue, 29 Apr 2008 18:56:37 +0000 http://www.sportsofboston.com/?p=1216 According to the Boston Globe, former Patriots’ punter Mike Patrick died at age 55. No cause of death was given.

Patrick spent four seasons with that Pats from 1975-78. Signed originally as a free agent, he ranks fifth on the Pats all-time list with 8,481 yards on 225 punts in 43 career games. In his first NFL game in 1975 against Houston, he punted 62 yards, which at the time was their longest since 1968.

Patrick was originally from Austin, Texas. He attended Mississippi State, where he still ranks fifth on the school’s career punting list. Patrick resided in Biloxi, Mississippi where he died. Funeral services will be held in Biloxi tomorrow.
