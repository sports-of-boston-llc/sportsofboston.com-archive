The surging Red Sox welcome to town the AL East leading New York Yankees this weekend for a 3-game set at Fenway. The Sox are 8-2 in their last ten games while the Yankees have been 5-5 in that same span. Boston is now just 5.5 games back of the division lead but still with all four other AL East teams in front of them.

It’s a tight race of teams hovering around .500 heading into the All-Star break. This series gives Boston a chance to get within a few games of the lead before a restful weekend coming up. The Red Sox are not used to having this many players actually get to rest on All-Star weekend but this year’s representation will be limited to Brock Holt and possibly Xander Bogearts.(...)
