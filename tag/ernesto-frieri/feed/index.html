It might have been one of the best back-and-forth games for any major league team this season, but unfortunately it also was arguably the worst loss the Boston Red Sox have suffered all year. Once leading 6-0 early, the Red Sox blew two notable leads in disastrous innings, the first an eight-run third for the Los Angeles Angels, and the second a three-run ninth after the Sox took a late two-run lead, all part of a demoralizing 14-13 defeat in ten innings and sweep at the hands of LA.

Going in, the game featured a potential southpaw duel between C.J. Wilson and Franklin Morales, but each starter allowed at least six runs (Morales two earned runs). Both offenses broke out, combining for 27 runs on 38 HITS. The Red Sox offense took an early lead with the hopes of salvaging the final game of their series with the Angels.(...)
