Three warm nights in early May translated to a romping at Fenway Park for the upstart Red Sox, who outscored the Oakland Athletics 40-15 over three games en route to a sweep. This is a team that is fun to watch on most nights, and if they stay healthy, will be able to challenge the Orioles throughout the summer for the AL East title.

The Sox have scored 196 runs, backed by David Ortiz’s 9 home runs and 29 RBIs. This is good for the most runs scored in the league (the Cubs also have 196 runs scored). Pair that offensive support with a pitching staff that can typically keep their opponents under five runs (4.24 ERA average), and the Red Sox are suddenly eight games above .500.

The Astros are coming to town, and they seem to be doing everything that the Red Sox are not. Houston sits in last place in the AL West at 14-21, and are just coming off of a 16 inning marathon victory against Cleveland. The Astros lack punch both offensively and defensively, and this is a series the Red Sox can easily take. However, the question remains: can the Red Sox sweep yet another team coming to Fenway Park?
