I challenge you to find a draft class worse than the Patriots’ 2007 crop. New England had a total of nine draft picks, but just one within the first three rounds. Right off the bat we’ll state the obvious. Let’s remember that the Patriots traded a second and seventh round pick for Miami Dolphins wide receiver Wes Welker, and a fourth rounder for disgruntled Raiders playmaker Randy Moss.

You could say the two worked out pretty well.

The receiving duo helped lead the Patriots to an undefeated 2007 regular season and Super Bowl appearance. But, the Patriots failed in the scouting department and inevitably turned in Bill Belichick’s weakest draft class during his tenure in New England.(...)
