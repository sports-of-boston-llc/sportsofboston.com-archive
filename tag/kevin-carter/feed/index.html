According to Mike Reiss of the Boston Globe, former Miami Dolphins and Green Bay Packers defensive end Vonnie Holliday visited with the Patriots on Monday. The 33-year-old from the University of North Carolina played with the division rival Dolphins from 2005 to just last season, when he amassed 46 total tackles and started 15 of the 16 games he played in.

Last week, Kevin Carter worked out for the team and it was reported that there was an 80% chance that he would in fact sign with the Patriots. However, Carter did also get offered a contract with the woeful Detroit Lions. So, perhaps the Patriots have a hunch that Carter is choosing playing time over the chance to contend for a Super Bowl. Now this could also be the Patriots front office simply doing their job and exploring all available options.

One thing is clear, however, and that is that Patriots coach Bill Belichick is looking to add depth to the defensive end position.
