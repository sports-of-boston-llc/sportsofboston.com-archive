If there was any remaining doubt as to whether Rajon Rondo belongs in the discussion among the NBA’s elite, that question was firmly put to rest on Wednesday. The NBA announced its annual All-Defensive Teams and Rondo found himself implanted firmly at the top of the heap. Orlando’s Dwight Howard was the only player to receive more votes than Rondo, tallying 57 points overall, including 28 First Team votes to Rondo’s 50 points overall and 23 First Team votes. The honor comes as Rondo’s first, as he was voted on by the league’s 30 head coaches.

As Rondo’s offense has continually improved over the last several years, defense has always been a high priority, particularly underscored by the arrival in 2007 of teammate Kevin Garnett, a six time All-Defensive first-teamer.(...)
