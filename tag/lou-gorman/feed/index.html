Former Red Sox general manager Lou Gorman died early Friday morning at the age of 82.

Gorman was the Sox GM from 1984-1993, and built the American League champs in 1986 that featured Roger Clemens, Wade Boggs, and Jim Rice. Ironically, he also had a hand in building the Mets team that beat the Sox that year, as he worked with New York from 1980-83.

He made one of the most controversial trades in franchise history, dealing away minor league third baseman Jeff Bagwell in 1990 for Astros middle reliever Larry Anderson. Advantage: Astros.(...)
