It’s crystal clear: the Washington Capitals are far and away the best team in the Eastern Conference and maybe the NHL. They finished the 2009-2010 season with at least 20 more points than any other East squad, tallying 54 wins against just 15 regulation losses. They carry a 50-goal scorer, a 40-goal scorer, a 30-goal scorer, and four 20-goal scorers as part of the NHL’s best offense (316 total goals).

The Bruins, who feature the NHL’s worst offense, faced the daunting task of playing the Capitals in Washington twice in the season’s final week. Needing to rack up points and wins to ensure a playoff spot, the Bruins did just that. They suffered an overtime loss to the Caps last Monday, and countered with a 4-3 overtime win of their own against the same Caps on Sunday.

In collecting three points over the Caps this week, the Bruins moved themselves out of the eighth spot and into the sixth spot in the Eastern Conference playoffs. Therefore by beating the Capitals, the Bruins avoid facing them in the first round of the playoffs (and likely face an early exit). Instead, they will take on division-rival Buffalo or the New Jersey Devils next week.(...)
