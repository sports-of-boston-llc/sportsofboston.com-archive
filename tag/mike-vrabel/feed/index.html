Patriots All Decade Team:

QB: Tom Brady

Honorable Mention: Drew Bledsoe, Matt Cassel

It was the easiest selection on the list. The day Drew Bledsoe was knocked out of a game in 2001, was the day Tom Brady emerged as one of the greatest quarterbacks of all time. He is a three time Super Bowl Champion, (two time MVP), regular season MVP on a team that went undefeated in the regular season, owns the single season touchdown record and still seems to get better each season. He is a two time Sporting News Sportsman of the year, a four time Pro Bowl selection and was a first team all pro selection in 2007. He is the MVP of the last decade for New England and hopefully he can have the same success next decade.

RB: Kevin Faulk

Honorable Mention: Antowain Smith, Corey Dillon, Laurence Maroney

The hardest selection. There is one thing Smith, Dillon and Maroney have in common, they were all backed up by Faulk. Since 1999, Faulk has played in Foxboro and has been a favorite target of Brady. With rookies reporting to training camp yesterday there was little doubt that second round pick Patrick Chung – though unsigned at the time – would show up to camp and not hold out. Instead of making their rabid fanbase sweat a little, the Patriots and Chung ended all inkling of contractual unrest by agreeing to a deal.

Chung was selected with the 34th overall pick, which the Patriots acquired in the trade with the Chiefs for Mike Vrabel and Matt Cassel. Chung will be in the mix with Brandon Meriweather and James Sanders for time at safety.

The Pats also have played five players on the active physically unable to perform list, including defensive end Ty Warren and outside linebacker Shawn Crable. Warren is recovering from groin surgery and it’s unknown what’s wrong with Crable. Because they are on the active PUP list and not the reserve PUP list, both Warren and Crable can return to training camp when the team feels they are ready.
