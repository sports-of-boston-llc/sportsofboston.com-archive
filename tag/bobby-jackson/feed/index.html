It should not be a secret to any Celtics fan that the team is without a true backup point guard. In the 2007-2008 campaign, the team picked up Sam Cassell in March, and he helped guide the team towards their 17th NBA Championship.

Last season, the Celtics again entered the campaign without a true backup point guard (Cassell was on the team, but he never suited up). After disgruntled point guard Stephon Marbury finally worked out a buyout with the Knicks, the Celtics swooped in and signed him. Marbury was not the saving grace that the team hoped he would be, and the Celtics fell to the Magic in the second round of the playoffs.

For the third season in a row, the Celtics will not have a true backup for Rajon Rondo. The team has some options in-house, and there may be some options out there for the Celtics to pick up. So where does the team go from here?(...)
