The Tampa Bay Lightning held the Boston Bruins to just eight shots in the second period of Monday’s Game 5 of the Eastern Conference Finals at the TD Garden.

The Bruins only needed two.

Goals by Nathan Horton and Brad Marchand in the second period gave the Bruins the lead, and Tim Thomas stopped 33 shots in a row to power the Bruins to a 3-1 win over the Lightning. The Bruins will have a chance to secure their first trip to the Stanley Cup Finals since 1990 on Wednesday in Tampa Bay.(...)
