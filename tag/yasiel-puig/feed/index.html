When the Boston Red Sox visit the Los Angeles Dodgers this weekend, the anniversary of the blockbuster trade the teams agreed upon last summer will remind us of how far both sides have come in rebuilding greatness.

The trade seemed incredibly one-sided on the surface, as the Dodgers received three significant big league players capable of making a difference in a close National League West race, while the Red Sox essentially gave themselves flexibility for a cloudy future.

Yet the Dodgers struggled at the close of last season despite their new weapons, while the Sox enjoyed a busy winter retooling the roster by signing players with the strategy of succeeding now while planning ahead.(...)
