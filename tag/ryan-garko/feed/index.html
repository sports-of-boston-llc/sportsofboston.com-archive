The Rangers have claimed first baseman Ryan Garko off waivers from the Mariners, effectively ending all interest they had in Red Sox third baseman Mike Lowell. The Rangers and Red Sox originally agreed to a deal in December that would send Lowell and $9 million to Texas for catching prospect Max Ramirez. The deal was called off more than a week later after Lowell underwent surgery to repair an injured right thumb.

Texas and Boston reportedly revisited the trade recently, but some reports suggested the Rangers wanted the Red Sox to eat even more money (Lowell is scheduled to earn $12 million, the Sox were willing to pay $9 million before), and why not? The Rangers had the position of leverage: they had the prospect, and the Red Sox were in the awkward situation with Lowell.

“There hasn’t been much trade activity, if at all, with him this spring,’’ general manager Theo Epstein said.
