The Braves are hoping to beef up their offense and make a charge in the N.L. East where they trail Philadelphia by 5.5 games. As it stands as of this writing, the Braves are 11th in the N.L. with 4.38 runs scored per game, 12th in HR, and are hitting .258 as a team. The Pirates were almost neck and neck with Braves for offensive futility by being 12th in runs scored (4.33), 10th in HR, and hitting .264 as a team.

Enough of the real world implications. We are here to see what impact, if any, that this trade has on Nate McLouth. When McLouth makes his debut for the Braves, he will already be the team leader in HR, RBI, and SB.
