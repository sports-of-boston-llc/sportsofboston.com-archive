One of the most colorful players of this generation, Jose Lima, was found dead at his home in Los Angeles on Sunday morning. He was 37 years old.

His wife, Dorca Astacio, told ESPN.com that Lima died of a heart attack.

“Jose was complaining while sleeping and I just thought he was having a nightmare,” Astacio told ESPNdeportes.com. “I called the paramedics, but they couldn’t help him.”

Famous for the catchphrase “Lima Time,” Lima was 89-102 with a 5.26 ERA in 13 major league seasons with the Dodgers, Astros, Tigers, Royals, and Mets. He had his lone All-Star appearance in 1999 with the Astros, when he won a career-high 21 games with a 3.58 ERA.(...)
