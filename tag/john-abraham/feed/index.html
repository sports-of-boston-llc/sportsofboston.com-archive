In an off-season that perhaps is even more exciting than last year’s lockout-shortened free agency period, the Patriots have plenty of needs. None are bigger, however, than wide receiver and defensive end. Let’s now take a look at defensive end.

You will hear people say over the next few days that the Patriots don’t really need to pursue a pass rusher.  They’ll tell you that Andre Carter had 10 sacks in 14 regular season games.  They’ll tell you that Mark Anderson had 10 sacks in 16 regular season games.

What they won’t tell you is that Carter had four sacks in one game against the Jets in November, leaving him with six sacks in the remaining 13 games he played in.  What they won’t tell you is that Anderson had double digit sacks for the first time since the 2006-2007 season in Chicago.(...)
