With Opening Day a week away, the Boston Red Sox are having an organizational meeting on Monday to start setting the 25-man roster. In addition to determining the fifth starter to replace the injured Eduardo Rodriguez and filling out the bullpen, Dave Dombrowski and John Farrell have an interesting decision to make regarding the fate of outfielder David Murphy.

After receiving little interest in the offseason, Murphy signed a minor league deal with the Red Sox that included an opt out date to allow him to become a free agent and sign with another major league ball club. That opt out date was Sunday, and Murphy exercised it rather than risk spending time in his 11th season playing at Triple-A Pawtucket, waiting to be called up.

Now the Red Sox have until Tuesday to either let Murphy walk or add him to the 25-man roster for Opening Day. What should they do with the 34-year-old veteran?(...)
