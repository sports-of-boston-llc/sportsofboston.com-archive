Bill Belichick is making more curious moves in his secondary. The Patriots released 35-year-old cornerback Shawn Springs yesterday, an unexpected roster move that seems to be part of an effort to get younger players involved on the Patriots’ defense.

“It’s hard to evaluate and give young guys a shot if you’ve got me,’’ Springs said to Boston Globe reporters.

Springs’ release comes less than a month after Belichick surprised Patriots fans by drafting Rutgers cornerback Devin McCourty in the first round of this year’s draft. Belichick has been building a young secondary over the past couple of years, also drafting Terrence Wheatley, Darius Butler, and Jonathan Wilhite, who all saw time on the field last season and are ready to contribute this year. Quarterback: A+

Tom Brady returned to his ’07 form against the Jaguars on Sunday, completing 23/26 passes for 267 yards and four touchdowns.  Brady played an almost perfect game on Sunday, as his 149.0 quarterback rating would indicate. He threw three touchdown passes to Randy Moss, and another to tight end Chris Baker.  Brady continued his mastery of the Jacksonville Jaguars. When these two teams met back in the 2007 AFC Divisional Playoffs, Brady completed 26/28 passes and threw three touchdowns. Here’s Brady’s combined stats in his last two games against Jacksonville: 49 of 54 (90.7%), 529 yards, 9.8 YPA, 7 TD, 0 INT, 147.1 passer rating.

Running Backs: B+

There were some new faces carrying the load for the Patriots’ rushing attack on Sunday. Laurence Maroney’s goal line fumble on the Patriots opening drive earned him a seat on the bench, and a spot in Bill Belichick’s doghouse. Maroney has four fumbles in his last seven games, two of which occurred in the red zone.

Sammy Morris picked up the slack, racking up 95 yards on 12 carries. Morris scored on a one-yard touchdown run late in the second quarter putting the Patriots ahead 21-0.  Injury plagued Fred Taylor returned to action for the first time in 10 games and ran 11 times for 35 yards in the fourth quarter. Taylor was given mop-up duty on Sunday, but look for his role to expand as the Patriots gear up for the playoffs.

Kevin Faulk continued his steady weekly contribution with 41 yards on 6 carries. The Patriots had 197 net yards rushing on Sunday.

2. Secondary: Who’s going to step up?

The Patriots are a year removed from having one of the league’s worst secondary’s. So what did they do? Bill Belichick and company completely reshaped the positions by trading last season’s number one cornerback, Ellis Hobbs, to the Philadelphia Eagles on draft day in exchange for two fifth round picks. Second year players Terrence Wheatley and Jonathan Wilhite are the lone holdovers from the cornerback position from a year ago that seem to have a chance to make their mark this season.

In free agency the Patriots made it a point of emphasis to upgrade the position. They signed former Washington Redskin Shawn Springs to a three-year deal worth around $10 million in an incentive-laden contract. Springs, who is 34 years old, could potentially see time at both free safety and cornerback. The versatility that he brings to the secondary will allow him to have an immediate impact for this defense. Along with Springs the Patriots added former Cleveland Browns starter Leigh Bodden. Back in 2007, Bodden picked off opposing quarterbacks seven times, which was second best in the NFL. However Bodden, along with most Detroit Lions players, struggled in 2008. After testing the free agent market for two months, Bodden settled for a one year deal with the Patriots on the eve of the NFL Draft. At 6-1, 190 pounds, Bodden provides much needed size to a secondary that has a history of smaller cornerbacks. As of right now it appears that Bodden will be vying to be a starter with the Patriots in 2009.(...)
