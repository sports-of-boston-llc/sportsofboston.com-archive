The Red Sox are peering across the world for quality pitching this offseason, looking first at Cuba’s Aroldis Chapman before he signed with Cincinnati, and then to Japan at lefty Hisanori Takahashi. Now, the team may be focused on one of the best pitchers in Cuba, righty Yuniesky Maya. The “28-year-old” (age subject to change) features four plus pitches (fastball, curveball, changeup, slider) and figures to be a back-of-the-rotation starter in the major leagues.

Maya, who played for Team Cuba from 2005-2009, was named the country’s top right-handed pitcher in 2008-2009.

If Maya were to join the Sox, he would almost certainly head to the minor leagues first, which is a sticking point in his negotiations with the team. If another team is willing to take him on and plug him into their starting rotation on Day 1, then he probably would not be headed to Boston.(...)
