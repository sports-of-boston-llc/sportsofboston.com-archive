Former Patriots fullback and ace special teamer Mosi Tatupu, the father of Seahawks linebacker Lofa Tatupu, died suddenly on Tuesday at the age of 54. His passing stunned Patriots owner Bob Kraft and the organization.

“I know that I share a heavy heart today with Patriots fans everywhere who have learned of Mosi Tatupu’s passing,” said Robert Kraft through a team press release. “I was shocked by the news this morning. My sons and I loved to watch Mosi. He was one of our favorite players for more than a decade.”

The native of American Samoa played with the Patriots for 13 seasons from 1978-1990. Tatupu, who had 612 carries and 2,415 yards in his career with the Pats and Rams, was wildly popular during his time in New England, as he even had his own cheering section during home games called “Mosi’s Mooses.”(...)
