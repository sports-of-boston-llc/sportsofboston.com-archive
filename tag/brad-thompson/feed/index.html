The Boston Red Sox finished off their month of May in suitable fashion. On New Hampshire Day at Fenway Park, the Red Sox beat the Kansas City Royals 8-1 to salvage a split of the series heading into tomorrow’s day off. After a much-needed sweep of the Tampa Bay Rays, the Sox came into this four-game series with the Royals confident and collected. They have been steadily getting healthier (with the exception of the bizarre on-goings of Jacoby Ellsbury and Josh Beckett), and climbing the standings in the very competitive A.L. East.

The first two losses of this series were somewhat overshadowed in this city by the Celtics finally overcoming the Orlando Magic to head back to the finals, but this was a series the Boston could not overlook, as the Royals are right at the top of the league in team batting average. After the most impressive pitching match-up of the weekend lived up to it’s hype on Saturday night, with Clay Buchholz one-upping Zach Greinke, the Sox entered Sunday needing a split to end this impressive month of May on a high note. And they got it.
