Especially twice in one night. And especially down by one in the 9th with one man on. Because David Ortiz has the magic. And tonight was no different. Ortiz faced Al Reyes who he had been 0-4 in his career against. And, with Julio Lugo on first, Papi put a ball just over the far right field fence on an inside fastball. Tonight, Ortiz was responsible for all five runs driven in. Ortiz now has 31 HRs and 105 RBIs.

Thoughts: With the playoffs only three or so weeks away it’s great to see David Ortiz hit a walk-off homerun tonight. The Red Sox need Ortiz to be clutch, or at least it would definitely help, if the pitching can’t win it for us or the offense can’t get in rythm on it’s own. Boo-ya Ortiz. Boo-ya.
