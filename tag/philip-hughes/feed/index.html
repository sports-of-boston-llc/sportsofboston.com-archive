They just keep flip-flopping on whether Philip Hughes it worth any trade for Santana. The Twins have drawn these conversations out so long it’s looking like the race is between the Red Sox and Mets to acquire the former Cy Young award-winner. Or, it’s looking more and more likely that the Twins keep him.

Here’s the full story from ESPN

For the second time this offseason, the Yankees have pulled their Phil Hughes-centered trade offer for Johan Santana off the table.

The Yankees, then, will not restart trade talks with the Twins unless Hank Steinbrenner has another change of heart, a baseball official with knowledge of the talks told 1050 ESPN Radio’s Andrew Marchand.

The Yankees’ desire Monday to turn their attention away from the two-time Cy Young winner leaves Boston and the New York Mets as Minnesota’s trade partners, the official told Marchand. The Twins could also choose to keep Santana, who is under contract through this season.

Its believed the Yankees have the offer on the table of Philip Hughes, Melky Cabrera, pitching prospect Jeff Marquez and another prospect, according to ESPN. If so this would seemingly be the best offer on the table. But the Yankees have been very hesitant to trade away Philip Hughes. Whether the organization has changed its mind, or just Hank, its not clear.

The Red Sox best offers have been Jacoby Ellsbury, Jed Lowrie, and a third prospect or Jon Lester, Coco Crisp, Jed Lowrie and a 4th prospect. Whether this is enough no one knows but the Twins.

The Mets have also said they are in the running. Although they are not willing to part with Jose Reyes like the Twins want, they have a lot of other prospects that in combination could get the deal done.

Steinbrenner also had to say, “I either have to do it [a Santana deal] or don’t do it, same thing for the Red Sox, I guess, I think the Twins would like to keep him, so I don’t think there’s any hurry on anyone’s part. It all depends on what he asks for in an extension. You obviously have to be very careful with pitchers, for obvious reasons.”
