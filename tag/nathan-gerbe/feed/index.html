As Boston sports fans, we grow fond of many players that play their heart out in the great state of Massachusetts. It is usually the professional athletes that we idolize. We buy their jerseys and adore them until management cuts them loose. As quickly as they go, another professional athlete comes into the picture and they become our new favorite.

The same thing happens on the college front. Whether it is Boston College, Boston University, Northeastern, Harvard, or even my school, Bentley University, players come and go every four years. These athletes come into our city, attend school and play their hearts out as long as they can possibly remain a student.

There are a lot of local alumni succeeding in professional sports today. We know how some are doing because they are succeeding at the highest level, such as Matt Ryan. However there are many players who we forget about because they either play on a small market team or they are stuck in the minor leagues. It’s not anyone’s fault that we stop following these players. Every year a new recruiting class enrolls at local colleges and become the new generation of student-athletes gracing our campuses.(...)
