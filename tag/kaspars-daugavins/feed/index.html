The Boston Bruins defeated the Pittsburgh Penguins 1-0 at home Friday night to complete the sweep of the Penguins and advance to their second Stanley Cup Finals appearance in the last three years. Tuukka Rask posted his second shutout of the series and Adam McQuaid scored on a snipe from the point late in the third period to propel the Bruins past this elite Penguins team.

Boston will face either the Chicago Blackhawks or Los Angeles Kings when the Bruins look to win their second Stanley Cup title in three years.(...)

The Bruins took a commanding 3-0 lead in the Eastern Conference Finals, but were dealt a big blow with Gregory Campbell going down with a broken leg late in the second period while killing a penalty. Campbell will now miss the rest of the post season and while it is a huge loss for the Bruins, the sequence of events during that penalty kill will put Gregory Campbell in legendary status in Boston sports history.

Campbell laid out to block an Evgeni Malkin shot from the point during one of the Penguins power plays. He was successful blocking the shot, but took it off an area between the top of his skate and shin pads where there is virtually no protection. He yelled in pain and everyone could see he was immediately hurt. Most players in this position would stay down, or try to get to the bench, but Campbell knew that would leave his team too vulnerable and showed how tough he is by getting up and continue killing the penalty. Struggling to move on the ice, he still was trying to get his body and stick in the lanes of potential shots on net. The Bruins finally cleared the puck out of the zone and Campbell was able to hobble off the ice to a huge ovation by the crowd and a pat on his back by his teammates.(...)

The Boston Bruins never led in the game, but came back to beat the Ottawa Senators 3-2 in a shootout after trailing 2-0. David Krejci scored the game winning goal in the fourth round of the shootout and also had an assist to cap off his 400th career NHL game. Bruins net minder Tuukka Rask made 30 saves to improve to 13-2-3 on the year. The Bruins beat the Senators last on Feb. 28th in OT.

Unfortunately it was not all good news for the Bruins in the win. Chris Kelly collided knees with Senators forward and former teammate Chris Neil early in the opening moments of the second period. Kelly was visibly in pain and was helped off the ice and down the tunnel to the locker room and did not return. The hit did not look malicious.(...)
