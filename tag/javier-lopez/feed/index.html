Garza took a perfect game into the seventh inning and finished with one hit and one walk in 7.2 innings, striking out 10 to earn the win. The one hit he allowed was an extremely weak ground ball up the middle off the bat of Jacoby Ellsbury. Garza nearly made a play on it, but the ball got by him and Ellsbury raced down the line to beat the throw and earn the infield single. That was it in a 13-0 blowout.

Last week, the Red Sox inked middle relievers Dan Kolb and Dan Miceli to minor league contracts with an invitation to Spring Training. If either pitcher makes the squad, they will earn $650,000 plus incentives.

The Red Sox also agreed to terms with reliever Kyle Snyder and lefty specialist Javier Lopez. Lopez is scheduled to make $840,000 next season while Snyder will earn $835,000. Both players avoided arbitration with their deals.

The only Red Sox player that remains eligible for arbitration is gold glove first baseman Kevin Youkilis. Youk, who made $424,500 last year, has asked for $3.7 million, while the Sox have countered with $2.525 million.
