You can’t blame the Cleveland Cavaliers if their minds were elsewhere during Tuesday night’s 106-87 drubbing at the hands of an inspired Celtics team. With LeBron James and the Miami Heat returning to Cleveland for the first time since he tore their hearts out, a letdown seemed imminent. “On Thursday night, the Cavs will host theÂ Miami Heat,” the loudspeakers trumpeted to the few fans left in Quicken Loans Arena. A predictable cacophony of boos followed.

Despite the impending LeBron distraction, a basketball game did indeed take place. The Cavaliers actually came out hot, and led through the first quarter. But like Cleveland, Rajon Rondo was shot out of a cannon himself — only he was able to maintain the pace for all four quarters. Despite a flimsy hamstring and hobbled foot, Rondo scored a season-high 23 points with 12 assists, leading the charge as the Celtics won their fourth straight. Sure, he missed his first three shots, but he followed by nailing his next 11 out of 14 as the Celtics took off in the second quarter.

The Celtics bench continues to impress, with Glen Davis and Marquis Daniels chipping in with 17 and 16 points, respectively. Daniels lived in the lane (see above photo) and was rewarded for his efforts. Davis also pulled down 11 rebounds, and Coach Doc Rivers couldn’t have been happier.

“I thought the bench turned the game around. They pounded it down low to [Davis] and Marquis,” Rivers beamed. “I thought those were the two guys changed that changed [the] game for us.”

The Cleveland Cavaliers were expected to be a complete afterthought in the NBA this season after they lost their star, LeBron James, to the Miami Heat this summer. However, on Wednesday night, they proved that they shouldn’t be forgotten just yet and beat the Celtics 95-87.

The Celtics led after every quarter until the end of this game when the Cavaliers were able to put on impressive run late in the fourth, capped by a controversial three-pointer made by Anthony Parker. The shot by Parker was apparently made with just one very long second left on the shot clock that allowed Parker to catch the ball put it back down on the ground, bring it back up, and still shoot the ball before the time ran out. Maybe the Cavs should do an employee assessment of their clock guy.

The Celtics had trouble meshing their starters and bench players together and still seem to be searching for their roles a bit in the early going of the season. This came after the team looked like a much more put-together team than the Miami Heat just one night ago.(...)
