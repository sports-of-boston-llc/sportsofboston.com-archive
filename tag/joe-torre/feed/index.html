Joe Torre expressed concern on behalf of Major League Baseball on Sunday with the reports of the Red Sox starting pitchers drinking beer in the clubhouse.

“It’s something we’re concerned about, just to make sure that we get all the facts and that’s my area,” Torre said. “I know I have plans just to talk to some people.”

Jon Lester and Clay Buccholz specifically admitted to drinking in the clubhouse, while John Lackey and Josh Beckett admitted to doing something wrong. All four pitchers, however, denied the report they drank beer in the dugout during games.

It appears MLB was paying attention to the reports.

Despite all the drama, the crazy rumors, and the occasional bad times during His era in Boston, Friday June 18, 2010 was still to be the night when He came back to us for the first time. Back to Fenway, the site of his dramatic game-winning home run that was replayed on NESN so often, along with his other famous plays. He would be back in town at last. Those who could get tickets would be there in person, eager to greet him with cheers or boos. Those that couldn’t be at Fenway would be figuratively glued to their TVs, eyes wide with excitement. This would be perhaps the most anticipated game of the season.

Then in late May, the Dodgers cut Nick Green, spoiling the whole thing.

Joe Torre had an offer on the table for a one-year deal for 5 million with incentives that could reach 8 million. And Joe said no.

This is the first step to major turnover on the Yankees. A lot of the players who are on the cusp of free agency (Mariano Rivera, Posada, etc.) or just canceling their contract (A-Rod), were looking for Torre to return. Without him in New York a lot of guys might want to move on.

Next question is who will be the next manager in New York.

Tim Kurkjian of ESPN thinks the next Yankee manager will be Don Mattingly. Kurkjian had to say Mattingly because: “He’s a Yankee and he’s beloved.” Also it would give the Yankees an the edge in resigning players who would have stuck around for Torre.

Torre seems happy.
