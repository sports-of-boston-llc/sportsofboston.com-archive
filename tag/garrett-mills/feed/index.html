The New England Patriots have signed free agent tight end Garrett Mills, as announced by the team on Sunday.

The Pats released safety Bret Lockett, who was injured in Thursday’s preseason game, and offensive lineman Zach Roth to clear the roster spot for Mills.

The Patriots originally drafted Mills in the fourth-round of the 2006 NFL Draft, but the Tulsa alum was placed on injured reserve during the middle of his rookie season. New England released Mills following the 2006 campaign, and the young tight end was signed by the Minnesota Vikings off waivers. Mills played three seasons with the Vikings, and was released prior to 2010.

Mills, entering his sixth-season, split last year between Cincinnati and Philadelphia. He played in seven games for the Eagles, making three starts while hauling in two receptions for 19 yards. He has nine career catches for 110 yards.

Mills will compete with rookies Lee Smith and Will Yeatman for the final tight end spot on the roster.
