While Boston certainly isn’t contending this season and has gone into rebuilding mode, they do have some time to try out some new pieces before 2013. Two new members of the team saw game action with Boston for the first time Sunday, as the Red Sox defeated the Royals 8-6. James Loney, acquired from the Dodgers in their blockbuster deal, started at first base for the Red Sox, while Pedro Beato, the player to be named acquired from the Mets for Kelly Shoppach, pitched in relief.

Boston struck first in the 1st inning, when Cody Ross singled in a run with two runners in scoring position. Unfortunately, Loney then grounded into a double play to end the inning in his first at bat with the Red Sox. Mike Aviles later doubled in Mauro Gomez from first in the 2nd inning to pad the lead.

After failing to take advantage of an error in the 3rd, Kansas City broke the floodgates open in the 4th. After two quick outs to lead off the inning, they hit three straight singles followed by a big three-run homer from Lorenzo Cain to quickly jump ahead 4-2.
