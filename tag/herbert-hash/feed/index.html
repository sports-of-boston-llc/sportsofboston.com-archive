Hash, the oldest living former Red Sox pitcher, died Tuesday, according to the University of Richmond, where he played several college sports. He was 97.

Hash played two seasons in the majors, both with the Red Sox, in 1940 and 1941. Hash was 7-7 over 34 games (12 starts) in the 1940 season at age 29. A soft-tosser, Hash got through 120 innings and finished with just 36 strikeouts to go along with 84 walks and a 4.95 ERA.
