David Ortiz is having one of the best seasons of his career.  The way he’s swinging the bat, it doesn’t look like he’ll be slowing down anytime soon.  Ortiz looks locked in this season.  He’s batting a cool .320, has a OBP of .405, a slugging percentage of .695, and an absurd OPS of 1.101.  Yes other guys have hit these numbers this early in the season, however, Ortiz is 40 and for a 40-year-old these stats are mind-blowing.  Lefty pitchers are usually tough on Ortiz, but in his final season he’s hitting .286 against them and has had mostly productive outs against southpaws.

For example, look at today’s game against the Houston Astros. Ortiz went 3-5 with a Home run, a game tying triple in the bottom of the ninth, and then a game winning double in the bottom of the 11th.  If it wasn’t for Hanley Ramirez bunt attempt in the bottom of the ninth, we might not have got the Ortiz dramatics(that everyone is used to) in the 11th.  So thanks for that Hanley.  Anyways this was Ortiz first game ever with a, double, triple, and a home run in the same game.  Also he joined a rare club today  joining Hank Aaron and Barry Bonds as the only players in history to have 500 Home runs and 600 doubles in their career.  Today was also Ortiz 20th game winning hit not counting the postseason, and we all know what he’s done there; sorry Yankee fans.

Ortiz wants to win bad, and you can tell his teammates want to win for him in his final season.  This team has good chemistry right now, they can’t wait to get to the ballpark on game days and enjoy each others company.  With the weather getting better expect more of the same from Ortiz and the rest of the lineup.

See you in October Boston.
