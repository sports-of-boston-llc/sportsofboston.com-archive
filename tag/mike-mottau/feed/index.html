The Boston Bruins defense was once again one of the best in the NHL, as the team finished with the highest team goal differential at plus 67 over 82 games. It starts and ends with the captain Zdeno Chara, who had a stellar season and should have brought home some hardware, but the young phenom from Ottawa had one of the best offense seasons from a defensemen we have seen in a while.

With a stud prospect in Dougie Hamilton getting ready to join this cluster of good players, this group is only going to get better.(...)

The Senators came into Boston and shut the Bruins out 1-0. Erik Karlsson scored the only goal of the game on the power play in the first period. The Bruins had 32 shots on goal, but once again struggled to put goals on the board. The power play unit went 0-4, including a last chance in the final minutes of the third period. Tim Thomas played extremely well, making 37 saves, but his counterpart Robin Lehner stopped all 32 Boston shots.

Bruins suffer fifth shutout loss in February
