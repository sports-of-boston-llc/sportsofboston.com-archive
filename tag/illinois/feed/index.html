As the 2012 NFL Draft approaches, the New England Patriots have some glaring needs. One of those needs, as many will agree, is at Defensive End. With the losses of Mark Anderson and presumably Andre Carter, the Patriots will need to fill the void left by their two best pass-rushers.

The Patriots have never truly had a dominant pass rusher in the last decade. Richard Seymour was a great defensive lineman, but was never a game-changing pass-rusher. They’ve had other linebackers that could attack the passer, but they’ve never had a Michael Strahan (or a number of other New York Giants) type player that could single-handedly alter a game, or even a season.(...)

Heading into the 2003 season, the Patriots signed veteran safety Rodney Harrison to play along side one of the league’s best in Lawyer Milloy. When Milloy wouldn’t restructure his contract, the Patriots released the Pro-Bowler just five days before the season. Milloy joined AFC east foe Buffalo. The immediate result? A 31-0 thrashing from the Buffalo Bills — yes, those Buffalo Bills.

Milloy left a huge void to fill and at the time, nobody thought it would be converted cornerback, and 2003 second round pick, Eugene Wilson to thrive in the situation. But, Wilson did just that. Statistically speaking, Wilson had one of the better rookie seasons a safety could have. Wilson recorded 61 tackles and four interceptions as the Patriots won Super Bowl XXXVIII. Good enough to land Wilson on ESPN’s All-Rookie team.(...)
