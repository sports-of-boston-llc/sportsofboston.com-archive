On the heels of a five-game losing streak, Tuesday’s news that Mike Cameron and Jacoby Ellsbury would both be placed on the disabled list contributed to the Red Sox ongoing misfortune. Assuming they could use an extra glove, the Red Sox called Darnell McDonald up from Pawtucket, giving him word only that they might need him. With 90 minutes to go before the game, McDonald got the official call after Ellsbury’s batting practice revealed a chest contusion. After bouncing around the minors for 12 years, McDonald would have to wait just a little bit longer to make his Red Sox debut. Entering the game in the bottom of the eighth to bat for his Pawtucket counterpart Josh Reddick, McDonald quickly launched a game-tying two-run home run over the Green Monster.  Luckily for Boston, McDonald wasn’t finished there.

Kevin Youkilis opened the ninth with an infield hit that ricocheted off of Rangers closer Frank Francisco, and advanced on a passed ball and was sacrificed to third by Bill Hall. Mike Lowell was intentionally walked, Adrian Beltre popped out, and Jason Varitek walked before McDonald ingnited the Fenway crowd for a second time.With two outs in the bottom of the ninth, he met the Monster again, this time lining the game-winning single off the left field wall to clinch a much-needed 7-6 victory for the Red Sox.(...)
