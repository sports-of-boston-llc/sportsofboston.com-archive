No, Goodnight Kornheiser https://sports-of-boston-llc.github.io/sportsofboston.com-archive/2009/05/19/goodnight-canada-no-goodnight-kornheiser/ https://sportsofboston.com/2009/05/19/goodnight-canada-no-goodnight-kornheiser/#comments Tue, 19 May 2009 14:24:20 +0000 http://www.sportsofboston.com/?p=22534 In the ever revolving door of the Monday Night Football TV crew, it’s now out with the old and in with the Chucky. Former Raiders and Buccaneers coach John Gruden will be the third wheel in the MNF football crew starting this year, replacing ESPN Pardon the Interruption host Tony Kornheiser.

Kornheiser claims to be relieved with the move as he is afraid of flying (what’s the deal with all these color guys afraid of planes?) but it was obvious that not many people liked him in that role. On PTI along with Michael Wilbon, Kornheiser’s banter in a half hour show format makes for great TV, but spend three hours in an important game listening to him talk about anything BUT football and you have trouble. While he has much more sports experience than Dennis Miller had, viewers just don’t seem to like anything but football chatter on Monday nights.

As far as John Gruden goes, who knows what kind of announcer he’ll be but at least we know he’ll be talking X’s and O’s and not about pop culture.
