Brock Holt hit for the cycle in the Red Sox 9-4 victory over the Atlanta Braves to split the two games at Fenway. He is the first Red Sox player to hit for the cycle since John Valentin on June 6th, 1996. This is only the 21st cycle in franchise history.

Holt started out going to the opposite field with a double high off the monster in the first inning. He would later score on a Mookie Betts RBI double. Holt singled to right in the fifth inning before hitting a 388-foot home run to left-center field over the Green Monster in the seventh. In the eighth inning Boston opened up the lead with four more runs to make it 9-2. The last of the runs coming from an RBI triple into the triangle from Holt, who was able to leg out the three bags, and most difficult part of the cycle to complete the historic accomplishment.(...)
