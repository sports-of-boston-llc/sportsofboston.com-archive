I’d say that in many ways, a lot of people could start their first ‘white collar’ stint, straight out of h.s. What college does is provide that extra adolescence plus a bit of the old resume polish but that’s about it except for perhaps a six year BA-MD program where the end result is being in a top profession.

Likewise, for b-ball, it provides a framework to extend adolescence but w/ a full scholarship and some coaching. Now, here’s the problem, an NBA career is really a under 35 years old game. One really can’t waste those years between 18 and 22, playing for free, esp if a monied position is in the wings. So now, we’re looking at an alternate world where the career starts at 18 but has the potential for a full 17 to 20 year run which is more than enough, for most people. Afterwards, it’s probably not a bad idea to try something different (i.e. coaching or media announcer) for some variety in one’s career or ride the pine at veteran’s minimum till 46 like Parish for the ’97 Bulls.
