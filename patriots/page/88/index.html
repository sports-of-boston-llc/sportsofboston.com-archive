Urban Meyer Defends Against Criticism in Aaron Hernandez Case

July 7, 2013 at 2:54pm in Featured, Opinion, Patriots
Ying Tao writes
Add a comment

After reports surfaced the University of Florida and its coaching staffed had concealed failed drug tests by Aaron Hernandez during his time as a Gator, Urban Meyer, now head coach at Ohio State, felt compelled to finally break his silence to address those rumors, which he immediately dismissed, among others.

Meyer, who coached Hernandez at the University of Florida for three years, had been rather quiet when it came to the Hernandez situation until now. Meyer has come out to address the criticism of how he and his coaching staff handled disciplinary actions when it came to Hernandez, who is currently being held without bail on a first-degree murder charge.

Meyer and his team have been the center of mounting criticism on the way they handled Hernandez as a collegian. Critics have blamed the coach for Hernandez’s transgressions in college, some even going as far as to blame the coach for Hernandez’s present choices, arguing the coach should have done more to discipline the tight end. We are finding out more about the evidence authorities have compiled against Aaron Hernandez in the murder investigation of Odin Lloyd. Massachusetts State Police recently executed search warrants on a condo owned by Hernandez in Franklin, Mass. and found some damaging evidence against the former Patriot.

Among the items recovered were 11 boxes of ammunition including five boxes of .45-caliber ammunition, the same ammunition used to kill Lloyd, according to the Boston Herald.

According to USATODAY.COM, Tim Tebow apparently tried to stop Aaron Hernandez from throwing a punch at a bar in Florida back in 2007. At the time, Hernandez was a 17-year-old freshman.

Aaron Hernandez Moved to Solitary Confinement; Acclimating Well

July 2, 2013 at 8:19pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
Ying Tao writes
Add a comment

According to the Bristol County Sheriff, former Patriot Aaron Hernandez has been moved into a “Class 2” jail cell after spending five days in a medical unit having his mental health evaluated. According to Sheriff Thomas Hodgson, Hernandez is isolated in his new cell with no interactions with the general population.

Jail officials were evaluating Hernandez’s mental health because of his drastic change in lifestyle going not just from a professional athlete to accused murderer but from a million dollar 7,100-square-foot home with a gourmet kitchen, home theater, and luxury bathrooms to a simple 7-by-10 foot cell with a toilet. Going from a professional hero that people placed on a pedestal to someone that people want nothing to do with could mess with anyone’s mental health, but that does not seem to be the case for Hernandez.
