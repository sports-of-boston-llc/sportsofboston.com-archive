Chris writes
4 comments

Continuing an ongoing story, according to an ESPN news report, the NFL has confirmed that the “New England Patriots violated league rules Sunday by videotaping defensive signals by the coaching staff of the New York Jets.” As a result:

Goodell is considering severe sanctions, including the possibility of docking the Patriots “multiple draft picks” because it is the competitive violation in the wake of a stern warning to all teams since he became commissioner, the sources said. Jeff’s Touchdown Tuesday

September 11, 2007 at 1:50pm in Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
Add a comment

This is the first of a weekly column on all things New England Patriots.

Sunday was just great. There’s nothing like the opening day of the NFL. It may not have the history of opening day in Major League Baseball, but seeing your team run out of the tunnel onto the field for the first time still gives me goosebumps.

Before the 2004 season (the year the Pats took down the Eagles), I remember looking at the schedule and the Patriots roster and commenting to a friend, ‘In all honesty, we shouldn’t lose more than a game or two all season.’ Well, the Patriots proved me right, losing only to Pittsburgh on the road and that bizarre collapse by Brady and the boys on Monday Night Football against the Dolphins. Other than that, the prophecy was filled and the Pats beat both co-NFL MVPs on their way to a Super Bowl XXXIX win against the Eagles.

Get ready New England, because our team is locked, loaded and ready to start the march towards Super Bowl XLII in Phoenix.

The Patriots were simply dominant in Sunday’s 38-14 win over the J-E-T-S, Jets Jets Jets. Despite missing two of their defensive stop gaps in Rodney Harrison and Richard Seymour, the Pats never seemed in any trouble of faltering in against the unproven Jets. That’s the key thing to remember here. The Jets may be the best competition in the AFC East this season but they are far from an elite team. After consulting several different media outlets it’s apparent that not only are the Patriots receiving heaps of accolades from their performance, but everyone seems to be keeping the good start in perspective. The Pats have no room for error with the San Diego Chargers coming into town next Sunday night.

Patriots Stealing Signals Against Jets?

September 10, 2007 at 3:55pm in Patriots
Chris writes
2 comments

ESPN.com reports that a cameraman may have been up to some mischief during yesterday’s opening game of the Patriot’s NFL season.

The Patriots’ cameraman was suspected of aiming his camera at the Jets’ defensive coaches who were sending signals to their unit on the field, the sources said. The league also is investigating some radio frequency issues that occurred during the game.

The same cameraman has apparently been in trouble for these kind of actions before. In a game against the Green Bay Packers last year on November 19, 2006, he was escorted off of the field by NFL security.
The camera and its recordings were confiscated and sent to the NFL competition committee. Both teams declined to comment on the story.

Patriots Look Super in Win

September 9, 2007 at 4:08pm in Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
Add a comment

The Patriots came out and dominated for most of the game today against the Jets, leaving little doubt that they are the Super Bowl contenders as billed.

Tom Brady, Laurence Maroney and Randy Moss all showed why on paper the Patriots have perhaps the most talent in the NFL. Maroney and Moss showed no ill effects from their injuries and Brady looked sharp all afternoon. Moss’ performance (9 catches, 183 yards and a touchdown) was remarkable considering he had not seen any game action.

The key to today’s game however belonged in the trenches. The Patriots offensive line pulled off an incredible performance as they gave Brady all day to throw and the new daddy wasn’t sacked a single time. Defensively, Richard Seymour was not overly missed as Vince Wilfork, Ty Warren and Jarvis Green dominated and allowed the linebackers to penetrate.

It’s tough to see how any mediocre team will be able to stop the Pats, but we’ll see their true medal next Sunday night against the Chargers.

More on the game later, what are your thoughts on today’s 38-14 win?

Patriots-Jets Preview

September 9, 2007 at 11:58am in Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
One comment

Week 1 is finally here. More than seven months have passed since the Patriots fell to the Colts in the AFC Championship game. Lots has changed for the Patriots and they now – on paper – will be fielding the most talented team in the Bill Belichick era.

Today’s opener against the Jets will not come without its obstacles. Absent from the starting lineup are defensive end Richard Seymour (knee) and safety Rodney Harrison (suspension). New WR sensation Randy Moss hasn’t played a competitive down this preseason (hamstring) and shut down corner Asante Samuel has been ruled eligible to play but he also has yet to see any game action (contract dispute).

The Patriots face the rival Jets coached by Eric Mangini. The gamesmanship has been prevalent in the last week, with both teams bringing in cut players from their counterpart to, perhaps, try to get a sense of what their opponent is planning.

Asante Samuel is Ready for Game Day

September 9, 2007 at 1:01am in Patriots
Chris writes
One comment

According to ESPN.com, Asante Samuel has been added to the 53-man roster for today’s game.

To make room on the roster for Samuel, who was on the NFL’s exempt list and did not count against the Patriots’ player limit, New England released 11-year veteran cornerback Tory James, who in April signed a one-year contract as an unrestricted free agent.
It is still uncertain if Samuel, who tied for the NFL lead in interceptions in 2006, with 10, will start on Sunday afternoon. But he almost certainly will play in the opener against the division rival Jets.
