Jason Taylor Signs With Dolphins

May 14, 2009 at 5:23am in Featured, Patriots
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Free agent defensive end Jason Taylor has decided to re-sign with the Miami Dolphins after being cut by the Redskins earlier in the off-season. Taylor preferred playing near his home in South Florida, and joins a Dolphins squad coming off a successful 11-5 season.

“My heart has always been in Miami, and so I’m truly excited to call myself a Dolphin once again,” Taylor said in a statement. “I was presented with a number of different opportunities, but in the end the combination of this being the best situation for my family, my love for this community and my tremendous loyalty to a great organization made this an easy decision.”

Tyrone McKenzie Tears ACL

May 7, 2009 at 9:54am in Featured, Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
Add a comment

Rookie linebacker Tyrone McKenzie has torn his ACL and will miss the 2009 season for the Patriots. The Pats picked the South Florida alum in the third round as the 97th overall pick and he was expected to compete for a backup outside linebacker job.

McKenzie was hurt during a drill at rookie mini camp on Saturday when he collided with a running back. Even though McKenzie walked off on his own power, later tests revealed a right knee ACL tear.

My Take: Anytime you see a recent draft pick go down it hurts. Many pundits saw McKenzie as a diamond in the rough, considering he played at three different colleges and has his best days in front of him. Thankfully the Patriots have a lot of young options at linebacker but it seemed as though McKenzie could’ve been a guy who was going to contribute in his rookie season.

Patriots Draft Analysis

April 26, 2009 at 2:08pm in Featured, Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
7 comments

The Patriots have been awfully busy in the first six rounds of the 2009 NFL draft, trading down numerous times and picking up nine players thus far. The biggest news just broke as Ellis Hobbs was traded to the Eagles, leaving room for an entire new secondary in 2009, not such a terrible thing. Here is my analysis on the Pats draft picks and other thoughts on the draft.

2nd Round 34th overall: Patrick Chung – Safety, Oregon: The Pats traded down from 23 to 26 in the first round before trading down yet again and took Chung with the pick received from the Chiefs in the Matt Cassel trade. Chung is smart, versatile and tough as four years starting at Oregon made him the best strong safety in the draft. He’s a guy who can play on special teams as well, seems like a guy who can step in on day 1 and contribute somewhere on the team. Pick Grade: A

Patriots Trade Third-Rounder to Titans

April 26, 2009 at 11:47am in Patriots
KC Downey writes
2 comments

The Patriots traded their 89th overall pick (a third-round selection) to the Tennesee Titans for their 2010 second round pick. By my count, they now have three 2010 second-round picks.

The Patriots have a tremendous amount of depth and have already drafted five players in the first three rounds, so it would appear that they’re now preparing to stockpile for next year’s draft, which is a good move for the future.

Call me crazy, but I don’t think Bill Belichick is done wheeling and dealing.
