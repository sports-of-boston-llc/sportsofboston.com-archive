Arena Football League to Fold

August 5, 2009 at 4:59am in News, Patriots
Mike writes
Add a comment

The 22-year-old Arena Football League appears on the brink of declaring bankruptcy and ceasing operations. The League announced that it was shuttering for 2009 to re-organize amidst its troubles after the last Arena Bowl, hoping to return in 2010. Los Angeles and New Orleans were the first to drop, leaving the league with 15 franchises, but others have folded in the months since as the inevitable is turning into reality. The league had been aired on NBC and ESPN, with the latter holding a small, non-operational, stake in the organization.

New England Connections

The league also had New England connections and had expansion plans (how you have expansion plans as the league is crumbling, I cannot answer) with Boston among the leading sites to land a team. One comment

The days of Marion Butts, Curtis Martin, Robert Edwards and Corey Dillon seem to be over in Foxboro. The Patriots’ offensive scheme has not only changed to pass-oriented, but now employs a strategy sweeping the NFL: running back by committee. Coming into this year’s training camp the Patriots possess numerous options in the backfield which will allow an innumerable amount of options during game situations. Here’s a rundown of the current crop of running backs.

Laurence Maroney

2008 stats: 28 carries, 93 yards, 0 TDs
2009 Forecast: Needless to say, this is a make or break year for Maroney. As a first round pick out of the University of Minnesota, Maroney burst onto the scene in his rookie year as a compliment to Corey Dillon and filled Patriots fans’ heads with visions of the next LaDainian Tomlinson. Since then, injury and ineffectiveness have taken it’s toll on Maroney’s future in New England and it now seems he has perhaps one more chance to prove he belongs in the fold. With the addition of a prime-time runner in Fred Taylor, the Patriots now have created enormous competition for a majority of carries and now that Maroney is healthy, he has no more excuses.

According to Mike Florio of ProFootballTalk, the Patriots on the “verge” of signing former Oakland Raiders quarterback Andrew Walter. The 27-year-old out of Arizona State University has certainly had a very poor professional career. At 6-6, 230 pounds, Walter sure looks the part, but sometimes size doesn’t matter. Michael Vick Visits Patriots? Not Yet…

July 31, 2009 at 6:39pm in Featured, Patriots
KC Downey writes
One comment

So, did Michael Vick visit Foxboro on Friday afternoon?

Many people alleged they saw Michael Vick on a flight to Boston’s Logan Airport. The rumor, according to ProFootballTalk.com, picked up some steam when WEEI broadcasted the rumor on the air.

The Miami Herald then reported that Vick was seen working out with the Patriots on the practice field. The rumor got so hot, that barstoolsports.com reported it as “fact” that Vick was meeting with the Patriots today (Friday).

Bill Belichick, meanwhile, did nothing to stop the rumors from spreading: “Have we ruled it out?” Belichick said about potentially signing Vick.  “I don’t know that it’s ruled in or ruled out.  I don’t think it’s ever been put that way, so I can’t answer that.”

So Michael Vick is a Patriot, right? Wait just a second. Vick, according to the AP, was at a bankruptcy court hearing…in Virginia, which is about 600 miles south of Boston (if you’re driving). So much for the Vick hysteria. Hasn’t this city seen enough breaking news in the last two days with David Ortiz and Victor Martinez?

Patriots Sign Patrick Chung; Place Five on PUP List

July 28, 2009 at 9:06am in Featured, Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
One comment

With rookies reporting to training camp yesterday there was little doubt that second round pick Patrick Chung – though unsigned at the time – would show up to camp and not hold out. Instead of making their rabid fanbase sweat a little, the Patriots and Chung ended all inkling of contractual unrest by agreeing to a deal.

Chung was selected with the 34th overall pick, which the Patriots acquired in the trade with the Chiefs for Mike Vrabel and Matt Cassel. Chung will be in the mix with Brandon Meriweather and James Sanders for time at safety.

The Pats also have played five players on the active physically unable to perform list, including defensive end Ty Warren and outside linebacker Shawn Crable. Warren is recovering from groin surgery and it’s unknown what’s wrong with Crable. Because they are on the active PUP list and not the reserve PUP list, both Warren and Crable can return to training camp when the team feels they are ready.

Five Burning Questions: Part II

July 27, 2009 at 8:47am in Patriots
Matthew Marcantonio writes
One comment

In my previous edition we dove into the question about Tom Brady and his knee. We’ll now shift to the defensive side of the ball and explore a position that has seen a face-lift from a year ago.

Part I: Tom Brady | Part II: The Secondary | Part III | Part IV | Part V

2. Secondary: Who’s going to step up?

The Patriots are a year removed from having one of the league’s worst secondary’s. So what did they do? Bill Belichick and company completely reshaped the positions by trading last season’s number one cornerback, Ellis Hobbs, to the Philadelphia Eagles on draft day in exchange for two fifth round picks. Second year players Terrence Wheatley and Jonathan Wilhite are the lone holdovers from the cornerback position from a year ago that seem to have a chance to make their mark this season.

In free agency the Patriots made it a point of emphasis to upgrade the position. They signed former Washington Redskin Shawn Springs to a three-year deal worth around $10 million in an incentive-laden contract. Springs, who is 34 years old, could potentially see time at both free safety and cornerback. The versatility that he brings to the secondary will allow him to have an immediate impact for this defense. Along with Springs the Patriots added former Cleveland Browns starter Leigh Bodden. Back in 2007, Bodden picked off opposing quarterbacks seven times, which was second best in the NFL. However Bodden, along with most Detroit Lions players, struggled in 2008. After testing the free agent market for two months, Bodden settled for a one year deal with the Patriots on the eve of the NFL Draft. At 6-1, 190 pounds, Bodden provides much needed size to a secondary that has a history of smaller cornerbacks. As of right now it appears that Bodden will be vying to be a starter with the Patriots in 2009. Continue reading Five Burning Questions: Part II »
