Patriots-Packers Trade Details Confirmed

April 25, 2009 at 7:24pm in Patriots
Mike writes
One comment

The Patriots traded the 26th pick and the 162nd pick, both recieved in the Ravens trade to the Packers for their second rounder, number 41, and two third rounders, numbers 73 and 83.  So, essentially, this boils down to giving number 23 away for 41, 73, and 83.  If the Patriots do not see someone they love, this is a great move.  I like it as the draft has a lot of depth, but a dearth of talent at the top once you move outside about 15, so getting more picks in the upper rounds seems like a smart move.

Patriots Make Another Trade

April 25, 2009 at 7:02pm in Patriots
Mike writes
Add a comment

The Patriots are dropping down again, trading the 26th pick they acquired moments ago from the Ravens to the Packers, who took USC Outside Linebacker Clay Matthews, son of former longtime Lion Clay Matthews and nephew of longtime Oiler/Titan Pro Bowl offensive lineman Bruce Matthews.  To complete the Ravens trade, the Patriots got the Ravens fifth rounder.  More details on this as they become available.  This is typical Patriot drafting, stockpiling picks when there is no one they love available.  Eric Mangini is using the blueprint today as well as the Cleveland Browns have traded their fifth pick down three times, amassing more picks and first string players.

Patriots 2009 Draft Preview

April 24, 2009 at 2:28pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
Jeff Christenbury writes
3 comments

It’s that time of year again to dust off the jersey, get your favorite six pack and plop down for the annual April ritual, the NFL draft. A lot has already happened for the Patriots since they played their last game. Matt Cassel, Mike Vrabel and Josh McDaniels are gone, leaving holes in the linebacker corps. There are plenty of needs for the Patriots and plenty of options as they own the 23rd overall pick and three second round picks.

With many picks to trade up with there are rumors floating around that the Pats want to move up in the first round and Sport Illustrated’s Peter King says it may be in the top 10:

“The Patriots are trying to move up into the top 10 of the draft — but low in the top 10. Executives from two teams tell me New England offered its first-round pick and middle of three second-round picks (the 23rd and 47th picks overall) to Jacksonville for the eighth pick in the draft. Jacksonville GM Gene Smith, I’m told, said no … but I also hear the Patriots might be looking to Green Bay at nine to move as well.”

King also said his sources told him they would be looking for an offensive player at that draft slot. A low pick in the top 10 is a lot of guaranteed money but not nearly as much as a high top 10 pick. This draft seems to be very deep but lacks a lot of sure fire home runs. Andy Katzenmoyer Signs with Patriots

April 1, 2009 at 11:55am in Patriots, Top Story
Jeff Christenbury writes
3 comments

One of the prodigal sons of the Pete Carroll era is returning to New England as free agent linebacker Andy Katzenmoyer has made a shock return to the Pats, agreeing to a two-year, incentive laden contract.

Katzenmoyer, who apparently isn’t the same person as ESPN analyst Andy Katz, was drafted by the Patriots with the 28th overall pick in the 1999 NFL draft and played two seasons with the Patriots. He suffered a neck injury in his rookie season and missed the next year due to personal issues. With the Pats seemingly looking to set a record for most linebackers on a roster, Katzenmoyer, 31, says while he hasn’t played football in a few years is still in playing shape at 6’3″, 245 pounds.
