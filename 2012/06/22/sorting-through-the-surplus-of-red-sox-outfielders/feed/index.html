In fact, the outfield dilemma is a problem that most MLB managers would love to have.

Of more concern is the starting pitching, which has been inconsistent, at best through the first 69 games. A major shuffling of the starting rotation has to be made, sooner rather than later, in order to make a bonified charge for a playoff spot in the second half of the season.

There isn’t a starter among the lot who is performing as a #1 or #2 starter should. Closest to that level is Felix Doubrant, who was considered questionable at the start of the season. Buchholz has made progress but his ERA is still up in “John Lackey land” at around 6 rpg. Beckett and Lester are big disappointments and one or both should be put on the trading block. Perhaps shipping both, plus cash, to Seattle for King Felix might light a fire under the Sox rotation.

Better yet, put both in the bullpen, and let guys like Morales, Miller and Aceves have a go at a starter’s job. They cannot do any worse that the collective B & B performance to date.
