]]> By: Bob T. https://sportsofboston.com/2012/02/06/connellys-top-ten-manning-manningham-yes-brady-welker-no/comment-page-1/#comment-21271 Mon, 06 Feb 2012 17:16:22 +0000 http://sportsofboston.com/?p=62222#comment-21271 Here’s the NFL official intentional grounding rule:

“Intentional grounding will be called when a passer, facing an imminent loss of yardage due to pressure from the defense, throws a forward pass without a realistic chance of completion.
Intentional grounding will not be called when a passer, while out of the pocket and facing an imminent loss of yardage, throws a pass that lands at or beyond the line of scrimmage, even if no offensive player(s) have a realistic chance to catch the ball (including if the ball lands out of bounds over the sideline or end line).”

I think safety was the right call. Brady was in the “tackle box” or pocket. He threw the pass to avoid the sack by Tuck (“facing an imminent loss of yardage due to pressure from the defense”). There was no Pats receiver in sight of that pass.

The sad thing is that Brady had plenty of time on the play before Tuck actually arrived. Brady had options. He had to have that clock in his head saying he could not hold on to the ball for too long. He could have just thrown the ball away before there was any pressure (“facing an imminent loss of yardage due to pressure from the defense”),or he could rolled out of the pocket, continued to look for an open receiver, & then thrown it away when he felt pressure.

I think the play showed incredibly bad situational awareness by Brady. If Eli had done that, everyone would say that he was stupid, & Brady was much too smart to do something like that. Too bad. I thought Brady was brilliant for much of the rest of the game, but those 2-points played an important role in the closing minutes.
