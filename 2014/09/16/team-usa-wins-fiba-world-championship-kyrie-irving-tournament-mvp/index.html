Team USA Wins FIBA World Championship, Kyrie Irving Tournament MVP

By mhilperts in Featured, NBA ⋅ September 16, 2014 at 9:11pm ⋅ Post a comment

While there was really never any doubt that Team USA would ultimately win the tournament, there were in fact doubters (or devil’s advocates) who swore the Spanish squad could potentially defeat them, due to what they perceived as better big men on a more disciplined squad.  There was also the rhetoric coming out of these naysayers who claimed that Team USA would be misaligned by FIBA’s rules, due to its style of play that favors competition in the international game.

Team Spain, made up of the truly formidable trio of Marc Gasol, Pau Gasol and Serge Ibaka, lost to France, headlined by Boris Diaw and Nicolas Batum, who, in turn, lost to Serbia, a team with no household names.  While its one-game elimination style of play can certainly create underdogs, illustrated by Serbia’s exciting run to the finals, lightning could not strike a third time for them against the US.

On Sunday, which marked the day of the FIBA Championship game, Team USA squared up against Team Serbia and, to literally nobody’s surprise, utterly steamrolled them.  The entire conversation based around ‘can Team USA lose’, proved to solely exist to eat up minutes on television and fill columns.

This team was the youngest one the US has sent to FIBA since its inception, and to many, was thought to be too inexperienced and immature to win the gold.  Their logic was misplaced, considering there was really no doubt in any game since the tournament began.  Of course there were certain games where the score was close after one half, but with better coaching and better players, the team that came out of the locker room after halftime was truly unstoppable.

Kyrie Irving went 6-6 from 3 in a decisive 129-92 victory, while James Harden added 23 points on 8-for-11 shooting.  Kenneth Faried who, along with Irving, made FIBA’s All-Tournament Team (which also included Pau Gasol, Batum and Miloš Teodosić), had himself an overall monster tournament, where he illustrated to his NBA peers that he is no longer merely a role player but a formidable two-way player.

While the US won the tournament, Serbia deserves nearly as much recognition for its accomplishments.  Serbia had a much tougher road to the final, and for a country with 293 million less people, its talent pool is overwhelmingly smaller.  Thousands gathered outside of Serbia’s parliament to celebrate its silver medal on Tuesday, which goes to show how important these games are to countries outside of the US.

Due to the start of the NFL season, the FIBA tournament never really caught the attention of the American audience, but the tournament’s effect is very much palpable.  The tournament has given many international players the exposure they needed in order to make the jump up to the NBA.  The Phoenix Suns are reportedly considering signing Zoran Dragić, brother of their All-NBA Third Team point guard Goran Dragić, while the Los Angeles Clippers are close to a deal with Australian swingman Joe Ingles.

The debate rages on today as to whether the US should send proven stars or younger players, but as this year’s tournament showed, the US remains the favorite to win in international play.
