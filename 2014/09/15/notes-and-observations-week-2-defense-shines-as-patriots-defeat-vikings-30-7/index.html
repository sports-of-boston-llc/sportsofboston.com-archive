Notes and Observations, Week 2: Defense Shines as Patriots Defeat Vikings 30-7

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ September 15, 2014 at 9:02pm ⋅ Post a comment

Following last week’s woeful performance in Miami, the Patriots were going into a real no win situation against the Vikings. If they won people would react with “so what, it’s the Vikings.” Yet if they lost on Sunday then everyone in New England would be in full-on panic mode just two weeks into the season. Luckily for us the Patriots handled their business as they should have and walked out of Minnesota with an impressive 30-7 victory.

– After the first couple of drives, it had the feel of “here we go again.” The Vikings looked unstoppable on their first possession. They converted on third down twice and killed the defense with crossing patterns which is exactly what Miami did last week. On the touchdown to Matt Asiata, no one picked Asiata up coming out of the backfield and he went untouched for 25 yards for the touchdown. It was definitely frustrating watching the defense be so clearly over-matched on the same types of plays, but luckily they made the correct adjustments and this was the only Vikings score of the game.

– On the first drive for the Patriots it was a familiar three-and-out (I wonder what the odds were for that). However, it was three straight running plays that led to the punt. Stevan Ridley ran up the middle on first and second down for a total of two yards. Shane Vereen then ran the draw play on third down and came up just a yard short. Granted they were close to converting that third down on the ground, but it was confusing to say the least when Tom Brady didn’t even attempt a pass.
– The next drive is when the game, and season, started to turn in the right direction for the Patriots. On a 2nd and 16 play, Matt Cassel heaved a ball down the middle of the field. Devin McCourty was playing center field as the deep safety. McCourty read the pass and jumped in front of it for the interception. The blocks lined up and McCourty was able to bring the ball to the Minnesota one yard line. Ridley then pounded the ball for the short score.
– Cassel was to blame for the interception but credit to McCourty’s ability to read the play, jump the route, and make a good catch. This interception was the first of four, and it set the tone for the rest of the game on defense.
– Darrelle Revis recorded his first interception in a Patriots uniform. It was also the first time I have ever been happy to see a Revis interception. The pick was all Revis too. He stated after the game that he recognized the out route by Greg Jennings in film study. His recognition of the play led him to run the route before Jennings, and Cassel ended up leading Revis instead. Unlike last week, where Mike Wallace had a better game than anyone was expecting while being covered by Revis, this week Revis returned to shutdown form. He was on Jennings the majority of the day which led to a stat line for Jennings of one catch for four yards.
– It’s amazing what a difference a year makes in terms of this secondary. For the first time in a decade they are a strong point on this defense and they are loaded with depth. Logan Ryan, only a second year corner, has shown that he can be relied upon to cover some of the tougher receivers in the game. On Sunday he was tasked with slowing down Cordarrelle Patterson. All week Patterson was being hyped as someone who can hurt you in a number of different ways. Patterson finished the game with only four catches for 56 yards and the biggest stat is that he had no touchdowns. Ryan capped off his impressive day with an interception of his own.
– Ryan was filling in for Alfonzo Dennard who had a shoulder injury flare up while preparing for the game. As big of a Dennard fan as I am, the Ryan performance was exactly what this defense needed to show how deep their secondary is. It’ll only get better when Brandon Browner is back from suspension.
– Of course the standout performance of the day goes to Chandler Jones. Last week he was hindered by a couple of tough roughing the passer penalties. This week he wasn’t flagged and had a career game. Jones sacked Cassel twice and both were impressive. The left tackle he was going against, Matt Kalil, is no slouch. He was the fourth overall pick in the 2012 draft and is set to be their franchise left tackle. Jones was able to work around him for two sacks, and pressure on Cassel the majority of the game. The cherry on top of this magnificent defensive performance was on his blocked field goal at the end of the first half. Jones basically stepped over the offensive line and got his hands on the ball. He then picked up the ball on a bounce, in stride, and ran it 70 yards for a touchdown. It was one of the most athletic plays I’ve ever seen out of a defensive lineman and showed off all of Chandler Jones’ skills on one play.
– Dominque Easley, the Patriots scrutinized first round draft pick, showed what he’s capable of on Sunday as well. The interception he had was more right place at the right time, but it was his awareness that allowed him to drop back off the ball and pick it off in the first place. He took advantage of the focus on Chandler Jones and was able to create pressure and force Cassel to Jones’ side. It’s obviously still early to make a verdict on whether it was a good draft choice or not, but early on he is showing the signs of an interior lineman pass rusher that the Patriots haven’t had in a long time.
– Dont’a Hightower was another standout on defense Sunday. He finished the day with eight tackles and two sacks of his own. Hightower played more like Brandon Spikes from last year. He was around the line of scrimmage all night and was sent on blitzes throughout the game. This is when Hightower is at his best. When he’s allowed to attack the line of scrimmage he has the ability to blow plays up in the backfield. What was even more impressive is he did this on a day when Jamie Collins was inactive. The lack of Adrian Peterson plays into this as the defense was able to key in on stopping the pass. They had the freedom to send more blitz packages and Hightower took advantage of it.
– It’s been years since the defense has dominated the discussion for the Patriots in a positive light, but this is a welcomed change in New England. The offense didn’t have a particularly great game, but they reverted back to 2001 Patriots and did what they had to do to win the game.
– The offensive line looked to be more consistent this game as they kept the same five out there for the majority. I mentioned last week that consistency is needed on this offensive line and Sunday they had exactly that. Brady was sacked only once and that came when the game was already decided. Stevan Ridley was able to rattle off 101 yards on 25 carries and the touchdown mentioned earlier. This was vintage Patriots football from the championship years. Keep the ball moving, don’t turn it over, and let the defense make plays.
– There is a bit of a trend developing on this offense. Brady does not appear confident in his receivers not named Julian Edelman or Rob Gronkowski. Sunday Brady completed 15 passes, and 10 of them were to Edelman and Gronk. For a guy who made a career spreading the ball around to virtual unknown receivers, it’s troubling to see him struggle to find consistency with a group that appears to have the talent.
– For instance Aaron Dobson made his season debut yesterday. After last season, foot injury aside, there is a lot of hope that he can be the outside threat the team needs. He has the body type and minus the few drops last year, he does have some of the better hands on the team. Yet with all that he only had one catch on two targets.
– I mentioned last week that I don’t see Danny Amendola in a Patriots uniform next season and Sunday was another example why. He didn’t even show up on the stat sheet. That means that Brady is more confident looking at James Develin, two catches for 17 yards on two targets, than Amendola. Keep in mind Amendola is one of the highest paid receivers on the team. Unless his season takes a drastic turn, I can’t see how they keep him next year.
– Speaking of James Develin, what’s not to love about this guy? Since last season he has shown he can do whatever they ask of him. He can block for runs, he can run the ball himself for the tough yards, and now he has shown he has good hands and the trust of Tom Brady. Guys like James Develin are the difference makers on championship teams. They don’t make the pretty plays but they make the plays you need to have in order to win.
– It is far too early to say the offense is terrible or place blame on anyone in particular. In football you don’t know what type of team you have until after Thanksgiving. The offense didn’t do anything extraordinary yesterday, but they made the plays they needed to make and never turned the ball over. They will come together and Brady will find his rhythm with his receivers.
– All things considered this was a strong team win. They had contributions from offense, defense, and special teams, and handled a team the way they were supposed to. It didn’t get close and the defense made the Vikings offense look completely lost without Adrian Peterson. It’s a promising step forward from last week and now they can look ahead to the season opener against the Raiders.
