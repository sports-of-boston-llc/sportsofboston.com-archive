Redraft King: Week 4 Redraft Advice

By Jon Ellis in Fantasy, Featured, Opinion ⋅ September 25, 2014 at 9:00am ⋅ Post a comment

In the interest of full disclosure, before we dive to week 4 advice, let’s review where I was right and where I was wrong in week 3. If you missed it, here was my week 3 advice column.

Week 3 Recap:

Tom Brady turned out to be a terrible call, the Pats offense continues to struggle this season. Josh McCown was a laughable prediction—it’s probably for the best that he hurt his thumb before he had a chance to throw anymore interceptions, or perpetrate anymore fumbles. And my only decent call at QB from last week was EJ Manuel who scored 15 points by ESPN standard scoring.

All three of my running backs: Joique Bell, Matt Asiata and Khiry Robinson, despite a healthy number of touches, were all disappointments. Hopefully those players were cheap enough and you had leftover money to pay for elite RBs.

I faired better when it came to WR’s. A.J. Green had 102 receiving yards. Andrew Hawkins had 87 yards on 7 receptions. And Kelvin Benjamin had a big day, 8 receptions for 115 yards and a touchdown.

And thankfully despite minimal yardage, Rob Gronkowksi got into the end zone to salvage his fantasy week.

 

All in all, week 3 could have been worse. Now it is time to pick up the pieces and look at week 4 redraft players that can be acquired for minimal cost, but have substantial upside.

Week 4 Advice:

QB

Phillip Rivers ($7,700) – The 9th most expensive QB for week 4. The biggest thing that Rivers has going for him is the fact that he is playing Jacksonville (the worst defense in the league thus far) at home. The main concern with using Rivers is that the Chargers will probably up by so much that they will just run the ball in the 4th quarter. Nevertheless, even three quarters of Rivers should be worth the money.

Tony Romo ($6,700) – Romo hans’t been terrible this season from a fantasy football perspective. Everyone on the offense is healthy, and in week 4 Dallas takes on the Saints at home. Two high octane offenses, with two sub-par defenses. Expect Romo to have a nice showing here in what should be a shootout.

Teddy Bridgewater ($5,700) – The advantage to Bridgewater is that he is the second cheapest QB this week that is the slated starter. Let the record show: considering that this Sunday will be Bridgewater’s first NFL start, playing is a huge gamble. However, the Falcons defense isn’t great (despite how good the Bucs made the Atlanta defense look last week) and Bridgewater should be able to score some additional fantasy points with his legs. Buyer be warned; this is a risky but cost effective move.

RB

Ahmad Bradshaw ($5,800) – Clearly a better real life running back and fantasy running back than teammate Trent Richardson. Bradshaw will also catch some passes, where he does most of his damage. A great value for a reliable player.

Eddie Lacy ($5,600) – A preseason top 5 running back, who has had three bad weeks so far this season. Hence, the low cost. But bear in mind that between the Seahawks, Jets, and Lions—Lacy has faced some of the best run defenses. Chicago’s defense is comparatively weak and Lacy should have a monster day in week 4.

Donald Brown ($5,400) – Brown isn’t the most talented running back of all time. But in week 3 after Danny Woodhead left the game with a season ending injury, Brown rushed the ball 31 times. That type of volume simply doesn’t exist in today’s passing league. Keep in mind with 31 rushes Brown yielded a paltry 62 yards and no touchdowns. But, they are playing Jacksonville this week, and Ryan Matthews is still out. He’s more or less all they have at the running back position. With a huge number of carries comes an increased chance of finding the end zone.

WR

Jordy Nelson ($7,200) – The entire Green Bay offense had a terrible week 3 against the Lions. And Nelson has dropped to the 7th ranked receiver. Aaron Rodgers encouraged anxious fans to “relax” earlier this week, and Jordy Nelson is poised for a massive resurgence.

Alshon Jeffery ($6,600) – Jeffery has not scored a touchdown yet this season. It is simply a matter of time before he does. Brandon Marshall’s ankle seems like it is a greater injury concern than Jeffery’s bum hamstring. In week 3 with Marshall missing significant game time, Jeffery flourished seeing 13 targets for 8 catches and 103 yards. Jeffery will likely cross the end zone against a porous Green Bay defense, however, this decision is predicated on keeping an eye on the injury report.

Cecil Shorts III ($5,000) – Shorts saw eight targets from Blake Bortles once the rookie QB took over for Chad Henne in the second half. He only caught 5 balls for 35 yards, but he did find his way into the end zone. Now that Shorts is healthy he will be the top target. Against San Diego this week, the Jags, as usual, will probably be down in the second half and Bortles will take to the air in an attempt to bridge the gap. Assuming that is the case, Cecil Shorts III will be a beneficiary of the increased passing attack.

TE

Owen Daniels ($4,100) – Daniels enjoyed fantasy glory days when Gary Kubiak was his head coach in Houston. With Dennis Pitta done for the year with a hip injury, Daniels will be the go to receiving tight end. He is a viable red zone threat, and proved that in week 2 with two touchdowns on only 5 catches.

Travis Kelce ($3,700) – This move is dependent on the status of Jamaal Charles. If Charles plays against the Patriots on Monday night, New England’s defense will need to adjust their scheme to account for the big play running back, perhaps leaving the tight end open. Plus, adding to Kelce’s value, there are very limited passing options on the Chiefs roster for poor Alex Smith. Kelce is a very cheap option whose fantasy numbers will likely be dictated by whether or not he can cross the goal line.

 

Week 4 also marks the beginning of bye weeks in the NFL and six teams do not play this week. Therefore, weekly fantasy redraft value will be hard to come by. Remember, these are value picks that should allow you to spend money on multiple star players for your weekly roster. Happy hunting.
