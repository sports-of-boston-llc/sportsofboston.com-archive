Bruins End Disappointing West Coast Trip on Positive Note

By Jon Lyons (@TheRealJonLyons) in Boston, Bruins, NHL ⋅ December 8, 2014 at 9:00am ⋅ Post a comment

The Bruins played four road games in six nights this week, all on the west coast. They went   in the four games. The trip began with back to back games in Anaheim and Los Angeles against the Ducks and Kings, respectively. The Bruins lost a hard fought game to the Ducks, 3-2, and 2-0 to the Kings in another hard fought affair. Then, on Thursday, the Bruins traveled to San Jose and played one of their poorer defensive games of the year, losing 7-4 to the Sharks. The trip concluded with a 5-2 against the Arizona Coyotes, which saw the Bruins play a strong game wire to wire.

In the first game of the trip, the Bruins came out strong early on. Their first period was highlighted by two penalty kills that saw them generate several short-handed chances including a near goal by Daniel Paille. They got on the board early in the second period when Brad Marchand netted a power play goal. Their lead was short lived however, with the Ducks scoring less than a minute later to tie the game. The Ducks then added another goal to take a 2-1 lead into the second intermission. Anaheim then scored in the third to make it 3-1. The Bruins did have a surge in the third period and were able to make it a 3-2 game on Simon Gagne’s second goal of the year. The Bruins were not able to come all the way back and fell 3-2 in disappointing fashion. Coach Claude Julien summed it up best after the game saying, “”I’m certainly not going to criticize the effort.  I thought we played hard and I really feel like we should have won that game.”

In the second night of the back to back the Bruins traveled to Los Angeles to play the defending Stanley Cup Champion Kings. In the first period, the Bruins looked outmatched and struggled to get anything going. The Kings continued pressure paid off late in the first period with star center Anze Kopitar sending a slick pass to Tanner Pearson for a goal. The goal was the result of a turnover in the neutral zone by the Bruins. The Kings led 1-0 after one period. In the second, the Bruins played better but still not well enough to score. Claude Julien did some line shuffling that reunited Reilly Smith with usual line mates Patrice Bergeron and Brad Marchand. David Pastrnak, the young offensively gifted Bruins draft pick, looked physically outclassed by the Kings and played the remainder of the game with Milan Lucic and Carl Soderberg. The third period was capped off by a Tyler Toffoli empty-net goal to ice the game with less than a minute left. It was another decent effort overall from the Bruins especially after the first period. Decent efforts were not enough to begin this trip as they fell to 0-2 against two of the Western Conference’s top teams.

In the third game of the trip, the Bruins checked in with their best offensive effort to the tune of scoring four goals against the Sharks. However, they also contributed one of their worst defensive efforts of the season, giving up seven goals. The Bruins jumped on the board early, scoring two goals in the first five minutes of play. They led 2-0 after one period. The second period was one the wildest of the season. It saw back and forth action with a total of six goals scored. Tuukka Rask had faced 30 shots by the end of the second period. Both teams made defensive mistakes and played well offensively to make for an entertaining twenty minutes. The Sharks outscored the Bruins 4-2 in the second and it was a 4-4 tie heading into the third. In the third, the wheels fell off for Boston. They looked lethargic and over-matched; giving up three goals and going on to lose 7-4. Of the seven goals that Rask allowed, at least four were due to deflections out front in which Bruin defenders did not have a strong net front presence. The Sharks had three players with two goals apiece: Joe Pavelski, Tommy Wingels, and Tyler Kennedy. Reilly Smith snapped an 11 game goalless streak with two goals in the defeat.

The west coast swing concluded with a Saturday night game in Arizona to play the Coyotes. The Bruins came in looking to avoid a disastrous 0-4 road trip and they were able to do just that. Brad Marchand got the Bruins off to another hot start by scoring a goal less than a minute in that was set up by a great read by Dougie Hamilton in the neutral zone. The Bruins kept up the pressure and Kevan Miller added a goal later in the period and the Bruins headed to intermission up 2-1 and playing well. In the second, the Bruins added three more goals. The first came from Simon Gagne followed by Marchand again. Loui Eriksson broke a 12 game scoreless streak to make the game 5-1. Eriksson’s goal was also significant because it came on the power play, the Bruins first on the man advantage in their last 18 tries. The Coyotes added one more goal in the third but it was not enough, the Bruins left Arizona with a 5-2 win.

The trip overall was a disappointment for the Bruins with a finishing mark of 1-3. The first two games were hard fought losses in which offense was absent. The third game was a nightmare for the defensive minded Bruins and despite scoring four goals no one was happy with the seven that were given up. Things seemed to get back on track to close out the trip with a wire to wire solid win against Arizona. The Bruins will have some time to rest up as their next game is not until Thursday against the Blackhawks. The hope is that by Thursday they will start to get back some of their players that have been injured and missed significant time, most notably David Krejci and Zdeno Chara. The stretch without two of their best players has been a battle and the Bruins hope that battle is coming to an end soon.
