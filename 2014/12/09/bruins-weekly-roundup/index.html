Bruins Weekly Roundup

By Tim Wirzburger in Bruins, NHL, Opinion, Rumors ⋅ December 9, 2014 at 9:00am ⋅ Post a comment

Jordan Eberle Trade Target

There’s been a lot of buzz lately on the Bruins and Edmonton talking trades, specifically with Jordan Eberle being a target the B’s would love. This makes sense to some degree– the Bruins are hurting for a scoring winger and the Oilers are just desperate to shake things up. However, I really don’t see any package that gets this deal done that makes sense for both teams. People love to throw out the old, Kelly+Spooner+pick but truthfully that doesn’t even come close to being a good offer. Ryan Spooner just doesn’t have the value of being a top prospect anymore. Truth hurts.

There was also a rumor today of Bruins/Oilers discussing Nail Yakupov, with Krug+ being thrown the other way. I just don’t see this happening either. The Oilers have a similar player in Justin Schultz and I sincerely doubt that Julien and Chiarelli would deal away Kessel and Seguin but would be comfortable being patient with Yakupov. Ain’t happening.

Tyler Seguin Flourishing

This is a conversation we’ll probably be having for a long time. The two sides have become, “We gave up on him too soon, he’s a star, maybe he didn’t fit in our scheme but at some point we have to adapt our scheme to fit in superstars like him,” or the counter, “Seguin refused to play defense, he was a problem off the ice, he’s not a team player. The value we got him was much better than what he would give us.”

I like to look at the facts. Yes, Seguin is leading the league in points. But notice something else… The Stars are a basement dwelling team in the West. He can score three goals but if his team is giving up four, what good is it? I don’t blame Seguin for the Stars being bad, but at some point you need your studs to be the leaders in all three zones. As talented as he is, he’s not someone to build around. I would love to have him on my team, but I think the Bruins made the right move. Just like Joe Thornton has been unable to get anywhere with his Sharks, Seguin can score all the points he wants but until he leads his team to a championship I think the Bruins are better off without him.

Chara and Krejci Injuries

Zdeno Chara is getting closer to returning as he has been skating with Condition Coach John Whitesides. I think it’s important that the team doesn’t rush him back, no matter how bad we may be doing. He’s no young buck any more and we need him healthy in the playoffs a lot more than we need him now. By the way, I know it doesn’t feel like it but the Bruins are 3-4 games over .500 since Chara went down. Some of those wins were shaky, but they’re faring well all things considered.

Krejci is on the IR as well, but we should see him soon too. His return not only brings his production back to the offense but Milan Lucic’s as well. Lucic has been a shell of himself at times without Krejci setting him up. He also made Seth Griffith a productive first line player for a spurt there. I would like to see Pastrnak getting a shot on a line with Krejci and Lucic and see if they can get something going.

Plus/Minus Woes

Plus/Minus is one of those stats that people tend to disagree on its value in measuring a player’s performance, but I always thought it was a good indicator of how well a guy can do without the puck. It’s the stuff that doesn’t get on the stat sheet: back checking, finishing a hit, making a clean pass, etc.. And the Bruins have always done tremendously there over the past several seasons, consistently having players among the top of the league. Hell, Adam McQuaid led the league in +/- one year.

This season has been a different story. Through 28 games, Brad Marchand leads the team with a paltry +6. They’ve got several starters coming in at a minus, including perennial Selke nominee Patrice Bergeron. Does this worry you? It worries me a bit.

Redemption of Jordan Caron

One of the pinnacles in a string of disappointing prospects drafted by Chiarelli, Caron has been underwhelming at the NHL level. He has not been able to latch onto a starting job on the roster and projectors to be a 3rd/4th liner at best in this league. However, he has also shown flashes of being a useful, serviceable player at times. He can protect the puck well, play physical, and he has decent hands.

Which brings me to the question: if Jordan Caron reinvented his game as a fighter, would he be someone you need on your team? He’s not much of a fighter now but he does have a handful of bouts on his resume. The Bruins find themselves lacking some toughness with Thornton and Boychuk donning different colors this year. Is Jordan Caron, a strong kid who has some offensive touch to his game in addition to being a brawler, someone you need to have on your 20 man roster? What say you, Bruins fans?

Trade Malcom Subban?

Just a quick note here. A lot of the Bruins rumors have included Subban in deals going the other way. I’m not surprised– I’m sure a ton of GMs are asking about him. But if I’m Chiarelli, that’s a chip I’m keeping in my pocket. Do I think he’s going to overtake Rask for the starting job some day? Of course not. But he’s a young goalie and he’s got a bright future. His value is only going to go up. I think the best thing to do is keep him around, let him develop his game, and deal him when he’s closer to being a legit NHL goaltender.
