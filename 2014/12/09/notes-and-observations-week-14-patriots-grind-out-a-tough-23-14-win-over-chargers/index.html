Notes and Observations Week 14: Patriots Grind Out a Tough 23-14 Win Over Chargers

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ December 9, 2014 at 8:21am ⋅ Post a comment

Sunday night wasn’t pretty by any means, but games in December and January usually aren’t. Both the Patriots and Chargers needed the victory but each had their own reasons. The Patriots needed the win to keep pace in the AFC and hold onto the number one seed. The Chargers, on the other hand, needed the win to keep their playoff hopes alive against the competitive AFC field. With the offense not exactly looking sharp, the defense rose to the occasion on the road. Strong play all around defensively kept the Patriots in it long enough for Tom Brady and the offense to finally put it together. The end result was a bounce back win on the road, against a tough, playoff-caliber team.

– “Next man up,” has always been the Patriots philosophy, but no more so than the last two seasons. We all remember how decimated the team was with injuries last season, only to still make it to the AFC championship game. This year, they again lost Jerod Mayo for the season, and Sunday night saw Dont’a Hightower on the inactive list. Hightower had stepped up in a major way becoming the signal caller on the defense in Mayo’s absence. He along side Jamie Collins had successfully held down the linebacker positions. Without Hightower, a drop off in production could have been expected. Instead Jamie Collins played his best game as a pro.

– Collins stepped in as the signal caller which could have led to some issues as he is still only in his second NFL season. Rarely was the defense out of position prior to the snap, and Collins seemed to be in on just about every play. He finished with a team-high nine tackles, eight of which were solo. He also added two sacks where he simply ran through the middle of the offensive line virtually untouched. He’s been timing his blitzes well all season, as well as successfully disguising blitzes/coverage on a regular basis. Belichick is huge on the “jump from years one and two,” for young guys. It’s safe to say Collins has made that jump.

– You simply cannot say enough about Belichick’s mid-season pick-ups in Alan Branch, Akeem Ayers, and Jonathan Casillas. Branch alongside the returning Sealver Siliga, and Vince Wilfork clogged the lanes for the Chargers’ rushing attack. They managed just 53 yards on 17 carries. Ayers added to his list of accomplishments with a timely interception after the refs called back Devin McCourty’s pick-six (more on that in a moment). Casillas was in on five tackles of his own. The biggest stat is that Ayers and Casillas played in the majority of the defensive snaps, 54-56 and 50-56 respectively.

– Going into this season the Patriots had only one back-up linebacker. The worst happened and they lost Mayo for the season. Since then all we’ve seen is Hightower and Collins step up to be the players the Patriots hoped they’d be when they drafted them. We’ve all seen unknown commodities such as Ayers and Casillas find success after they could hardly even find the field on their old teams. The “buy low” philosophy doesn’t always work, but this season that strategy has paid substantial dividends.

– On McCourty’s called back pick-six. Of course the call was absolutely bogus. Brandon Browner could not have hit Ladarius Green more cleanly. It was all shoulder and Browner’s helmet, at the most, barely grazed Green’s. That was a reaction call by the officials to seeing Green’s head snap back. Unfortunately that bad call cost the Patriots a touchdown. Of course everything worked out and they pulled out the win, but in a tight game that call could have been a game changer. Those calls should be able to be reviewed. In game-time speed it’s almost impossible to decipher who hit who and with what. If you want to argue “defenseless receiver,” keep in mind that Green was bobbling the ball, was not airborne, and neither was Browner. That was a clean hit and a bad call.

– Darrelle Revis had another outstanding game as he took Keenan Allen completely out of their offense. Allen finished with just two catches for three yards, and wasn’t even targeted until midway through the third quarter. Revis makes this defense work and the man needs to be locked up long term.

– A big factor on defense was the play of the defensive line and the front seven. They were able to take advantage of the issues at center for the Chargers sending a lot of pressure up the middle. Collins registered both his sacks rushing up the middle, and in general Philip Rivers had a tough time stepping up in the pocket all game. Rivers was sacked four times and finished the game 20-33 for 189 yards and just the one touchdown. Other than the Malcom Floyd touchdown, the defense didn’t allow another score.

– Rob Ninkovich has become the iron man. He played every snap Sunday night and was able to successfully generate pressure from the outside for the majority of the game. With Chandler Jones still out Ninkovich has stepped up to become that individual pass rusher. He was also big in the run game, wrapping up running backs for a couple tackles for loss. Hopefully in a week or two Chandler is able to make his return which will only benefit this already impressive defense.

– Devin McCourty was asked to play cornerback for an extended period on Sunday and he delivered. The Patriots have certainly had their issues with opposing teams’ tight ends, and going up against one of the game’s best in Antonio Gates could have been a daunting task. A combination of McCourty, Chung, and at times, Browner held the potential hall of famer to only five catches for 34 yards. Belichick has been able to take away a team’s top two targets for the majority of the season and Sunday night with Allen and Gates was no different.

– Sealver Siliga made his return and fit in nicely on the defensive line. They rotated Wilfork, Siliga, and Branch throughout the game. The one constant was whichever two of them were next to each other made it nearly impossible for the Chargers to run up the middle. All three of them are nose-tackle style defensive lineman, but they all have the ability to break into the backfield as well. That ability was shown on Siliga’s half-sack he recorded along with Chris Jones. With Siliga back and Jones a week or two out, the defense can only get better.

– Another quick note about Siliga, what is Chris Collinsworth’s problem? Why does he insist on calling him “Silver Silinga”? Where is he finding an “N” in his name? This also goes for everytime he pats himself on the back for only slightly mispronouncing Michael Hoomanwanui’s name. Silly me thinking a national broadcast color commentator would do a little prep work.

– This was another less than stellar showing from the offensive line. Similar to last week they had some protection miscues which caused Brady to dance around more than he would have liked to. Nate Solder in particular had a really tough time with Dwight Freeney. I’m a little surprised seeing as Freeney has done the same exact spin move his entire career. It’s literally the only move he ever does. You would think that Solder would have taken the time to study his one move. They finally got it together for the fourth quarter, but it was cringe-worthy for the majority of the game.

– Rob Gronkowski and Julian Edelman had huge games as they were Brady’s go-to targets the entire game. Edelman is clearly playing hurt as on a few occasions he came up limping, but he still toughed out the entire game. It was an impressive game at that going for eight catches, 141 yards and a huge 69-yard touchdown catch. I have a strong feeling Gronk later joked about the yardage of the catch.

– As soon as Al Michaels mentioned Brandon Flowers was on Gronk, everyone knew Brady was going to him. Flowers may be one of the better corners in the league but it doesn’t change the fact he’s 5’9” covering the 6’6” Gronk in the redzone. Brady saw the mismatch and delivered a laser right to Gronk for an easy score. Through these last few weeks everyone is just praying Gronk stays healthy because we have still yet to see him fully healthy in the playoffs.

– Brady hasn’t been lighting the world on fire statistically the past couple weeks by any means. Last week he was a few plays away from notching a win in Green Bay, while this week he made just enough to plays to grind out a tough one in San Diego. The fact is these past two weeks have been tough games on the road, against two playoff-caliber teams. Not every game is going to be a blowout and you should take comfort in knowing that this team is in it until the end every single game.

– For instance this game could have gone south very quickly. After the Brandon LaFell fumble that was returned for a touchdown it was quickly 14-3. The offense was having a difficult time getting anything going and were about to take the lead before the half until Brady through an awful interception to Manti Te’o. Instead of sulking the defense stepped up and held the Chargers scoreless the rest of the game. Brady and the offense were able to make enough plays to lock up their 10th win of the season.

– Credit to Brandon Bolden as well. He may not play much but he tends to make an impact when he’s on the field. He’s had more than a few big tackles on kickoffs this season. Last week he had a nice rushing touchdown. This week he had a huge blocked punt that swayed momentum and ended up knocking Mike Scifres out of the game. It’s the little things that matter in December and January and the Patriots have a strong cast of role players that will come in handy.

– The last three games are no joke. They’re against the Dolphins at home, the Jets on the road, and the season ends at home against the Bills. The Dolphins as we all remember handed us a big loss in week one. They are also wildly inconsistent. Playing them in the cold of Gillette is much different than playing them in heat of summer in Miami. Division games are always tough since the opponent knows you so well. If the Patriots handle their business like they should, then they will be sitting comfortably with the number one seed in the AFC.

– We’re onto Miami.
