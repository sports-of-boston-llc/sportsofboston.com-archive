Redraft King: Week 6 2014 Advice

By Jon Ellis in Fantasy, Opinion ⋅ October 9, 2014 at 2:15pm ⋅ Post a comment

Unfortunately for this column I have set the precedent of reviewing my advice from the prior week. I wish I hadn’t. But I did. Here it goes.

Week 5 Recap:

Why didn’t I promote Austin Davis, Branden Oliver, Kendall Wright or Tim Wright! My week 5 advice didn’t happen to highlight those relatively obscure players who lit up fantasy scoreboards last week. I’ll take my lumps and continue to forge ahead seeking out players who have high upside and comparatively low cost. If you want to see the full carnage just check out last week’s column for yourself. 

Andrew Luck was good but not as great. And Mike Glennon repaid his low cost ($5,500) with 249 yards 2 touchdowns and a pick.

Marshawn Lynch had another great game. Rashad Jennings was off to a nice start but left the game early with a sprained MCL. And my speculation that the Steelers would blow out Jacksonville and hand the rock to LeGarrette Blount was dead wrong.

Wes Welker had a fairly poor performance and it seems so far in Denver this will be Welker’s role going forward. Michael Floyd hung fantasy owners out to dry with one catch for seven yards, you should wait on Cardinals wide receivers until Carson Palmer returns to the helm. And to top things off for my week 5 advice, Kelvin Benjamin had his worst game of the season.

And that was it.

Wait. What? I provide advice about tight ends too? Rats. I hoped you had forgotten.

Zach Ertz was abysmal last week. But on the bright side Jordan Cameron caught every pass thrown to him. All three. For thirty-three yards. Nine yards less than his buddy Zach Ertz. (Note: they may or may not actually be ‘buddies.’)

 

Week 6 Advice:

QB

Jay Cutler ($8,000) – Cutler is the 7th most expensive signal caller this week and has a great matchup against a poor Atlanta defense. This game is two high-powered offenses going against two sub-par defenses and their should be fantasy points aplenty.

Brian Hoyer ($6,100) – This is why you play the game. Anything can happen on any given Sunday. Yada yada yada. Who thought Hoyer would still have the Clevland QB job in October? I didn’t. Most of America didn’t. But he hasn’t been half-bad this season. Against a not-so-great division rival in Pittsburgh, coming to the Dog Pound, I think Hoyer has a nice day.

Blake Bortles – ($5,700) – If you want to be even stingier with your fake money and pass on Hoyer—how about Blake Bortles? Here’s my thinking. It is unlikely the (0-5) Jags have a winless season. What better time to rack up some wins against a demoralized Titans team that has to play at home again after losing the largest lead ever for a home team. You think those fans will be happy to see them? No. But maybe, just maybe, the Jags put a tick under the win column and in order to do so Bortles has a nice game.

RB

Lamar Miller ($5,800) / Knowshon Moreno ($4,200) – The Packers defense has given up well over a hundred yards on the ground each game so far this season. Moreno looks iffy to play. If Moreno does not suit up Lamar Miller should have a great day on the ground. If Moreno is in he has shown to be the preferred back in Miami, the only hesitation would be that the elbow is still an issue and if he comes back Moreno may see limited snaps. If Moreno plays, expect a little bit less for Miller.

Branden Oliver ($5,500) – Great matchup against a wayward Oakland team. The Chargers signing Ronnie Brown is indicative of how beaten up their running back corps is. Week 6 may be Oliver’s last time uncontested in the backfield. Of course, watch the status of other San Diego running backs if you choose Oliver.

Bishop Sankey ($3,500) – Sankey has been playing second fiddle to Shonn Greene for most of his rookie season. One day soon the Titans will realize that Sankey should be playing first fiddle. Even if Sankey still splits carries with Greene this week, it is hard not to like the matchup against the Jaguars.

WR

Golden Tate ($6,300) – All indications are that Calvin Johnson will sit a week out to repair his high ankle sprain. If Johnson suits up Sunday he will only be used a one-legged decoy. Golden Tate will see the large majority of the targets from Matt Stafford.

Mohamed Sanu ($5,300) – AJ Green was carted off the practice field and coach Marvin Lewis thinks Green could be dealing with more than just turf toe. If Green sits, Sanu, who has already had a decent season should see an increase in targets. Also keep an eye on whether Marvin Jones returns (which as of Thursday looking unlikely) because Sanu might be the only regular receiver Dalton can target with confidence.

Roddy White ($4,800) – White has had a fairly quiet season. But as I mentioned in the top, the Bears at the Falcons should be an offensive bloodbath that is rife with offensive production. White is priced at a discount and is poised to enjoy a big day against a poor defense.

TE

Tim Wright ($3,000) – Week 5 all of New England collectively enjoyed a sigh of relief. In addition to Brady running around like a spring chicken, Tim Wright had a nice coming out party. Often times with the Patriots complex offense new pass catching additions do not learn the playbook (cough, Chad Ochocino, cough) but Wright seems to be stepping into the second tight end role which makes the Pats a dangerous offense yet again.

 

Good luck out there in week 6. By the point in the season there shouldn’t be too many surprises, except for the surprise players that see the field due to a teammates injury. Oh, and if you win big this week, tell them that the Redraft King sent you.
