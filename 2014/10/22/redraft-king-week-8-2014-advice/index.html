Redraft King: Week 8 2014 Advice

By Jon Ellis in Fantasy, Featured, Opinion, Top Story ⋅ October 22, 2014 at 9:31pm ⋅ Post a comment

Greetings millionaires! The Redraft King hopes that you consider donating a portion of your winnings from week 7 to a charity of your choice. Because if you followed my advice last week you probably vanquished all of your fantasy enemies and are now doing the backstroke through mountains of gold coins. So it is with great cheer and self-admiration (with one exception) I review my week 7 advice.

Week 7 Recap:

My prediction that Colin Kapernick and the 49ers would be in a hole against the Broncos was correct, but he was woefully unable to dig his team out. Alex Smith’s 15.7 fantasy points was almost exactly in line with his average fantasy points per game so far this season (15.8).

Ronnie Hillman was an absolute beast scoring 26.3 points in PPR formats, it appears that he has firmly entrenched himself as Denver’s starting running back. Jerick McKinnon had a very solid outing against a stingy Bills defense, rushing for 103 yards on 19 carries. I correctly assumed that Green Bay would blow the doors off the Panthers, but they didn’t feed Eddie Lacy the way I expected. That being said, he still had a great game with 16.3 fantasy points.

Last week I cited Terrance Williams’ unprecedented touchdown to reception ratio, he didn’t see many targets here, but the one pass he did haul in went for an 18 yard touchdown. Rookie Sammy Watkins had a great game-winning touchdown catch to cap off an extraordinary 36.2 point fantasy day. Odell Beckham Jr.’s fantasy day was saved by two touchdowns and an accumulation of 20.4 fantasy points.

I was wrong when I assumed receiver Kelvin Benjamin would miss this game, but it didn’t hinder Greg Olsen’s fantasy performance as he caught eight balls for 105 yards. And the Saints’ Josh Hill was easily my worst call of the week (1.8 points), it turned out that Jimmy Graham played but that didn’t impact the game and the Saints continue to look uncharacteristically terrible on offense.

Week 8 Advice:

QB

Kyle Orton ($6,300) – I never dreamed I’d be promoting career journeyman Kyle Orton. But, lo and behold, here we are. In three games this season he has scored 18, 19 and 17 fantasy points. In that span he has faced two solid defenses in the Patriots and Lions. Exceeding his 18 point average should be easy against the hapless Jets secondary. Considering the Bills top two running backs are injured and the Jets specialty is run defense, the veteran Orton will be called upon to increase the passing attack.

Colt McCoy ($5,600) – I also never thought I’d be suggesting Colt McCoy, whom until last week appeared to be an NFL dud. However, he looked confident in a comeback win against the Titans as he completed 11 of 12 passes. McCoy has been ruled the starter at this point in the week but coach Jay Gruden hasn’t shut the door on a possible RGIII comeback (be wary). Dallas’ defense has been far better than expected but has allowed an average of 230 yards through the air. Washington will likely be behind early and McCoy will need to take to the air.

Teddy Bridgewater ($5,100) – Bridgewater hasn’t lit up the box score yet this season, which is why he is the cheapest starting quarterback this week. The chance to see Bridgewater’s first breakout game is mathematically increased by facing the abysmal Tampa Bay defense. There is inherent risk as the young signal caller hasn’t had a good game yet but for the same cost as a lower tier receiver or running back, Bridgewater will allow you to spend big money on top tier runners and receivers.

 

RB

Jamaal Charles ($6,700) – Do you know how many touchdowns have been scored by Kansas City receivers this season? Zero. Jamaal Charles is the offense. Yes, he is pricey and the fourth most expensive running back. But he is $1,900 cheaper than Matt Forte and he easily has a chance to dominate at Arrowhead Stadium, one of the loudest places on earth.

Shane Vereen ($6,200) – For the moment the Pats running back situation has clarity: Shane Vereen then everybody else. In PPR formats he has added value. Don’t expect last week’s monster numbers, but he should excel against an inconsistent Bears team.

LeSean McCoy ($5,800) – Players are often “forgotten” when they come out of the bye week. Hello, LeSean McCoy. Granted he is going against the staunch Arizona run defense and it doesn’t look like McCoy’s counterpart, Darren Sproles, will be ready to go in week 8. Chip Kelly will have spent the bye week thinking up creative ways to get McCoy the ball in space.

 

WR

Doug Baldwin ($4,700) – With Percy Harvin gone, Baldwin is Russell Wilson’s top target in Seattle. They aren’t known as a throwing team but Baldwin’s cost is unbelievably low for being the clear cut number one receiver on the depth chart.

Brandin Cooks ($4,700) – The Saints will be desperate for a win at home in the dome against the offensive juggernaut that is the Green Bay Packers. Green Bay has a legitimate secondary so Drew Brees will manufacture some underneath targets for his new favorite weapon, Cooks.

Percy Harvin ($4,100) – Feel like gambling? Harvin represents low cost and huge potential reward. From the sounds of it Harvin has been meeting with coaches as often as possible to learn the Jets playbook. Rex Ryan, desperately trying to save his job, will want to find different ways to use the explosive new receiver they just acquired.

 

TE

Jordan Reed ($4,000) – Reed’s biggest pitfall is his inconsistent health. When he plays, he is dominant pass catcher. In the two weeks back from injury Reed has averaged 13.8 fantasy points, expect more of the same.

Zach Ertz ($3,200) – Just like teammate LeSean McCoy, Ertz was lost in the shuffle coming off of the bye. Arizona’s biggest weakness has been defending the tight end. If I know that. I assume Chip Kelly must know that. Plus, Ertz is one of the cheapest tight ends available this week.

 

 

Be aware: if you are thinking about playing any Lions or Falcons players they are in London this week. I’ve never been, but I hear it rains a lot. Typically London games have been fairly low scoring affairs so temper expectations for both of these underperforming offenses.

Also, I haven’t been ranking defenses but considering how many high cost defenses have been earning negative fantasy points (I’m looking at you Seattle), I’d recommend going as cheap as you feel comfortable when selecting your defense. That money is best spent elsewhere. #RedraftKing
