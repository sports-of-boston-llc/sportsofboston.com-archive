Notes and Observations Week 5: Patriots Return to Form, Blow Out Bengals 43-17

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ October 6, 2014 at 8:48pm ⋅ Post a comment

What a difference a week makes. After all the doom and gloom from the media that the Patriots were meeting their demise, Tom Brady was too old, and Bill Belichick wasn’t the same coach, the Patriots came out in prime time and handled the last remaining undefeated team in the NFL. If it wasn’t for the 12 penalties for 114 yards called on the Patriots, Sunday night’s game wouldn’t be as close as it was. The Patriots came out with fire and intensity and never relinquished the controls. A showing like Sunday night was exactly what the Patriots needed to reassure all the quaking media members, but it was expected from those who have seen what this team has done over the past 13 years after an embarrassing loss the week before.

– Through the early part of the season the offensive line has warranted some concern. Sunday night the Patriots started their third different offensive line combination in as many games. This combination was undoubtedly the best all season. Left to right the starters were Nate Solder, Dan Connolly, Bryan Stork, Ryan Wendell, and Sebastian Vollmer. The veterans of Connolly and Wendell provided the best guard play that the Patriots have seen all year. Solder looked like his old self and kept the pressure away from Brady’s blind-side. The rookie Stork looked good as well, creating holes in the run game and keeping the middle of the pocket clean. Of course this is just one game but Sunday’s game was the first positive sign the offensive line has given us all year so we’ll take it.

– Finally we got to see what Brady can do when he has time to pass. In case you forgot, Brady is still one of the best in the league when he doesn’t have four defensive lineman in his face as soon as the ball is snapped. Yes there were a couple bad passes where he under-threw Rob Gronkowski and Brandon LaFell on a few occasions. Even with a couple of missed passes Brady finished the game 23-35 for 292 yards and two touchdowns. The best stat of the night was that he was only sacked once and that came later in the game when it was already out of reach.

– Also that report saying Brady has tension with the coaching staff because of personnel decisions has as much validity as the report that said Aaron Dobson was at odds with Josh McDaniels. If you wonder why Belichick hates talking to the media it’s because they’re the idiots fabricating these reports all to get some internet clicks. Be better media.

– You would think by now people would realize how Belichick and the Patriots operate. They aren’t going to worry about wins and losses in September and October. Going into Sunday night they were 2-2 after three road games. There are plenty of other teams in the NFL who were in worse shape than the Patriots but the only panic was coming from the Boston media. Belichick treats the first half of the season like an extended pre-season. He moves guys around to see where they fit best like he’s doing with the offensive line and rookie defensive lineman Dominique Easley, and he also sits players when they have minor injuries to prevent them from becoming major. Case and point Alfonzo Dennard being held out a few games and Dont’a Hightower being held out Sunday night. You don’t know what type of team you have until after Thanksgiving. All the games leading up to that is simply preparation. Belichick has been doing this for a decade plus with this Patriots team and they continue to find success.

– Aside from Brady playing more like Tom Brady, the running game was the real bright spot. Combined the Patriots finished with 220 yards on 46 carries and Stevan Ridley scored a touchdown. Ridley had 113 of the yards on 27 carries while Shane Vereen had an impressive showing of 90 yards on just nine attempts. Vereen’s best run came in the third quarter on a 3rd and 16. They ran the draw and Vereen took advantage of a huge hole created by the offensive line and juked a couple defenders on route to a 19-yard gain for a first down. Ridley and Vereen were the perfect contrast of power and speed. When the offense is balanced between run and pass they are one of the toughest teams to beat in the league. Once again this all starts at the offensive line.

– Sunday night’s game also marked the return of the two tight end offense in New England. Tim Wright had his coming out party finally silencing the anti-Mankins trade crowd, for at least a week anyway. Wright showed how dynamic he is with five catches for 85 yards and a touchdown. Gronk finished with six catches for 100 yards and touchdown of his own. Wright and Gronk worked the seams without much resistance for the majority of the game. They both proved to fast for linebackers and too big for cornerbacks. Here’s hoping Tim Wright doesn’t have a less than legal history that will shockingly come to light.

– The rest of the receiving corps didn’t wow anyone but they made the catches they had to. Even Danny Amendola had a catch, and it actually didn’t get called back for a penalty. The game was dominated by the rushing attack and the stellar play of the tight ends. Next week could be an entirely different game plan but it’s good to see depth on the offense for the first time in the season.

– Defensively the Patriots looked solid. Darrelle Revis was successful for the most part covering AJ Green. He did finish with 5 catches for 81 yards and a touchdown, but the touchdown was over Logan Ryan after Revis left with the hamstring injury. Revis also had an impressive diving interception called back for a ticky tack illegal contact penalty. Green is one of the best wide receivers in the game and Revis made sure he didn’t have a direct impact on the game.

– One of the biggest surprises was the lack of playing time for Chandler Jones. He only played 18 snaps and it must have been in an effort to put big bodies on the line to protect against the run. For the most part the defensive line was Chris Jones, Vince Wilfork, Dominique Easley, and Rob Ninkovich. The lone sack of the game was credited to Chris Jones but Chandler was right next to him forcing the pressure. That was also the first sack of the season given up by the Bengals. I can see how people might question keeping Jones out for so many of the plays, but it’s hard to argue the results. I don’t see this as holding Jones out because of a lack of ability, on the contrary Belichick is attempting to play to the strengths of everyone on his defense.

– Alfonzo Dennard looked alright in his first game back. He did get beat by Mohamed Sanu for a touchdown, but I credit Andy Dalton for making the right read on quarters coverage. He saw the safety jump the route by the tight end which left the middle open to the post from Sanu. Dennard did get flagged on a few occasions for defensive holding. One was warranted but the other could hardly be classified as holding. The “point of emphasis” on illegal contact and defensive holding is getting frustrating when guys are getting called for barely making any contact. There were more than a few flags that should not have been thrown last night for both teams. It seems every time Peyton Manning gets dominated in the postseason the NFL comes out with more rules against the defense.

– Play of the night goes to Brandon Bolden and Kyle Arrington. Bolden forced the fumble on the kick return by Brandon Tate and Arrington picked it up and strolled into the endzone. That was the play that put the game away and it showed why Bolden has his place secured on this team. Special teams is part of the game and when you can make plays there Belichick will always find a spot for you. Just ask Matthew Slater.

– Credit has to be given to Patrick Chung as well. It was no secret in his first go around with the Patriots he made some bad reads, missed some tackles, took poor routes to receivers, and a few other characteristics that are natural of younger players. After a couple years away he’s returned a bit wiser. He played a substantial amount Sunday night after Devin McCourty left with a rib injury. He wasn’t making any spectacular highlight reel plays, but he was making the regular plays that need to be made. He finished with six tackles, four of them solo. I’ve said it before that the depth in the secondary this year is unbelievable compared to what it’s been the past few years.

– I like that they kept Brandon Browner out of the game. There’s a difference between being in shape and being in game shape. There’s no rush to get him out there. Let him have some time on the practice field, figure out how you want to use him, then get him out there.

– For all the negativity directed at the fans of Gillette, some of it warranted, they showed up in full force Sunday night. The “Brady” chants throughout the game gave me chills so I can only imagine how great it must have been at the game. It was a great way to show Brady and the team that the fans don’t doubt them no matter how bad they lose one game. We know that at the end of the season the Patriots will be one of the few left with a legitimate chance to win a championship. Gillette may not be as loud as Seattle and Kansas City, but it’s awfully hard to get geared up for a game the first weekend of October when we know it’s just a formality and the real games don’t start until January.

– The momentum has swung back to the Patriots. They need to build off the successes and failures from Sunday night as they head out to Buffalo to take on the Bills. The Bills defense is definitely solid and can pose a problem if the offensive line looks like they did during weeks one through four, but if they look like they did Sunday night then there is no reason they can’t put up enough points to take sole possession of first place. The Bills have one of the better run games in the league with CJ Spiller and Fred Jackson. I figure we’ll see a defensive scheme similar to the one utilized against the Bengals.

– Division games are always tough but it’s safe to say that the Patriots are onto Buffalo.
