Connelly’s Top Ten: Patriots Quiet Critics (Including Me)

By Michael in Featured, Now!, Top Story ⋅ October 6, 2014 at 6:49am ⋅ Post a comment

1. Patriots:

MISC

* Talking about shutting us up with a quality game – game plan excellent, intensity and crowd was as loud as I’ve ever heard it

* James Devlin blocks, runs, catches and is now assigned to giving Tom Brady high fives

* I don’t want to see Revis go down the Talib Stairs anymore

* Great games – Wright, Vereen, Wilfork, Offensive Line, Ridley, Bolden on special teams (played with fire – like a guy who wants to stay on the team), Gostkowski

* Bad games – Dennard, Ninkovich, Easley,

* I like they were picking each other up off the ground and had each other’s back

* Nice of Dalton to die his hair for breast cancer awareness month

* Blue hoodie again

* How did the independent doctor allow Amendola back on the field after being wobbly after a hit

FIRST DRIVE

* Ya a success – 7 points!

* OFFENSE

* It was so good that even Dobson and Amendola caught balls

* Good job by McDaniels incorporating Wright down the seams and staying with running game

* That was the most intense I have ever seen Gronk – could have had four more catches if Brady is more accurate / though his blocking is not there right now

* Brady could have broken his neck on the QB sneak at goal line

* Actually used the hurry up

* Eight receivers caught balls

* Slight complaint – Patriots aren’t finishing drives – 5 field goals – the Patriots EASILY could have scored 60 points

DEFENSE

* Revis Island is back!

* Vince Wilfork’s best game in two years

* Jamie Collins active – beat a couple of times but at least involved

* Defense don’t grab anymore shirts please

* Even Revis made some tackles

KRAFT SIGHTING – It was a Kraft-fest

* 2nd quarter – 0:02 left in quarter

* 3rd quarter – 6:00 left in quarter

* 4th quarter – 8:20 left in quarter

OFFICIALS

* Flags on Easley bad, flag on Gronk bad – other guy grabbed him

* One whole drive for Bengals was flags

* Bengals actually talked officials into late flag for gounding

* 12 flags for 114 yards – many more not accepted (Bengals 4 for 37)

STATS

* 11 rushing first downs

* Cincy 0-7 on third down

* 82 plays to 48 wow!

* 46 rushes

* 38 minutes to 21 minutes TOP

* Amazing day for SEC and the state of Mississippi both schools are now ranked THIRD!

* I was 3-1 this week bring me back up to 5-6

7. Old School I

You think Tom Brady will never leave – for those old enough in June of 1976 the greatest hockey player ever Bobby Orr left the Bruins for the Blackhawks. In the Globe on 6/9/76, Fran Rosa wrote: “Remember all the things he did. A million tomorrows will all pass away, ere you forget the joys he gave us. There will never be another Bobby Orr.

8. Old School II

The Ole Miss REBELS are 5-0 for the first time since 1962. In my book The President’s Team – I wrote a chapter on Ole Miss, integration and President Kennedy’s world’s intersecting during the Kentucky game.

* The team was 5-0 and was greeted to the field by fanatic fan base waving Rebel flags to the song “I Wish I Was Born in Dixie”

* Prior to the game every fan was handed a postcard with the White House address and a message already on the card that read: “Concentrate on Castro and Cuba and leave our fine state alone.”

* Prior to the game Mississippi Governor Ross Barnett (son of a Confederate soldier) agreed with the Kennedy’s to allow African American James Meredith to enroll in the school – at halftime he changed his mind grabbing the microphone on the field and saying to the crowd, “I love Mississippi. I love her people. I love our customs.”

* Ole Miss won 14-0 and on Monday Kennedy sent federal troops to escort Meredith to school – there were two deaths and forty-eight soldiers hurt

* Ross Barnett would walk in Kennedy’s funeral

9. Just a Little Bit Outside:

The Patriots are reportedly $14mm under the salary cap. Bob Kraft said in a 1995 Jackie McMullan story in the Globe: “I didn’t buy this team to make money,” said Kraft. “There are easier ways to do that. I wanted to be a sportsman.”

10. Randomocity:

* The biscuits at Sweet Cheeks in the Fenway might be the best I’ve had (everything else was average)

* What a Sunday night – Patriots, Boardwalk Empire and Homeland

* $16.3mm or almost 25% of the Bruins salary cap is spent on Marchand, Lucic, Kelly, Paille, Campbell

* Could it end bad for Michael Phelps?

* How long before drones are hovering over fields and the ice with amazing camera views

* Agents that caused local teams trouble – Alan Eagleson, Jerome Stanley, Bob Wolfe, Jerry Kapstein
