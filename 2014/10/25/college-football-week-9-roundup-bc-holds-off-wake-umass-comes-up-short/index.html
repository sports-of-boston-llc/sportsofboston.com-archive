College Football Week 9 Roundup: BC Holds off Wake, UMass Comes up Short

Boston College, 23; Wake Forest, 17

Boston College improved to 3-0 on the road this season with a six-point win over Wake Forest (2-6) at BB&T Field. The Eagles looked like they were going to cruise early, but ended up having to stave off the Demon Deacons’ comeback bid.

Alex Howell got BC on the board with a 30-yard field goal to cap off an openingÂ drive that took up more than half of the first quarter. On the last play of the period, running back Jon Hilliman gave the Eagles a 10-0 lead with a 33-yard touchdown run. Hilliman scored again late in the second, carrying it into the end zone from one yard out for a 17-0 halftime advantage.

Kicker Mike WeaverÂ gaveÂ the Deacons their first score with a 28-yard FG in the third quarter to make it 17-3, but quarterback Tyler Murphy hit wide receiver Shakim Phillips for a 37-yard TD early in the fourth to seemingly put the game away. Wake responded with a 15-play, 75-yard drive that led to a three-yard TD run by WR E. J. Scott. On the ensuing kickoff return, RB Myles Willis fumbled the ball at his own 15-yard line, and four plays later RB Isaiah Robinson made it a one-score game with a one-yard TD run. The Deacons had one last chance to win it, but defensive back Justin Simmons intercepted a John Wolford pass at the BC 37 and the offense ran out the clock.

The Eagles D limited Wake Forest to 19 yards rushing, and Boston College outgained the home team 367-261 in total. Murphy was 11-for-15 for 165 yards and a TD, and Hilliman added 101 yards and two scores on 20 carries. Wolford finished 22-for-30 passing for 242 yards and a pick. With the win, the Eagles improved to an even 2-2 in Atlantic Coast Conference play, 5-3 overall, good for fourth place in the Atlantic Division, two games behind #2 Florida State.

Notes: Murphy surpassed 1,000 yards passing on the season in the game. He finished the game with 1,034 with six TDs and six INTs, and has 843 yards and eight TDs rushing… Hilliman had his second 100-yard game and his fourth with multiple scores. He has 572 yards and nine scores.

Next Eagles game: November 1 @ Virginia Tech (4-4), 12:30 pm ET

Massachusetts, 35; Toledo, 42

A much improved UMass team came close to pulling off the road upset of Toledo (5-3) at the Glass Bowl. Not for the first time this season, the Minutemen led at theÂ break but couldn’t hold on in the second half.

UMass went 80 yards in 14 plays to open the game, and quarterback Blake Frohnapfel found wide receiver Jalen Williams for a 15-yard touchdown pass, which Logan Laurent followed up with a missed extra point attempt. Frohnapfel and Williams hooked up again on the next possession for a 35-yard score, but this time the Rockets responded with a 12-play, 75-yard drive and a 14-yard TD by running back Terry Swanson to make it 13-7.

On the first play of the second quarter, defensive back Khary Bailey-Smith intercepted Toledo QB Logan Woodside at the Rockets’ 30-yard line, and five plays later Frohnapfel threw a 15-yard TD pass to WR Shakur Nesmith. Once again the home team came back with a big possession, marching 94 yards in 13 plays, and Woodside connected with tight end Michael Roberts for a 14-yard score. Woodside threw his second pick late in the period, to DB Trey Dudley-Giles, deep in UMass territory, and the Minutemen went into halftime up 20-14.

Woodside redeemed himself with a 42-yard TD pass to WR Corey Jones to open the second half. It looked like the Minutemen were coming apart when Laurent missed a 43-yard field goal and Frohnapfel was intercepted by safety Chaz Whittaker, leading to a 36-yard TD run for Kareem Hunt and a 28-20 Toledo lead.

But early in the fourth quarter, Frohnapfel threw a 36-yard TD pass to WR Tajae Sharpe, then tied the game with the two-point conversion. Woodside put Toledo back on top with a six-yard TD to WR Dwight Macon, but on the next play from scrimmage Frohnapfel found TE Jean Sifrin for a 76-yard score. After the teams traded punts, Swanson scored what would prove to be the winning 19-yard TD, as Frohnapfel’s desperation pass was picked off by cornerback Trevon Mathis to end it.

Woodside was 20-for-34 passing for 225 yards, three TDs and two INTs, and Hunt had 198 yards and a score on 26 carries. Frohnapfel completed 28 of 55 passes for 438 yards, five TDs and two picks.
