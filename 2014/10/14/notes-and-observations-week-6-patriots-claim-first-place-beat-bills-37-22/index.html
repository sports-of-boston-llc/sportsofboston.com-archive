Notes and Observations Week 6: Patriots Claim First Place, Beat Bills 37-22

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ October 14, 2014 at 9:00am ⋅ Post a comment

Every year the “experts” talk about how the Bills are right putting it together and are going to challenge the Patriots for the division. They create a little buzz with a few early wins and Chris Berman constantly reminds us that “nobody circles the wagon like the Buffalo Bills. No matter all the hype they’re still the Buffalo Bills, and Kyle Orton is their starting quarterback.

– I have a feeling there won’t be many people calling for a trade of Tom Brady this week. Two consecutive weeks, Brady has looked like himself and strong offensive line play is the reason. Last week more so than this week, but due to injuries to Bryan Stork and Dan Connolly they had to make some moves on the line. It was not a bad showing at all as Brady was sacked only twice. He’s had more time to  stay in the pocket and it has led to his two best games of the season, Sunday being his best. He finished going 27-37 for 361 yards and four touchdowns. The most impressive stat of the game was that he completed a pass to 10 different receivers. – It was refreshing to see Brady mix up the passes like he used to during the championship days, but when he needed the big play he still relied on Rob Gronkowski and Julian Edelman. During their final touchdown drive it was Edelman who made the big 20-yard catch on 1st and 15. Then it was Gronk who kept the drive alive with the sliding catch on 3rd and 16. There is still plenty of work that can be done in terms of how they spread the ball around as Gronk (7) and Edelman (9) finished with 16 of Brady’s 27 completions. Also other than Brandon LaFell’s four catches, the other seven players who caught a pass from Brady caught only one. The point is Brady is able to find other guys than Gronk and Edelman which is a huge positive.

– Gronk is starting to look more and more like his old self. Of course everyone held their breath when he took a little time to get off the field later in the game, but he only missed one play and looked completely unphased by it. It’s a shame his touchdown was called back for holding. That was his most impressive play of the season. It was a glimpse of the old Gronk and the spike was one of the best he’s ever done. For once it’d be great if he could stay healthy all year. Every week it’s obvious no team has a consistent answer for him. Other than Brady he is the one guy that they can’t afford to lose on offense.

– I was wondering how long it would take before the injuries would start to come into play. At least it took a couple weeks longer than last season to lose significant players for the season. It sucks to see both Stevan Ridley and Jerod Mayo go out for the season with injuries. Mayo is definitely a bigger loss than Ridley as he was the Tom Brady of the defense. He was the play caller and more often than not if there was a tackle in the middle of the field he was in on it. It’s a shame a player of his caliber is lost to injury early in the season two years in a row. I don’t question his durability at all as both were freak injuries that could happen to anyone. Here’s hoping the rehab goes well and he’s back stronger next season.

– Don’t get me wrong, Ridley is undoubtedly their most talented running back. All that talk about fumbling, which he hadn’t done all season, aside he was the most explosive runner they had. He could run with power and speed, while having a nose for the endzone. Shane Vereen has shown that he is a very serviceable running back both rushing and catching the football. He just isn’t the same between the tackles runner that Ridley is. Brandon Bolden will most likely see the majority of the carries unless rookie James White impresses enough to get additional touches. Between the three of them, and a few carries for Edelman and Develin thrown in here and there, the rushing game should be fine.

– Dan Connolly left the game with a head injury which is cause for concern due to his concussion history. If there’s even a hint of a headache within the next couple days I don’t think he plays against the Jets. Stork should be back for Thursday night’s game, but with the short week I don’t think they’d risk losing Connolly for an extended period. Hopefully they just kept him out on Sunday as a precaution and he’s good to go for Thursday because the Jets defensive line is a lock to pose some problems for them.

– The emergence of Brandon LaFell has been a pleasant surprise. He clearly has Brady’s trust and for good reason. LaFell is a big target and his two-touchdown performance Sunday was an impressive showing of what he can do with the ball in his hands. I think as Aaron Dobson continues to heal from offseason foot surgery they could end up with a three wide set of LaFell and Dobson on the outside with Edelman in the slot. It’s nice to see the potential of two bigger receiver targets who can stretch the field. There aren’t many people claiming Brady has no weapons this week.

– Last quick notes about the offense is that Danny Amendola had no catches and Tim Wright caught another touchdown. I keep thinking that maybe one game Amendola will be the secret weapon of the offense and have a game like Josh McDaniels believes he could. Then every week he doesn’t have a catch. I’d almost feel bad for him if he wasn’t making millions of dollars for literally doing nothing. Wright wasn’t utilized other than his goal line touchdown grab, but it’s good to see him getting a red zone look and producing when the opportunity presented itself. Week six is still pre-season territory for Belichick which leads me to believe we’ll see plenty more of Tim Wright as the season goes on.

– Other than their apparent inability to cover Scott Chandler, the defense had a strong showing. Rob Ninkovich made his presence known for the first time this season registering three sacks. He showed his power and speed around the edge all while tormenting Kyle Orton.

– Chandler Jones had the big play of the day that lost a little luster when the offense couldn’t fully capitalize on it. He had the impressive strip sack which he recovered at the end of the first half when he worked left tackle Cordy Glenn. Unfortunately the offense gaine no yards on three plays and only got a field goal out of it. They could have put the game on ice a lot earlier had they converted a touchdown after that turnover.

– Even with the missed opportunity it was impossible to fear the Bills when they did the most Bills thing ever with their last first half possession. CJ Spiller ran what should have been the last play of the quarter but Devin McCourty knocked the ball out. Tom Brady then connected with the reliable Edelman on the sideline with :03 left on the clock. Stephen Gostkowski, after missing his first field goal of the season in the first quarter, nailed a 53-yard field goal to put them up 13-7 at the half.

– Darrelle Revis had his best game of the year on Sammy Watkins. Aside from that 20-yard catch later in the game Watkins wasn’t heard from at all. Revis took their most dangerous threat out of the game. If it wasn’t for Scott Chandler turning in a great game filled with one handed catches, the Bills offense might not have kept up at all.

– I still can’t believe what a steal Alfonzo Dennard was as a seventh round pick. Granted he got beat by Chris Hogan on the touchdown that put the Bills one score down, he continued to be the physical presence they need at corner. Early on in the game he made a great tackle on Robert Woods on a 3rd & 2. Dennard broke off his block, caught up to Woods running and out and stuck him for a gain of only one. If the guy was willing to punch a cop of course he has no problem pushing around wide receivers.

– With Mayo being lost for the season Jamie Collins is going to need to step up. He started early Sunday with a great interception where he read the pass to drop off his zone and jump into the route. He showed great hands snagging the quick short pass attempt. He was in on four tackles which isn’t eye popping but he could still use work with consistency. Collins is in his second season so he’s not going to step right in and be Jerod Mayo, no one player can replace Mayo. Dont’a Hightower, who’s missed the past couple games with a knee injury of his own, will need to step up along with Collins to fill the void. The rest of the linebackers will be made up of unkowns like Deontae Skinner and Ja’Gared Davis so that makes Hightower and Collins the leaders.

– Skinner did show promise in the run game and he made a great play on his sack of Orton. He forced Orton to lock up like a deer in headlights and leveled him with a perfect tackle. Those are usually the plays that force the ball loose so credit to Orton for holding onto that one. He didn’t look too great in pass coverage, but he spent a majority of his time on Chandler who shredded everyone. Linebacker was their thinnest position on defense and unfortunately that bit them early on.

– Brandon Spikes had as much impact on the game as Danny Amendola. Maybe he’s just worn out from “four years a slave.”

– Can we stop calling it a rivalry between the Bills and Patriots? It’s not a rivalry when one team loses 21 out of 22 games to the other team. It’s just sad.

– Vince Wilfork looked great Sunday, pushing the offensive line back for most of the game. He’s starting to look like he’s getting the explosive push off the snap that he had pre-achilles injury. He was in on six tackles and was a main contributor to holding a good rushing team to only 68 yards on 23 carries. With Mayo now absent behind Vince in the middle, Wilfork will have to step up even more so than usual to blow runs up at the line of scrimmage to compensate.

– The offensive line will have their toughest test of the season against the Jets on a short week. If they’re not fully healthy it could end up being a long day for Brady. Rex Ryan is going to blitz all game especially now that their best corner Dee Milliner is out for the year to a torn achilles. To beat the blitz I see at least a few deep ball attempts and Brian Tyms could be the guy to look at. After coming down with that impressive touchdown in triple coverage you have to like his chances going against a weak Jets secondary.

– After all the panic and outlandish statements calling for the Patriots demise, they are in sole possession of first place in the AFC East in week six. The offense is starting to roll and the defense continues to make big plays. The injuries are tough but last year they made it to the AFC Championship with far less. As long as they keep Brady upright how could you not like their chances?
