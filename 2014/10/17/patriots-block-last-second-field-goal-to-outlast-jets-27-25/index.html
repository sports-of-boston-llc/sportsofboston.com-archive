Patriots Block Last Second Field Goal to Outlast Jets, 27-25

By Nick Bohlen in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ October 17, 2014 at 9:06am ⋅ Post a comment

Well, that was close.

The New England Patriots literally hung on by the tips of their fingers, blocking a last gasp 58-yard field goal attempt by the New York Jets to escape with a 27-25 win at home on Thursday Night Football.

Danny Amendola made a surprise appearance in Week 7, catching what turned out to be the game-winning touchdown catch on 3rd-and-goal from the 19-yard line before later thwarting Rex Ryan’s call for an onside kick attempt with 2:31 remaining. That gave the Patriots a chance to close out the game, but Tom Brady and the offense couldn’t get a first down and had to punt to the Jets with 1:06 remaining. That set up the last second dramatics for Nick Folk to try a 58-yard field goal, only to be rejected by Patriots defensive end Chris Jones. This after the Jets couldn’t get the two-point conversion to tie the game midway through the fourth quarter.

Here are a few notes from the course of the game:

D-FENSE?

The Patriots defense certainly played up to their “bend-don’t-break” philosophy for the game. Darrelle Revis and company surrendered over 200 yards rushing in allowing four trips into the red zone and four straight field goal scoring drives for the first half. Luckily they convened for some furious halftime adjustments and…promptly gave up an almost six-minute touchdown march on the ensuing kickoff to give the Jets a 19-17 lead. #coaching.

The Patriots did stop the Jets from tying the game on a two-point conversion with 2:31 left in the game, but that was more thanks to a poorly timed and executed throw by Geno Smith than good execution by the defense. Overall, the Jets piled up over 400 yards of offense, 218 of which came on the ground (Chris Ivory led the way with 107 yards rushing). The New England defense couldn’t even entice Mr. Turnover himself, Geno Smith, into coughing the ball up, as both teams came out even in the turnover column.

The “O” in Offense Stands for Zero

As in, zero help the defense got from the Tom Brady and the Patriots offense. The offense only gained 323 yards on the day (exactly 100 fewer than the Jets) and moved the chains only 16 times (compared to the Jets’ 28 first downs). The Patriots held onto the ball for just 19:06, while New York racked up over 40 minutes of time of possession.

Brady had a monstrous first half, throwing for 174 yards and two touchdowns on 12-for-22 completions. He ended the day with 261 yards and three TDs while going 20-for-37. Rob Gronkowski had five catches on 10 targets for 68 yards, and Brandon LaFell had 55 yards on four catches. Julian Edelman was the Patriots’ fourth-leading receiver with four catches for 44 yards. Topping them all, however, was this gentleman right here…

V(ereen) is for Victory

Shane Vereen was the story of the first half, catching three passes for 60 yards and two touchdowns. (He would finish the day with five catches on eight targets for 71 yards and 11 carries for 43 yards to go with the two touchdown receptions.) The first was on New England’s fourth play of their opening drive, when Vereen hauled in a 49-yard pass on a diving catch to score on a massive defensive breakdown in the Jets secondary. He caught his second touchdown pass with just over four minutes remaining in the first half on a quick three-yard out. In short, I wish I started him in my fantasy league.

But his real kudos came from his first touchdown celebration, in which he saluted fellow running back Stevan Ridley, placed on the IR with an ACL/MCL tear, by framing the door and kicking it down in tribute to his injured teammate’s signature celebration after taking it to the house.

Danny Amendola sighting!

It’s true, Patriots fan! The mythical leprechaun known as Danny Amendola does exist! He caught a crucial 19-yard touchdown pass on 3rd-and-goal to stretch the Patriots lead to 27-19, then later in the fourth quarter recovered the Jets’ onside kick after a failed two-point conversion left them trailing 27-25 with 2:31 remaining. with do a solid job in the Patriots kick return game, bringing a couple of balls out to the 25-30 yard line,  Break out the chorus of “Oh Danny Boy!”

Ridley’s Replacement

Jonas Gray also made his Patriots debut, albeit to a little less fanfare. The third year undrafted free agent out of Notre Dame was activated off the practice squad just before the 4:00 PM deadline to replace Ridley on the roster. He was the secondary back behind Vereen with 12 snaps (Bolden got 1 while Vereen was on the field for 48), earning a handful of carries. The move was somewhat surprising with Brandon Bolden and fourth-round selection James White were both already on the 53-man roster, but at 5’10” and 230 pounds (White weighs in at only 205, and was inactive), Gray was most poised to assume Ridley’s role as the power “big back” to run between the tackles.

No Mayo in the Middle

Jerod Mayo underwent surgery for a torn patellar tendon suffered in Week 6, the same injury that Victor Cruz was hit with last week for the New York Giants. Dont’a Hightower returned from injury to take over Mayo’s play calling duties in the middle. His health will be key with Mayo out for the remainder of the season and the Patriots’ linebacker depth lacking.

Browner Downer

Brandon Browner made his Patriots debut after signing a three-year, $17 million contract as a free agent this offseason. Thursday night was his first game being active serving his four-game suspension in violation of the NFL’s substance abuse policy and sitting out the past two games due to either an ankle injury or not understanding the Patriots defensive system (I guess it depends on whether you have more faith in the veracity of Bill Belichick’s injury report or in reports from the cracked out Boston media). Put it this way, the Patriots didn’t exactly get their money’s worth with two defensive holding flags thrown in his direction as he “gets his legs under him.”

Up Next

The Patriots are now 5-2 heading into their Week 8 matchup against the Chicago Bears, their second of three straight home games going into the Week 10 bye.
