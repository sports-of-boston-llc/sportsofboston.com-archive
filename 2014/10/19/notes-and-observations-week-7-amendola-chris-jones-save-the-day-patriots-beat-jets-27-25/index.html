Notes and Observations Week 7: Amendola, Chris Jones Save the Day; Patriots Beat Jets 27-25

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ October 19, 2014 at 12:28am ⋅ Post a comment

Well that was a lot harder than it had to be. Thursday night games all season have been blowouts by the home team. Of course when the Patriots play the Jets, it turns into the closest game both teams played all season and was decided by a last second blocked field goal. The defense looked terrible and the offense did just enough to squeak by one of the worst secondaries in the league. If you’re looking for silver linings in this game it’s that the Patriots got the win, they’re 5-2 in control of the division, they have 10 days off before their next game, and they escaped without any additional injuries.

– To start off, how about that redemption for Chris Jones? Remember it was Jones last year against the Jets who was called for that ridiculous penalty on a field goal in overtime which Nick Folk missed. Jones was called for pushing the lineman from the second level, or as we called it “Pushgate.” Folk then nailed the field goal from 15 yards closer and the Jets won the game. Throughout the game the Patriots were close to getting to Folk coming off the edges. The final play saw Jones go right through the middle to get his hands up and swat the ball down. A fitting end to a brutal nail-biter. 

– All the Jets fans are crying about the ref moving Dont’a Hightower off the long snapper prior to the kick to avoid a penalty. Newsflash, the refs will tell players all game if they are lining up incorrectly to avoid penalties. They do it all game on offense telling receivers if they’re not lined up along the line of scrimmage correctly. The same idea applies on defense. I think the Jets fans are just upset because their team played as good of a game as they possibly could have played against a Patriots team that played as badly as they could have played defensively, and they still came up short. You almost feel bad for the Jets fans but then you remember how insufferable they all are.

– Have to give credit where it’s due and Danny Amendola came up huge Thursday night. For the first time as a Patriot he was returning kicks and he did well when he had the opportunity to take it out of the endzone. He continually brought it past the 20-yard line showing some explosiveness that hasn’t been seen in the return game in quite some time. He then made an amazing catch on the go-ahead touchdown. Tom Brady rolled out to his left, Amendola broke off his route towards the endzone and Brady hit him in the back shoulder. Amendola contorted his body in mid-air to make the impressive catch. Amendola was also the one who picked up the onside kick attempt from the Jets late in the game. Folk hit it as good as they could have hoped. The ball stayed on the ground and Brandon LaFell booted it. Amendola was able to fall on it to preserve the game. I and many others have been hard on Amendola all season but he showed up in a big way Thursday night.

– With all that being said I still don’t see how they keep him next year without him taking a huge paycut. Sorry Danny.

– Everyone was asking how the Patriots would replace Stevan Ridley. Shane Vereen just gave everyone the answer. We all knew how good of a pass catcher he was, as was evidenced on his two touchdown catches against the Jets. He also finished with 43 yards on 11 carries. Not eye popping numbers by any means, but he showed the ability to turn the corner and accelerate. Vereen still isn’t the guy you can count on banging between the tackles all game, but I expect to see more of Jonas Gray and Brandon Bolden for those purposes. Vereen finished the game with 11 carries, five catches, two touchdowns, and 114 yards from scrimmage.

– Brady had his third consecutive strong performance going 20-37 for 261 yards and three touchdowns. Also for the third straight game he did not throw an interception. It’s funny how there isn’t anyone talking about the decline of Tom Brady and the Patriots anymore. If you write a eulogy for the Patriots you better make sure they’re dead.

– Rob Gronkowski continues to look better each week. He is back to his old ways of standing up with two guys on his back trying to bring him down, while he lunges for an extra yard or two. Every week we go with him staying healthy is a win in my book. In regards to his injury history, where are we on Terminator 2 endoskeleton technology? Are we advanced enough where we can just replace Gronk’s bones with titanium?

– All in all the offensive line looked good considering Bryan Stork and Dan Connolly were both held out after dealing with concussions last week. For most of the game the Jets employed their strategy which they used against Peyton Manning and the Broncos of just employing a three man rush. Every now and then they would send an extra rusher or two but Brady was only sacked once. I was a little surprised Brady wasn’t able to spread the ball around more with the amount of time he had in the pocket compared to how poor the secondary of the Jets is. They seemed to utilize a lot of zone to compensate for the lack of depth in their secondary. Brady didn’t have a bad game by any means but compared to last week where he picked apart the Bills he didn’t look as on point throughout. Chalk it up to a short week and the Jets treating every game against the Patriots as their Super Bowl.

– Onto the defense who played as poorly as you could have played but still somehow squeaked out a win. Once again they gave up big plays on third down, and the run defense was atrocious. We knew with the absence of Jerod Mayo in the middle there would be a drop off in the run game, but was Mayo the only guy on this defense who could effectively tackle? There were far too many missed first tackles and guys were over pursuing as opposed to staying in their lanes. The Jets gashed them for 218 rush yards on 43 carries. It’s baffling that when you play Geno Smith you don’t stack the box, force him to make pass plays, and let your defensive backs cover like they’re supposed to. When they did stack the box it forced Geno to hesitate and allowed the Patriots to rack up three sacks.

– With Mayo gone the Patriots utilized a Nickel 5-2 set for the majority of the game. Tavon Wilson came in on sub-packages to play more of linebacker role. Clearly not satisfied with the defensive showing against the run, Belichick switched back to a 4-3 later in the game putting Deontae Skinner in the middle linebacker spot. Hightower is now the signal caller on defense and again he showed how great he is when he’s playing up at the line of scrimmage. He drew a holding penalty when he time his blitz perfectly and ran right through the offensive line. The holding penalty negated a touchdown, but if he wasn’t held he would have nailed Smith for the sack. Hightower again showed his tremendous pursuit of running backs on another blitz he chased Chris Johnson to the outside where the rest of the defense was able to string out the play and make the stop for no gain. He’s showing why they had no problem letting Brandon Spikes walk in the offseason.

– Chandler Jones had another solid performance with four tackles and a sack. He also had a sack called back on a bogus holding penalty on Brandon Browner. He also had a big hit on Mike Vick behind the line of scrimmage called back on another ticky tack holding call on Browner. His ability to simply run past offensive tackles is absurd and I wouldn’t be upset if they signed him to a 10-year contract today.

– Speaking of Brandon Browner, he was definitely shaking the rust off in his first game back, but from the replays they showed he shouldn’t have been flagged for holding. Darrelle Revis also got flagged for holding on Eric Decker when Decker simply tripped over his own feet. I also don’t think pass interference should have been called in favor of Danny Amendola when Antonio Allen was flagged. It really seemed like the refs were doing what they could to keep this Thursday night game close after all the other ones have been blowouts. The cheap calls need to stop because it’s keeping drives alive for both sides when the defense is actually making the plays to stop them.

– With Browner back he rotated in and out and Logan Ryan as the second corner opposite of Revis which moved Alfonzo Dennard to the primary slot corner role. Kyle Arrington was only on the field for one defensive snap which is a little surprising since he has been playing very well in the slot role all season. I don’t think this is the corner combination we see all season as Dennard struggled in the slot for most of the game. He was beat on a few inside routes and it seems that he is more suited as an outside corner. With that being said depth at corner is always a good thing and I see this group having success as the season goes on.

– It wasn’t pretty but a win on a short week is still a win. No one came out of that game with season ending injuries which is another plus. Now they have 10 days before they have to play the Bears in Gillette. As tough as the quick turnaround is for a Thursday night game, the additional time off before the next game can’t be overlooked especially after winning the Thursday night game. They are now in complete control of first place in the division which is what was expected by this point in the season.
