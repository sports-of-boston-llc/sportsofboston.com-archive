Notes and Observations Week 11: Patriots Extend Lead in the AFC by Beating Colts 42-20 in Primetime

By Steve Bastek in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story ⋅ November 17, 2014 at 7:33pm ⋅ Post a comment

Until Sunday night, the Patriots had never won in Lucas Oil stadium. It stood as the place where Ahmad Bradshaw reluctantly scored a Super Bowl winning touchdown, and of course the infamous “4th and 2” play. Last night the Patriots once again asserted their dominance in the AFC.

In the process they took another one of the media darlings, Andrew Luck, and brought him back down to Earth. Luck, like Peyton Manning two weeks earlier, had a good game statistically. However, like Manning, he was unable to find the endzone when he needed to thanks to the best defense they’ve had in a decade.

– It’s impossible not to start off by mentioning the MONSTER game Jonas Gray had. It was truly a shame they couldn’t call one last running play to gain him one yard for an even 200, but 199 isn’t too shabby. He didn’t do anything fancy but he successfully employed the Antowain Smith and Benjarvus Green-Ellis style of falling forward for 2-3 yards. If there were any instances of him going down on first contact they were few and far between. He is a dangerous combination of strength and just enough speed to turn the corner on outside runs. Obviously it is hard to compare him to Marshawn Lynch as Lynch is one of the best in the game, but their styles are very similar. I don’t foresee 150+ yard games on a weekly basis from Gray, but his style is perfect for games in December and January.

– Last year in the divisional round of the playoffs LaGarrette Blount had similar success against the Colts gashing them for four touchdowns of his own. I do like Stevan Ridley but it’s performances like Gray’s that show you can find success in the running game without overpaying for it. It’s why they let Blount go and even before Blount, it’s why they let Green-Ellis go.

– The biggest reason for Gray’s dominance was the outstanding play of the offensive line. The Patriots didn’t do much to disguise their runs as they utilized a lot of jumbo sets with two running backs, and often times brought in Cameron Flemming as an extra body on the line. It’s hard to believe this is the same personnel that looked clueless at the beginning of the season, but it is. They still utilized the starting five of Solder, Connolly, Stork, Wendell, and Vollmer. Just like every game after the Kansas City debacle, they have rotated Marcus Cannon and Cameron Flemming in and out as well. Everyone has been playing well and the biggest reason for their success is their communication. Stork is doing a fantastic job calling out assignments as a rookie center. Defenders coming in unblocked has been a rare occurrence lately as evidenced by Tom Brady being sacked just twice over the last three games (no sacks last night).

– Another key contributor to the successful blocking was Rob Gronkowski. He was only targeted five times in the passing game leading to four receptions for 71 yards and one bruising, impressive touchdown. They had him going after defensive ends and on multiple occasions he pulled out to block a linebacker at the second level. Every week he looks more and more like the Gronk of old, and last night might have been one final PSA to the league that he’s back. His touchdown late in the game was pure dominance. It was Gronk daring the defense to do their job and tackle him and none of them could bring the big man down. In regards to his abuse of Sergio Brown, I’m all for it. Yes it is a stupid penalty to take and in a closer game I don’t think that happens. However I don’t think it’s ever a bad idea to remind a player like Sergio Brown just how insignificant he truly is.

– Tom Brady threw a couple of interceptions that can be looked at as “bad decisions.” I 100% agree the second interception which gifted the Colts a late first half touchdown was an awful choice. On third and one when your running back has been pounding through the defense the entire game you don’t check off to a play action. You need to trust in your line and running back that they can pick up the one yard. A defender came in free and Brady rushed the pass off his back foot leading to the easy interception. Hindsight is always 20/20 and I can see the mismatch Brady saw as Gronk was breaking wide open. The hurried pass caused the pick, but I stand by the fact that championship teams convert 3rd and 1 by running it. The first interception intended for Brian Tyms was another result of a hurried pass. Brady had Tyms if he threw the ball further and to the right. Brady go hit as he threw the pass causing it to stay in the middle without nearly enough distance.

– Other than those two plays Brady looked as good as he has the past six games. He finished 19-30 for 257 and two touchdowns. As always he didn’t let the early mistakes affect his play in the long run. He made some key third down passes, most notably two to LaFell and one to Edelman, as the Patriots were 9-12 on third down. For all those negative Nancies who claim that Brady “has no weapons!” keep in mind he’s led this offense to the second most points scored in the NFL trailing only the Green Bay Packers by seven. Yes that means he has more points than the magical Peyton Manning and Andrew Luck, but we should still bench him for Jimmy Garoppolo.

– For those of you counting touchdowns on the season we stand at Tim Wright: 4, Logan Mankins: 0.

– Defense wins championships and this defense is championship caliber, for sure. Most of the big plays the Colts had came when the Patriots were in zone, but they came from Luck having to go to a third option. TY Hilton was barely even heard from with only three catches for 24 yards. Darrelle Revis played most of the game on Reggie Wayne, and aside from one miscommunication with safety Devin McCourty he kept Wayne contained. He did finish with 5 catches for 91 yards, and at first glance that looks like a bad game from Revis. One of Wayne’s catches went for 46 yards when Revis thought he had safety help over the top but McCourty stayed in the middle after a Luck pump fake. Take that one play away and Wayne’s day is only four catches for 45 yards.

– Coby Fleener did have a great game, but it was clear Belichick was focused on stopping other aspects of the offense. They focussed on the run holding the Colts to 19 yards on 17 carries. 15 of those yards were from Andrew Luck. Keep in mind that the Colts backfield consists of Trent Richardson, who somehow has had two first-round picks wasted on him, and a very capable Ahmad Bradshaw. The defensive line controlled the line of scrimmage the entire game. Coupled with the defensive line was more outstanding play from Dont’a Hightower and Jamie Collins. Both of whom are playing the best football of their young careers.

– As we know, Hightower is the signal caller in Jerod Mayo’s absence. He did have the opportunity last year to fill in the same role, but you could tell he was playing over his head. This year it’s a complete 180. Hightower is playing with confidence which is allowing him to hit the right gaps when he blitzes, but also run down receivers and running backs in coverage. Injuries to Mayo have definitely forced Hightower to accelerate his progression and he has responded brilliantly.

– Collins, in only his second season, is playing like a multi-year veteran.Â Like Hightower, he is playing with confidence which has led to a more aggressive style. He led the team in tackles last night with eight, five of them solo, and two for a loss. He’s still playing solid in coverage but his play closer to the line of scrimmage has improved drastically in just a year and a half. With Mayo out, Collins very well may be the best tackler on the team in terms of pure form.

– I am a little surprised that Alfonzo Dennard continues to be a healthy scratch. It shows that the coaches are more confident in Malcolm Butler than Dennard. I believe that has a lot to do with the addition of Browner. With Browner on the team it leads to Revis and him on the outside, a spot that was occupied by Dennard last season. In his few attempts covering the slot this season Dennard hasn’t looked good and Kyle Arrington continues to excel in that role. Butler has shown the ability to cover the inside and outside routes which gives the defense more flexibility, and unfortunately makes Dennard the odd man out. There is still plenty of time left in the season and it’s always to have too much secondary depth than not enough.

-Next week the Lions come to town for another big match-up. They’re a tough team to figure out as one week they look like one of the best in the league and the next they look like a bad high school team. I said it after the Broncos game that I don’t think this team loses another game similar to the 2003 team, and I stand by that. There are some talented teams on the horizon like playing the Packers in Green Bay in two weeks. This Patriots team has the talent and the momentum to beat any team in the league. After 11 weeks they’re the #1 seed in the AFC and in control of their own destiny. If they continue to take care of their business the road to the Super Bowl goes through Foxboro and I can guarantee no team wants to play the Patriots at home in January.

– We’re onto Detroit.
