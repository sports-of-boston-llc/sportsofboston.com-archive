Celtics Blow another Lead, Lose to Cavaliers 122-121

By mhilperts in Celtics, Featured, Opinion ⋅ November 15, 2014 at 7:15pm ⋅ Post a comment

 

After Friday night’s loss, the Celtics have scored the second most points in the league while allowing opponents to score the second most points as well. Additionally, the Celtics have been outscored by opponents  72-41 in the fourth quarter of the last two games. Keep in mind that the C’s had sizable leads in both of these games. These two stats are the most indicative of this year’s Celtics team and its struggles. Closing out games has presented itself as the key issue for Boston early in the season.

Last night marked LeBron James’s first time in the TD Garden as a Cavalier since the Celtics defeated Cleveland in the six-game series which eventually resulted in James “taking his talents” to the Miami Heat. James typically receives a slightly colder reception in Boston than other cities, considering his history with the Celtics, and generally plays a little bit more aggressively as well, averaging over 30 points a game. Last night was no exception, as even when most of his teammates were struggling early on, James was finding and taking easy shots, eventually finishing with 41 points on an efficient 16-27 shooting.

At one point during the fourth quarter the Celtics were up 103-84.  This lead quickly evaporated after an underperforming Kyrie Irving snapped out of his funk and scored 12 of the Cavaliers’ next 15 points. The Celtics’ lack of a true rim protector became glaringly visible at this junction of the fourth quarter as centers Kelly Olynyk and Tyler Zeller essentially had to foul James and Kevin Love whenever they had the ball in the paint.

On the final possession, with seven seconds left in the game, and down 122-121, Rajon Rondo had the ball on the Cavaliers’ end of the court, and instead of looking for a layup or finding a teammate to take the shot, he sized up rookie Joe Harris and fumbled the ball just a second before time expired.  While this is certainly Rondo’s fault, it seemed as if nobody on this Celtics team wanted to be the closer. What this team could use is a Paul Pierce archetype, unafraid to take the final shot.

Player Grades

Kelly Olynyk: A-

21 points, 6 rebounds, 1 assist, 2 steals, 1 block

Olynyk continues his outstanding offensive play this year, going 8 for 13 from the field and 2 for 5 from long distance. He’s shooting over 60% from the field this season with a PER over 21. I think Olynyk should be more aggressive with his shooting considering his efficiency at this point of the season. His defense, though, continues to be weak.

Jared Sullinger: A-

19 points, 10 rebounds, 3 assists

Sullinger should be a constant double-double threat, and on nights like this when he goes 19 and 10, it makes you think whether or not he could ever be a consistent 20-10 threat. Sullinger also did a solid job defensively on Love, limiting the All-Star to just 2-10 shooting.

Marcus Thornton: B+

12 points, 2 rebounds, 1 assist

Thornton had some nice outside shooting last night. His defensive struggles lead him to early foul trouble which perhaps could explain his limited minutes. Thornton is just your prototypical off-the-bench gunner. Nothing new here.

Rajon Rondo: B

6 points, 8 rebounds, 16 assists, 3 steals

Had Rondo had made that final shot and not lost his head on that final possession, his grade would have been an A, but the way he handled that last possession was just simply abysmal. I think Brad Stevens needs to find a new player to take the final shot at the end of games. Still, 16 assists…

Avery Bradley: B

12 points, 5 rebounds, 2 assists

Bradley made some clutch mid-range jumpers throughout the game which were sorely missed down the stretch. His defense was not at quite the same level as we have grown accustomed to. I think he misses his fellow defensive specialist Marcus Smart.

Evan Turner: B

12 points, 3 rebounds, 5 assists

While I still have not entirely forgiven Turner for his abysmal performance against the Raptors, he’s slowly winning me back. He’s at his best when he pushes the ball up the court quickly and looks to either create a shot for a teammate or quickly pull up for a midrange jump shot. What drives me crazy is when he tries to isolate himself against a defender and stops the ball movement.

Brandon Bass: B

12 points, 2 rebounds, 2 assist, 1 steal

Bass can hit that mid range shot pretty damn well. If you’ve seen Bass play once, you’ve seen almost every performance of his. Last night was no exception.

Tyler Zeller: B-

8 points, 1 assist

Zeller and Rondo have a nice little two-man game going on. This can explain how Zeller is shooting over 80% from the field this season. He had four fouls in just 15 minutes, which is not entirely his fault, since the perimeter defenders were constantly allowing their guys to blow past them, leaving Zeller exposed.

Jeff Green: C+

19 points, 3 rebounds, 1 assist, 1 steal

Jeff Green had a less-than-efficient night on the offensive end, which could be explained by the fact that he was guarded by James. His poor numbers can be excused for this reason. However, what cannot be excused was allowing James to score 41 points. While James is expected to score 25-30 points on any given night, allowing him to lead a fourth quarter comeback from 19 points down is unacceptable.

Celtics: C

Nobody had an awful game on the team, but collectively, they just cannot hold onto a lead. They have to find a player who is fearless enough to take the final shot. While Smart displayed some irrational confidence down the stretch with his three-point shooting, at least he was aggressive, unlike his teammates. It also would have been nice to see the rookie cover James. He couldn’t have done any worse than Green.

The Celtics finish their homestand against the Phoenix Suns on Monday.
