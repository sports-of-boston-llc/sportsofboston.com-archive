Pablo Sandoval to Decide Next Week on Red Sox’ 5 Year, $95M Offer

By Nick Bohlen in Featured, Opinion, Red Sox, Top Story ⋅ November 22, 2014 at 9:49pm ⋅ Post a comment

According to a report in the Boston Globe, the Boston Red Sox have made a five year, $95 million offer to free agent third baseman Pablo Sandoval. Is that going to be enough to sign the Kung Fu Panda away from his former team, the defending World Champion San Francisco Giants?

Per Sandoval’s brother and agent, Michael, we’ll know the answer to that question by no later than next week. (Which makes sense, as I can’t imagine Sandoval letting anything get in the way of enjoying his Thanksgiving feast. And yes, I’m aware he’s Venezuelan.)

In addition to the Giants, the Red Sox are also, surprisingly, trying to fend off a late charge by the San Diego Padres, who are looking to keep Sandoval in the NL West.

Hopefully nearly $100 million will be sufficient to lure the Panda out of the West Coast dynasty he’s helped build. Hopefully he was able to reach a certain comfort level in visiting Boston and meeting with the Red Sox front office last week. Hopefully that sixth year that he and his agents have been lobbying for won’t be a sticking point.

While some Red Sox fans don’t feel Sandoval is worth the investment, I’m putting all my weight behind the Panda’s signing (as if he needs my help). While I’m in complete agreement the Red Sox desperately need a front line starter more than anything (Come back, Jonny!), the Red Sox are also in dire need of some help on offense.

After so many years of being among the league leaders in runs scored, it’s easy to forget last year’s Red Sox actually ranked 11th in the American League in offense (the same rank as the pitching staff, actually). It’s amazing how much deeper and stronger the Red Sox lineup looks when inserting Sandoval into the fifth slot:

Betts
Pedroia
Ortiz
Cespedes (for now)
Sandoval (fingers crossed)
Napoli
Castillo
Bogaerts
Vazquez

Third base in particular was essentially a gargantuan black hole straight out of Interstellar; Brock Holt hit .219 in the second half, Xander Bogaerts struggled to adjust to big league pitching in his rookie season, and Will Middlebrooks was either injured or might as well have been. Hell, Ryan Roberts was once deemed a solution at third base! Sandoval’s stats in the cavernous reaches of the NL West would represent a huge upgrade for the Red Sox, let alone the statistical bump he’d get from playing half his games at Fenway instead of AT&T Park.

Another overlooked factor is how the Red Sox plan to fill David Ortiz’s shoes when he inevitably retires. Okay, so no one can actually fill the shoes of arguably the greatest designated hitter in baseball history. But the Red Sox will need someone to anchor the lineup when age eventually catches up with Ortiz. Sandoval would be among the better options to fill the DH slot, both in productivity (.294/.346/.465 for his career) and body type (big boned).

Besides, the Red Sox have only $114 million committed to the 2015 payroll, and that drops to a microscopic $42 million in 2016. Even if that weren’t the case, something tells me they could afford to pay Sandoval and still go after Lester or another marquee starter without going into debt.

One way or another, it will all become clear this coming Thanksgiving week. As a supporter of adding a proven playoff performer at a position of need, I’m hoping I’ll be able to add the signing of Pablo Sandoval to my list of things for which to be thankful.
