College Football Week 14 Roundup: BC, UMass on Opposite Ends of Blowouts

By Nick GarcÃ­a in College, Featured, Top Story ⋅ November 29, 2014 at 10:17pm ⋅ Post a comment

Syracuse, 7; Boston College, 28

For the second straight season, Boston College will appear in the postseason with a 7-5 record. It was something of a rocky year after losing quarterback Chase Rettig and star running back Andre Williams, with new starter Tyler Murphy bringing an entirely different style to that of his predecessor.

The Eagles were at their best against their toughest opponents, upsetting #9 USC on September 13 and losing to #24 Clemson and #3 Florida State by a combined seven points. In the regular season finale against Syracuse (3-9), they got just their third home win as Murphy did what he does best and set a new Atlantic Coast Conference record for rushing yards by a QB.

RB Myles Willis started the game by returning the opening kickoff 95 yards for a touchdown. Syracuse QB A. J. Long tied the game up late in the first quarter on a four-yard scoring run, but the Orange would not reach the end zone again. They did have one chance when Willis lost a fumble at the BC 44-yard line, but the defense held to preserve the 7-7 tie.

Kicker Mike Knoll missed a 36-yard field goal, a common theme of the Eagles’ season, after a drive that took up almost nine minutes. On their next possession, Murphy threw a 26-yard TD pass to wide receiver Sherman Alston, putting BC up 14-7 at halftime.

The Orange had the ball to start the third quarter, but Long’s pass was intercepted by linebacker Mike Strizak at the Syracuse 30. Four plays later, RB Jon Hilliman scored on a seven-yard rush to make it 21-7. RB Tyler Rouse closed the scoring with a 42-yard TD run in the fourth, and LB Sean Duggan picked off backup QB Austin Wilson deep in Syracuse territory to end it.

Murphy completed 13 of 17 passes for 160 yards and a TD in the game, and the defense held Syracuse to 128 total yards. With a 4-4 record in ACC play, the Eagles finished in fourth place in the Atlantic Division, four games behind #1 FSU.

Buffalo, 41; Massachusetts, 21

UMass had an anticlimactic end to its third season at the Football Bowl Subdivision level, limping to two losses without injured quarterback Blake Frohnapfel. With Frohnapfel under center, the Minutemen won three games this year, one more than in their first two FBS seasons combined. They also had plenty of close losses, late collapses and brutal mistakes by one of the worst field goal kicking units in all of college football. The season ended on Black Friday with a blowout home loss to Buffalo (5-6).

After the two teams tradedÂ three-and-outs in their first four drives, the Bulls finally broke through, but kicker Patrick Clarke made like a Minuteman and missed a 33-yard field goal. The visitors would get on the board in the second quarter with a 17-play, 99-yard drive that took more than eight minutes off the clock and ended with QB Joe Licata finding wide receiver Devon Hughes for a 28-yard touchdown. With 12 seconds remaining, Licata hit WR Jacob Martinez for a 31-yard score. Clarke missed the extra point attempt and the Bulls went into halftime up 13-0.

Licata started the second half like he ended the first, throwing a 22-yard TD pass to WR Ron Willoughby. WR Marken Michel gave the Minutemen their first points on the next drive with a 50-yard TD run, but Licata came right back with another TD throw to Hughes, this one from four yards out. Running back Anthone Taylor made it 34-7 for Buffalo with a three-yard score late in the third period.

QB Austin Whipple, filling in for Frohnapfel for the second time, connected with WR Tajae Sharpe for a seven-yard TD, but it was already too late for a comeback even before Taylor broke out for a 76-yard scoring run. Whipple and Sharpe would link up again before the end on a 24-yard TD pass to close out the season.

Licata was very efficient for Buffalo, completing 20 of 27 passes for 255 yards and four TDs, while Taylor gashed the run defense for 237 yards and two scores on 36 carries. Whipple was 13-for-28 passing for 168 yards and two TDs, and RB Shadrach Abrokwah ran 15 times for 100 yards.

There is one matchup remaining in the Mid-American Conference, the Buffalo-Kent State game that was postponed due to the winter storm, but the Minutemen are locked into fifth place in the MAC East at 3-5 (3-9 overall), just two conference games worse than division winner Bowling Green, which will face heavy favorite Northern Illinois in the championship game. The best news for UMass? Frohnapfel has one year of eligibility remaining and will try to guide the Minutemen to loftier goals in 2015.
