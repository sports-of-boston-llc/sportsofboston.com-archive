Marquis Daniels to Join Celtics

July 18, 2009 at 5:44pm in Celtics, Featured, Rumors
Nathan writes
4 comments

Multiple sources are reporting that free agent forward, Marquis Daniels, has agreed to join the Celtics. The versatile 6’6″ wingman became a free-agent when the Pacers declined to pick up his $7 million option for the 2009-10 season.

Daniels could join the C’s for the $1.9 million bi-annual exception, but it is believed that both the Celtics and the Pacers would prefer to go the sign-and-trade route. If a sign-and-trade was to be worked out, the deal would most likely include Brian Scalabrine or Tony Allen, or a combination of both players.

A six-year veteran, Daniels will become the primary backup to Paul Pierce, but the forward’s versatility could make him a valuable contributor at any position he plays. Stop Laughing Right Yao: Could the Rockets Center be Done for?

June 30, 2009 at 10:42pm in Celtics, Opinion, Rumors
Jon writes
Add a comment

So, this might not directly affect us as Celtics fans, but anytime someone of Yao Ming’s stature looks like he could be leaving the NBA, it causes ripples. The giant Chinese superstar suffered a hairline fracture of one of the bones in his foot during a playoff loss against the Lakers in Houston. Initial reports indicated that he’d be unable to play for eight to 12 weeks. Since nobody really gave the Rockets much of a shot at getting past Los Angeles anyway, it was thought to be a big deal, but nothing too grave.

New details have emerged, however. The Rockets announced last week that he would be consulting with doctors to “map out a new course of treatment,” according to ESPN.com. It is thought that Yao could miss at least half of next season, and with fellow Houston cornerstone Trace McGrady set to miss a similar amount of playing time, one of the tougher teams in the Western Conference is in real trouble. Worse, Rockets team doctor Tom Clanton told the Houston Chronicle on Monday that the center’s injury “could be career-threatening.”

Shaq to the Cavs?

June 14, 2009 at 2:58pm in Celtics, Rumors, Top Story
Jeff Christenbury writes
One comment

The Lakers haven’t even lifted the Larry O’Brien trophy yet and already the NBA rumor mill is heating up. The latest gossip is the possibility of a trade that would send Shaquille O’Neal from the Phoenix Suns to the Cavs in exchange for bottom feeders Ben Wallace and Sasha Pavlovic.

If this leaves you scratching your head, take a number. However, it seems to be all about money. The Suns realize they have little chance of winning another NBA title in their current form so they must rebuild. Shaq makes $20 million and trading him for high priced Ben Wallace ($14 million) and Pavlovic ($4.9 million) would free up a lot of space for the Suns (who would try to buy out Wallace) and gives the Cavs a big upgrade under the basket. ESPN.com reports that no deal is imminent, however.

My Take: Shaq is no longer the dominating force he once was. That said, he’s an upgrade for the Cavs as they pretty much give up nothing in the trade. It’s tough to say how this would affect the East next year but as a Celtics fan, it’s definitely not the best news, if it happens.
