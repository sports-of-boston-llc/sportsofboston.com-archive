Brett Favre Wants Release From Packers

July 11, 2008 at 5:13pm in News, Rumors, Top Story
KC Downey writes
One comment

According to ESPN’s Chris Mortensen from a few Packers sources, legendary QB Brett Favre, already retired from the NFL, is seeking his unconditional release from the Packers. He apparently wrote a letter to the Packers front office, in which he wrote his desire to play in 2008.

Favre’s agent sent that letter Thursday night, and it arrived to the Packers facilities Friday morning. Favre did not want to force the Packers’ hand and write a request to remain on the 80-man active roster. Under league rules, the Packers would either have to grant his wish or release him.

He also didn’t request a trade because he wants the freedom to decided wherever he ends up.

PACMAN TRADED TO PATRIOTS

April 1, 2008 at 9:35am in Patriots, Rumors
Jeff Christenbury writes
2 comments

Just days after it seemed Titans CB Pacman Jones would be thugging it up in Big D next season, talks broke off between the Titans and Cowboys which allowed the Patriots to swoop in and trade for the talented but oft-arrested Jones.

The departure of all-pro corner Asante Samuel left a gaping hole on the corner for the Patriots and despite the near dozen arrests for Jones in the past few years, the Patriots seem to be the best chance for him to get on the right track.

Jones is still under suspension from the NFL for repeated off the field incidents but the Patriots organization is confident that NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell will lift the penalty when the trade is approved.

For compensation, the Patriots have sent over a sixth round pick but also had to guarantee Jones had a 24 body guard to “bust skulls at da club” and daily bags of Mary Jane to keep him calm through the transition to New England.

Not Another Pink Hat – 3-11

March 11, 2008 at 4:19pm in Opinion, Rumors
Jenn writes
Add a comment

Breaking News: Randy Moss is innocent!

From Spygate to Tom Brady’s baby mama drama, the Patriots have been the center of many stories in 2007-08. This was probably the reason Rachelle Washington decided to come forward with allegations that Randy Moss hit her and prevented her from seeking treatment in her home on January 6, 2008. Whether we believed her or not, I think it’s safe to say that the general feeling was that she was just after a portion of Moss’ fame and fortune. I was anything but surprised when ESPN.com reported today that Ms. Washington requested that the restraining order be dropped and the entire case dismissed.

My take: Is Randy Moss really innocent? Perhaps. Did he just pay her off so she’d drop the case? I hope not, but maybe. Did Rachelle just realize that her allegations didn’t have the impact she’d hope for? Probably. What it comes down to is that I really don’t care. I like Randy Moss because he catches the football, not because he doesn’t hit women. All I can say is, Randy Moss, for the sake of your fans, your team, and your sanity, please stay away from this crazy woman.
