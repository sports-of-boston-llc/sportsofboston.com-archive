Pierce’s Buzzer-Beater Has Celtics Thinking Sweep

April 24, 2010 at 12:19pm in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Andrew Kipp writes
Add a comment

Paul Pierce scored 32 points, including a buzzer-beating 21-foot jumper, helping the Boston Celtics defeat the Miami Heat 100-98 at America Airlines Arena in Miami on Friday night.

Through the first three quarters of the game, it was a familiar re-run episode of Celtics vs. Heat. Boston’s “Big Three” and Rajon Rondo, all in double-figures, fueled a balanced offensive attack, while Heat superstar Dwayne Wade hit big three-pointers, slammed home fast-break dunks, got to the free-throw line by attacking the rim, and did everything but a triple back-flip to keep his team in the game up until the very end.

Then, the fourth quarter came, and Paul Pierce put his mark on the game. The Heat decided they’d had enough of Ray Allen making wide-open three-pointers, and focused on shutting down the Celtic’s three-point assassin down the stretch. Pierce immediately took advantage, nailing a 25-footer with 1.7 seconds remaining in the third quarter to cap off an 11-2 Boston run.

With less than two minutes remaining in the game, Pierce connected from beyond the arc, but his shot was answered by Dorell Wright, who hit a three-pointer of his own with 1:18 remaining tying the game 98-98. Roethlisberger Suspended Six Games By NFL

April 21, 2010 at 2:13pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
John writes
3 comments

NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell has announced that embattled Pittsburgh Steelers’ quarterback Ben Roethlisberger will be suspended for the first six games of the regular season. Some behavioral evaluation by medical experts will also be required.

For those of you who have been living under a rock for the last few months, Roethlisberger was accused of rape (again) in a Georgia nightclub, but was not charged, possibly due to the lone investigating officer (who has since resigned) having bias in Ben’s favor. Still, Roethlisberger’s behavior has been deemed a violation of the league’s good behavior rules.

In the meantime, the Steelers have been trying to rid themselves of their burden by trading it. Quick talks with the Rams regarding trading Roethlisberger as part of a package to get the #1 pick (and Sam Bradford with it) went nowhere when the Rams weren’t interested. Few teams were even interested in Roethlisberger to begin with, and the suspension eating up his value early on has only killed that chance.
