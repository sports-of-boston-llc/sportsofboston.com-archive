Aaron Hernandez Moved to Solitary Confinement; Acclimating Well

July 2, 2013 at 8:19pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
Ying Tao writes
Add a comment

According to the Bristol County Sheriff, former Patriot Aaron Hernandez has been moved into a “Class 2” jail cell after spending five days in a medical unit having his mental health evaluated. According to Sheriff Thomas Hodgson, Hernandez is isolated in his new cell with no interactions with the general population.

Jail officials were evaluating Hernandez’s mental health because of his drastic change in lifestyle going not just from a professional athlete to accused murderer but from a million dollar 7,100-square-foot home with a gourmet kitchen, home theater, and luxury bathrooms to a simple 7-by-10 foot cell with a toilet. Going from a professional hero that people placed on a pedestal to someone that people want nothing to do with could mess with anyone’s mental health, but that does not seem to be the case for Hernandez. Bruins Have Decisions To Make This Offseason

June 30, 2013 at 10:48am in Bruins, Featured, Top Story
Jared Smith writes
Add a comment

After a great, but tragic, season, the Bruins now look ahead to the offseason with some decisions to make. With some contracts expiring and the potential for trades to be made, there are some moves that need to be made. Some things are already known with Jaromir Jagr, Andrew Ference and Nathan Horton, who are set to be unrestricted free agents and will not be returning to the team. However with the core of the team coming back, there are a few players the team has to make decisions on. Though one of those decisions is an easy one.

Tuukka Rask is a must-sign, and after the playoffs that he had, he proved to everyone that he is worth top goaltender money. In the two seasons that Rask was the starter from the regular season into the playoffs, he put up great regular season numbers and though he had his struggles in the 2010 playoffs, he was clearly the best player on the Bruins during the this year’s cup run. Rask will be in line to make somewhere in the six-million-dollar range, and he deserves to be paid like an elite goalie. This will be an easy decision for the Bruins, and since Rask is a restricted free agent, they won’t have to worry about competing with other teams. Tuukka Rask looks like he is in line to be the Bruins goalie for most if not all of the next decade. Continue reading Bruins Have Decisions To Make This Offseason »

Breaking: Garnett, Pierce Will be Traded to Nets

June 28, 2013 at 12:47am in Celtics, Featured, NBA, Top Story
David O'Brien writes
Add a comment

It was only a matter of time before the Boston Celtics decided to completely move forward from the “big three era”. After sending Doc Rivers to the Clippers on Sunday, Adrian Wojnarowski reports  the Celtics have agreed to a trade with the Brooklyn Nets that would send future Hall of Famers, Paul Pierce, Kevin Garnett, along with Jason Terry to the Nets for Gerald Wallace, Kris Humphries, Tomike Shenglia, Reggie Evans, Keith Bogans and future first round draft picks in 2014, ’16, and ’18.

Garnett agreed to waive his no trade clause after the Nets agreed to pay the remaining $12 Million on the third year of his deal in 2014-2015 season. The deal will not be made official until July 10th, following the NBA’s moratorium period.
