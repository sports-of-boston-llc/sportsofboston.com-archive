Ducks Open Anaheim’s Sweep of Boston With 6-1 Thrashing of Bruins

October 9, 2009 at 5:49am in Bruins, Featured, Top Story
Erikk writes
2 comments

Two-and-a-half hours before the Boston Red Sox took to the field to play the Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim in the first game of the American League Division Series, Boston and Anaheim, thanks to a scheduling coincidence, were also facing-off on the ice. In what can now be called a predictive match of what was to come for the Red Sox, the Bruins were thoroughly handled by the Ducks in a 6-1 loss.

Despite a strong effort in the first period in which the Bruins took a 1-0 lead after a Marco Sturm put one of the team’s 19 first-period shots past Ducks goaltender Jonas Hiller at 16:33 on a crisp pass from Marc Savard, Boston seemed to unravel after Teemu Selanne’s scored his first goal of the game (and season) during a 5-on-3 advantage 2:13 into the second period after Matt Hunwick and Sturm were sent to the penalty box (interference and hooking, respectively). Selanne scored again 83 seconds later as the Ducks remained on the power play and Anaheim never let the Bruins back in. Fred Taylor to Have Ankle Surgery

October 8, 2009 at 2:51pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
Jeff Christenbury writes
Add a comment

ESPNBoston.com is reporting Patriots runningback Fred Taylor is going to undergo right ankle surgery. The severity of the injury hasn’t been disclosed but sources are saying the surgery – barring complications – shouldn’t keep Taylor out for the rest of the season. However, he will definitely miss some time

Taylor, 33, played in limited action on Sunday after rushing for more than 100 yards against the Falcons two weeks ago. He hurt the ankle on his final carry against the Ravens.

My Take: This news isn’t what the Patriots were looking for. Taylor has given them some hard running so far this year and with the struggles of Laurence Maroney, fans would like to have all their weapons available for this weekend’s showdown at Denver. Only time will tell how serious this turns out to be.

Rodney Pokes Fun at Brady, Pats Defense Stepping Up

October 7, 2009 at 6:42am in Featured, Opinion, Patriots, Top Story
George writes
6 comments

The Patriots are officially back. Tom Brady is back to making the throws he needs to make. The offense is back to being able to do enough to win games. The defense is back to being that opportune Belichick defense that will let up yards, but will make the stops when they are needed. It’s all back. It might not be quite 2007, but it feels a lot like 2004, and one of those years ended on a good note.

The offense will only get better as Brady finds his rhythm with the new receivers and the return of Wes Welker was just what the doctor ordered. Jerod Mayo will make his return soon, which will only help a defensive unit that has been impressive so far. Sunday was a complete team performance as both the offense and defense did enough to give the team a chance to win the game. I have no doubt in my mind that this team will win the division and will spoil another team’s perfect season next week.

Bruins Sign Milan Lucic to Contract Extension

October 6, 2009 at 5:22pm in Bruins, Featured, Top Story
KC Downey writes
2 comments

The Bruins have signed winger Milan Lucic to a three-year contract extension that will keep him with the team through the 2012-2013 season. Per club policy, terms of the deal were not announced.

The 21-year-old Lucic had a career year last season with the Bruins, scoring 17 goals with 25 assists (42 points). His toughness is his best quality, as exemplified by his 136 penalty minutes. Like many teammates, he also had an exceptional plus/minus rating, with a +17.

This season in two games, Lucic has one assist and five penalty minutes as the Bruins have gone 1-1.
