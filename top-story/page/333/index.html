David Ortiz Should Sit, Not Move Adrian Gonzalez to Outfield, on NL Road Trip

June 23, 2011 at 10:32pm in Featured, Opinion, Red Sox, Top Story
Matt Goisman writes
One comment

Terry Francona has a problem: without a DH for the upcoming nine-game NL road trip, should he play David Ortiz (.313 BA, 17 homers, .977 OPS) at first and move Adrian Gonzalez (.359, 15, 1.019) to right field, or keep Gonzalez at first and use Ortiz primarily as a pinch hitter? Moving Gonzalez preserves the core of the best offense in the majors; sitting Ortiz preserves the third-best defense.

So far, all Francona has done is acknowledge the issue; he has given no indication of which way he’s leaning. But taking into account both his recent struggles and who the Red Sox will play on this road trip, the smart move is to sit Ortiz. Red Sox Honor Bruins, Beat Brewers 12-3; Buchholz Hits DL

June 20, 2011 at 12:59am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Greg writes
Add a comment

These aren’t your father’s Red Sox. No, these are the Red Sox who have learned from the fellow sports teams in Boston on how to win. Before the game, the Red Sox honored the Stanley Cup Champion Boston Bruins by holding their own duck boat parade around the Fenway warning track. After the mini-parade around the park, the team, led by Zdeno Chara hoisting the Stanley Cup, each Bruin threw out the ceremonial first pitch.

As a nice touch, the Sox honored the B’s each time the Sox scored by playing the same foghorn and music the Bruins use when a goal is scored.

As for the game, the Red Sox got off the a quick 6-0 lead in the first on a three-run home run from Kevin Youkilis and RBIs from Jacoby Ellsbury and Adrian Gonzalez. The Sox went on to to score 12 runs while holding the Milwaukee Brewers to just three runs for the 12-3 victory on Father’s Day.
