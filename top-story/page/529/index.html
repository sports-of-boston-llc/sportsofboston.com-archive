Steroid-Tainted Trainer Linked to A-Rod, Ortiz, Pedro

February 20, 2009 at 1:43pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
8 comments

According to the New York Daily News, Alex Rodriguez has been linked to a trainer that has been banned from all MLB clubhouses. That trainer, named Angel Presinal, was banned from all private areas in every major league ballpark after an October 2001 incident involving an unmarked gym bag full of steroids.

In addition to A-Rod, Presinal has worked with Red Sox slugger David Ortiz and former Sox Ace Pedro Martinez. He’s also connected to Juan Gonzalez, Vladimir Guererro, Bartolo Colon, Miguel Tejada, Adrian Beltre, Moises Alou, Jose Guillen, Ervin Santana, Ruben Sierra, Francisco Cordero, Jose Mesa, and Juan Guzman, among others. Garnett Injured in Loss to Jazz

February 20, 2009 at 8:03am in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

The Celtics suffered two rare losses Thursday night against the Jazz. They held a 66-60 lead after the third quarter and by as much as 11. Then, Utah outscored Boston 30-19 in the fourth while shooting 12-17 from the field to earn the comeback win, 90-85. How did the Celtics manage to blow the lead?

Well, they played the entire second half without their best defender, Kevin Garnett, who left the game with a right knee strain after a botched alley-oop. Garnett warmed up before the second half, but was held out as a precaution. He said after the game that his knee will be re-examined Friday. Continue reading Garnett Injured in Loss to Jazz »

Larry Izzo Linked to BALCO

February 17, 2009 at 11:56am in Featured, News, Patriots, Top Story
Jeff Christenbury writes
Add a comment

Patriots veteran Larry Izzo is set to testify that he has met with Greg Anderson, the personal trainer of Barry Bonds. The testimony not only hurts Bonds but links Izzo to steroids again. From the story at Boston.com:

Izzo will testify that he contacted Anderson by phone, then met with the trainer and submitted a number of urine samples in the process of obtaining the performance-enhancing substances, prosecutors stated in a document outlining their witness list for Bonds’s trial.

My Take: While it’s good Izzo is standing up and telling the truth, it’s unfortunate another Patriots player is involved with controversy. We’ll see if there are any ramifications from the NFL on his testimony.
