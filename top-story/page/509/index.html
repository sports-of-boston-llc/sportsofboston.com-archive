Sox Slug Out 16 Hits in Win Over Mets

May 24, 2009 at 6:29pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
One comment

Kevin Youkilis hit a deep foul ball (that looked like a home run) in the fifth inning. After the second replay review in as many days, the umpires confirmed the ball was foul. Youkilis then flied out to right, and was obviously fuming as he headed to the dugout. In his next at bat in the seventh inning, Youkilis slammed a three-run home run that was clearly fair, which helped the Sox to a 12-5 win over the Mets.

Mike Lowell went 3-5 with a three-run shot in the second inning, J.D. Drew went 4-5 to raise his batting average to .273, and Jacoby Ellsbury extended his hitting streak to 19 games as part of a 16-hit attack for the Red Sox. It was more than enough support for Tim Wakefield, who didn’t have his best outing. Wake allowed five runs on seven hits and four walks in six innings to earn his sixth win of the season. Papelbon Blows Save, Game Against Mets

May 24, 2009 at 1:26am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
2 comments

Josh Beckett is starting to look like the Sox ace again. After a win against Seattle last time out, Beckett took the hill and was dominant Saturday night. He allowed one unearned run (though he recorded the error) in eight innings. He allowed just five hits and one walk with five strikeouts and was in line for his fifth win.

Jonathan Papelbon came on in the ninth, and walked the first hitter (Gary Sheffield) he faced. After blowing away both David Wright and Jeremy Reed for back-to-back strikeouts, the Red Sox look poised for the win. Coming to the plate was backup catcher Omir Santos with two outs.

Again, Paps brought the heat, sending a 97 MPH fastball to home plate. Santos was expecting fastball (and why wouldn’t he?), and managed to catch up to the pitch and launch it to deep center field. The ball hit the top of the Green Monster and was initially ruled a double. Replays confirmed to umpires that it was in fact a home run, and Papelbon had officially blown his first save of 2009. For Santos, it was just his second career home run, and handed the Mets the 3-2 victory. Continue reading Papelbon Blows Save, Game Against Mets »

Michael Vick to be a Patriot?

May 21, 2009 at 6:53pm in News, Patriots, Rumors, Top Story
Jeff Christenbury writes
2 comments

Fresh off the heels (athankyou) of his release from federal prison for dog fighting, Michael Vick has returned to his home in Virginia to serve out the remaining two months of his 23 month sentence. Vick will work construction during that time but then will be able to petition the NFL for reinstatement. This has already set the media pundit world into a tizzy predicting where Vick could end up. It’s apparent from all accounts he will never wear a Falcons jersey again, but could he be taking his game to Gillette Stadium of all places?

Peter King of CNNSI.com thinks so. So does ESPN’s John Clayton. What say you?

My Take: Two words: No thanks. The argument used here is that Vick could help the Patriots take the Wildcat offense to new levels. Whether the Wildcat is going to evolve into a legitimate offense or is just a fad formation is another topic, but the baggage Vick will bring to whatever franchise he joins will be immense. You think it took some “Dennis” for the Celtics to bring in Stephon Marbury? That was nothing compared to a convicted felon. I can’t see owner Bob Kraft – who thankfully is very conscientious about the image of the franchise – allowing Vick to make his return to the NFL in Patriots blue. What say YOU?

David Ortiz Homers to End Drought, Sox Win

May 21, 2009 at 7:32am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

In what seemed like an eternity, over the course of 149 long at bats, David Ortiz finally cracked the big one in at bat #150. In the fifth inning Wednesday night against Toronto starter Brett Cecil, Ortiz slammed a deep blast to the hardest part of the park to hit a home run – straightaway center field – to end his historic homerless streak that dated back to last season. Papi’s HR gave the Sox a 5-0 lead on thier way to an 8-3 victory.

Ortiz’s home run seemed to invigorate the rest of his teammates, who launched four other homers in a nice power display against the first place Blue Jays. Jason Varitek hit two bombs in his first multi-homer game since 2005. Joining Tek was Mike Lowell and Jason Bay, who each hit home runs to help the Sox pull within 1.5 games of the Blue Jays for first place in the AL East.

Wakefield Pitches Sox Past Jays

May 20, 2009 at 6:07am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
One comment

When the knuckleball is not working for Tim Wakefield, he can be one of the worst pitchers in baseball. When it’s on, he’s nearly unhittable. In his previous start, he allowed 11 hits and seven runs in a loss against the Angels. Against the East-leading Blue Jays Tuesday night, Wake was in top form again, allowing just one run (a Kevin Millar HR) on five hits in eight strong innings to earn the win.

David Ortiz started at DH in the No. 3 spot in the batting order for the Sox after three games off to clear his mind. The time off had little effect at least initially, as Ortiz went 0-3 with two strikeouts and a walk in Boston’s 2-1 win.

The Red Sox did all their damage in the second inning with the bottom of the order against Toronto SP Brian Tallet. With two men on, Jeff Bailey singled home Mike Lowell for the first run. George Kottaras hit a sacrifice fly to drive in J.D. Drew, and that was it for the Sox scoring. Two runs got it done Tuesday night, but on most nights it’s not enough.
