Aaron Cook Injured, Battered by Orioles in Red Sox Loss

May 6, 2012 at 12:07am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Matthew Bond writes
Add a comment

Aaron Cook was hoping for a better first start in Boston. A much anticipated debut for the Red Sox on Saturday was shattered early, as a second inning collision with Chris Davis left a large gash in his leg. He amazingly stayed in the game, but probably would have been better off hitting the showers, as he was knocked around in a seven-run Orioles third inning. The Sox offense was relatively quiet again as Jason Hammel pitched into the seventh inning for Baltimore.

Cook was actually solid out of the gate. Throwing entirely sinkers, he retired the first five batters he faced, inducing four ground ball outs in the infield. Consecutive singles from Davis and Wilson Betemit put runners on the corners, and a passed ball by Jarrod Saltalamacchia allowed Davis to head home. Rajon Rondo had his 7th career triple-double with 17 points, 14 rebounds and 12 assists to lead the Boston Celtics past the Atlanta Hawks in overtime, 90-84, Friday night at the Garden. The victory gave the Celtics a crucial 2-1 series lead over the Hawks in the Eastern Conference quarterfinals.

Joe Johnson scored 29 points for the Hawks and put in two huge buckets to tie the game as the end of regulation neared. Jeff Teague continued his strong play in the series by adding 23 points.
