Celtics Dominate Suns for 5th Straight Win

January 20, 2009 at 5:27am in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Geoff writes
Add a comment

The Celtics continued their resurgence Monday night against the Phoenix Suns, winning 104-87 in Boston for their fifth straight victory. The Celtics were in complete control of this one from the get-go and steadily maintained just about a 30-point lead through much of the game. They were up by as much as 35 points at one juncture. Once again, the “Big Four” of Paul Pierce, Kevin Garnett, Ray Allen, and Rajon Rondo were able to sit out the entire fourth quarter as the bench finished this one off.

Rajon Rondo led all scorers with 23 points and seven assists. Ray Allen added 20 points and Kevin Garnett and Paul Pierce added 16 and 12 points respectively. The bench also had another decent game with Glen Davis adding nine points and grabbing nine rebounds. Bill Walker had a nice game again in his third appearance since coming up from the D-League by putting up seven points and continuing to show a good amount of talent. Down 2-1 entering the third period, the Bruins were in need of a lift…and they got it in a two-minute span late in the third. Michael Ryder scored his 18th goal on the power play at 15:16, PJ Axelsson added a power play goal of his own 19 seconds later to give the Bruins the lead, and Zdeno Chara padded the lead 80 seconds later.

The explosive Bruins offense had just run up the score with only minutes left in the game. Would it be enough? How about NO! How you ask? The Blues’ David Perron scored a power play goal with just 1:20 left in regulation to cut the lead to 4-3, and David Backes scored his 16th goal with less than a second remaining to force overtime.

Add a comment

All it took was a dose of the Atlantic Division, but it appears the Celtics are back in track after easily dispatching the Nets for their fourth straight victory, winning 105-85. The “C’s had a little trouble in sweeping the Raptors in back to back games, but they easily swept the Nets twice in a row, doing most of their damage against New Jersey’s starting five.

Rajon Rondo had a Jason Kidd-like game with eight points, nine rebounds, and a game-high 14 assists, and Kevin Garnett finished with a double-double (12 points, 10 rebounds) as the Celtics starters just plain destroyed the Nets. January 16, 2009 at 7:48am in Bruins, Featured, Top Story

The Bruins have now started a new hot streak, earning their fourth straight victory Thursday night thanks to a 2-1 win over the Islanders.
