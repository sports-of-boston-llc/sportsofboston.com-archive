The charges were dropped in exchange for his testimony against the other defendants, but this is not the same an an acquittal, which requires a trial and a not guilty verdict. When someone is acquitted, they can never again be charged with the same crime, but when the charges are dropped in a plea bargain, the charges can be reinstated if the defendant does not live up to their end of the bargain, for example, if they do not testify fully and truthfully, after promising to do so.
