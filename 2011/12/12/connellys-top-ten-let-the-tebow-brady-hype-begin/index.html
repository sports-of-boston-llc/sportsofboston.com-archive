Connelly’s Top Ten: Let the Tebow-Brady Hype Begin!

By Michael in Featured, Now! ⋅ December 12, 2011 at 7:00am ⋅ Post a comment

1. Patriots Hold On Again:

OFFENSE

* Caution – Tom Brady showing up receivers might be coming to a head. First you see Wes Welker swearing at Brady after a bad read and then I love offensive coordinator Bill O’Brien getting after his Brazil dancing quarterback after Brady yelled at Tiquan Underwood for not getting his pass when he should have thrown it in the stands – kicked the field goal – and made it a two-possession game
* Never seen Tom Brady and wide receivers so far off the same page
* At least 75% of Ochocinco’s receptions have been on the out.
* Speaking of #85 – he is so nervous, he is just catching the ball and going to the ground giving him no yards after catch
* The run by Rob Gronkowski in the first quarter down the sideline was as good as Tommie Frazier of Nebraska (if you see a replay look at rocket scientist DeAngelo Hall just standing their watching Gronkowski outwork the Redskins)
* Another big drop by Aaron Hernandez
* Brady: 10 of 13 games with quarterback rating over 100

DEFENSE

* Great effort by Vince Wilfork to get off the ground on the 2-yard line and dive on the loose ball in the end zone for a touchdown (late reports from Washington DC officials is that Wilfork’s dive caused a crack in the Washington Monument)
* Love that delay blitz by Jerod Mayo
* Devon McCourty might have had the worst three quarters in cornerback history. His penalty on the five yard slant when it was third and 18 was the stupidest play in the NFL history and almost cost them the game
* Andre Carter – 1 sack, three quarterback hits, five tackles
* Julian Edelman had 5 tackles
* Redskins were 7-14 on third down / averaged 5.0 yards a carry / 463 total yards

REFS

* Officials missed an obvious helmet to helmet call on the hit of Gronkowski on the goalline by DeJon Gomes – that will be a fine
* The penalty on London Fletcher for hitting Tom Brady was a disgrace
* The penalty on Andre Carter for hitting below the knees of the quarterback was wrong. He hit Rex Grossman in the thigh.
* Call on Vince Wilfork for roughing was correct

OTHER

* The streak of showing Robert Kraft during a game continues at 2:32 of the second quarter and 12:43 of the fourth quarter
* Just a suggestion when building a stadium – don’t color the seats in a bright color (like yellow) – when they are unoccupied, it is conspicuous
* Lot of Patriots fans there (made more noise that a Foxboro crowd)
* Do you think wide receivers practice spinning the ball at home in preparation for after catch histrionics?
* With the Redskins first and goal and two minutes left – the Patriots should have used their three time outs to save Brady some time in case Washington scored
* Danny Woodhead should not be taking the ball out five yards deep in the end zone when he can only get 20 yards
* Redskins song Hail to the Redskins only second to New England Whalers Brass Bonanza

2. Around the NFL:

* Tebow magic continues – they say that Denver has done a good job creating an offense tailored to Tebow. I disagree – they need to open it up and go hurry up where his athletic instinct takes over. They should run a Doug Moe / Paul Westhead offense
* By the way, kickers banging 50+ yards through the goal post like its nothing is not good for the game.
* Just wondering – how does a free agent wide receiver like Anquan Boldin pick Joe Flacco over Tom Brady?
* Last week against the Patriots, Dan Orlovsky threw 30-37 for 353 yards. This week against Baltimore: 17-37 for 136 yards
* Something like 17 Packers have scored touchdowns this year – crazy
* Here come those Jets – look out

3. College Football Stuff:

* Great to see Robert Griffin III win Saturday night – acceptance speech was perfect
* Navy wins for the tenth straight time – glad to see the President present
* How cool would in be if we were getting ready for the College Football quarterfinals next weekend?
* The quotes from Boston College football people in the Mark Blaudschun Globe article Sunday is exactly what losers say
* Charlie Weis now in Kansas – he’s no Dorothy

5. NFL Disparity:

The spread between the Colts (0-13) and Packers (13-0) after 13 games is 13 games.

In 1972, Miami was 14-0 and the worst team was the Houston Oilers, who were 1-13 with Dan Pastorini at quarterback.

7. Amazing Stats:

8. Old School:

In his 1978-1979 season with the Celtics, Bob McAdoo averaged 17 field goal attempts a game in 31 minutes of time. He averaged 20.6 points a game.
