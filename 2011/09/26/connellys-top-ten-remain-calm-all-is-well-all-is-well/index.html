Connelly’s Top Ten: “Remain Calm, All is Well, All is Well!”

By Michael in Featured, Now! ⋅ September 26, 2011 at 7:00am ⋅ Post a comment

At 4:30 p.m. on Sunday afternoon, my entire weekend rested on the right shoulder of John Lackey…

1. Inexcusable loss by Patriots:

Offense

* Wes Welker is a freakin’ warrior. On that fourth down, he willed himself to extend beyond his physical limitations for the first down – what an honor to watch him play.

* I would cut Chad Johnson this week: His drop in the red zone cost the Patriots three minutes / His lack of detail in his route cost the Patriots an interception (I thought he was supposed to be a great route runner) / On his two catches, he went immediately down giving him and his Patriots no yards after the catch / He is disinterested in downfield blocking

* Everytime Tom Brady runs – the blood flow of New Englanders’ arteries are tested

* I like the burst I’m seeing from Steve Ridley

* Deion Branch shut out

* Didn’t agree withÂ Bill Belichick going for a field goal from the two-yard line in the third quarter to make it a seven-point game – he knows his defense stinks.

Defense

* First play for scrimmage for Buffalo: Leigh Bodden was toast

* Kyle Arrington’s second interception was selfish – it was fourth down, costing the Patriots yards

* Devin McCourty made one play the whole game, and it was that ill-advised tackle on Jackson, allowing Buffalo to run out the clock

* Sergio Brown trying to tackle Fred Jackson looked like a futile attempt at kill the man with the ball when he jumped on his back

Buffalo

* Is there anyone tougher in the league to tackle than Fred Jackson?

* Tough guy Nick Barnett picks Danny Woodhead to body slam (should hear from league)

* Buffalo will not make the playoffs!

CBS

* Marv Albert always gives a broadcast a biting edge

* Is it in the NFL television contract to show Niagara Falls during every game at Buffalo (and that white steeple when at Foxboro)?

* Considering the fact that extra points are guaranteed and kick offs are 70% touchbacks – the time from a touchdown to a real play is at least five minutes with commercials (touchdown / extra point / commercial / kickoff / touchback / commercial).

Officials

* Disgraceful calls on Nate Solder for holding, Rob Ninkovich for a hit to the head (he went out of his way to get his head over Fitzpatrick’s shoulder), Kyle Love, Sergio Brown’s pass interference (biggest play of the game)

* The fact that there were so many questionable calls – disgraceful that Rich Gannon and Marv Albert didn’t have the intestinal fortitude to criticize an official even once

Stats

* There were ten plays between 20-29 yards/Â  There were four plays between 30-39 yards/Â  There was one play over40 yards

* Tom Brady is on pace to throw for 7,077 yards and 59 touchdown passes.

Other

* I have to mention – the lack of effort by Brady refusing to try to tackle Drayton Florence before going into the end zone with the interception

* Must have smelled nice in the stadium with all the Buffalo fans with no shirts

2. Red Sox:

* I don’t care if the Red Sox only had eight players – I would never put J.D. Drew back on the field.

* Jacoby Ellsbury 30-30 guy – hats off, but he’s motivated by money and a future contract (Angels). The rest of the bums are being suffocated by the pressure to win.
