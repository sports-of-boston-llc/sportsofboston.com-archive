Connelly’s Top Ten: Patriots Put Their Foot Down

By Michael in Featured, Now! ⋅ October 10, 2011 at 11:38am ⋅ Post a comment

1. Patriots vs. Jets:

Offense

* Hats off to the offensive line – I haven’t seen them knock a team off the line like that since 1985 (you know the John Hannah days)
* The Law Firm with 28 more touches without a fumble
* Ochocinco’s second false start of the year – no wonder he was looking for a place to live
* An interesting stat would be Tom Brady’s completion percentage when he has more than four seconds to throw – I bet its under 50%
* Thought the Patriots got the benefit of doubt on three interference calls (or illegal contact)
* Drop by Aaron Hernandez at the end of the first half is the kind of play that can change the entire course of a season
* Deion Branch almost choked on his touchdown catch letting the ball get to his chest
* Amazingly – Wes Welker’s 5 receptions for 124 yards is one of his worst games of the season

Defense

* Is it me or does Brandon Spikes fill the wrong gap more than Junior Seau?
* I like the energy and effort from Mark Anderson on the defensive line
* Devon McCourty showed another gear running down Joe McNight from behind on the Jets’ 88 yard kickoff return
* James Ihedigbo does a lot of chirping for a JAG
* Andre Carter’s best game as a Patriots (7 tackles / 1 QB hit)
* Is Shaun Ellis washed up?

Jets

* Love the story that the Jets wide receivers had a mutiny this week – can’t have bad people like Plaxico Burress and Santonio Holmes and not have bad things happen
* Jets should be 1-4
* Darrelle Revis is the best cover man in the history of the NFL
* Nick Mangold is to the Jets as Logan Mankins is to the Patriots

Other

* Another big game for Zoltan Mesko, averaging 51.3 yards – look out Rich Camarillo
* Love the old school uniforms
* Cool to see the Bruins coming out with the Stanley Cup – can’t see it enough
* Who’s in charge of cleaning the ref flag in Coach Belichick’s sock?
* Speaking of the red flag – Bill was 2 for 2
* Any truth to the rumor that spectators in the club section put down their wine glasses to have the guy with the “Noise” sign arrested?
* When the cameras are flashing on the cheerleaders and CBS shows a sponsor’s logo in front of the cheerleader – isn’t that bad for the company to have so many people at home cursing them?
* Great win, but the Patriots have proved that regular season wins are not postseason wins

2. Around the NFL:

* Andy Reid’s three quarterbacks (Michael Vick, Donovan McNabb and Kevin Kolb) have a combined record of 3-12
* With Seattle leading 29-25, Seahawk defender Brandon Browner intercepted a pass and ran it back 94 yards for a touchdown with 1:08 left. I wonder at any point in his run back if he thought – the only way the Giants can win this game is if I run this into the end zone?
* The Harbaugh boys are a combined 7-2 (Ravens / 49ers)
* The Ryan boys are 4-7 (Jets / Cowboys)
* Why are people so reluctant to give Tebow a chance? Who can deny he’s a winner – isn’t that worth more than whether he holds the ball at his hip or shoulders?
* You know that Brett Favre sits at home rooting against the Packers

3. Around College Football:

* Curious what BC is doing to promote Luke Kuehchly for the Butkus Award and Bronko Nagurski Award after failing him last year (I haven’t got anything in the mail – have you?)
* Big 10 stinks this year
* Rankings from 21-24 are all Texas teams – Texas, Texas A&M, Baylor and Houston
* If the season ended right now – some of the matchups if you had a 16-team playoff: Boise State – Georgia Tech / Oklahoma – Nebraska / Clemson – Oregon

4. Baseball Playoffs:

* Former Boston players in postseason: Victor Martinez, Adrian Beltre, Mark Kotsay, Takashi Saito, Darren Oliver, David Murphy, George Kottaras, Brad Penny
* Rank of the remaining teams in payroll: Tigers 10th ($106mm) / Cardinals 11th ($105mm) / Rangers 13th ($92mm) / Brewers 17th ($85mm)
* Nyjer Morgan’s favorite players – Carl Everett, Milton Bradley, Joaquin Andujar

6. He Kills Us Either Way:

Carl Crawford against the Red Sox for his career: .300 average / 62 stolen bases (caught 4 times)

7. Amazing Stats:

Tony Larussa ruined Rick Ankiel in the 2000 postseason starting him against the Atlanta Braves: 2.2 innings / 6 walks – 4 hits / 4 runs / FIVE wild pitches

8. Old School:

The most under-rated no-hitter in baseball history – Mike Scott’s: gave up no hits to the Giants on September 25, 1986 to clinch the National League West. He struck out 13 and walked two.

9. Just a Little Bit Outside:

Top outside patios around town:

* Devlin’s in Brighton
* Union Street in Newton
* Charlie’s Kitchen Harvard Square
* Tia’s on the Waterfront (pricey)

10. Randomocity:

* Separated at birth: John Lackey/Josh Beckett and Vince Wilfork/Kyle Love
* Saw video of Brides Maids – completely over-rated
* Are you kidding me with this weather? – A+
* Traffic has been horrendous since school started – I thought the real unemployment was 18%
* I know Robin Williams is in a slump, but he still had five very good movies: (1) Dead Poet’s Society, (2) Good Will Hunting, (3) Good Morning Vietnam, (4) Mrs. Doubtfire, (5) Death to Smoochy
