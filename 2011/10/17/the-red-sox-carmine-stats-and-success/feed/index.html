No, I don’t think anyone should just accept that the Red Sox know what they are doing, but one month, no matter how bad, should not discredit many years of work. Even the Yankees had a nearly decade long stagnent period where the team was good but not great.

Fans should be disappointed in how things ended and should put pressure on ownership and the fornt office to improve. This is a team that has the resources to fix nearly any problem over the winter. But we saw a great team for four months this season. That wasn’t a fluke.

Baseball is a long haul during the season, players have a long developmental period, and the Red Sox face three very good teams in their own division. For now, I think the team still gets the benefit of the doubt. We’ll see if they take action in baseball operations or marketing this winter.
