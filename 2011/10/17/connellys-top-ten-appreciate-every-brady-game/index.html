Connelly’s Top Ten: Appreciate Every Brady Game

By Michael in Featured, Now! ⋅ October 17, 2011 at 7:00am ⋅ Post a comment

1. Patriots:

Offense

* Should have stayed in the hurry-up offense
* If they lose that game – the big play of the game is the fumble by Aaron Hernandez
* The last drive featured four perfect throws by Tom Brady including the touchdown to Hernandez
* Brady’s quarterback sneaks and Larry Bird’ free throws in the clutch – they never fail
* Brady was 1-5 with four seconds or more to throw, including an interception (Watch for it in a game)
* I sometimes have to check myself when I feel bad for a guy who’s made probably close to $100mm, but the Ochocinco thing has become sad and pitiable. They actually cleared a whole side of the field for him and he still ran the wrong route. He was booed coming off the field.
* The Ryan’s have Brady’s number a little bit
* Does the official that calls Welker out on the one-yard line get reprimanded? Clearly a touchdown with the naked eye but despite that it forces a five-minute replay.
* Patriots are starting to miss Dan Koppen up the middle – the line looks confused
* Huge tackle by Danny Woodhead on the Terence Newman recovery, keeping him from scoring

Defense

* Albert Haynesworth looks in shape when he stands next to Vince Wilfork
* Speaking of Wilfork, he almost had his third interception – he’s becoming a shutdown corner
* Wastes of time – arguing with City Hall, looking for a parking spot in Boston and Rob Ninkovich rushing the quarterback.
* Andre Carter has the most spark on the defensive line
* Haynesworth has two tackles on the SEASON – Danny Woodhead had two tackles Sunday alone

Special Teams

* Someone mentioned it on sports radio last week. Wes Welker is too valuable to have him returning punts – how about Kevin Faulk?
* Patriots miss Brandon Tate on kickoff returns – Matthew Slater had an inexcusable fumble and Stevan Ridley doesn’t have enough burst

Cowboys

* No comment by Troy Aikman or Thom Brennaman with regards to Tony Romo’s first interception where he threw the ball and went and hid under the covers
* Rob Ryan is the daughter Buddy always wanted – nice hair
* Will the league look into Tashard Choice spearing Kyle Arrington in the face (I know Choice had the ball but it was vicious)
* Can the Cowboys equipment guy get Dez Bryant a new pair of pants so I don’t have to see his bum twice after tackles

Other

* Enough with the pink – you are diluting the message
* Patriots on pace to go 13-3
* Very good piece by Fox featuring Vince Wilfork’s interceptions with the Chariots of Fire song in the background
* I find it interesting looking at former players like Troy Aikman and seeing their entire body change after being out of the game – no shoulders or muscle definition
* Does the ball boy need batting gloves?
* Are the men in Bob Kraft’s box required to wear a blue blazer and blue button-down shirt?
* Officials didn’t allow for any flow by throwing 20 flags in the game
* It comes down to the Patriots vs. Ravens for the AFC Championship

2. Red Sox stuff:

* Brilliant move by John Henry going over to 98.5 the Sports Hub on Friday. He held his own, relatively speaking, and he separated himself from Larry Lucchino by making clear it wasn’t him that leaked Tito Francona information
* Wondering if John Henry asked Larry permission to go to the bathroom and then snuck out the window?
* Speaking of Tito – just throwing this out there – could it have been a bitter family member that leaked the story?
* Tito and Theo Epstein leave on bad terms but both will be in the Red Sox Hall of Fame
* With all apologies to reader Maureen C and Jeff K – but for everyone who buys season tickets, shirts and EVEN follows this team on the road – you are suckers. You care more than the players.

3. Gillette Marketing:

Who got fired in Gillette marketing for signing Roger Federer, Tiger Woods and Thierry Henry to mega-million dollar deals right before their careers bottomed out?

4. Around College Football:

* Sean McDonough (the speaker at the November 8th Blanket Dinner, might I say) – offered this note in the Texas – Oklahoma State game: Oklahoma State quarterback Brandon Weeden turned 28 Friday, and he’s older than 15 NFL QB’s
* Personal fouls by Michigan State players, Marcus Rush and William Ghoulston, should have been ejections and suspensions
* Texas A&M has the next Wes Welker in Ryan Swope
* How about Kansas State, who’s now 6-0 with 72-year-old Bill Snyder coaching the team, for whom the field is named after, and his son is an assistant coach and grandson is on the roster
* November 5th: LSU travels to Alabama to determine who will play Oklahoma in the championship
* Russell Wilson continues his run to the Heisman
