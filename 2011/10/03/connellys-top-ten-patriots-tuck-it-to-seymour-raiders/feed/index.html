The schools look at a bowl game as an event for the school – where the team, the students, the administration, and alumni use it as a reunion and travel to the bowl game en mass. (Don’t use BC as the benchmark)
If there is a multiple game playoff format, then how could the schools organize an “event” that has become tradition in college football. The sport is different. You can’t play more than one football game per week – it’s not basketball or hockey or baseball where you can play twice in a weekend. Players can’t walk without pain until Wednesday after a Saturday game.
Fans and students would wait to see if their team won, because people can’t afford multiple destination events per year in back to back weeks. The only thing that would work is to have the higher ranked team host the event as a home game. (This would accomplish the dream of eliminating sterile pro venues vs. electric college venues – think LSU’s home stadium as opposed to the Superdome). Rose Bowl should be mandated by a Constitutional ammendment to host every National Championship game if this ever happened by the way.

I think that a plus one format would be the best way to preserve tradition and minimize the chances of a team getting jobbed. I don’t want the regular season to get diminished. Every week is a round of the playoffs for Alabama, Oklahoma and Wisconsin as it stands right now.
