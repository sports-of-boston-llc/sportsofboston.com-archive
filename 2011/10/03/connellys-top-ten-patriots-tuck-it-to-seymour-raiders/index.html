Connelly’s Top Ten: Patriots TUCK it to Seymour, Raiders

By Michael in Featured, Now! ⋅ October 3, 2011 at 7:00am ⋅ Post a comment

1. Patriots:

Offense

* Welker – wow!!!!
* Can you name three running backs in Patriots history that can put their foot in the ground, gets shoulders square and run away from defenders like Stevan Ridley? Maybe Curtis Martin (not as quick as Ridley), Dave Meggett maybe, and Mack Herron needed more space. Ridley gives the Patriots something they haven’t in the backfield in a long time.
* Thought Tom Brady got a little too cute with the two-minute drill at the end of the first half not using his two time outs causing a crisis to go downfield – he got two plays off from 1:12 to 22 seconds.
* Aaron Hernandez has now missed five of the Patriots last seven games
* Deion Branch caught his first pass in seven quarters – four yards on the day.

Defense

* Not a big fan of the “bend don’t break” defense come playoffs
* Oakland went 8 for 13 on third downs
* Thought Vince Wilfork’s efforts on the end-around by Jacoby Ford was not worthy of money he made for that play
* Watching the lack of pass rush – it makes me wonder if the Patriots really needed to spend a second round pick on running back Shane Vereen?
* Wilfork showed a good pair of hands on his second interception of the season.
* Who is worse covering backs out in pass routes – Gary Guyton, Dane Fletcher or Rob Ninkovich?
* How come when Brandon Spikes got manhandled in the hole allowing Darren McFadden’s 41-yard run – I didn’t see him separate himself from his teammates to do the sledge hammer?
* Hats off to Patrick Chung playing one week after surgery
* Devon McCourty’s best game of the year – both in coverage and supporting the run defense

Raiders and Seymour:

* Thanks Richard Seymour – I appreciate you doing something you didn’t do in New England after your big contract – and that is playing inspired ball but not smart ball, giving the Patriots the momentum and eventually the game
* Think of those idiot Raider fans going home on the subway after a loss with their face painted and shoulder pads with spikes on them
* Rob Gronkowski got some attention from the Raiders

Other:

* Thought Solomon Wilcox was refreshingly candid on a number of his analyses
* Bill Cowher said he was looking forward to these two tough teams slugging it out – Patriots haven’t been tough since Rodney Harrison’s prime
* Zoltan Mesko averaging 48 yards a punt last two weeks
* Patriots starting to get beat up a little bit – Mayo and Woodhead and Love all were hurt

2. Red Sox:

* If I was in Vegas, I would put money on the option that John Henry’s injury on the boat was contrived to miss the press conference Friday
* Theo – just know that in Chicago – Alfonso Soriano, Carlos Zambrano, Ryan Dempster have $50mm promised to them next year
* It would be fun to see Theo and Tito go to Chicago together
* Red Sox team collectively remind me of Curtis Rowe’s famous comment after the team was lectured by Red Auerbach – “Don’t worry boys, they don’t put W’s and L’s on our paychecks.”
* When is the 2001 Red Sox team going to donate to the 9/11 fund (they were the only team that didn’t)
* Reader Steve has said this for three years – Tim Wakefield has to go. His constant denials of his own futility and now use of the word “I” in every statement is getting repulsive
* Didn’t like David Ortiz’s comments this weekend that he knew that their problems in the clubhouse but that was front office’s job to fix it – Rodney Harrison, Dave Cowens, John Hannah, Terry O’Reilly all would have fought their teammates before they would allow a team they played on disgrace the uniform and the spirit of competition.

3. MLB Playoffs:

* Disgraceful that Major League Baseball allowed the Yankees-Tigers game to start Friday night wasting two aces and thus not showcasing their brilliance to the fans.
* By the way, Jeremy Giambi was safe at the plate on Derek Jeter’s “famous” back up play:

* I’m rooting for Tampa Bay – I like teams that care (Don’t see it much at Fenway more in the first base dugout)

4. Around the NFL:

* Big day for the Patriots with Jets, Bills and Dolphins losing (not to mention Pittsburgh)
* Jets could be 1-3 right now
* Said after the Jets – Cowboys debacle that I would have traded Tony Romo Monday morning – he’s not a winner (might be tough – but that’s different)
* Wow Lions!!
* The victims of the Patriots three Super Bowl victories (the Rams, Eagles and Panthers) have a combined record this year of 2-10

5. Around College Football:

* Now that season is starting to play itself out – I will renew my lament about lack of playoffs (it would be the biggest money maker in sports history – except for those bowl directors – someone should follow the money)
* Its a three-team race now: Alabama, Oklahoma and Wisconsin
* Diamond Nuts, Carquest and other bad bowls – Boston College won’t be bringing their 850 fans to their bowl this year
* Russell Wilson keeps his lead for the Heisman
* An update on the stat all of College Football is talking about – Baylor QB Robert Griffin III this year so far: Incompletions 20 / Touchdowns 18
* Navy had 105 plays to Air Force’s 51 – and lost 35-34 on a missed extra point after a bogus taunting penalty on Navy

6. For you Bruins fans:

In the season after they won a Stanley Cup:

  • 1930: 38-5-1, lost in finals
  • 1940: 31-12-5, lost in semifinals
  • 1942: 25-17-6, lost in semifinals
  • 1971: 57-14-7, lost in quarterfinals
  • 1973: 51-22-5, lost in quarterfinals

7. Amazing Stats:

Jason Campbell is the first quarterback the Patriots have held for a QB rating below their career number over their last six games:

Quarterback Career QB Rating QB Rating vs. Pats
Tyler Thigpen 73.7 91.2 (Week 17, 2010)
Mark Sanchez 72.5 127.3 (Div. Round, 2010)
Chad Henne 76.0 96.6 (Week 1, 2011)
Philip Rivers 96.4 97.7 (Week 2, 2011)
Ryan Fitzpatrick 75.6 92.6 (Week 3, 2011)
Jason Campbell 83.1 79.4 (Week 4, 2011)

8. Old School:

In 1985, when the Patriots went to the Super Bowl – Pats QB’s threw 20 touchdowns to 22 interceptions:

  • Tony Eason – 11 TD to 17 Int
  • Steve Grogan – 7 TD to 5 Int
  • Craig James – 2 TD to 0 Int

9. Just a Little Bit Outside:

John Lackey and Josh Beckett working their way to the clubhouse on an off day for their starts.

10. Randomocity

* I like the term for Providence Bruins – “Wanna B’s” I read in Kevin Paul Dupont’s column
* Brockton High Football 0-3 – wow
* Still can’t watch Ultimate Fighting – too brutal
* From CNN – three things that are being stolen during a tough economy: Pigs – over a 1,000 this year in Minnesota and Iowa / Dogs / Brass from fire Hydrants
* Credit to John Tomase for breaking the pitchers drinking in the locker room story
* Interesting talking to a policeman this week to hear that most custody drop offs are now being done in police parking lots
* Walter Payton book much like Joe DiMaggio biographies – destroying heroes? Or fair journalism?

* Don’t forget to look for the Top Ten every Monday and Friday and to take a look at the Great Book of Boston Sports Lists now on sale (Book Signing coming up October 13th at the CBS Sports Scene 7-9)
