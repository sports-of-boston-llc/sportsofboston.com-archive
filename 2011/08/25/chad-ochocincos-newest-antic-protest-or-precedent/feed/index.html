Yes, but I Ochicinco seems like a sweet, generous guy. The offer to move in with a fan suggests an unspoiled guy who doesn’t mind where he lays his head (so long as he has an XBox nearby). There’s something impressively egalitarian and easygoing about this gesture, no? And his willingness to pay the fine for another guy? Yes, I suppose you could interpret this as challenging the NFL’s status quo/rules. But again it seems like a generous thing to do.

In an age when a lot of NFL players are out engaging in morally dubious activities–sexually harassing women, engaging in cruelly aggressive and under-handed tactics on the field, purchasing insanely oversized and ostentatious homes in a time when others are struggling to get by–I find Ochocinco a refreshingly sweet, magnanimous fellow. I wish more players were like him.
