Four days to go to the 5th Annual Blanket Dinner – Need your Help – Please come and support the charity (100% of proceeds donated to 7 shelters), speaker Sean McDonough, three-course meal, band, auction items (Baseball Hall of Fame private tour / Run with Bill Rodgers / Sports Tickets / Hotels in DC, NY, Boston)

 1. Giants stuff:

* Head-to-Head, Patriots are 5-4 against the Giants (one of those losses was big)
* Giants running backs that have run for at least 1,200 yards in a season: Tiki Barber (5), Joe Morris (2), Ahmad Bradshaw
* Giants wide receivers that have received at least 1,200 yards in a season: Amani Toomer, Steve Smith, Plaxico Burress, Homer Jones
* During their 2007 run to the Super Bowl, their margin of victories  in the postseason: 10 / 4 / 3 / 3
* Top three picks in the draft: 1994 – Carl Banks #3 /  1981 – Lawrence Taylor #2 / 1974 – John Hicks #3 / 1951 – Kyle Rote #1 / 1948 – Skippy Minisi – #2 / 1965 – Tucker Frederickson  #1

3. Bruins Stanley Cup Hangover?

How many days was it from each team’s last championship to the following year’s first game?

* Bruins win Stanley Cup June 15, 2011, first game October 6, 2011 — 113 days
* Celtics win championship June 17, 2008, first game October 28, 2008 — 133 days
* Red Sox win World Series on October 28, 2007, first game March 25, 2008 — 149 days
* Patriots win Super Bowl on February 6, 2005m first game September 8, 2005– 214 days

4. Compensation Picks:

With the Red Sox letting Jonathan Papelbon and David Ortiz venture into free agency, which could net them four first round picks in exchange, what do the other local teams have in additional picks?

* Patriots – New Orleans Saints first pick in 2012 / Raiders second round pick / Eagles 7th round pick
* Celtics –  Clippers 2012 first round pick (top ten protected until 2015) / Celtics have Minnesota Timberwolves 2013 2nd round pick / Celtics have New Jersey Nets 2014 second round pick / Celtics have Sacramento Kings 2015 second round pick / Celtics have Sacramento Kings 2017 second round pick
* Bruins have traded their 2nd round pick and 4th round pick

5. Current players from each team that will be recognized after their careers:

League Hall of Fame: Patriots: Tom Brady / Celtics: Ray Allen, Kevin Garnett, Paul Pierce / Red Sox: None / Bruins: Zdeno Chara, Tim Thomas

Number Retired: Patriots: Tom Brady / Celtics: Paul Pierce / Bruins: Zdeno Chara / Red Sox: None

Team Hall of Fame: Patriots: Tom Brady, Kevin Faulk, Matt Light, Wes Welker / Celtics: Paul Pierce / Bruins: Zdeno Chara, Tim Thomas / Red Sox: Dustin Pedroia, Jason Varitek, Tim Wakefield

6. With Dice K coming back from surgery and Scott Boras as his agent, look for the “Maestro of Gyro Ball” to pull a “Roger Clemens August and September” as the Rocket-man did in his contract year of 1996 – hmmmm:

* On August 1st Clemens was 4-11 with a 4.36 ERA
* From August 1st to September 18 – 6-1 with 1.90 ERA

Wayne Gretzky: Traded fromEdmontonto Los Angeles Kings for Jimmy Carson, Martin Gelinas, three first round picks and cash.

Wayne Gretzky: Traded by Los Angeles to St. Louis for Craig Johnson, Patrice Tardif, Roman Vopat

Wilt Chamberlain: Traded from San Francisco Warriors to Philadelphia76ers for Connie Dierking, Paul Neumann, Lee Shaffer and cash

Wilt Chamberlain: Traded from 76ers to Los Angeles Lakers for Jerry Chambers, Archie Clark and Darrall Imhoff

Dave Cowens: Traded from the Boston Celtics to the Milwaukee Bucks for Quinn Buckner

Willie Mays: Traded from San Francisco Giants to New York Mets for Charlie Williams and cash

8. Old School:

Track and Field a dying but underrated sport:

* Most exciting track and field star in the sport’s history – Steve Prefontaine (cover of Sports Illustrated / Two movies made on his life)
* Most Sports Illustrated covers Carl Lewis – 6
* Most exciting events: the mile followed by relays
* Most exciting youtube of track and field: Dave Wottle’s comeback run – keywords “1972 Olympic 800 Final”
* Best venue to watch an event Hayward Field in Oregon

 9. Just a Little Bit Outside:

After watching another amazing 30 on 30 – this one on Chris Herren and his drug addiction (you forget how good he was) I then read the following head lines:

* Bubba Smith dies of diet pill overdose
* Democrats urge baseball to implement HGH tests
* Crowell (Georgiarunning back) suspended over failed drug test
* Disgraced NACAR driver (Mayfield) in bigger trouble this time

 10. Randomocity:

* Speaking of Chris Herren – his high school coach Skip Karam did his top Massachusettshigh school basketball players lists for the book the Great Book of Boston Sports Lists
* Amazing no one took a flyer on Tiki Barber
* 1980 superstars Tom Hanks, Harrison Ford, Tom Cruise are slumping badly
*Boston College football coach Frank Spaziani will be the third high profile coach fired around here in two months
* LSU–Alabama tomorrow night – I’ll go withAlabama but the Mad-hatter has a crazy way to pull these games out (he’ll do some crazy fake punt or field goal)
* My upset pick this weekend –Iowa over Michigan

Holidays around the corner – don’t forget the Great Book of Boston Sports Lists – fits in stockings or next to dreidel
