I’d love Maholm on my real-life baseball team. He’s a lefty, he’s young, and he’s pretty talented.

But in the fantasy world, I’m not that interested due to his low k-rate. If a pitcher like Randy Johnson or Edinson Volquez struggles, at least they get K’s in a loss. Maholm, if he struggles, will hurt you in ALL categories.

And, K’s are a good indication of the pitcher doing all the work to get the out. I don’t know the specific numbers, but I do know that being a contact pitcher means you need more “good luck” than a strikeout pitcher.

In most leagues, quality starts don’t count as a single stat. QS’s are a little flawed anyway. I’d much rather have 9 IP, 4 ER, 10 Ks (not a QS) than 6 IP with 3 ER and 3 Ks (a quality start). I know it’s an extreme example, but I’m just sayin.

Either way, I agree Maholm definitely is worth a pick up, just make sure you’re dropping someone like Reed Johnson and not someone like Jonathan Sanchez (high K-rate).
