If the Pats were going for it, they should have lined up without a huddle and quickly ran the play, catching the Colts by surprise. Instead, they called a timeout and gave the Colts a chance to prepare. At that point, having surrendered the tactical advantage, the Pats should have just punted.

I think if you re-did the “statistical analysis” and accounted for how the play was run–by surprise or telegraphed to the defense–you would find that going for it only makes sense in the former situation, not the latter.
