Premier League Weekly: Liverpool Flops, Arsenal Rampant

By Jeff Christenbury in Featured, Soccer ⋅ August 17, 2009 at 12:27pm ⋅ Post a comment

This is the first in a weekly installment of the week that was in the English Premiership. What’s this have to do with Boston sports? Nothing really in particular, but we will be including information/reviews on Americans in action.

After a few months of World Cup qualifiers and international friendlies, the world’s most popular soccer league finally kicked off it’s new season this past weekend. The first match day always brings out the hopes and dreams of the newly promoted teams and those vying for European places and the EPL title. However, as we saw on Sunday afternoon, the marathon of a title-winning season can nearly be derailed in the first week.

The biggest result of the weekend came yesterday afternoon at White Hart Lane in London as Tottenham Hotspur used a debut goal by former Newcastle man Sebastien Bassong to defeat Liverpool, 2-1. Near the end of a first half mostly dominated by Spurs they took a deserved 1-0 lead in the 43rd minute thanks to an unstoppable strike from outside the box by Benoit Assou-Ekotto – his first ever goal for Tottenham. Liverpool equalized in the 55th minute thanks to a Glen Johnson run where he drew a penalty and “Captain Fantastic” Steven Gerrard converted to make it 1-1. But just three minutes later off a free kick Bassong outmatched both Johnson and Jamie Carragher to score a beauty header to give Tottenham the lead for good. Liverpool did have a legitimate shout for a penalty late on as Andriy Voronin (who looks remarkably like James Bond actor Daniel Craig) was knocked to the ground in the box, but the referee didn’t think it warranted another spot kick. Liverpool – who lost just two matches in all of last season – were outclassed for much of the match and now face an uphill battle for their 19th league title.

The other major outcome of the weekend took place at Goodison Park, home of Liverpool’s rivals Everton as they went out and were thrashed 6-1 by Arsenal. The Gunners scored three times in the first half and never looked back as captain Cesc Fabregas netted a brace (soccer lingo for two goals in one game) and sent Everton (along with helpless American goalkeeper Tim Howard) to an embarrassing defeat on opening day.

Five Things We Learned

  1. As Wayne Rooney goes, so will go Manchester United. The three time defending champions are without Cristiano Ronaldo and Carlos Tevez this year and will be relying on Rooney for goals and attacking flow. His winner on Sunday against newly promoted Birmingham City shows how important his influence will be. One would have thought, however, that Man United would have more easily handled a side which was coming into the Calderon of Old Trafford on the first week.
  2. Liverpool have a lot of work to do. Though they were beaten by two fantastic goals and probably should’ve received another penalty, Liverpool were outplayed thoroughly for most of the game at Spurs. Squad depth is an issue for the Reds and despite strong debuts by fullbacks Glen Johnson and Emiliano Insua, the ineffective play of Ryan Babel and Lucas hurt the squad going forward. Don’t be surprised to see Yossi Benayoun starting in attack for the next few games.
  3. Home sides didn’t fare well on opening day. On Saturday just two teams won at home – Chelsea and Stoke City – and on the weekend six road teams took all three points away from home. Also, there wasn’t a single draw in the EPL this weekend. Home sides were outscored a combined 15-5 on Saturday.
  4. EPL popularity in the US will grow thanks to ESPN. In a sort of an under the radar move, ESPN2 will now be showing live EPL games on Saturday mornings and some Mondays. Though it’s no secret ESPN picked up the rights to broadcast games in the U.K. thanks to Setanta Sports going bankrupt, they’ve now reached an agreement with Fox Soccer Channel to air a select number of early kickoffs and Monday games. On Saturday, Chelsea and Hull was broadcast live on ESPN2 at 7:45am. Not only will this expose the EPL to more US viewers but ESPN – who has rights to the World Cup next summer – will probably make more of an effort to promote the sport, as we saw on Sunday when Didier Drogba’s wonder goal against Hull was No. 1 on Sportcent’s Top Plays. Seeing it in HD was pretty amazing as well.
  5. It could be a long season for the new boys. The excitement of being promoted to the Premiership is very tough to quantify to the average American fan. Imagine you cheer for no sports team except the Pawtucket Red Sox, and one year they are so good that they earn a spot in Major League Baseball. However as Burnley, Wolverhampton and Birmingham found out on the first weekend, it can be a tough, long road to stay in the top English division. Losses to Stoke City, West Ham and Manchester United respectively aren’t horrific results, but the zero goals combined between the three teams is disconcerting going foward.

Three Up

  1. Didier Drogba: The Ivory Coast striker, who has been linked to a move to Milan for a few years, came through in a big way Saturday by scoring an injury time goal against Hull which broke a 1-1 tie. He lofted a ridiculous floating shot over the keeper to give Chelsea all three points at Stamford Bridge.
  2. Arsene Wenger: The Arsenal gaffer took a lot of heat for selling off Emmanuel Adebayor and Kolo Toure to Manchester City in the summer but his young team responded with a real tonking of Everton over the weekend. It’s been a long four years without a trophy for the Gunners but Wenger, for the time being, seems to have things running smoothly at the Emirates.
  3. Wigan Athletic: There’s been much talk around the EPL that Aston Villa has as good a shot as any team to jump into the top four this season, but Wigan really took it to them at Villa Park in their 2-0 victory. Wigan controlled the midfield and really seemed to have another gear while Villa looked as though they felt they were entitled to three points. Wigan will still probably finish well in the bottom half but showed they can beat a quality team on a given day.

Three Down

  1. David Moyes: The Everton manager has been applauded the last few seasons for having success with little funding on Merseyside. But this saga with the on again, off again transfer of Jolean Lescott obviously isn’t doing the team any good. The 6-1 defeat aside, Moyes has seemingly lost a grip on his team and the fan base.
  2. Phil Dowd: The referee for Liverpool-Tottenham didn’t have his best game. Not only did he deny Liverpool a pretty obvious penalty in the second half but his card-giving was inconsistent throughout the match. Both sides had challenges which warranted cards but went unpunished yet were awarded cards for much less at other moments. It’s early in the season for the officials as well but there is much to be improved.
  3. Fox Soccer Channel: I’m a big fan of FSC but can someone please tell me why A) they are not in high definition yet and B) why they have the same lame music/graphics package from two years ago. With ESPN now showing some games in HD, its time for FSC to step up to the plate (disclaimer, FSC is apparently going HD in December but I’ll believe it when I see it). The pre-match show featuring Nick Webster is also unchanged from last year, and he’s better doing the off-the-cuff Fox Football Fone-in than feeding softball questions to some no name commentator.

How Americans Fared

  • Tim Howard (Everton) – Tough game for the American ‘keeper and he was hit for six goals allowed. Everton’s defense left several players unmarked in the first half which led to three goals and it was all downhill from there. Grade: C
  • Clint Dempsey (Fulham) – A solid effort from Dempsey, who did not play well against Mexico last week. His long range shot took a wicked deflection off Bobby Zamora and went in for the game’s only goal. Good win for Fulham at Portsmouth, a team they should beat. Grade: B+
  • Jonathan Spector (West Ham) – The fullback came on as a second half substitute and helped West Ham keep a clean sheet in their 2-0 win over Wolves. Spector may have solidified himself in the US starting XI simply by letting Steve Cherundolo go out and have a poor game against Mexico. Grade: B
  • Jozy Altidore (Hull City) – Did not play against Chelsea because of a work permit issue. Hopefully it will be resolved by Tuesday so he can play, because Hull certainly need some attacking options. Grade: INC

Lots of action coming in midweek in the EPL, stay tuned!
