The Celtics dropped a tough one to the Nets in Brooklyn last night, 102-97. Paul Pierce, despite an efficient 22 points on 8-for-12 shooting, clanked two free-throws late in the fourth quarter that basically sealed the game for the Nets.

Rajon Rondo missed the game due to a sprained ankle and Leandro Barbossa got the start in his place. Barbossa delivered 17 points and 2 steals in a team-high 30 minutes of game time.

Celtics Win Third in a Row Despite Rondo Injury

November 15, 2012 at 4:31pm in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Jeff Miller writes
Add a comment

The Boston Celtics won their third straight, and fifth in six games, with a 98-93 win against the Utah Jazz at the TD Garden. Paul Pierce led the way for the C’s with a game-high 23 points, 15 of them in the 3rd quarter, and three field goals from beyond the arc.

Celtics Bench Stepped Up

Jeff Green and Leandro Barbosa each had 16 points off the bench and went a combined 11-of-17 from the field. With just over seven minutes left in the game, Green made a play for the highlight reel. After a pump fake in the corner, Green drove to the basket and posterized former Celtic Al Jefferson with a one-handed slam that erupted the Garden and even had Rondo jumping for joy (on one leg). Green picked up a technical foul for staring down Big Al, but it was a much deserved stare-down. Chris Wilcox chipped in 7 points and 5 rebounds in huge minutes for the Celtics. Posterized: Jeff Green Throws Down on Ex-Celtic Al Jefferson

November 14, 2012 at 10:31pm in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Nick Bohlen writes
One comment

With just over seven minutes left in the fourth quarter of Wednesday night’s game against the Utah Jazz, Jeff Green finally showed off the side Celtics fans have all been waiting for – the aggressive, angry, in-your-face Jeff Green from the preseason.

After a pumpfake from the corner, Green drove to his right, rose up in the paint, and absolutely owned former Celtic Al Jefferson with a vicious one-handed slam. In fact, he threw it down with such authority that even Green himself couldn’t help but stare down Jefferson, breaking away from his normally impassive, almost timid nature. It earned him a taunting violation, but given Green’s struggles so far this season, no one seemed to mind him showing off a little (much deserved) swagger.
