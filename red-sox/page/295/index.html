2010 MLB Regular Season Expert Picks

April 4, 2010 at 9:36pm in Featured, Now!, Opinion, Red Sox
John writes
Add a comment

With baseball season here at last, it’s time for SoB’s annual MLB Pick ‘Em. After the Yankees won the World Series last year for the first time since 2000 and made it for the first time since 2003, and gave Dennis Leary a stroke in the process, everyone jumped on the Yankees bandwagon, including many of our experts, at least as far as predicting goes.

Seven of nine of the picks this year have the Yankees winning the American League, and for that matter, the Phillies winning the National League, making a World Series rematch the favored outcome. In fact, if both teams do end up winning their leagues, and the probabilities of each successive seed in a league making it to the World Series follow a geometric sequence with ratio 1/x, the odds of both the Phillies and Yankees making it in for a rematch is .2304.

Aside from that prediction, most of us agree that the Red Sox will be the Wildcard team once more, and most of us agree on the Pirates taking over for the Nationals in the basement of the league. The biggest disagreement is over who will win the NL Wildcard; the Braves have the mode. We’re also pretty split between the Rockies/Dodgers as to who will win that division. Red Sox Spring Training Wrap-Up (3/29 – 4/3)

April 4, 2010 at 1:37pm in Featured, Red Sox
Matt Goisman writes
Add a comment

Welcome to the final installment of Red Sox Weekly Review, covering all things Spring Training. The Red Sox had a full slate of games this week in an effort to get their starters fully stretched out and ready for Sunday night’s opener against the New York Yankees at Fenway. So let’s get right to the games.

Red Sox vs. Rays

Monday’s matchup against Tampa Bay featured a strong performance from starting pitcher Josh Beckett. He went six innings and only gave up two earned runs while striking out eight. The problem with this game wasn’t the starting pitching, it was the relief pitching and the hitting. Boston didn’t get on the scoreboard until the seventh inning, and by that point the Rays were already up 5-0. Despite 13 hits from the Red Sox, nobody had a particularly strong day. There were no home runs, and none of the starters had multi-hit games except for Marco Scutaro, who started this game despite being slated for ninth on the final roster. Compounding problems was Alan Embree, who gave up three earned runs in less than an inning of work. His ERA sits at over 40, and he clearly is not capable of pitching in the major leagues. I wonder if he ever will be again. The final score was Tampa Bay 9, Boston 3. Continue reading Red Sox Spring Training Wrap-Up (3/29 – 4/3) »

Red Sox 2010 Roster Preview: Fourth Outfielder

April 2, 2010 at 12:00pm in Featured, Red Sox
Greg writes
Add a comment

The fourth outfielder is starting to become a very popular position in the Red Sox organization, as well as the rest of the MLB, and this year is no different. Just like teams in the NHL are learning, it’s always good to have at least two capable goalies, and for baseball teams it’s the same thing. Teams like the Red Sox are starting to implement the fact that having a good backup outfielder is very important and that the player could be considered a starter at any point in the season.

Last year the Sox had Rocco Baldelli and then added Brian Anderson in a mid-season trade that sent another former starting outfielder in Mark Kotsay to the Chicago White Sox. In years past, it was very common to see just three starting outfielders and then maybe another player who could fill in if need be, but that player would be by no means a starter.

Things have changed since then, and the Red Sox continue to keep a viable starter on the bench and rotate him in on a regular basis. That player this season is Jeremy Hermida, who was a starter for the Florida Marlins since coming into the league in 2005. Another potential fourth outfielder is 23-year-old Josh Reddick, whose work ethic has brought his name into the discussion as well.    Continue reading Red Sox 2010 Roster Preview: Fourth Outfielder »
