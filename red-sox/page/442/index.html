Preview: Rays at Sox 8/14

August 13, 2007 at 10:54pm in Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment

Fenway will erupt on Tuesday when players are announced. Not because it’s Dice-K, Beckett, or Schilling running out from the bullpen but because John Lester has finally come home. His first four starts have all been on the road. When he returns to Fenway it will be the first start Lester has had since recovering from cancer.

Lester has struggled so far accumulating a 6.43 ERA. Although all of Fenway will be behind him, he’ll have a battle on Tuesday against Tampa.
Game 1 was a 3-0 shutout for the Sox. But Sox batters were held to one run in six innings by the Tampa Bay second best starter James Shields. Game 2 will feature Tampa’s best pitcher, Scott Kazmir. Kazmir has always been trouble for the Red Sox. In 13 starts Kazmirs had a 2.55 ERA. Even worse for the fans who wish Lester to return home with a win, is Kazmir since the All-Star Break is 4-1 with a 1.16 ERA.

All the peripherals of the pitching show Lester will have a tough time outperforming Kazmir. It wouldn’t be a magical moment unless there was some tough adversity. Depending on how Lester does, Tuesday could be one of those games you remember a long time after its done.

Notes:

– The hitter with the best success against Kazmir (min 10 AB) is Mike Lowell with a .286/.412/.500

– Kazmir’s last start against BOS was 6ip, 8k’s, 0 ER. Lester’s last start against TAM (7/28) was 6.2 IP, 3K’s, 4 ER.

– Coco may still have a virus and miss tuesdays game

– –In 1978 the Yankees were 7 1/2 games out at this time

Red Sox Recap 8/13 v. Rays

August 13, 2007 at 10:20pm in Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment

Going into Monday’s game it looked like the Sox needed an offensive pick me up. But when the lineups came out 3 regulars were out. Varitek to be expected in a Wakefield start but also Hinske in RF and Cora at 2B. But offense did matter much today behind the arm of Tim Wakefield. Wakefield held Tampa hitless into the 7th inning when Carl Crawford ended the bid on a hit to right.Tim’s final line was 8 innings, 2 hits, 2 walks, no runs allowed and 6 K’s. Arguably his best performance of the year. The other 2 stars of the game were Julio Lugo and Tampa Bay’s bullpen.Julio Lugo went 3-4 with a 2B, R and RBI. Improving his avg to .238. In 31 games Lugo’s average has climbed from .189, the lowest it was this season, to 49 points higher. It was also Lugo’s 6th multi-hit game this month in 11 games.Tampa’s bullpen in 2 innings allowed 2 runs, in the 7th and 8th. Giving Boston a buffer to allow for a smoother situation in the 9th where Jonathon Papelbon closed his 28th game of the season.

Notes:
– Wakefield improved to 18-2 vs Tampa Bay lifetime
– The Yankees remain 4 games back from the division lead

AL East wrapped up? Not so fast…

August 12, 2007 at 10:49pm in Opinion, Red Sox
Dev writes
Add a comment

WHAT is goin on with the Sox? Everyone in Red Sox Nation was told by various sources (coughESPNcough) that the AL East race was over in May. At that point, the Red Sox had a 14.5 game lead on the Yankees. In fact, the Bronx Bombers were in a tight race for last place with the perennial AL East loser, the Tampa Bay Devil Rays.

But now in the middle of the dog days of August, we find that our beloved Red Sox have lost four of their last six games and now the Evil Empire has climbed within four games of the Sox. What gives? Even more disheartening, the last two losses came at the hands of our once lights-out bullpen. The Okie-Doke of Hideki Okajima has begun to hang in the strike zone and our prized trade deadline acquisition, Eric Gagne has blown up. Multiple times.
