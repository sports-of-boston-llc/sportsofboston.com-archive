Red Sox Visit White House

February 27, 2008 at 6:58pm in News, Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment
David Ortiz holding the World Series trophy next to President George Bush and Vice President Dick Cheney (Courtesy of MLB.com)

The Red Sox finally got congratulated today for winning the World Series. Of course its happening right before the beginning of the next season, but whatever. Tradition is tradition.

A large showing of Sox fans showed up to watch the ceremonies on the South lawn.

Manager Terry Francona, players, coaches and staff were accompanied by principal owner John Henry, chairman Tom Werner, president/CEO Larry Lucchino and members of the Red Sox’s ownership group and front office.

Everyone had to take a short break from work to be in Washington but it was worth it.

One of the few not to go was Manny. Bush noticed Manny was absent and joked, “Manny Ramirez isn’t here, I guess his grandmother died again.”

Bush had another good joke talking to Dice-K, “I’d like to welcome Dice-K to the White House South Lawn,” Bush said of Daisuke Matsuzaka. “His press corps is bigger than mine.”

Captain Jason Varitek had the honor of presenting President Bush with a Red Sox jersey that read “Bush 07” on the back.

All and all it was a good time to revel in 2007’s World Series success and also a good time to finally bury that prior success and now focus fully on this year.

Sox and Bartolo Colon agree to Minor League Deal

February 24, 2008 at 5:36pm in News, Red Sox
Dev writes
Add a comment

ESPN.com reports that the Red Sox reached an agreement on a minor league contract with the former 2005 Cy Young Award winner.

This could be a good thing for Boston, now without Curt Schilling, the club could use the insurance.

Bartolo had an awful 2007 for the Angels, putting up a 6-8 record and a 6.34 ERA, a career worst. He is looking to re-establish his value to the league.

Many of us forget how good he used to be, but “used to be” is the key phrase. Can he rebound to be anything close to his Cy Young form?

February 24, 2008 at 12:31pm in Red Sox
Jeff Christenbury writes
One comment

The Red Sox and manager Terry Francona have finally agreed on a contract extension which will keep Tito as the Sox manager until 2011. The deal also has two options for 2012 and 2013.

From this morning’s press release:

“On behalf of John Henry, Tom Werner, Larry Lucchino, and the Red Sox organization, I am very pleased to make this announcement,” GM Theo Epstein said in the release. “Terry Francona has done an outstanding job in managing the Red Sox over the last four seasons. This is demonstrated not only in the three playoff appearances and two World Series victories but also in the way in which he handles the demands of managing in Boston on a day-to-day basis, both on and off the field. It also represents a strong and sincere commitment by our ownership.”

Red Sox-Athletics Japan Opening Series Sold Out

February 21, 2008 at 7:28pm in Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment

Tickets for the March 25-26 games at the 55,000-seat Tokyo Dome have just sold out. The Boston Red Sox and Oakland Athletics will begin their season there before heading back to the states to finish up the preseason.

The only native Japanese player who looks to have a chance to play out there is Hideki Okajima. Dice-K’s wife will be giving birth around that time and Dice refuses to leave her or make her travel around that time.

It’ll be very exciting to see a whole new audience and stadium. Having spent my whole life watching the same stadiums over and over its fun to see a change. The casual fan might not appreciate it as much as one whose seen it all. It’ll be interesting to see how receptive Japanese fans are.

If you’re still looking to find Red Sox tickets in Japan, they can still be found. On March 23rd the Red Sox will be playing a preseason game against the Yomiuri Giants.

Coco Crisp or Jacoby Ellsbury

  • Terry Francona has said Coco Crisp is the incumbent, meaning Jacoby Ellsbury still needs to win the job. Baseball: Pick-Ups and Drop-Offs

February 20, 2008 at 11:46am in Fantasy, Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment

A short segment on fantasy baseball players to look for, either during your draft or in the current free agent pool. Depending on your own league settings, you might want to look deeper into the stats and see what secret sexy baseball Casanova you can find. Don’t get too charmed by speed: a guy who just steals, but still sucks (i.e. Julio Lugo), can ruin your team in other ways.

Keep your eyes on these guys…

Pick-Ups (Try to get them)

  • Josh Hamilton, Tex (CF/RF)
    • Josh is sexy in all the right ways. He plays CF in Texas and is a former #1 overall draft pick with power. He is very undervalued in most drafts. With the most recent release from Texas being they won’t platoon him against lefties, a full-time job for Hamilton along with a healthy year could mean a major All-Star and diamond in the rough.
  • Joey Votto, Cin, 1B/LF
    • If you believe Cincinnati and Votto’s body will allow him to play +150 games, then he’s a steal going in the 14th round or later. At +150 games you could have a 100 R, 100 RBI, 28-32 HR guy. To be more conservative I’d say expect 90 R, 90 RBI and 27 HRs. If you avoided getting a 1st baseman or Util guy till late, Votto is your man.
  • J.R. Towles, Hou, C
    • Looking for a starting catcher? But its the 15th round? Well you obviously waited too long to get a guaranteed guy. But if you want to take a shot at getting one late anyway Towles could be it. Towles will have the full time catching job in Houston and has had a decent hitting career in the minors. If his bat continues to progress he could be a better than average catcher.
