Red Sox Won’t Raise 2013 Ticket Prices, Avoid PR Disaster (For Once)

October 16, 2012 at 9:40pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Nick Bohlen writes
Add a comment

In their first positive public relations move in recent memory, the Boston Red Sox have decided NOT to raise ticket prices for the 2013 season.

After all, it’s not like they could slip the media some “anonymous” story about ticket holders having a prescription pill problem to justify a price hike. And sadly, they had already used up the fried chicken and beer explanation that would have worked perfectly for bloated ticket costs.

The decision comes in the wake of the team’s last place finish in the AL East, with its worst record since the 1965 season (two seasons before the Impossible Dream! Hope abounds for…2014!). And that doesn’t even address the quarter of a billion dollars in payroll the Red Sox managed to pawn off on the Los Angeles Dodgers in August’s blockbluster trade. 2012 MLB Playoffs Expert Picks: League Championship Series

John writes
Add a comment

Now that the League Championship Series are about to commence with the Tigers invading New York, it’s time to present this year’s selections for the LCS Round of the MLB Pick ‘Em. This year’s match-ups of the Tigers/Cardinals and Giants/Cardinals is creating some interesting divides.

There’s not much of a consensus on winners, with each series being picked 3-2. The idea that does jump out is that all but one selection on both series combined was for a series length of at least six games. And interestingly enough, everyone who picked a pitcher to win series MVP also picked that series to go exactly six games.

Speaking of which, the scoring category of MVP for each series has been introduced for this round. It will be worth one points, as will the series length, while picking the winner of a series is still worth two points.

So while you’re enjoying mid-October baseball at its finest, be sure to follow along below to see how your favorite expert is doing with their picks.
