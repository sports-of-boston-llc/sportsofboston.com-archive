Manny Ramirez Fined $10,000-$15,000 for Shoving Incident

July 19, 2008 at 5:17pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
One comment

The Boston Globe’s Gordon Edes confirmed with his sources within the Red Sox organization that Manny Ramirez was fined between $10,000 and $15,000 (though closer to $10k) for shoving the Red Sox traveling secretary instead of the six-figure estimate reported on the radio.

Everyone involved (Manny, the Red Sox, Scott Boras, and the MLBPA) signed off on the fine without filing a grievance and his money is reportedly going to charity.

Ortiz Homers in Rehab Start

July 17, 2008 at 10:14pm in Red Sox
Boston Bot writes
Add a comment

David Ortiz homered in his rehab debut at Triple-A Pawtucket tonight as he continues to work his way back from a wrist injury that has kept him out of the lineup since May 31.

The crowd began chanting “Papi!” and Ortiz responded, slamming a 1-2 pitch into the visitors’ bullpen. Ortiz popped up to shortstop in his first at-bat.

Oritz is expected to return to be back in the Red Sox lineup by July 25, just in time for the opener of a weekend series against our beloved New York Yankees.

Yesterday, we featured the changes to the team from last year’s championship club. Today, we will look at how the team has performed in each aspect of the game and name the first half MVP.

Jon Lester appeared to prove himself worthy of a spot and threw a no-hitter to solidify himself in that role.
