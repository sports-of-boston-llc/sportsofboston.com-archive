Sox Beat Twins to Win Mayor’s Cup

March 12, 2008 at 5:50pm in Red Sox
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

The Red Sox won the fifth and decisive game against the Minnesota Twins this Spring, taking the annual Mayor’s Cup 12-7. The catchers led the way in the win. Sox captain Jason Varitek started and went 2-2 with a HR, 2 RBI and a walk. Backup Kevin Cash subbed in for Tek and hit a 3-run HR in the 7th inning to break open the game.

Rookie Clay Buchholz started and went 3 2/3 innings, allowing two runs on three hits while allowing one walk. Mike Timlin also pitched, giving up a solo shot to Joe Mauer.

Lefty Francisco Liriano started for the Twins, going 2+ innings and allowing 3 runs on 4 hits. Of his 53 pitches, only 28 were strikes. He is still recovering from left elbow surgery (his pitching arm), which knocked him out for the entire 2007 season.

Sox slugger Manny Ramirez went 2-2 with a walk and an RBI double, while DH David Ortiz went 2-3 with an RBI.

Tags: Clay Buchholz, Francisco Liriano, Jason Varitek, Kevin Cash, Minnesota Twins

Papelbon Adds Slider To Arsenal

March 12, 2008 at 3:51am in Red Sox
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Red Sox closer Jonathan Papelbon has added a slider to his pitching arsenal, which should be most effective against left-handed hitters. He used the pitch as a starter, but as a closer he became a two-pitch pitcher with a sharp fastball and a falling splitter.

Papelbon, on throwing a slider:

“I used to throw it when I was starting so it’s not like a new pitch, really. It’s a natural arm slot for me to throw this pitch. It’s not like I’m going out of my delivery to execute this pitch. To me, it’s just a matter of staying one step ahead of the competition.”

The right-hander, who saved 37 games in 40 chances last year including three saves in the World Series, is trying to avoid scenarios where a left-handed hitter continues to watch or foul off his splitter, just waiting for the fastball. Bringing in a slider, which looks like a fastball, would keep certain hitters guessing.

“The slider looks like a heater and then it moves down in on the back foot (to lefties). There’s so many situations (last year) where I’m out there and I’m saying, ‘God, if I had the slider, he’d be done.'”

Beckett Likely Out For Opening Day

March 12, 2008 at 2:14am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Red Sox ace Josh Beckett is now likely out for Opening Day with the back spasms. He’s gone from feeling good the day after the injury, to feeling “crappy” the next, and just O.K. the third. All signs point to no as far as flying to Japan with a bad back.

His “crappy” day is what had him and Red Sox brass worried. Without Beckett for an extended period of time, the Red Sox are a considerably weaker team. The team calls his injury “discomfort” in the lower back, and will take all precautions to ensure a healthy recovery.

Terry Francona told ESPN.com that no final decision had been made, but it makes sense to wait until April 1 or 2 in Oakland or April 4-6 in Toronto.

Said Francona:

“I don’t think we know [for sure] yet. But we’re certainly not going to rush him back to pitch a game March 24 if he’s not ready.”

Josh Beckett Injury Update: Back Spasms

March 9, 2008 at 9:17am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Red Sox ace Josh Beckett left yesterday’s Spring Training game before it started with back spasms. He reportedly tweaked his back on his first warm-up toss when he slipped on some mound dirt that gave way.

Beckett didn’t speak to the media, but according to Boston Globe Sox Blogger Amalie Benjamin, he communicated a message through a team spokesman: “We’ll just have to wait and see how it feels tomorrow.”

He will undergo some more testing today and could miss his next spring start. Sox medical staff will try to determine the severity of the injury.

Papelbon Thinks He Set Precedent for Closers

March 7, 2008 at 9:48pm in Red Sox
KC Downey writes
One comment

A day after signing a one-year, $775,000 deal, Jonathan Papelbon said that he’s proud he was able to set a precedent for young closers not yet ready for arbitration.

“I’m pleased because I feel like I was able to somewhat set a precedent for pitchers to come. That was definitely one of my goals. I felt like the Red Sox were on board with me to do that. Now it’s something I don’t have to worry about and we can just move forward.

“I feel like the Red Sox were fair and I felt like I was fair.”

Reaction: I understand wanting a pay raise, but what is the deal with “setting a precedent” for future young closers of the game. Is he trying to say the position is undervalued? I’m not sure what he’s getting at. I think he should be pleased the Red Sox were willing to give him a pretty good raise when he had no right to get one, and not pleased he forced the Sox hands to set a precedent for closers of the future.

Dice-K Likely to Join Sox in Tokyo

March 7, 2008 at 9:40pm in Red Sox
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

When the Red Sox travel to Japan in late March, native pitcher Daisuke Matsuzaka will likely follow the team to Tokyo, and may start in one of the two opening games against the A’s.

Initially, it had been reported that Dice-K’s baby was due at around the time the Sox would travel to Japan. Thus, Dice-K wasn’t sure if he’d be able to join his team’s visit to his own country.

Now, his wife’s due date is March 19, which is the same day as the team’s flight to Japan. He may catch a later flight if his baby is due on time. The Sox will play exhibition games against Japanese clubs on March 22 and 23, and will play their first regular season game on March 25. So, it’s possible that Dice-K could join the team for the March 25 or 26 game.

Red Sox Sign Papelbon

March 6, 2008 at 8:20pm in Red Sox
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

The Red Sox and closer Jonathan Papelbon agreed on a 1-year, $775,000 contract. Papelbon earned $425,550 last season while earning 37 saves and 1.85 ERA. So if I do the math, Paps will be earning almost twice as much as he did last year.

Papelbon wanted a new multi-year deal, but he does not have any leverage due to his small amount of ML service. He’ll begin to get some leverage after the 2008 season when he enters his arbitration years.

At 27, the best is yet to come for one of the best closers in baseball.

Also today, the Sox agreed to 1-year deals with 18 other players including Jacoby Ellsbury, Dustin Pedroia, Clay Buchholz, and Jon Lester. With the singings, the Sox filled out their 40-man roster.
