2 comments

Depression to Blame for Remy’s Extended Absence

According to the Boston Globe, Jerry Remy will appear in the Red Sox booth Wednesday night for an inning, a sign that he could be returning to the booth permanently in the near future. He was at Fenway Park attending Terry Francona’s press conference Wednesday, the first time Remy visited the park since his illness.

Remy spoke to the media before the game Wednesday, and said his return was delayed while he battled depression. He told Channel 5’s Mike Lynch he doesn’t know when he’ll come back, but he expects to return this season “You battle through and you get through it and it’s nice to get back to the park where I’m supposed to be,” Remy said. Kevin Youkilis Suspended Five Games

August 12, 2009 at 4:26pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Pete Rampino writes
3 comments

Espn.com reports that Red Sox 1B/3B Kevin Youkilis has been suspended five games for his involvement in the brawl that took place on Tuesday night against the Detroit Tigers. Detroit pitcher Rick Porcello, who threw at Youkilis, was also suspended five games. Porcello has appealed the decision, but Youkilis will start serving his suspension tonight.

Detroit pitcher Edwin Jackson was fined an undisclosed amount for “aggressive actions”.

Mike Lowell looks to get the start at 3rd base with Youkilis out and Victor Martinez will hold down the fort at first base.

Kevin Youkilis Brawls with Tigers

August 11, 2009 at 8:33pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Pete Rampino writes
9 comments

Update, (8/12/09): Kevin Youkilis suspended five games.

Red Sox 1B/3B Kevin Youkilis was ejected from tonight’s game against the Tigers for charging the mound after being hit by a pitch from Detroit’s Rick Porcello. Both benches cleared and when the dust settled, Youkilis and Porcello were sent to the showers.

Porcello most likely hit Youkilis in retaliation for Sox starter Junichi Tazawa hitting Tigers’ slugger Miguel Cabrera on the hand in the first inning. Cabrera would leave the game due to injury during his next at-bat in the second inning.

Porcello tried to hit 1B Victor Martinez in the bottom of the first, but missed. Feeling like justice had not been served, he then hit Youkilis who led off the bottom of the 2nd. Hopefully this is the last batter hit in tongiht’s game.

Red Sox End Losing Streak, Beat Tigers 6-5

August 11, 2009 at 11:17am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

After scoring 18 runs last Sunday against the Orioles, the Red Sox managed just 14 runs over the next six games against the Rays and Yankees (0-6). So, after that disgusting stretch, the Sox needed just a little home cooking right? 10 pitches into their first game back home against the Tigers, Dustin Pedroia hit a two-run blast to left field to give his team a 2-0 lead.

The Sox built a 4-0 lead on a Nick Green solo shot and a David Ortiz RBI single. They needed all the runs they could get with Brad Penny on the hill, but he pitched pretty well. Penny tossed six innings and allowed three runs on six hits. He was in line for the win with the Sox clinging to a 5-3 lead after Jason Bay’s fifth inning HR.

But, Manny Delcarmen gave up the lead, allowing two runs in the seventh inning on a Placido Polanco RBI single and a Marcus Thames RBI double. The Sox didn’t wait long to respond, as Nick Green brought J.D. Drew home with a sacrifice fly in the bottom of the seventh to cap the scoring. Sox Suffer Through Worst Week of the Season

August 11, 2009 at 2:57am in Featured, Red Sox
Geoff writes
Add a comment

After a good end to the series in Baltimore last Sunday, the Red Sox had an off-day followed by a trip down to the Trop in Tampa Bay for a short two-game series with the Rays followed by a trip to New York for a longer four-game series with the Yankees over the weekend. Neither series went exactly as the Sox wanted and they suffered their worst week of the season to date by losing six games in a row to both the Rays and Yankees.

They now see themselves staring up at the first place Yankees while they sit in second place, back six and a half games before play on Monday night.

1. Bye Bye John Smoltz

The Red Sox gave up their attempt to get former All-Star John Smoltz back into form this season after he had surgery on his shoulder last season. Smoltz was designated for assignment after his terrible start against the Yankees on Thursday night. Smoltz finished the season with the Sox going 2-5 with an 8.33 ERA with 33 strikeouts.
