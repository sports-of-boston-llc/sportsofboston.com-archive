Beckett vs Wang

September 15, 2007 at 2:41pm in Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment

Well I called the first game. New York won. But Boston blowing a 7-2 lead in the 8th is more Boston losing then New York winning. And it was Boston’s two best relievers, Okajima and Papelbon who lost it. One has to start to question how reliable they are this last part of the season to give up six runs in one inning.

“It came apart in a hurry,” Red Sox manager Terry Francona said.

In baseball luckily everyone gets second chances, tonight Boston’s second chance is Beckett versus Wang. Both are going for baseballs first 19 game winner. Wang has won his last five starts. In that time he has a 2.04 ERA in that time. Beckett has been very good recently going 8-1 in his last 10 starts with a 2.66 ERA.

Game 2 Starters Record HR BB K ERA WHIP
Josh Beckett 18-6 14 36 173 3.27 1.14
Chien-Ming Wang 18-6 8 52 91 3.69 1.27

It ought to be an even match up. I give the edge to Beckett and almost Beckett alone. A long solid outing against the Yankees, one that a bullpen can’t blow. Boston’s offense is still hot and can put up some runs against Wang and the bullpen. Of course Chamberlain will be available tonight. Having yet to allowed an ER, Boston will have to put this rookie to the test.

Hot List

  • David Ortiz lifetime against Wang: 12/27, 3 2B, 2 HR, 8 RBI
  • Eric Hinske lifetime against Wang: 10/22, 4 2B, 2 HR, 3 RBI
  • Manny is 13/22 against Wang if he is available

Against Beckett Lifetime:

  • Jason Giambi: 5/13, 2 HR
  • Robinson Cano: 6/15, 3 XBH
  • Melky Cabrera: 7/19 for a .368 avg

Tags: Chien-Ming Wang, David Ortiz, Hideki Okajima, Joba Chamberlain, Jonathan Papelbon, Josh Beckett

Manny Ramirez Might be Back Friday

September 14, 2007 at 4:18pm in Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment

According to the Boston Globe Manny Ramirez took batting practice Wednesday for the first time since straining his left oblique August 28th.

He could return for the weekend series against the Yankees. This year against the Yankees Manny is hitting .392. Ellsbury in Manny’s absence has been amazing. Ellsbury is batting .373 since being called up. The Red Sox have 15 games left and Manny has missed 15 games.

Opinion: The sooner the better for Manny to return. Manny has been a lot better in the second half hitting with a line of .309/.384/.549. Red Sox nation has to hope Manny’s time off hasn’t thrown off his bat.

Yankees vs Red Sox in September Baseball

September 14, 2007 at 2:35pm in Red Sox
Dan writes
Add a comment

The last meeting the Boston Red Sox (89-58) and New York Yankees (83-63) had was in August when the Yankees swept three games from Boston in NY. Now its September and the Yankees are in Boston. Boston is 5.5 games ahead of New York for the division lead. But New York sits 3.5 games up over Detroit in the wild card standings. So this weekend isn’t much about a playoff spot, as it is about pride and confidence.

Friday September 14th, 7pm

My Prediction:

  • Red Sox take two of three. Pettitte over Dice-K, Beckett over Wang and Schilling over Clemens.

Notes:

  • The Red Sox are 7-9 in this year against the Yankees.
  • Unless the Red Sox sweep the Yankees, neither team is in risk of missing the playoffs. There are about 15 games left for the Red Sox whose magic number is 11 for the AL East. The Yankees have 16 games left and their magic number is 13 for the Wild Card.

Big Papi Hits Walk-Off 2 Run Shot!

September 12, 2007 at 10:37pm in Red Sox
Dan writes
One comment

Nothing’s better then watching David Ortiz hit the long ball. Especially twice in one night. And especially down by one in the 9th with one man on. Because David Ortiz has the magic. And tonight was no different. Ortiz faced Al Reyes who he had been 0-4 in his career against. And, with Julio Lugo on first, Papi put a ball just over the far right field fence on an inside fastball. Tonight, Ortiz was responsible for all five runs driven in. Ortiz now has 31 HRs and 105 RBIs.

Thoughts: With the playoffs only three or so weeks away it’s great to see David Ortiz hit a walk-off homerun tonight. The Red Sox need Ortiz to be clutch, or at least it would definitely help, if the pitching can’t win it for us or the offense can’t get in rythm on it’s own. Boo-ya Ortiz. Boo-ya.
