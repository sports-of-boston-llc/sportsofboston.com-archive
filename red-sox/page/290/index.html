Red Sox Allow 17 Hits…and Beat Orioles Again

April 25, 2010 at 2:36am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

So I guess it’s not just the Red Sox versus the Orioles anymore. The Sox are trying a new game: how many ways can you try to lose, yet beat the Orioles again? The Red Sox figured out yet another way on Saturday night against the boys from Baltimore, hibernating for the first six innings before exploding for six runs in a 7-6 win.

John Lackey got the nod, looking to recover from his horrible start the previous outing against the Rays (3.1 innings, 9 hits, 8 ER) in that very forgetful series. He pitched pretty well on Saturday, scattering 10 hits over seven innings with just three earned runs allowed to get the win.

The win didn’t come easy though, as for a while, it seemed the Red Sox wouldn’t be able to muster anything off of O’s hurler Brian Matusz. Through six innings, the Sox had just one run (on a Jason Varitek HR, of course) and five hits.

Who Is Darnell McDonald?

April 22, 2010 at 3:49pm in Featured, Red Sox
Justin Power writes
2 comments

In 13 seasons of professional baseball, Darnell McDonald has amassed 1,408 games of service and all anyone wants to talk about is the time he raced a horse.

“Somebody’s always got to bring up the horse,” McDonald told the media Wednesday. “Yeah, I was the first person to lose to Zippy Chippy.”

Zippy Chippy was a racehorse without a win, and somebody within the Rochester Red Wings organization dreamed up a publicity stunt where McDonald would race the gelding in a 50-yard dash as part of the pre-game festivities.  It would go down as Zippy’s only win.

“I was volunteered for it,” said McDonald, obviously a reluctant participant.

In two short days, Darnell McDonald has done everything in his ability to wipe out his most famous, albeit it non-baseball related, hallmark. It is appropriate that it’s his baseball skills that are doing the trick.  With two homers in as many days and a walkoff single in between, McDonald has gotten off to the hottest start for a Boston newcomer since Gabe Kapler homered twice in his Sox debut against the Marlins in 2003. April 22, 2010 at 6:02am in Featured, Red Sox

Add a comment

The Red Sox managed to avoid another loss in wild fashion against the Rangers on Wednesday night, with more unlikely heroes emerging to send Texas to its sixth straight defeat in an 8-7 win in 12 innings.

Josh Beckett was far from magnificent, allowing seven runs on seven hits and five walks over seven innings pitched. He gutted through the game after a rough early start, as he dug the Red Sox in a 4-0 hole after allowing one run in the first and three more in the second.

Starting in place of David Ortiz at DH against lefthander Matt Harrison, Mike Lowell clubbed his first home run of the year (he has one more than Mr. Ortiz) to get the Red Sox closer. Facing a 4-1 deficit, J.D. Drew hit a surprising grand slam later on in the third inning, scoring Bill Hall, Darnell McDonald, and Marco Scutaro (what a trio).
