2010 NHL Playoffs Expert Picks: Round 1

April 14, 2010 at 8:16pm in Bruins, Featured, Now!, Opinion
John writes
One comment

There’s been plenty of hype about the playoffs this year, which I say as if it’s a revelation. The Bruins rebounded quite nicely to pick up their best possible match-up in the 1st round, and four of our six experts have picked them to win, with five of us (including those four who think the Bruins will win) guessing that the series will go a full seven games.

Four of the eight series were selected by our experts to produce unanimous winners, and surprisingly, it’s not all #1 and #2 teams that have been chosen; Pittsburgh has been named to take down Ottawa in six games, though one of us did say they would do so in five. The Capitals by far have the easiest series, with four of us thinking of a sweep and the other two thinking five games.

As far as upsets go, the Red Wings are going to beat the Coyotes according to five of us, and the Bruins will win according to four, though the Black and Gold (according to most of us) are going to have a protracted battle either way. Continue reading 2010 NHL Playoffs Expert Picks: Round 1 »

Add a comment

Late Night host Jimmy Fallon wants to know which sports franchise has the most dedicated fans. So? Well, the reason I’m letting you, the reader, know is that the Red Sox are one of the teams on the list. So are the Yankees.

That brings me to my second point: if you care enough to make change, you need to vote early and often for the Red Sox.
