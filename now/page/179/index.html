2010 FIFA World Cup Expert Picks: Round of 16

June 26, 2010 at 11:54am in Featured, Now!, Opinion, Soccer
John writes
Add a comment

It’s now time for the Round of 16, kicking off with Uruguay and South Korea. There’s plenty of action between now and Tuesday, so turn on your TiVos (TiVoes?) and catch all of the action. I hear the North Korean squad’s execution will be broadcast live on ESPN2. With the Group Stages over, the SoB staff (the remaining seven of us anyway) are hear to give you their choices for the next round. Was Japan able to garner some votes? Can the U.S. move through? Spain and Portugal (#2 and #3) are set to face off, so who will win? Can England, after their embarrassing GOAL!!!!!!!! against the Americans, pull through against WWII rivals Germany? These questions will be answered … right now.

Brazil and the Netherlands were picked unanimously to win, which would set up a huge quarterfinal contest. Uruguay, Argentina, and the United States were all all but unanimously picked, while Spain should defeat Portugal, Paraguay should beat Japan and Germany should beat England, but at 5-2, those games could produce upsets easily, at least easier than the other games. The United States and Ghana is looking to be the game that will go the furthest to the wire, assuming you think America will win. Perhaps not so surprisingly, Brazil looks to have the easiest time.

A correct pick for the winner of each game in the first sudden-death round is worth two points. In addition, experts will also be picking the minute of the game in which they think the winning goal will be made. (If, for example, someone thinks the U.S. will win 3-1, they’ll be guessing the minute in which they think the U.S. will score their second goal.) A correct pick to within fifteen minutes earns one point, and a correct pick to within five minutes earns two points instead. 2010 FIFA World Cup Expert Picks: Group Stage Results

June 25, 2010 at 8:46pm in Featured, Now!, Opinion, Soccer
John writes
Add a comment

After a couple of weeks of work, the Group stages of the 2010 FIFA World Cup are complete, with less than 24 hours between said completion and the Round of 16. There were plenty of surprises, with Japan making a surprise entry into the second round to face Paraguay, in what should be a fun match to watch. There were significantly more surprises that were negative for the teams involved, most notably last time’s winner (Italy) and runner-up (France) both getting booted out, and finishing at the bottom of their groups at that. Of the six African teams, only Ghana advanced. South Africa beat up a broken France, and Cote d’Ivoire beat up North Korea, so we can’t count those games as success.

Argentina and the Netherlands are on pace to meet in the Finals, having been the only two to score a perfect nine points. Cameroon and North Korea were the only ones to finish without any points. I can’t imagine Kim Jong Il will be happy. If I were a North Korean soccer player, I would just refuse to go back to North Korea, and maybe get a job as a FIFA referee, and work a U.S. game. Those refs can obviously do whatever they want without consequences. Congratulations to the U.S. for winning their group with seven points, on a related note. SoB MLB Power Rankings 6-21-10

June 21, 2010 at 10:21pm in Featured, Now!, Opinion, Red Sox
John writes
Add a comment

The Rays aren’t doing so well lately, and it’s cost them. They’re now tied with the Red Sox, and dropping in our rankings. After Mark Teixeira’s grand slam, the Yankees are back on top alone. Now, they’ll visit Arizona, for three easy wins. The 2001 World Series probably still has the Yankees hating. The Rangers just keep winning, moving up to the AL’s #2 seed despite being the league’s fourth best team and baseball’s fifth best. The Twins are fortunate enough to be 1.5 games ahead of Detroit after beating Philadelphia. In the DH-less side of the majors, Atlanta just keeps getting better, Cincinnati had a mini implosion in the Cardinals’ favor, and San Diego is still leading by 1.5 games despite dropping a game to Baltimore.

In our rankings, four, count ’em four, teams have been replaced, with the newest member debuting at #5. Boston’s winning streak has vaulted them six spots in the rankings, nipping at the heals of the Yankees. The Mets’ wildcard position has them on the rankings, and the White Sox have been winning a lot lately to make the rankings, as have the Tigers.

A first place vote earns a team 10 points, a second place vote earns nine, and so on. Ties are broken based on who received the most first place votes, then the most second, and so on.
