In case you haven’t heard, the stepfather of Marquis Daniels was arrested and Tasered during Game 2 of the Celtics-Magic series on Wednesday night. Yes, Tasered…because he was resisting arrest.

The altercation began when the man, Willie L. Buie, was taunted and hollered at by Magic fans. The argument spilled into the stands, where Buie was arrested and brought out of the arena. Video: NBA Referee Joe DeRosa Throws Ball At Fan During Game 2

May 19, 2010 at 3:32pm in Celtics, Now!
KC Downey writes
2 comments

During halftime of Game 2, veteran NBA official Joe DeRosa threw a basketball at a taunting fan. The fan threw the ball right back, and DeRosa ordered that security remove the fan from his seat.

According to The Orlando Sentinel, the fan is Wyndham Vacation Ownership CEO Franz Hanning, who was relocated to another seat. The Sentinel also said Hanning is an acquaintance of Boston Celtics coach Doc Rivers, who lives in Orlando.

The NBA is reportedly reviewing the incident. What should they decide? Should DeRosa be fined/suspended? What about Hanning? Check out the video to decide for yourself.

Drew Bledsoe Enters Wine Business

May 14, 2010 at 4:03pm in Featured, Now!, Patriots
John writes
5 comments

That’s right, Drew Bledsoe, former Patriots quarterback and Tom Brady prototype, has his own wine. His vineyard, the Doubleback Winery, is located in Walla Walla Washington, and just finished aging his 2007 Cabernet Sauvignon, which Bledsoe was in town to hype. It’s not as cool a name as Skywalker Ranch or Goldeneye, but I suppose it’ll work.

Patriot Place hosted a private tasting; Tom Brady, Kevin Faulk, and Max Lane were among the former teammates that Bledsoe hoped to enamor with (not-so) cheap booze. The party then moved on to Marty’s Liquors in Newton, where we plebeians were able to bask in his glory.

We all know former players have plenty on their hands when they retire, but how many are expected to do things like put out wine? You’d expect them to become coaches, analysts, or at least do something with money; they do have a lot by the time they’re done. But still, Bledsoe’s new wine isn’t the strangest celebrity endorsed product; there are plenty of ones out there that just don’t make much sense.
