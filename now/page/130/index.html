Connelly’s Top Ten: Appreciate Tom Brady!

November 28, 2011 at 7:00am in Featured, Now!
Michael writes
3 comments

1. Patriots:

OFFENSE

* Appreciate every game Tom Brady plays (134.6 quarterback rating, which I can’t figure out, but looks wicked good)
* Tom Brady almost got snapped in half. He’s taking some hits. Sebastian Vollmer going backwards?
* Tiquan Underwood – thanks for stopping by. He threw up on that easy touchdown pass
* Should we be concerned about the reliability of Steve Gostkowski?
* Somehow, the Patriots were able to replace Chad Ochocinco’s production

DEFENSE

* Rob Nickovich got away with a nice face mask on his sack
* Is Vince Wilfork starting to show miles?
* Julian Edelman looking surprisingly at home on defense – great open field tackle on Vince Young, saving 7 points

EAGLES AND FANS

* Vince Young gets 400 yards passing
* Eagles were late-hitting and chippy the whole game
* Fans come out of the womb booing?
* Speaking of fans – the biggest cheer of the game was when Rob Gronkowski was nicked up on a play
* Two fake injuries were used to slow down the Patriots hurry up

OTHER

* Glad to report that the streak of showing Robert Kraft was extended (and although its terrible to lose someone – the five months of mentioning Myra’s death is superfolous – they’re better off mentioning the phone number to one of her charities)
* Tom Brady is more likely to get hurt sliding (or dancing in Rio) than by sack

STATS

* The 28 yards rushing is his second highest in his career – he ran for 31 against Jacksonville back in 2006
* Nice 7:27 drive in the fourth quarter on 14 plays

2. Around the NFL:

* Sorry John Elway – My main man Tim Tebow does it again – woo!
* Amazing how much Buffalo was going after Revis Island – Steve Johnson 8 receptions / 75 yards / 1 TD – but picked up a killer unsportsmanlike penalty and dropped a game-winning pass
* Jets are luckiest team in football – love the crowd booing Mark Sanchez, who had four touchdown passes
* NFL has to do something about the goal post – so far this year, there have been 68 field goals over FIFTY yards!
* Kickers in the Oakland-Chicago game were 8 for 8
* Shane Lechler of Oakland had an 80-yard punt
* Eric Decker of Denver scored a touchdown and whipped the football off the end zone wall – a fan, worker or security will eventually get hurt by that

3. Around College Football:

* My Heisman picks right now: Matt Barkley, Trent Richardson, Robert Griffin III, Andrew Luck, Luke Kuechly
* The interception return by Luke Kuechly against Miami is what they will show when he’s picked in the top 15 this year in the NFL draft (what is BC doing to promote him?)
* I would rank 5-5 Iowa State over Houston, who is now ranked 7th. Look at the rank of eight of the teams they have played this year (teamrankings.com): 119, 105, 101, 94, 93, 92, 84, 77
* Charlie Weis still on a cane
* Alabama – LSU national championship rematch will be boring

4. Bruins Stuff:

* B’s win 57.1% of the games when trailing after the first period (best in NHL)
* B’s haven’t lost if they were leading after two periods
* B’s win 69.2% of their games when they outshoot opponent
* B’s win 55.6% of their games when they are outshot

5. Local NCAA Hoops:

* How about 6-0 Harvard upsetting Florida State 46-41 and winning the Battle 4 Atlantis tournament which included Florida State and U Conn
* How about 3-1 Northeastern upsetting St. John’s 78-64

6. Brady’s Career:

Take a look at his career numbers in NFL lore:

* Completed passes – 13th
* Passing yards – 14th
* Touchdown passes – 8th
* QB Rating – 4th (behind Tony Romo, Steve Young, Aaron Rodgers)

7. Amazing Stats:

In a 1961 tournament game against Kentucky, Ohio State player Jerry Lucas scored 33 points and grabbed 30 rebounds.

8. Old School:

Doug Griffin’s highest batting average in a season: .266 in 1974 (0 home runs / 2 stolen bases)

9. Just a Little Bit Outside:

The Boston College Athletic Director in Good Standing…

* You have to call him Coach
* He has been through three baseball coaches, three football coaches, two women’s hoops coaches, two men’s basketball coaches
* He can’t stand that the Hockey Coach isn’t his guy, but he can’t get rid of him
* The basketball, baseball and football team are 23-45

10. Randomocity:

* Found it ironic that while calling the Jets-Buffalo game that Marv Albert would read the promo for the coming Victoria’s Secrets show on CBS considering he reportedly wears such items
* As I said two weeks ago, Jim Boheim could be following Joe Paterno
* Fighting Henrys pull out a 1-1 draw against first place Manchester City
* Good piece on Matt Rodgers on E 60 on ESPN

2011 Turkey of the Year: Josh Beckett

November 24, 2011 at 2:14pm in Featured, Now!, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Josh Beckett spent much of his 2011 season pitching well…really well actually. He had his best season as a Red Sox, finishing 13-7 with a career-best 2.89 ERA and 1.026 WHIP.

But when his team needed him most, he tanked.

Plagued by his perceived addiction to eating fried chicken and drinking beer during games he wasn’t pitching (Hell Yeah, He Liked Beer), and his reported disgust for his inability to win the Cy Young because of Justin Verlander’s unbelievable season, Beckett truly faltered in September.
