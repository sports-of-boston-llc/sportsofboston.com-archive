Ice Breakers: Bruins-Flyers, Game 4

May 7, 2010 at 3:14pm in Bruins
Brian Kintz writes
Add a comment

The Bruins look to put the final nail in the Flyers’ coffin Friday, as they have their sights set on completing a four-game sweep at the Wachovia Center in Philly this evening. The Bruins are 16-0 all-time in series when leading 3-0, having swept 12 of the 16.

But while the Flyers have been losing games on the scoreboard, the Bruins have been losing healthy bodies on their roster. Just minutes into Game 1, the B’s lost their leading goal scorer Marco Sturm, who tore his ACL and MCL and is likely out for 6-8 months. Follow that with Wednesday night’s pair of injuries, one of which is a dislocated wrist to one of the team’s top point producers David Krejci, and the other a lower-body injury to defenseman Adam McQuaid. McQuaid is doubtful for Friday’s Game 4, while Krejci is gone for good, having undergone surgery on Thursday.

All of these bumps and bruises have caused head coach Claude Julien to juggle and rejuggle and rejuggle again, the lineup from game to game. He’ll likely get back defenseman Mark Stuart for Game 4, as he has been medically cleared to return to the lineup for the first time since April 1. He is considered a game-time decision, but look for him to be paired with Andrew Ference on the third defensive pairing Friday in McQuaid’s absence. After winning the Bruins’ Seventh Player Award (as well as the NHL Plus-Minus Award with a +37) last year, Krejci logged in another strong performance this year with 17 goals and 35 assists in 79 regular season games.

David Krejci Injured – In Hospital

May 5, 2010 at 10:21pm in Bruins
Chris writes
2 comments

Update 10AM: David Krejci has a broken wrist. Krejci is out for the rest of the year.

Bruins head coach Claude Julian announced during the post-game press conference that David Krejci was sent to the hospital. Krejci was drilled near center ice by Fliers captain Mike Richards. Krejci’s pass, just before he was hit by Richards, made it’s way to Milan Lucic who passed to Miroslav Satan to give Boston the 2-1 lead. The injury occurred only three minutes into the game; Krejci never returned.

Adam McQuaid was also injured during the game and did not return. Despite the Bruins victory, the Fliers brutal assault on Game 3 has left its marks on the Bruins team.

More updates to come as we learn about them.
