2011 Bruins Preview: Goaltenders

September 12, 2011 at 12:00pm in Bruins, Featured
Matt Goisman writes
Add a comment

With the Bruins’ training camp just getting underway, Sports of Boston will be previewing the goaltenders, defensemen and forwards. As last season’s playoffs proved, a hockey team’s success hinges on the quality of its goalie. A good goalie can bail out a poor defense, kill penalties single-handedly and frustrate even the fastest, craftiest shooters. A bad goalie, on the other hand, can take out a team so early in the game that his teammates won’t know what hit them (see 2011 Stanley Cup, Game 6).

Since the position is so crucial, let’s start our previews with the Bruins’ goaltenders:

Tim Thomas

Tim Thomas had the kind of 2010-11 season that goaltenders dream about. He set an NHL single-season record with a .938 save percentage (for which he won the Roger Crozier Saving Grace Award) that, combined with a 35-11-9 record and 2.00 GAA, won him his second Vezina Trophy (best goaltender in the league). In the playoffs, he performed even better, posting a .940 save percentage and a 1.98 GAA en route to winning the Conn Smythe Trophy (playoffs MVP) and the Stanley Cup. No goalie had ever before won those four trophies in the same season. Continue reading 2011 Bruins Preview: Goaltenders »

Marc Savard Will Not Play in 2011-’12 Season, Career Likely Over

September 1, 2011 at 12:12am in Bruins, Featured
Pete Rampino writes
Add a comment

The news does not come as a complete shock, but it is still sad to hear. Bruins center Marc Savard is going to be shut down for not just the 2011-’12 season, but possibly for the rest of his career due to post-concussion syndrome. Savard received his second concussion within one year’s time back on January 22nd against the Colorado Avalanche which sidelined him for the remainder of Boston’s Stanley Cup-winning season.

Bruins GM Peter Chiarelli confirmed reports earlier today:

“Marc Savard won’t play this year. Nothing has changed in our monitoring. He’ll be examined and he’ll be declared unfit to play.”

Savard’s battle with post-concussion syndrome has been well documented here in Boston starting back in March of 2010 when Pittsburgh’s Matt Cooke delivered a blindsided hit to the head of Savard. As we all know, Cooke was not punished at all for his hit and Savard did not play for the remainder of the regular season. He did however return for the conference semi-finals against the Philadelphia Flyers where Boston lost the series 4-3.
