The Baltimore Ravens came, saw, and conquered the New England Patriots on Sunday night, asserting their will in the second half to advance to Super Bowl XLVII in New Orleans with a 28-13 triumph. The Ravens outscored New England 21-0 in the second half, sending Patriots fans home disappointed at AFC Championship at Gillette Stadium, in Foxborough, Mass.

Thorough Dominance

The second half was all Baltimore, as their veteran defense forced three fourth-quarter turnovers, possessed the ball for 19 minutes in the second half, and simply dominated the tone to advance to their second Super Bowl in franchise history. Ravens QB Joe Flacco dissected the limited the New England secondary (Aquib Talib left in the first with a leg injury) to the tune of 240 yards on 21/35 passing, with three touchdowns and zero interceptions. Baltimore’s ground game was equally effective, as Ray Rice and Bernard Pierce extended drives, killed the clock, and kept the potent New England offense off the field. Anquan Bolden continued his postseason resurgence with five receptions for 60 yards and a pair of scores to seal the victory. Torrey Smith and Dennis Pitta contributed with 4/69 and 5/55/1 touchdown, respectively.(...)
