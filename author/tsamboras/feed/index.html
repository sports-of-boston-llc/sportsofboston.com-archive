Currently, the Boston Celtics are looking good going into  the NBA Eastern Conference Semi-finals. The Celtics are colliding with the defending NBA Champion Cleveland Cavaliers for the Eastern Conference title. After disposing of the both the Indiana Pacers and Toronto Raptors in four straight, the Cavaliers have had time to prepare.

Should Head Coach Brad Miller’s charges make it to the conference finals, there will be plenty of NBA expert picks and naysayers saying the Celtics don’t have the horses to match up with Kyrie Irving, Kevin Love, Lebron James and company. On the season, the two teams split two games, successfully defending its home court. If either team has an advantage, it lies in the fact Boston holds home court advantage due to its #1 seeding.

For all the tipsters who are releasing NBA expert tips naming the Cavaliers as the play, they might want to consult with Boston’s little mighty mite Isaiah Thomas. Even at 5’9”, Thomas has proven to be a nightmare for opposing defenses all year long. Taking everything into consideration, it wouldn’t be a shocker to see Thomas take the league MVP honors over players like Russell Westbrook, Stephen Curry (two-time reigning MVP), James Harden and Kawhi Leonard.

On the regular season, Thomas finished third in the league in scoring with 28.9 PPG on 44.3% shooting from the field, 36.7% from beyond the arc and 87.6% from the charity stripe. No one will soon forget game two of the current series when Thomas and Washington point guard John Wall engaged in an old fashion NBA shootout with Thomas taking the honors with 53 points in a big overtime win. What made the performance all the more impressive was Thomas had given a eulogy for his sister just days before the game. He had also been in the hospital on the day of the game for treatment on the teeth that had been knocked out in the prior game.

As current NBA expert picks would indicate, the Celts are going to need other contributions in order to topple James and the red-hot Cavaliers. That help will have to come from Avery Bradley, who has been feast or famine throughout the playoffs. After averaging only 6 points per game in games 3 and 4 versus the Wizards, Bradley responded with a spectacular performance in game 5. He scored 29 points on 12 of 19 shooting from the field to go along with 6 rebounds and 3 assists. If he performs anywhere near that level against the Cavaliers with center Al Horford controlling the middle, the Celtics would seem more than capable of springing a surprise on the way to the NBA Finals.
