Mike Reiss Leaves Boston.com, Joins ESPN

September 11, 2009 at 7:14pm in News, Patriots
KC Downey writes
2 comments

Mike Reiss, the beat Patriots blogger for Boston.com for the past four years, will now officially be joining the new ESPNBoston.com when the site launches Monday. He officially said goodbye Friday on his Reiss’s Pieces blog on Boston.com.

As the transition is made from “Reiss’s Pieces” to “Extra Points”, this is one final goodbye from me. After four-plus rewarding years at the Boston Globe and Boston.com, I’ll be moving on to a new challenge on Monday.

Thank you to all the readers who made blogging here the favorite part of my job, along with the weekly mailbag.

One of the hardest parts of saying goodbye is leaving great people. There are many of them at the Globe, starting with the person I worked most closely with, Christopher Gasper. Behind the scenes, there are remarkable people on the Globe copy desk who do unsung work. The Boston.com staff is terrific and it was great working for Globe sports editor Joe Sullivan, who along with his trusty assistants has had to fight battles those before him haven’t.

This will be my last post at this address, but I know there are many more updates to come here.

Thanks for sharing this great experience with me.

We wish the best to Mike Reiss. As for ESPNBoston…good luck entering this well-covered Boston market. Since it’s ESPN behind it, they can definitely succeed here. It will be interesting to see…

Patriots Team Captains: 2009-2010

September 11, 2009 at 5:42am in News, Patriots
KC Downey writes
2 comments

The Patriots have confirmed their team captains for the 2010 season.

Offense: Tom Brady, Randy Moss
Defense: Vince Wilfork, Jerod Mayo
Special Teams: Sam Aiken

  • Brady, Mayo, and Aiken were all preseason captains.
  • Aiken replaces current Jet Larry Izzo, who was the Patriots special teams captain for eight straight seasons.
  • Last year, the Patriots captains were Brady, Moss, Matt Light, Wilfork, Rodney Harrison, Tedy Bruschi, Mike Vrabel, and Izzo.

Papelbon Fined $5000 For Being Slow

September 4, 2009 at 5:20am in News, Red Sox
John writes
Add a comment

Red Sox closer Jonathan Papelbon has been fined $5000 for violating the majors’ new “anti-pacing” rule, which gives pitchers two minutes and 25 seconds to come to the mound. Papelbon says he has been fined seven times this season for pacing violations (including an initial fine of $1000) for a total of more than $10,000.

“I don’t know why they keep coming after me. It’s probably because I’m not doing what I’m supposed to be doing. I’m not obeying the rules. I’m taking too long getting to the rubber…I know it’s a new rule and everything and they’re trying to enforce it. I guess I’m just the one they decided to enforce it on,” said Papelbon.

There is a bright side to the fine: according to the U.S. Tax Code Title 26162, “There shall be allowed as a deduction all the ordinary and necessary expenses paid or incurred during the taxable year in carrying on any trade or business.” The courts have upheld that this includes player fines for athletes, provided that no laws were broken to incur these fines, meaning Papelbon has more than $10,000 in extra deductions when he files his return next April.

OF Josh Reddick Recalled From Pawtucket

September 3, 2009 at 4:59am in News, Red Sox
Erikk writes
One comment

Outfielder Josh Reddick has joined five other Pawtucket Red Sox players on the Boston Red Sox roster, making him eligible to play in the playoffs.

This will be Reddick’s third go at the majors, his most recent attempt coming when he was called up on August 6th.  In nine games, he batted a lowly .188 with four doubles (two in his first game), one home run (which came in his second game), and two RBI. The 22-year-old outfielder – ranked fifth amongst Red Sox prospects by Baseball America – was optioned back to Pawtucket on the Aug. 15. In the 18 games Reddick has played in since being sent down, he has gone 9-for-71 (.127) with six RBIs.

With his return to Boston, the Red Sox will have seven outfielders with 31 of 40 playoff roster slots filled.

Analysis

Josh Reddick doesn’t have much of a bat – which the team desperately needs right now – but he will be able to provide a strong presence in the outfield if J.D. Drew needs rest or, more likely, injures himself.
