Vollmer, Price, Cunningham Inactive for Patriots

September 12, 2011 at 7:00pm in News, Patriots
Taylor writes
Add a comment

The New England Patriots have announced that OL Sebastian Vollmer (back), DE/LB Jermaine Cunningham (groin), WR Taylor Price (hamstring), LB Jeff Tarpinian (knee) and OL Ryan Wendell (calf) have all been downgraded to out for Monday night’s season opening against the Dolphins.

The Patriots also listed LB Brandon Spikes, QB Ryan Mallet and RB Shane Vereen as inactive, while signing OT Thomas Welch off the practice squad. Tarpinian was released to clear the spot for Welch.

Vollmer’s absence means that rookie OT Nate Solder should get the start on the right side in his first career game.

The losses of Cunningham, Price and Tarpinian for tonight should not play a big role, as the Patriots are deep at both defensive line, wide receiver and linebacker. Continue reading Vollmer, Price, Cunningham Inactive for Patriots »

NBA Players And Owners Meet At Negotiating Table

September 1, 2011 at 3:20pm in Celtics, NBA, News, Now!
Brian Moller writes
Add a comment

NBA owners and players met Wednesday for about six hours in an attempt to get the proverbial ball rolling at the negotiating table and hopefully lay the ground work for a new CBA. This was the first bargaining session since August 1 and only the second since the lockout was imposed.

Union President and Lakers guard Derek Fisher said, “Everyone loses if we don’t reach an agreement, that’s something that I think has always been understood.”

Neither side was willing to reveal their positions or if there was any progress, but they did acknowledge the need to work quickly if they wish to open the 2011-2012 season as scheduled. Continue reading NBA Players And Owners Meet At Negotiating Table »
