NFL Lockout Over: Looking Back & Looking Forward

July 27, 2011 at 11:00am in Featured, Patriots
Taylor writes
One comment

Over the past three years, NFL coaches, media members and fans have wondered the same question: Will there be a professional football season in 2011? After the league owners agreed to unanimously opt-out of the Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) in May 2008, the threat of no football has lingered for three seasons. That fear can finally be put to rest however, as both the NFL and NFL Players Association (NFLPA) have agreed to a deal that will last through the 2021 season.

The Background

Displeased with their two-year old CBA, the NFL owners exercised their option to opt-out of the deal prior to 2008, ending the deal after the 2010 league year. Citing stadium operating costs (despite many new stadiums being publicly financed) and rising player salaries, all 32 owners agreed to end the CBA after the 2010 season in what appeared to be a clear money grab. Book Review: “The Greatest Game” by Richard Bradley

July 27, 2011 at 10:00am in Featured, Now!, Opinion, Red Sox
Matt Goisman writes
One comment

The Greatest Game is Richard Bradley’s exploration of the 1978 one-game playoff between the Red Sox and Yankees for a spot in the AL Championship. The game is best remembered for Bucky “F—ing” Dent’s three-run home run in the seventh inning that turned the momentum in New York’s favor. The Yankees won 5-4, beat the Royals to win the pennant and the Dodgers to win the World Series. Bradley’s account is a pitch-by-pitch look at the game, with brief biographies of the players and managers sandwiched in between. Bradley also takes the reader through the Yankees’ and Red Sox’s regular seasons.

Not a Book for Red Sox Fans

I had planned to write how, were this book published in 2003 instead of 2008, it would have capitalized on the masochistic side of being a Red Sox fan and sold millions. However, two-thirds of the way in I realized that this isn’t a book for Red Sox fans at all; it’s for Yankees fans to learn more about one of their team’s crowning moments.
