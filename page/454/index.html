Kelly Shoppach Traded to the Mets

August 14, 2012 at 5:35pm in Featured, Red Sox
Fudge writes
Add a comment

Kelly Shoppach, the Sox backup  catcher and rightful heir to Jason Varitek’s facial hair, has cleared waivers and been dealt to the New York Mets for the always exciting “player to be named later.” Shoppach has been as productive as a backup catcher could be [.250, 5 HR, .789 OPS], but his days were numbered once Ryan Lavarnway came up the Lou Merloni Expressway from Pawtucket a couple weeks ago.

Congrats to Shoppach on being traded away from the Sox for the second time in his career, and to the Sox, because dealing a backup catcher with only 158 plate appearances is just what you need to pull an underachieving team out of an 11.5 game hole. I’d say this like rearranging deck furniture on the Titanic, but the Titanic actually went somewhere before it ended in disaster.

Rest In Peace Johnny Pesky, A Legend in Every Sense of the Word

August 13, 2012 at 6:07pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Steve Bastek writes
Add a comment

On August 13th 2012 Red Sox Nation lost one of its most beloved members. Johnny Pesky passed away at the age of 92.

Pesky played ten seasons in the major’s, and missed three more from 1943-1945 due to military service. He spent a little more than seven seasons with the Red Sox before he was sent to Detroit. He was a career .307 hitter and a dependable infielder, playing third, short, and second. His number 6 is retired and displayed on the right field roof deck in Fenway Park for everyone to see.

Pesky is more known for the foul pole down the right field line which is named after him. It is believed that former teammate and Red Sox broadcaster Mel Parnell named the pole after Pesky after Johnny slapped a game winning home run just around it in 1948. The pole was officially dedicated to Pesky on September 27th 2006, and a commemorative plaque was placed at the base.
