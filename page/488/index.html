Add a comment

The Boston Red Sox defeated the Baltimore Orioles at Fenway Thursday night 7-0, behind a shutout performance from pitcher Clay Buchholz.

The Red Sox offense started early as Orioles’ starting pitcher Brian Matusz struggled to find the strike zone. Dustin Pedroia walked in the bottom of the first inning and was joined on base by Kevin Youkilis after a single to left, and Will Middlebrooks after the second walk of the inning. Adrian Gonzalez appeared to break his mini-slump and grounded a double to right field, scoring Pedroia and Youkilis, and giving the Red Sox and early 2-0 lead.

Mayor Menino Praises Celtics’ ‘KJ’ and ‘Hondo’

June 7, 2012 at 9:08pm in Celtics, Now!, Video
Michael Ricci writes
Add a comment

Is Boston Mayor Tom Menino watching the same team we are? Did Danny Ainge make a last ditch effort to sure up his bench by securing mystery players named KJ and Hondo?

No, that’s right, he was talking about KG and Rondo.

I suppose we should be used to it.

Remember when Menino openly mocked the Colorado beer that “hasn’t made it East yet?” He obviously forgot about the icy cold refreshments bottled by Coors.

Better yet, do you remember when he referred to Patriot pass catchers Wes Welker and Rob Gronkowski as Weckler and Grabowski?

It’s only right that the Celtics got their turn, and Boston sports fans are all better for it.

We could probably use the comic relief going into Game 6 after all.
