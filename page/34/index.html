Some Quick Thoughts On The Patriots 2016 Draft Class

May 7, 2016 at 1:11pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
cpalczynski writes
Add a comment

In total, the Patriots selected 9 players overall during the 2016 NFL Draft. Without a first round pick, none of these players are particularly spectacular, but most, according to NFL Scouting Reports, seem coach-able. Regardless, all nine will be fighting for their jobs when training camp rolls up in late July. So without further ado, all nine picks (complete with reasoning as to why Belichick may have been interested).

#60 overall: Cyrus Jones (5’10”, 197), CB, Alabama: As a fifteen game starter last year, Jones had two interceptions. This may not seem overly impressive, but Jones is a versatile cornerback that should fit well into a nickel system. What is interesting about this cornerback is his punt-returning ability. Jones returned 42 punts for a 12.6 average, complete with four touchdowns. This could provide immediate relief for already beleaguered wide receivers Danny Amendola and Julian Edelman.

#78 overall: Joe Thuney (6’5″, 304), OL, North Carolina State: Thuney, a three year starter at NC State is a valuable player because he has spent time at every position on the offensive line. Versatility and flexibility in terms of position is a trait that Belichick appreciates (e.g. Troy Brown and Julian Edelman both spending time at CB in seasons past). Currently, the Patriots unofficial depth chart lists Thuney as a left guard, but this could change quickly depending on need.

#91 overall: Jacoby Brissett (6’4″, 231), QB, North Carolina State: In 2015, Brissett led the Wolfpack to a 7-6 record, throwing for 2,662 yards, 20 touchdowns, and 6 interceptions. This is a mobile quarterback that rushed for over 300 yards, paired with 6 touchdowns, and is seemingly competition for Garroppolo. Brissett will still begin the season third on the depth chart, behind a suspended Brady and Garroppolo (who will be completely overwhelmed in Week 1).

#96 overall: Vincent Valentine (6’4″, 329), DT, Nebraska: Last year, Valentine, after recovering from a knee injury that more or less sidelined him for the first half of the year, started seven games, recording ten tackles and three sacks. Valentine was likely picked up for his size, which is reminiscent of Vince Wilfork. Both are able to draw the attention of double teams (but only one has proved himself on an NFL level).

#112 overall: Malcolm Mitchell (6’0″, 198), WR, Georgia: With some grooming, Mitchell might develop into a dependable receiver for Brady to look to. NESN.com reports that Mitchell only had 3 drops on 93 targets in 2015 (compare with Julian Edelman, who had eight drops on 88 targets). To make matters more interesting, Mitchell’s NFL scouting report iterates how he is able to make the tough, contested catches across the middle.

#208 overall: Kamu Grugier-Hill (6’2″, 215), OLB, Eastern Illinois: Eastern Illinois’ Pro Day turned out to be wildly beneficial for Grugier-Hill. The linebacker was able to add a 38.5″ vertical leap and a 4.45 forty yard dash to his resume, which is rounded out nicely by his 2015 campaign: 70 tackles, 6.5 sacks, 3 fumble recoveries, and a single interception.

#214 overall: Elandon Roberts (6’0″, 235), ILB, Houston: Defenses need someone who is able to be a coach on the field, and with the departure of Jerod Mayo, the Patriots are seemingly lacking in that department. Enter Elandon Roberts, complete with a scouting report (from NFL.com) with buzz words such as “leader”, “passion”, and “coach’s dream”. The downside? “Slow to accelerate”. “Below average speed”.

#221 overall: Ted Karras (6’3″, 307), OG, Illinois: A captain during his senior year, Karras possesses one statistic that Belichick could not ignore; Karras has committed only two penalties over his last two seasons. At 307 pounds, Karras was able to open up running lanes consistently at the college level. Whether this will translate to the NFL or not remains to be seen, but the 4-year starter is planning on it.

#225 overall: Devin Lucien (6’2″, 195), WR, Arizona State: According to Pro Football Focus, Lucien had only three drops on 98 targets in 2015. Wide receivers with steady hands are a hot commodity in New England at the moment, and the ASU receiver opened eyes with his last three games, in which he had 534 yards and 5 touchdowns. This is certainly a low-risk/high-reward pick to round things out.

What’s Next for Pablo Sandoval?

May 5, 2016 at 12:05am in Featured, Red Sox
Zach Tavano writes
Add a comment

This past Tuesday, Pablo Sandoval underwent a season-ending surgery to repair a torn labrum in his left shoulder. The back up third baseman will be sidelined for the remainder of the season and is expected to make a full recovery for the 2017 season.

Sandoval came to Boston in 2015 with very high hopes of becoming a franchise player. He signed a five year, $95 million deal with the Sox in late 2014. Sandoval had a disappointing first season with the Red Sox as he posted a .245 batting average (worst of his career), 10 home runs, and 47 RBIs.

After the 2015 season, Sox fans everywhere were left wondering what the team was going to do with Sandoval. The questions raised when word came out of Hanley Ramirez moving from the outfield to first base. Also rookie Travis Shaw made a very good impression at first base and at the plate in the 65 games he played so questions of where he was going to be placed raised as well.

By the time spring training came around in March, we learned that it would be a battle between Sandoval and Shaw for the starting third base position. Sandoval collected 10 hits in 49 plate appearances (.204 avg.). Meanwhile Travis Shaw collected 22 hits in 65 plate appearances (.338 avg.) It was clear who won the starting job. Sandoval came into spring training over weight and not ready for the season so he lost the job that he was so heavily paid for.

With the latest news of Sandoval’s surgery, the biggest question is what’s his future in Boston? Through 26 games this season, Travis Shaw has posted a .312 batting average and .516 slugging percentage, among one of the highest on the team. Shaw seems like he might be at third base to stay. But the Red Sox seem fully committed to getting Sandoval healthy and ready for the 2017 season. But are they better off with him or without him?

There is a possibility that Sandoval could take up the DH spot with David Ortiz retiring after this season. The DH position seems like the only way Sandoval could see the field in a Red Sox uniform at this point. But from what we’ve seen, he has not put up any decent numbers at the plate with Boston. Some may say it’s due to his weight problem and that very well might be the problem but either way something has to change. I mean this guy is a former World Series MVP and has won three world series championships with the San Francisco Giants where he was the stud third baseman, he’s got the skills but he just hasn’t produced yet. Maybe it’s his transition from San Francisco to Boston. Sometimes it’s tough to transition to a new team. There was so much hype around his name and he was expected to be big for the Sox so the pressure may have got to him. I believe there’s a possibility that Sandoval comes back next season much healthier and lives up to the hype.

There’s also a possibility that the Red Sox try to trade Sandoval but I feel like this is less possible to happen considering the amount of money they owe him. I don’t know how many teams will be willing to pay him that contract with the results he’s produced in the past season.

Bottom line the only thing stopping him is himself. If Pablo wants to be an everyday player for the Sox, he needs to take his rehab, workout program, and diet seriously. He’s a professional athlete and needs to be responsible for his body. The Red Sox did not pay him $95 million to ride the pine. They paid him to help turn this franchise around and become contenders in October again.

All that being said I hope Pablo’s recovery goes well and he gets healthy. I really want to see him turn this around and live up to his name. When he signed with the Sox at the end of the 2014 season, I was ecstatic and nothing would make me happier than seeing him in the lineup and executing at the plate.

A Preview of the Suddenly High-Profile Red Sox and White Sox’s Upcoming Series

May 3, 2016 at 6:05pm in Red Sox
cpalczynski writes
Add a comment

The Red Sox are flying into Chicago to take on the high-powered White Sox, who boast an 18-8 record, and a 3 game lead in the AL Central. Both teams have won eight of their last ten, and are both coming off of a much-needed day of rest.

The White Sox last week, split a four game series with Chicago, and the Red Sox completed a sweep of the New York Yankees in Boston. The last time the Red Sox swept the Yankees was in 2013-the year that they won the World Series.

Game 1 features knuckleballer Steven Wright (2-2, 1.37) against Jose Quintana (3-1, 1.47). The only thing more impressive than Quintana’s current ERA is Wright’s, at 1.37. Wright, with the possible exception of Porcello, is the most consistent pitcher the Red Sox have had this season. With more run support, Wright would be 4-0. However, he will go for his third win against a mediocre Chicago offense. This should be a low-scoring game that is won in a later inning. Final score: BOS 2, CWS 1

On May 4th, Clay Buchholz (0-3, 6.51) is going up against Carlos Rodon Danks (1-3, 4.33), who is moving up a day to take the place of John Danks, who was released. Buchholz has progressively looked shakier and less confident. His curveball seemed insignificant against an Atlanta Braves team that is destined to have the worst record in the league. What will become of Buchholz if he turns in yet another poor start? Final score: CWS 7, BOS 5

The final game of the series is seemingly a winnable game-for both teams. Henry Owens (0-0, 4.82) goes against Erik Johnson, who will be making his season debut. Owens is coming off of a game in which they both went at least six innings. Owens only gave up two earned runs against the suddenly panicking Yankees. Offense will be the deciding factor in this high scoring game, and if the Red Sox can get to the bullpen early, they might be able to run away with a win-and a series-in Chicago. Final score: BOS 8, CWS 6

One more reason to get excited for the Red Sox to take on the White Sox: As of right now, the Red Sox have a .5 game lead over the Orioles in the AL East. This is a small lead that could grow if the Red Sox perform. The Orioles, suddenly vulnerable, are taking on the Yankees in Baltimore.
