Rajon Rondo’s Triple-Double Not Enough to Push Celtics Past Carmelo Anthony, Knicks

January 25, 2013 at 12:27am in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Brian Roach writes
Add a comment

With his 27th career triple-double (20th on national television), Rajon Rondo  tried to bring the Celtics back into Thursday’s contest with the Knicks, but his efforts weren’t enough as the Celtics dropped their fifth straight game, 89-86 . Rondo finished with 23 points, 11 assists and 10 rebounds all while making 10-of-19 shots from the field, his his fourth triple-double this season.

Carmelo Anthony tallied 28 points and nine rebounds after a slow start and the New York Knicks came out victorious at the TD Garden.

The defining moment of the game came with about 1:30 left in the game when the Celtics turned the ball over and J.R. Smith made a huge three-pointer to push the lead to five at 89-84. Rondo came back with a jumper, but two costly turnovers in the final minute, one forced by Smith with seven seconds left in the game, handed the Knicks the game.

Bruins Suffer First Loss of the Season to Rangers

January 24, 2013 at 5:55am in Bruins, Featured, Top Story
Jeff Miller writes
Add a comment

Marian Gaborik capped off his hat trick night by batting home his rebound past Tuukka Rask on a breakaway with 27 seconds into overtime to give the New York Rangers a 4-3 win at Madison Square Garden.

Gaborik scored his first two goals just over two minutes apart early in the first period. The Rangers dominated the first half of the first period while out shooting the Bruins 8-2 at one point. The one area that the Bruins did dominate in the first period was faceoffs, 14-4.

The Bruins took over for most of the second period with goals coming from Brad Marchand and Milan Lucic. The lead was short lived however when Taylor Pyatt gave the Rangers the lead back just 46 seconds later. Nathan Horton tied the game up with a shot from in between the hashmarks with just under five minutes to play. The goal was Horton’s first since Jan. 19 of last season. Nevermore: Ravens Top Patriots 28-13 in AFC Title Game

January 21, 2013 at 10:54pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
Taylor writes
Add a comment

The Baltimore Ravens came, saw, and conquered the New England Patriots on Sunday night, asserting their will in the second half to advance to Super Bowl XLVII in New Orleans with a 28-13 triumph. The Ravens outscored New England 21-0 in the second half, sending Patriots fans home disappointed at AFC Championship at Gillette Stadium, in Foxborough, Mass.

Thorough Dominance

The second half was all Baltimore, as their veteran defense forced three fourth-quarter turnovers, possessed the ball for 19 minutes in the second half, and simply dominated the tone to advance to their second Super Bowl in franchise history. Ravens QB Joe Flacco dissected the limited the New England secondary (Aquib Talib left in the first with a leg injury) to the tune of 240 yards on 21/35 passing, with three touchdowns and zero interceptions. Baltimore’s ground game was equally effective, as Ray Rice and Bernard Pierce extended drives, killed the clock, and kept the potent New England offense off the field. Anquan Bolden continued his postseason resurgence with five receptions for 60 yards and a pair of scores to seal the victory.
