Bruins Remaining Schedule: Ten Games, Ten Questions

By Brian Kintz in Bruins, Featured, Top Story ⋅ March 25, 2010 at 12:00pm ⋅ Post a comment

The roller coaster that has been the Boston Bruins 2009-2010 regular season is pulling back into the station, as the B’s begin the final leg of their 82-game schedule: 10 games to playoff glory or postseason elimination. There have been highlights (7-2 crushing of Carolina in the first week of the season, the overtime win in the Winter Classic), and there have been lowlights (10-game winless streak, the Marc Savard/Matt Cooke saga), but after all the trials and tribulations that the regular season has thrown at the Black and Gold the truth remains, if the Bruins can finish strong in their last 10 contests, the B’s will make the playoffs. If that happens, well it’s a whole new season: a rebirth of hope, and a reason to believe that anything can happen.

Let’s look now at 10 questions that are on the minds of the Bruins and their fans, heading down the stretch through their last 10 games of the regular season… 

1. Where will the offense come from?

After a season where Boston ranked at the top of the league in goals scored, the B’s face the harsh reality of possibly finishing dead last in offense this year. They currently have just 180 goals to their credit, six less than the Rangers, who sit in 29th league-wide. If the B’s are going to get into the playoffs and think they’re going to make any noise if and when they get there, the team’s top two forward lines are really going to need to step up their game. They’ve shown signs lately that it can happen, but consistency over the next 10 games will go a long way for the team’s confidence if they head into the postseason.

2. Will they stay healthy?

Beyond the questionable return of Marc Savard, which is looking more and more doubtful with each passing day, the team is otherwise relatively healthy at this point. Coming down to the wire, maintaining the health of the rest of the team will be a huge key to their success.

Add to that, the team may have potentially lost the services of Vladimir Sobotka, depending on the severity of the injury he sustained Tuesday in Atlanta, and the B’s could face a weakness up the middle of their forward lines. If their stars can stay on the ice though, there’s no reason the team shouldn’t see the postseason.

3. How’s the remaining schedule stack up?

Not ideal, let’s just say that. The team has six home games left out of the 10, which one would think would be considered a benefit. But at just 15-14-6 on home ice, by far the worst home record of any of the teams currently in the top 16 playoff-bound teams, Claude Julien’s squad may be better off if they had a Canucks-esque 10-game road trip to end their season. Instead, the team will host two playoff hopeful teams in Tampa and Calgary, then take on division-leading Buffalo for the first of two remaining bouts with the Sabres.

Down the stretch, they do have what should be potential wins over Carolina, Toronto, and Florida, but a visit to New Jersey and two visits in April to conference-leading Washington loom large and could mean the difference between a playoff berth or tee times in mid-April. If you’re a Bruins fan, you’re hoping that the Caps have the top seed in the East locked up well before they take on the B’s, with the hopes that they’ll rest their stars for the playoffs and take it easy on our guys. Can’t count on that though.

4. How do the other playoff contenders’ schedules compare?

Well, the B’s do have a game in hand over Philadelphia and Montreal, each of whom sit just one point ahead of Boston, in a tie for sixth in the East. And both teams have struggled lately, with Philly searching for an answer in goal, after losing Michael Leighton and Ray Emery to injury, and Montreal, who can’t seem to get out of their own way, losing games they should be winning.

Looking below the Bruins in the standings, Atlanta now sits three points behind Boston in ninth, but their last week of the season is absolutely brutal, facing off twice with Pittsburgh and twice with New Jersey to round out the season…ouch. The Rangers, who now sit seven points back of Boston, have to hit the road for seven of their last 10 games, which won’t come easy for the Blueshirts. So in comparison, no team has an easy road to the final round of eight, but Boston’s isn’t so bad when you compare it to the other teams fighting for the spots.

5. What are the odds the Bruins will match up with the Penguins in the first round?

Oh, how sweet that would be! The odds would get much better if Pittsburgh is able to edge New Jersey for the Atlantic Division championship. If they do that, the Pens would likely end up in the #2 seed in the East, meaning they’d start off facing the #7 seed in the playoffs, which is definitely a possibility for Boston to end up with. On the other hand, if Pittsburgh doesn’t win their division, they’ll likely end up in the #4 slot, which would face off with #5, and with the B’s five points behind Ottawa for that #5 seed, that matchup would be far less likely.

For entertainment value purposes, I am absolutely rooting for the B’s and Pens to line up against each other, but if I’m looking for the most favorable first-round matchup, I think I’d rather see Boston end up in the #6 spot, to likely meet up with Buffalo. Buffalo is just clearly a more winnable series than the likes of Washington or Pittsburgh, or even New Jersey. So my fingers are crossed for the Bruins to face Ryan Miller and the Sabres.

6. Which goalie are we going to see down the stretch and come playoff time, Timmy or Tuukka?

My money is on Tuukka, hands down. Rask just has the hotter hand right now, plain and simple. Until he falters, I don’t see how Julien can stray from starting Tuukka in at least eight of the final 10 games. Boston has two sets of back-to-back games left on their schedule. Vezina Trophy or no Vezina Trophy, let Thomas get a start in each one of those sets, but otherwise, I want to see the NHL’s goals-against-average leader between the pipes for the B’s, and this year, that’s Tuukka Rask.

7. Who deserves this year’s 7th Player Award?

In my mind, there are only two options: Patrice Bergeron or Tuukka Rask. And again, my money’s on Tuukka. He has quickly become a fan favorite, and without him, Boston would not even be sniffing a playoff spot nowadays. Granted, Bergeron’s game has finally rounded into shape, now over two years removed from his Grade 3 concussion, and the way he has raised his game this season cannot go unnoticed. But, I have to believe when it comes to the fan’s vote later this season, it will be Rask’s name we’ll hear announced as this year’s winner.

8. How low will Toronto finish in the standings?

The focus for Bruins fans down the stretch has to be centralized on the team’s playoff hopes, no doubt about it. But true fans also have kept an eye all season long on where the Toronto Maple Leafs rank among the league’s bottom feeders. Boston of course owns the Leafs’ first-round pick this June, which they acquired in the Phil Kessel trade this past off-season. Currently, Toronto ranks dead last in the Eastern Conference at 64 points, but with their latest surge (7-3 in their last 10), they are just four points back of the Islanders. Edmonton, who has 55 points out west, has all but cinched the league’s worst record, and will likely have the best odds at nabbing the first pick in the upcoming draft. But if Toronto can remain just stinky enough the rest of the way, Boston should grab a top-3 pick, which will make fans in Bruins Nation extremely happy.

9. If the Bruins do make the playoffs, can they win?

Sure, why not? Is it probable? No, I wouldn’t think so. But is it possible? Absolutely. Now if they end up matching up against the Capitals in the first round, you won’t see me riding high with visions of Stanley Cup championships dancing in my head. But if they get into a favorable first-round matchup, find a way to get their offense clicking, and their defense and goaltending can keep them in games, there’s no reason why Boston could steal a series, or even two. All of that might be too much to ask for, but a boy can dream, can’t he?

10. Lastly, have the Bruins met your expectations this season? Have they over or underachieved?

I don’t know that this year’s Bruins have met anyone’s expectations. But when I look at it, I take this team and look at them in a two-year chunk instead of just the one season. Last year, no one gave them a shot, and they completely exceeded everyone’s expectations. This year, because of the elevated expectations, they were set up to fail from the start. So which is it? Did last year’s team overachieve or were this year’s Bruins a gang of underachievers?

Honestly, I think the “real” Bruins are somewhere in the middle. They’re not an upper-echelon team yet like Washington or Pittsburgh, but they’re better than the Rangers, Thrashers, and the other teams they find themselves battling with for playoff positioning. They have been able to build a solid core of young talent and lock them up with long-term deals, meaning this team will be contenders in the years to come, as they continue their growth and development. Fans have to like where the team is headed…but after last season’s results, fans were just given the false hope that the bright future was already here for this team. The truth is, they’re still not the Capitals, still not the Blackhawks, and still not the Sharks. But they will be…I think. I hope! Maybe these next 10 games will tell the true story.
