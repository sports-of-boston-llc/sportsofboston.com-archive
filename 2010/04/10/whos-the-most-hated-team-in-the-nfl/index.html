Who’s the Most Hated Team in the NFL?

By John in Featured, Now!, Patriots ⋅ April 10, 2010 at 12:30pm ⋅ Post a comment

Following some recent chatter across the Internet, which I’m told is a series of tubes, the Jets are starting to overtake the Patriots as the most hated team in the AFC East. But after all the Spygate scandals, which have been captured on videotape, and the superb seasons with an elite quarterback, how can the Jets be doing worse. Apparently, it’s the holier-than-thou attitude of their quarterback and coach, and getting way further in the playoffs than they should have.

But how should we feel about who is more hated than who? How easy is it to hate all the teams in the league? Check below to find out.

Rank Team Comments
#32 Lions The Lions are perennial losers of the league; by this time, they’re rather lovable losers.
#31 Rams Going from 3-13 to 2-14 to 1-15, the lowly Rams are another patsy to build up records against.
#30 Bills The Bills haven’t had a winning season since 2004 and haven’t made the playoffs since the 1999 season. Marv Levy is likable and the team lost four straight Super Bowls.
#29 Seahawks It took the Seahawks so long to do anything meaningful in the playoffs, and now they’re terrible again. Unless Pete Caroll has anything to say about it.
#28 Saints Despite winning a Super Bowl, the Saints are still pretty likable. It was their first, Drew Brees was probably the best quarterback to never have won one, and who doesn’t love Mardi Gras?
#27 Browns Another fun team, they’ve only made the playoffs once since their re-inception. They have 13 ever ties, including two seasons with two. And anything Drew Carey likes can’t be bad. Except skill-wise.
#26 Raiders Ever since they lost the Super Bowl in the 2002 season, they’ve gone downhill, seven straight season with at most five wins, actually. Sure, they got Richard Seymour, but they’re always a good opponent to beat up on.
#25 Redskins They’ve got McNabb, Shanahan, and are in the process of a huge re-tooling. We can all respect a team with the wherewithal to do that, despite their racially insensitive name.
#24 Panthers Peppers is gone, which leaves a big hole in the team’s defense. They’ve done alright for a team that didn’t exist when I was born. The Panthers haven’t really done much other than have the threat of Peppers to be disliked.
#23 Jaguars The farthest they’ve ever gone is the Conference Championship. They’ve been the division’s bottom dwellers for the past two years and have some work to do to work back up.
#22 Titans If they can give stability to their quarterback situation and stay away from injuries, the Titans might be relevant again. And any team that can upset the Colts’ streak of division titles is alright.
#21 Chargers They’ve lost a Super Bowl and three Conference Championships; the Chargers just can’t seem to get over that hump. But they can still be counted on to win their division pretty regularly.
#20 Ravens Joe Flacco isn’t that bad, especially after his rookie season. And I know the Ravens embarrassed the Patriots in the playoffs, but the Pats had their own issues, hence the Ravens not being near the top of the list.
#19 Bengals The Bengals started last year great, then fell off, then dropped from the playoffs unceremoniously; Cincinnati doesn’t know what they want to do. And they interrupted the Steelers/Ravens dominance we all though was in place in the division.
#18 Buccaneers Remember when they won the Super Bowl in the middle of the Patriots’ streak? That’s one reason to hate them. But they haven’t done much since then.
#17 Cardinals Now that Kurt Warner’s gone, the Cardinals will need Matt Leinart to fire off a few deep balls to Larry Fitzgerald to keep themselves alive out West.
#16 Texans Matt Schaub is pretty much the reason why the Texans still aren’t in the gutter. At this point, we’re all waiting for this team to have a breakout season.
#15 Packers The Cheeseheads have been up and down since the days of Vince Lombardi, and now that the Brett Favre era is over in Green Bay, it’s time for Brett Favre Jr. (aka Aaron Rodgers) to continue his rise to fame.
#14 Falcons Matt Ryan dipped a little from his rookie year. The Falcons are probably going to be hit and miss for awhile at this rate. And as a bonus, any BC alums probably received a recorded phone message from Ryan before he became a Falcon asking for donations for BC. What a tool.
#13 Chiefs After a disappointing season, the Chiefs turned to Matt Cassel. He was also disappointing, leading the Chiefs to the AFC’s worst record. Eat it Chiefs.
#12 49ers Except for a brief growth spurt in 2001 and 2002, they haven’t been very good from 1999 on. We’ll see if they can overtake the Cardinals and hold off the Seahawks in 2010.
#11 Vikings With an arm like Favre, we should hate the Vikings more. But with an oft irked high school principal for a coach and Grandpa at quarterback, how can you not chuckle. As Shakespeare once said, “To retire or not to retire, that is the question.”
#10 Bears Chicago is a town that loves sports. Or maybe that’s just a side effect of Michael Jordan. Jay Cutler didn’t work out so well, but he might not have to with Julius Peppers minding the backfield. If the Bears win it all next year, prepare to witness an outpouring of bravado from cocky fans.
#9 Broncos They steal our coaches and beat up our team. Denver has made enemies of several teams throughout the league for various reasons, and despite only two losing seasons in the 11 years since their Super Bowl victories, their enemies have had the last laugh.
#8 Dolphins Back and forth over the last few seasons, the Dolphins aren’t that good. They’ll move back down in the hatability category, but not until the angst over Joey Porter wears off.
#7 Giants A Super Bowl, another good year, and then a disgrace of the Meadowlands. Everyone hates New Yorkers, even other New Yorkers, but when a team struggles like the Giants did last year, we all have to revel in it.
#6 Cowboys Aside from being from a definitively red state, why should we hate the Cowboys? The Romo/Simpson affair helped us do so. They’re so big that even people who know nothing about football know the Cowboys. But most of all, we’re all jealous that their women have caused more embarrassment amongst high school teenagers than anything else ever.
#5 Jets So what if they’re mouthy? So what if their coach is a fat jerk? The new scheme the Jets have going is working pretty well. They’ll get better, which will only make us hate them worse. That, and they’re from New York.
#4 Patriots Spygate? Check. The first 16-0 season? Check. Passionate fans that are often mistaken for crude and obnoxious, like New Yorkers? Check. We’ll be hated for a long time, but if Brady can be like his old self and not be hampered from the injuries, and if we can play some defense, we’ll be able to back it up.
#3 Colts Manning is reason enough to hate the perennial Super Bowl contender, but if that weren’t enough, they can’t seem to figure out how to play their starters and avoid rust at the end of the season. Fantasy owners and even Colts fans have been irate year after year for that very reason.
#2 Steelers What could possibly make the Steelers more hated than an (alleged) sexual predator at quarterback? And to top it all off, the same quarterback doesn’t seem to realize that when riding a motorcycle, it is possible to fall off and injure yourself, especially without a helmet. At least Roethlisberger doesn’t have a brain to damage.
#1 Eagles The Eagles traded their best quarterback, and within the division at that. I can’t even imagine what people in Philadelphia are feeling right now, but it can’t be as bad as 18-1. And keeping a sub-human monster on the roster? That only riles the blood more.

So there you have it. Plenty of teams to love, and plenty to love to hate. But how do the teams that give you high cholesterol come together? By all means, leave your comments and tell us. That’s how this whole issue got started in the first place.
