Bruins Clinch Playoff Spot

April 4, 2008 at 10:14pm in Bruins, Featured
KC Downey writes
One comment

The Bruins beat the Ottowa Senators 2-1 to clinch a playoff berth. They were playing without Marc Savard but still managed to win and get in with just one more game left in the regular season. Tim Thomas helped the cause in tonight’s game, making an incredible point-blank save to hold the Sens scoreless to that point.

The B’s became the fifth straight Boston sports team to make the playoffs, including the Red Sox, Revolution, Patriots, and Celtics.

More coverage of the Bruins to come as the team begins their pursuit for the Stanley Cup. Check out the Bruins Schedule to see how the team got to where they are now.

Beckett Likely Out For Opening Day

March 12, 2008 at 2:14am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Red Sox ace Josh Beckett is now likely out for Opening Day with the back spasms. He’s gone from feeling good the day after the injury, to feeling “crappy” the next, and just O.K. the third. All signs point to no as far as flying to Japan with a bad back.

His “crappy” day is what had him and Red Sox brass worried. Without Beckett for an extended period of time, the Red Sox are a considerably weaker team. The team calls his injury “discomfort” in the lower back, and will take all precautions to ensure a healthy recovery.

Terry Francona told ESPN.com that no final decision had been made, but it makes sense to wait until April 1 or 2 in Oakland or April 4-6 in Toronto.

Said Francona:

“I don’t think we know [for sure] yet. But we’re certainly not going to rush him back to pitch a game March 24 if he’s not ready.”

Randy Moss’ Restraining Order Dropped

March 11, 2008 at 4:19pm in Featured, Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
Add a comment
Randy Moss is FREE!.(Courtesy of NFL.com)

A restraining order placed on Pats WR Randy Moss in Florida has been dropped. A woman had claimed domestic violence against Moss but just more than a month after filing for the order she asked the case be closed on March 3.

Rachelle Washington had claimed Moss hurt her and then denied her medical attention on January 6th. Moss had defended himself saying he never denied her medical attention, never harmed her intentionally and alluded that she had experienced money problems in the past.

Moss’ lawyer Richard Sharpstein said his client was pleased the ordeal was over.

“There has finally been acknowledgment that the injuries were not intentionally inflicted,” Sharpstein said.

My Take: It’s nice to see some good news on the off-the-field front for the Pats. From the beginning it didn’t seem as the charges had much merit and now Moss can concentrate on football and chasing that elusive Super Bowl victory.

Matt Walsh Close to Deal with NFL

Add a comment

Former Patriots video assistant Matt Walsh is closing in on a deal with the NFL where he would hand over supposed tapes full of evidence against the Patriots. Walsh had been asking for full protection from the league, probably because he has the tapes illegally.

Josh Beckett Injury Update: Back Spasms

March 9, 2008 at 9:17am in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Red Sox ace Josh Beckett left yesterday’s Spring Training game before it started with back spasms. He reportedly tweaked his back on his first warm-up toss when he slipped on some mound dirt that gave way.

Beckett didn’t speak to the media, but according to Boston Globe Sox Blogger Amalie Benjamin, he communicated a message through a team spokesman: “We’ll just have to wait and see how it feels tomorrow.”

He will undergo some more testing today and could miss his next spring start. Sox medical staff will try to determine the severity of the injury.
