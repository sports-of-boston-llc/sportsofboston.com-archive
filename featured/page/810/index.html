Ellsbury Injured in Sox Win Over Royals

April 11, 2010 at 6:46pm in Featured, Red Sox
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Making the move from center to left, the speedy Jacoby Ellsbury gives the Red Sox a tremendous amount of range in away ballparks (left field in Fenway isn’t very big) — certainly a lot more than Jason Bay ever did. The one issue Ellsbury seems to have this season is his decision-making, a problem that has plagued him at times in the past. In the first inning on Sunday, Ellsbury failed to call off shortstop Bill Hall on a catchable ball, leading to an error and eventually a run for the Royals.

Later on in the ninth, Ellsbury failed to call off Adrian Beltre on a tough play in foul territory, and paid the price when the two collided. Beltre’s knee appeared to slam into Ellsbury’s chest, and the left fielder was forced to the leave the game. Details of his injury are not known, but it didn’t look pretty. After the game, Terry Francona said Ellsbury was getting X-rays on the left side of his rib cage and was “in pain.”

As for the game, the Red Sox used 12 hits and eight runs in support of Clay Buchholz, who lasted 5.0 innings and allowed three runs (two earned) to earn his first win of the year. The bullpen coughed up three more runs on the afternoon, but the Sox held on to beat K.C. 8-6. Continue reading Ellsbury Injured in Sox Win Over Royals »

Bruins Cap Off Season With Shootout Win Over Capitals

April 11, 2010 at 4:32pm in Bruins, Featured, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

It’s crystal clear: the Washington Capitals are far and away the best team in the Eastern Conference and maybe the NHL. They finished the 2009-2010 season with at least 20 more points than any other East squad, tallying 54 wins against just 15 regulation losses. They carry a 50-goal scorer, a 40-goal scorer, a 30-goal scorer, and four 20-goal scorers as part of the NHL’s best offense (316 total goals).

The Bruins, who feature the NHL’s worst offense, faced the daunting task of playing the Capitals in Washington twice in the season’s final week. Needing to rack up points and wins to ensure a playoff spot, the Bruins did just that. They suffered an overtime loss to the Caps last Monday, and countered with a 4-3 overtime win of their own against the same Caps on Sunday.

In collecting three points over the Caps this week, the Bruins moved themselves out of the eighth spot and into the sixth spot in the Eastern Conference playoffs. Therefore by beating the Capitals, the Bruins avoid facing them in the first round of the playoffs (and likely face an early exit). Instead, they will take on division-rival Buffalo or the New Jersey Devils next week. Continue reading Bruins Cap Off Season With Shootout Win Over Capitals »

Sox Blast 5 HRs to Beat Greinke, Royals

April 11, 2010 at 2:47am in Featured, Red Sox
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Saturday’s game against the Royals featured the season debuts of three members of the Sox bench, including the soon-to-be retired Mike Lowell. Lowell started, played third base, and hit sixth in the batting order. He didn’t commit an error and made a good play in the second inning, but he didn’t light the world on fire in a 1-4 effort. The rest of the team, however, picked up the slack offensively to the tune of five home runs in an 8-3 victory.

Even more shocking than the power surge is the hitter responsible for two of the blasts: Red Sox backup catcher Jason Varitek. Yes, VARITEK. He followed up a fifth-inning homer with one in the ninth as part of a 2-4 day. Tek was starting at catcher in place of Victor Martinez (0-5), who was starting at DH in place of David Ortiz.

Varitek’s fifth inning blast followed a Jeremy Hermida home run a pitch earlier off starter Zack Greinke, who allowed four runs on five hits in 6.2 innings, but did rack up five strikeouts. Greinke certainly wasn’t as sharp as he was last season, as he has now allowed five runs over his first two starts after allowing just six runs over his first eight starts in 2009.
