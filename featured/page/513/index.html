Manning Stuns New England Again in 24-20 Giants Win

November 6, 2011 at 8:58pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story

Add a comment

New York Giants quarterback Eli Manning replicated his Super Bowl XLII success on Sunday, rallying the New York Giants (6-2) to a last-minute 24-20 come-from-behind victory against the New England Patriots (5-3) at Gillette Stadium in Foxborough, Mass.

Gigantic Performance

Down 20-17 with 1:36 remaining, Manning orchestrated an 8-play, 80-yard drive that culminated with a 1-yard, third and goal strike to Jake Ballard to give the Giants the win. Former Red Sox third base coach Dale Svuem appears to be the favorite to replace Terry Francona as Red Sox manager, “rival executives” told FOXSports.com’s Ken Rosenthal.

Svuem was the third-base coach in 2004 and 2005, and while he is held in high regard by Red Sox GM Ben Cherington, his skills as a third base coach leave a lot to be desired. Still, partly under his watch, the Red Sox won it all in 2004.
