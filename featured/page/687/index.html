Garnett Basket Helps Celtics Win Ninth Straight, Beat 76ers

December 10, 2010 at 12:59am in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

Sometimes, it’s better to be good than lucky. When you’re on an eight-game winning streak, it’s always better to be both. Going for their ninth straight win, the Celtics were in serious jeopardy of losing the streak late in their game against the Philadelphia 76ers when Andre Iguodala (14 points, 11 assists) hit a hook shoot to give Philly a 101-100 lead with 6.6 seconds to go.

Leave it to Rajon Rondo to have it all figured out. The C’s point guard zipped an alley-oop pass to Kevin Garnett, who hit the game-winning basket with 1.1 seconds to go, giving the Celtics the 102-101 victory.

“Rajon threw a perfect pass and I just banked it,” Garnett told reporters after the game. “The crazy part about all that is that I messed that play up so many times in practice. It’s only right that the basketball gods gave it to me tonight. It was a good win for us, a grind-out win. This team has been playing unbelievably.”

The Sixers loss snapped Philly’s five-game home win streak, and gave the Celtics an impressive 18-4 record to begin the season, tops in the Eastern Conference. Continue reading Garnett Basket Helps Celtics Win Ninth Straight, Beat 76ers »

Shaq to Play with Turkish Team Besiktas Cola Turka?

December 9, 2010 at 11:04pm in Celtics, Featured, Rumors
Andrew Kipp writes
Add a comment

Many were shocked to see former NBA great Allen Iverson bring his talents to Istanbul, Turkey this fall. Now there are reports that Besiktas Cola Turka, the team that signed Iverson, is also interested in acquiring Boston Celtics’ center Shaquille O’Neal.

Shaq is currently in the first year of a two-year, $3 million contract with the Celtics. By the time the contract expires, he’ll be 40 years old. The idea of Shaq heading to Turkey to finish his basketball career at the age of 40 seems completely ridiculous.

However, Besiktas general manager Seref Yalcin claimed O’Neal told him, “I want to be a champion this season with Boston. But I’m coming to Turkey next year,” according to Hoopsnotes.

There are plenty of reasons to be skeptical of Yalcin’s comments. The most obvious being that Shaq, at the age of 38, is currently under a two-year contract. He’s also undergone a transformation in the first half of this season. After an up and down year in Cleveland in 2009-2010, the 18-year veteran is now averaging 11.2 points and 6.4 rebounds in 22.0 minutes per game with Boston. The successful five-year run at Florida has came to an end for coach Urban Meyer. According to ESPN, Meyer stepped down from his coaching duties Tuesday to spend more time with his family.

Boston Bruins’ defenseman Mark Stuart will miss four to six weeks with a broken fourth metacarpal joint in his right ring finger. Stuart suffered the injury during Tuesday night‘s contest against Buffalo.

Stuart was examined by Dr. Matt Leibman at Newton Wellesley Hospital, and the diagnosis was confirmed by GM Peter Chiarelli.

In the meantime, Stuart will be replaced by Steven Kampfer. In 20 games in Providence, Kampfer led the team in points with 16 (three goals, 13 assists).
