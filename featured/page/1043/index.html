Eddie House, Celtics Notch 9th Straight Win

January 29, 2009 at 5:37am in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Geoff writes
3 comments

The Sacramento Kings came into the T.D. Banknorth Garden and looked as if they were going to put up a bit of a challenge for the streaking Celtics, but that changed in a hurry. The Kings had an early 10-point lead on the Celtics, but Boston easily erased the deficit and were down by only two points to start the second quarter. They then went on a 25-9 run to put the game away. Believe it or not, the Celtics bench secured this win.

The starters had a decent showing, but none were great. Rajon Rondo had a good game, putting up 24 points and dishing out nine assists. Kevin Garnett and Ray Allen had 14 and 12 points respectively, but Paul Pierce was limited to just eight points with only one field goal during the game.

Patriots Season Grades: Running Backs

January 28, 2009 at 11:13am in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
Jeff Christenbury writes
2 comments

Our next stop on the postseason grading tour of the 2008 Patriots are the running backs, who were perhaps the most scattered position on the team with injuries.

Laurence Maroney: C-
Thoughts: Count me as a person who has completely jumped off the Laurence Maroney bandwagon. After a decent rookie season Maroney has been nothing but hurt or ineffective for the Patriots. This year was no different as the former first round pick had just 93 yards on 28 carries (3.32 yards per carry) and zero touchdowns. Maroney played just three games before being placed on IR for a shoulder injury. 2009 will be a make or break year for Maroney as without him the Patriots three headed monster at running back seemed to work pretty well. I hope for his sake his injury problems are behind him or we could see him take the Chad Jackson route right out of town.

Report: Brady on Track to Start 2009

January 28, 2009 at 9:32am in Featured, Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
One comment

Finally, some good news that’s confirmed. Just a day after ESPN.com’s Michael Smith reported a similar story, the Boston Globe reports Tom Brady’s rehab is going well and he should be ready for the 2009 opener. NFL medical sources say Brady shouldn’t need any more surgery to fight infection and other sources say he has actually been dropping back and throwing passes.

The first game of the 2009 season is September 9, just more than seven months away…

My Take: Nothing but good news here. The fact that he’s actually dropping back and throwing passes shows the knee is stable enough to take even a little pressure. It will still make for some interesting weeks shortly when the Patriots have the option to franchise Matt Cassel.

BU Remains #2 in Nation, Northeastern Still Tops Hockey East

January 27, 2009 at 6:34pm in College, Featured
Mike writes
Add a comment

Boston University continued its dominant play of late, sweeping New Hampshire this weekend 5-0 at home and 3-1 in Durham.  The wins helped BU strengthen its position in the second spot in the latest USCHO.com/CBS College Sports Division I Hockey Poll, closing the gap to 24 points behind Notre Dame, who is still #1.  Norte Dame and BU split the entire allotment of 50 first place votes, 37-13.  The sweep gives the Terriers four wins in a row, nine in the last 10 games and an 11-1-1 mark since late November.

Fellow Hockey East member Northeastern jumped two spots to third on the strength of their own sweep over Providence with Cornell and Minnesota following to complete the top five. Vermont took three of four points from UMass over the weekend in the only series that was not swept, boosting them to sixth.  Denver, Michigan, Princeton, and Miami round out the top ten.
