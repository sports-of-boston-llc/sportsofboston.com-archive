2012 NFL Playoffs Expert Picks: Conference Championships

January 22, 2012 at 3:01pm in Featured, NFL, Now!, Opinion, Patriots
John writes
Add a comment

It’s finally time for the Conference Championships. After several disappointing years, can the Patriots finally make it back to the Super Bowl? Will they get revenge from four years ago? Will a Harbaugh be guaranteed a title? What will happen is what we are all here to predict for you.

Here at SoB (in red) and SoNY (in green), we’ve brought you our choices for how those games will turn out. It’ll be worth two points for anyone who guesses the winner of any game, one point if they can guess combined touchdown totals of the two teams to within one (or two points if they guess the total exactly right) and one point if they can guess the total passing yardage of each game’s top passer to within 50 (or two points if they get within 10).

The Patriots are the most picked team in this round, no doubt thanks to the likes of Tom Brady and Rob Gronkowski. Meanwhile, the Giants are the team of choice in San Francisco, where a whole lot of rain is coming down. Turnovers galore could easily be afoot due to that.

Position Comparison: Patriots vs Ravens

January 22, 2012 at 12:51pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
zach writes
Add a comment

Quarterback: Advantage Patriots

This is an obvious one. With Drew Brees and Aaron Rodgers eliminated, Tom Brady is unquestionably the best quarterback left in the playoffs. After a regular season that saw Brady break Dan Marino’s yardage record, Brady followed it up with six touchdowns and 363 yards against the Broncos last week.

Joe Flacco on the other hand, was average both in the regular season and in his first playoff game. Flacco threw for 176 yards and two touchdown passes, but his defense was more responsible for the 20 points the Ravens put up than him: on both touchdowns the Ravens got the ball in great field position because of Texans turnovers. This kind of performance was sufficient against a rookie quarterback, but against Tom Brady, Flacco will have to be on top of his game for the Ravens to have a chance. Report: Red Sox Trade Marco Scutaro to Rockies

January 21, 2012 at 6:33pm in Featured, Red Sox
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

The Red Sox have completed their quest to free up some payroll, trading shortstop Marco Scutaro for the Rockies for pitcher Clayton Mortensen, according to ESPN’s Jim Bowden.

Scutaro, 36, will play second base in Colorado, who will also take on his $6 million salary. He had one of his best seasons last year, hitting .299/.358/.423 over 113 games.

Mortensen, 26, has a 5.12 ERA over his three-year career, which includes stints with Oakland, St. Louis and Colorado. He is still in his pre-arbitration years, and could be a bullpen candidate for the team. Continue reading Report: Red Sox Trade Marco Scutaro to Rockies »

The Rangers stunned the Bruins Saturday with a last second goal in overtime 3-2.

Sixty minutes wasnt enough time to have a winner in the game. It was a slow start to the game with no goals in the first period.
