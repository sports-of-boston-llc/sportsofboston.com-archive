NCAA Final Four: Star Watch

April 2, 2010 at 8:00pm in College, Featured, Opinion, Top Story
Andrew Kipp writes
Add a comment

Sports fans across the nation will have their eyes glued to their television sets watching the Men’s NCAA Final Four this weekend, and I will certainly be among them. Thousands of others will descend upon Lucas Oil Stadium in Indianapolis to watch four teams compete for a national championship and eternal glory. Michigan State, Butler, West Virginia, and Duke are all that remains of the 65 team field that participated in what may have been the most exciting NCAA Tournament ever.

To get you ready for the games on Saturday, I’ve compiled a list of the eight key players to watch for.

Kyle Singler (Duke):

Singler is the heart and soul of the Duke Blue Devils. One of the top five college recruits of the 2007 class, the 6’8″ forward has been a starter at Duke since his freshman year. He could have declared for the NBA draft after averaging 13.3 points and 5.9 rebounds per game in his sophomore season, but a loss to West Virginia in the second round of the NCAA Tournament left a bitter taste in his mouth, motivating him to return for another season. Singler, averaging 17.6 points 6.9 rebounds this season, has a chance for redemption on Saturday. Look for him to play with a BIG chip on his shoulder against the Mountaineers. Red Sox 2010 Roster Preview: Fourth Outfielder

April 2, 2010 at 12:00pm in Featured, Red Sox
Greg writes
Add a comment

The fourth outfielder is starting to become a very popular position in the Red Sox organization, as well as the rest of the MLB, and this year is no different. Just like teams in the NHL are learning, it’s always good to have at least two capable goalies, and for baseball teams it’s the same thing. Teams like the Red Sox are starting to implement the fact that having a good backup outfielder is very important and that the player could be considered a starter at any point in the season.

Last year the Sox had Rocco Baldelli and then added Brian Anderson in a mid-season trade that sent another former starting outfielder in Mark Kotsay to the Chicago White Sox. In years past, it was very common to see just three starting outfielders and then maybe another player who could fill in if need be, but that player would be by no means a starter.

Things have changed since then, and the Red Sox continue to keep a viable starter on the bench and rotate him in on a regular basis. That player this season is Jeremy Hermida, who was a starter for the Florida Marlins since coming into the league in 2005. Another potential fourth outfielder is 23-year-old Josh Reddick, whose work ethic has brought his name into the discussion as well.    Continue reading Red Sox 2010 Roster Preview: Fourth Outfielder »

The Rangers have claimed first baseman Ryan Garko off waivers from the Mariners, effectively ending all interest they had in Red Sox third baseman Mike Lowell. The Rangers and Red Sox originally agreed to a deal in December that would send Lowell and $9 million to Texas for catching prospect Max Ramirez. The deal was called off more than a week later after Lowell underwent surgery to repair an injured right thumb.

Texas and Boston reportedly revisited the trade recently, but some reports suggested the Rangers wanted the Red Sox to eat even more money (Lowell is scheduled to earn $12 million, the Sox were willing to pay $9 million before), and why not? The Rangers had the position of leverage: they had the prospect, and the Red Sox were in the awkward situation with Lowell.

Celtics Need to Finish Strong

April 2, 2010 at 9:49am in Celtics, Featured, Opinion
Andrew Kipp writes
2 comments

At 82 games, the NBA regular season is a marathon, especially for an older team like the Boston Celtics. So the big question is, should the Celtics sprint to the finish line, or coast into the postseason allowing their veteran starters to rest?

It’s an interesting question, and not an easy one to answer. The Celtics have eight games remaining on their schedule. They currently sit at 47-27, good enough for them to finish no worse than the fourth seed in the Eastern Conference, barring an epic collapse. The Atlanta Hawks, one game ahead of Boston, currently hold the third seed, and the Milwaukee Bucks, the fifth seed, remain six games behind the C’s.

If the Celtics finish the season as the fourth seed, they will most likely match up with the Bucks or the Miami Heat in a first round playoff series. Red Sox 2010 Roster Preview: Left Field, Jacoby Ellsbury

April 1, 2010 at 12:00pm in Featured, Red Sox
Greg writes
Add a comment

Roaming the outfield for the Red Sox are players with a lot of speed, leadership, and experience. But, can everyone avoid injuries? Health has been an issue for the Red Sox in recent years, especially if you look at who’s standing in right field.

Over the years, the Sox outfield has changed tremendously. Manny Ramirez is a couple years removed, Johnny Damon hasn’t been around since 2005, and Coco Crisp and Jason Bay never got fully comfortable in Boston. After this season, it’s looking like the outfield could be much different as well, but that is just how the business goes. Red Sox Nation will miss Bay and his power, but with the team’s new style of play there isn’t much need for home runs anyway.

Now the Red Sox have the perfect weapon standing atop the lineup in Jacoby Ellsbury. He fits in perfect for the defensive scheme the Sox are trying to implement this season, and it just happens that he is coming off one of his best offensive seasons of his young career. If the Red Sox want to play the role of the small ball/defensive team, then it will all have to start with Ellsbury at the top of the lineup.
