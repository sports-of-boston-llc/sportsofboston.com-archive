Ortiz, Red Sox Cap Off Impressive Month With Victory Over Royals

May 30, 2010 at 9:57pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Mike Martyniak writes
Add a comment

The Boston Red Sox finished off their month of May in suitable fashion. On New Hampshire Day at Fenway Park, the Red Sox beat the Kansas City Royals 8-1 to salvage a split of the series heading into tomorrow’s day off. After a much-needed sweep of the Tampa Bay Rays, the Sox came into this four-game series with the Royals confident and collected. They have been steadily getting healthier (with the exception of the bizarre on-goings of Jacoby Ellsbury and Josh Beckett), and climbing the standings in the very competitive A.L. East.

The first two losses of this series were somewhat overshadowed in this city by the Celtics finally overcoming the Orlando Magic to head back to the finals, but this was a series the Boston could not overlook, as the Royals are right at the top of the league in team batting average. After the most impressive pitching match-up of the weekend lived up to it’s hype on Saturday night, with Clay Buchholz one-upping Zach Greinke, the Sox entered Sunday needing a split to end this impressive month of May on a high note. And they got it.

Buchholz Out-Duels Greinke in Nail-Biting Pitchers’ Duel

May 29, 2010 at 10:56pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
John writes
Add a comment

It wasn’t always pretty (in fact, it wasn’t pretty), but the Red Sox defeated the Royals 1-0 in a game in which nobody could get any offense going. Each team mustered only five hits, and only Scott Podsednik of the Royals had multiple hits (one of those an error that was later reversed). The only run came in the bottom of the 2nd, when Adrian Beltre singled, J.D. Drew doubled, and Mike Lowell hit a sacrifice grounder.

The Red Sox had their chances, with a lead-off single and error in the 3rd wasted and back-to-back walks in the 5th leading to J.D. Drew grounding out with the bases loaded to end the inning. Adrian Beltre grounded into a double play to end the inning after an error loaded the bases. Still, there was nothing to be done by an oft-uninspired offense.

Celtics Return to Finals with Rout of Magic

May 29, 2010 at 10:09am in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Justin Power writes
One comment

A Bruins-like meltdown was never going to happen. It was a cute story, but the Boston Celtics are not like other sports franchises. With 17 Championship Banners and a team of ring-bearing veterans, they are no strangers to pressure-cooker playoff contests. After giving Orlando a puncher’s chance with two straight losses, the Celtics finally put any talk of a collapse to rest with a command performance at the Garden, defeating the Orlando Magic 96-84.

The Celtics found themselves in the same situation as their Causeway Street co-tenants, leading their opponent 3-0 in a best-of-seven series. The Bruins gave away four games straight, losing their series to Philadelphia and thereby joining a dubious list of history-makers. Inevitable comparisons to their hardcourt brethren abounded when the Celtics began yielding a three game lead of their own. The media had some fun with the comparisons, and early on, it became nauseating to listen to the constant reminders brought forth during the game broadcast.

It wouldn’t take long for the Celtics to start shutting people’s mouths.
