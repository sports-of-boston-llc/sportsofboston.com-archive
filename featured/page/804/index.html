Who Is Darnell McDonald?

April 22, 2010 at 3:49pm in Featured, Red Sox
Justin Power writes
2 comments

In 13 seasons of professional baseball, Darnell McDonald has amassed 1,408 games of service and all anyone wants to talk about is the time he raced a horse.

“Somebody’s always got to bring up the horse,” McDonald told the media Wednesday. “Yeah, I was the first person to lose to Zippy Chippy.”

Zippy Chippy was a racehorse without a win, and somebody within the Rochester Red Wings organization dreamed up a publicity stunt where McDonald would race the gelding in a 50-yard dash as part of the pre-game festivities.  It would go down as Zippy’s only win.

“I was volunteered for it,” said McDonald, obviously a reluctant participant.

In two short days, Darnell McDonald has done everything in his ability to wipe out his most famous, albeit it non-baseball related, hallmark. It is appropriate that it’s his baseball skills that are doing the trick.  With two homers in as many days and a walkoff single in between, McDonald has gotten off to the hottest start for a Boston newcomer since Gabe Kapler homered twice in his Sox debut against the Marlins in 2003. Add a comment

The Red Sox managed to avoid another loss in wild fashion against the Rangers on Wednesday night, with more unlikely heroes emerging to send Texas to its sixth straight defeat in an 8-7 win in 12 innings.

Josh Beckett was far from magnificent, allowing seven runs on seven hits and five walks over seven innings pitched. He gutted through the game after a rough early start, as he dug the Red Sox in a 4-0 hole after allowing one run in the first and three more in the second.

Starting in place of David Ortiz at DH against lefthander Matt Harrison, Mike Lowell clubbed his first home run of the year (he has one more than Mr. Ortiz) to get the Red Sox closer. Facing a 4-1 deficit, J.D. Drew hit a surprising grand slam later on in the third inning, scoring Bill Hall, Darnell McDonald, and Marco Scutaro (what a trio).

Roethlisberger Suspended Six Games By NFL

April 21, 2010 at 2:13pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
John writes
3 comments

NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell has announced that embattled Pittsburgh Steelers’ quarterback Ben Roethlisberger will be suspended for the first six games of the regular season. Some behavioral evaluation by medical experts will also be required.

For those of you who have been living under a rock for the last few months, Roethlisberger was accused of rape (again) in a Georgia nightclub, but was not charged, possibly due to the lone investigating officer (who has since resigned) having bias in Ben’s favor. Still, Roethlisberger’s behavior has been deemed a violation of the league’s good behavior rules.

In the meantime, the Steelers have been trying to rid themselves of their burden by trading it. Quick talks with the Rams regarding trading Roethlisberger as part of a package to get the #1 pick (and Sam Bradford with it) went nowhere when the Rams weren’t interested. Few teams were even interested in Roethlisberger to begin with, and the suspension eating up his value early on has only killed that chance.
