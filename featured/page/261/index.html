Jake Peavy Better Than Advertised in First Four Starts with Red Sox

August 22, 2013 at 8:01pm in Featured, Opinion, Red Sox, Top Story
Brian Roach writes
Add a comment

Jake Peavy may not be an ace in the Boston Red Sox rotation, but he has been a  proven commodity as a starting pitcher since the trade from the Chicago White Sox back on July 30.

In four starts with the Red Sox, the 32-year-old has a 4.18 ERA and a 1-1 record, but really only has one bad start. That came back on August 9 against the Kansas City Royals when  he allowed six earned runs in five innings of work. He struck out no one in the outing and allowed two home runs and walked one.

In his other three starts combined, Peavy has allowed five total runs in 18.2 innings of work with 15 strikeouts. The 2.41 ERA bodes well for the former Cy Young award winner as the playoff stretch is coming up for the Red Sox to try and win the AL East over the rival Tampa Bay Rays. Fantasy Football Draft Help: AFC Players to Watch

August 20, 2013 at 9:00am in Fantasy, Featured
Kevin O'Keefe writes
Add a comment

Player value depends on potential more than present day most before you draft. This is a rundown one player on each team who has great potential relative to where they are likely to be drafted in your league. Some of these guys are roster staples while others may not even warrant drafting but could be very useful in foreseeable circumstances.

AFC EAST

Buffalo Bills: E.J. Manuel has looked the part of the starter up until his injury this past weekend. At present, he’s a backup or bye-week replacement. He has shown flashes of brilliance in the preseason and even Bills fans can’t drink enough to tolerate Kevin Kolb. Manuel will start by midseason, if not when healthy.

Miami Dolphins: Brian Hartline will benefit from Mike Wallace’s world class speed. Hartline’s stock also gets a slight bump due to Dustin Keller’s season ending injury. He’s a solid reserve acquirable with one of your last picks.

New England Patriots: Tom Brady has looked sharp as ever with a new group of receivers (and no Gronkowski). This is the first time Brady may be available in the 4th round or later in years.
