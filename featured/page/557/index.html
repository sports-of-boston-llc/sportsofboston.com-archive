Red Sox Pound Rangers Again, Regain Lead of AL East

August 24, 2011 at 11:50pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Matthew Bond writes
One comment

Ron Washington must be shaking his head. A night after the Red Sox defeated the American League West leading Rangers handily, Boston again came out swinging the bats in a 13-2 win. Highlighted by a strong performance by Josh Beckett, who earned his 11th win of the season in his home state of Texas, a big first inning, and home runs by Jacoby Ellsbury, Carl Crawford, and Adrian Gonzalez, the easy win and a Yankees extra innings loss gave Boston a 1.0 game lead in the tight AL East.

Boston Bats Attack Harrison Early

The Red Sox came out swinging in Texas against southpaw Matt Harrison on Wednesday night. Boston scored four times in the first inning to get things started, with singles from Jacoby Ellsbury and Adrian Gonzalez to put two runners on for Dustin Pedroia, who drove in Ellsbury on a sharp ground ball to left field. Then, in his return to the Sox lineup, David Ortiz lined an RBI single of his own to right field, scoring Gonzalez and giving Boston an early 2-0 lead. The struggling Carl Crawford capped off the inning with a two-run double pulled down the right field line, scoring both runners because of a miscue by catcher Mike Napoli, who dropped what appeared to be an easy out at the plate. The four runs gave Josh Beckett a great lead to work with as he took to the hill on a windy night in Arlington. Continue reading Red Sox Pound Rangers Again, Regain Lead of AL East »

The 2011 season is shaping up to feature the most versatile group yet.
