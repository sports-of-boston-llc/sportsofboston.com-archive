Add a comment

Tiger Woods did his best to “let it all hang out.” OK, that was bad, but you get it. He really wanted to show he was ready to face most of his problems in his first press conference in over five months on Monday. He gave strong (but clearly rehearsed) responses to a variety of questions, revealing that his wife Elin will not be joining him this week at the Masters, and that he suffered numerous Achilles injuries in the last couple years.

He vehemently denied any connection to HGH, PEDs, steroids, and any other illegal drugs, insisting that his connection to a tainted Canadian doctor was for healing his torn-up knee with a procedure known as platelet-rich plasma (PRP).

“Look at what I was engaged in,” Woods said. “When you’re living a life that is a lie, life isn’t fun … that’s been stripped away. It feels fun again.”

Really Tiger? You weren’t having fun? You’re living in a dream world my friend. I refuse to believe that “seeing” all those women wasn’t a good thing in your mind at the time. Pff.

Beckett and Red Sox Agree to Four-Year Deal

April 5, 2010 at 6:48pm in Featured, Red Sox, Top Story
Pete Rampino writes
Add a comment

In a move we expected to happen, Josh Beckett will be staying in Boston for years to come. Beckett and the Sox came to terms this afternoon on a four-year, $68 million deal that will keep him here through the 2014 season.

The move comes less than 24 hours after Beckett turned in a poor performance against the Yankees in the Sox season opener on Sunday night. Beckett allowed five earned runs in 4.2 innings, but ended up with a no-decision after a late Sox rally.

“We couldn’t be more thrilled to keep Josh around. We were talking that he’ll be with the Red Sox for nine years when this contract is up. He’s earned it. Josh is an excellent contributor on and off the field, a big part of our team and pitching staff.” – Red Sox GM Theo Epstein

The deal cements the Sox rotation as one of the most talented in baseball as Beckett, Jon Lester, and John Lackey will anchor the staff for the next several seasons.

Celtics Blow Huge Lead, Get Big Win Over Cavs

April 4, 2010 at 9:32pm in Celtics, Featured
KC Downey writes
Add a comment

The Celtics held a 22-point lead in the third quarter against the Cavaliers on Easter Sunday at the Garden, and for once, it seemed like they’d keep that lead. Every time the Cavs knocked down the lead, the Celtics built it right back up through the third quarter, but the Cavs chipped away enough to face a 17-point deficit (98-81) entering the final frame. Among the league leaders in blown 10-point leads, the Celtics seemed poised to keep up the pace.

Then, LeBron James woke up from his slumber and showed up to play. He scored 20 of his 42 points (14-31 shooting) in the fourth quarter, helping his team completely erase the huge deficit, and even take a lead with less than three minutes left in the game. After Ray Allen hit a late three-pointer to give the Celtics a four-point lead, the Cavs battled back, and with 10 seconds remaining and the ball in LeBron’s hands, the Cavs needed two to tie and three to win.

As soon as LeBron took off with the ball after two missed Kendrick Perkins free throws, it seemed his motive was clear. He was going for the win. He tossed up a three-pointer, when a drive to the hoop probably would have tied the game. Hey, maybe he needed to get home for Easter dinner? Either way, LeBron missed, and Kevin Garnett hit two subsequent free throws to bring a thrilling game to its final score, 117-113. Continue reading Celtics Blow Huge Lead, Get Big Win Over Cavs »

But then why start the season with these games, and play them at the time when they have the least possible impact on the pennant race? Isn’t Opening Day at Fenway Park big enough, without forcing the Rivalry ™ into it? I guess not this year, because there won’t be an Opening Day at Fenway Park.
