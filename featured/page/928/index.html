After watching this week’s Wake Forest game, here are some observations on the football team’s progress.

QB Dave Shinskie seems to have control of the offense.

While Justin Tuggle relied on the deep ball, Shinskie relied on the short and medium range passing game. Always more suitable for conservative Offensive Coordinator Gary Tranquill, Shinskie played to his abilities, even showing a little movement in the pocket, and the offense looked back on track after a miserable Clemson outing. Yet, Shinskie still cannot throw the long ball and looked tight for the fourth quarter and overtime. Overall though, Shinskie looks like the solid starter going into the Florida State game, but I feel Tuggle could get a look if Shinskie struggles mightily against a stout Seminole defense. The Celtics and the “Anticlimactic” Division Preview

September 29, 2009 at 5:50pm in Celtics, Featured, Top Story
Nathan writes
5 comments

Less than a decade ago, the Atlantic Division appeared to be steamrolling to NBA dominance. In 2002, the division had just come off sending back-to-back teams to the Finals (76ers, Nets) and had witnessed the emergence of a Nets-Celtics rivalry in the 2002 Eastern Conference Finals. Allen Iverson was considered to be one of, if not the best guard in the league, Antoine Walker and Paul Pierce were thought to be the second best duo this side of Shaq-Kobe, and Jason Kidd was brought into Jersey to corral a talented bunch fronted by Kerry Kittles, Keith Van Horn and young wingman Richard Jefferson.

Fast forward four years, and we suddenly had the “Titanic Division” on our hands. What happened? We saw an Allen Iverson trade, a disinterested Vince Carter, and Raef Lafrentz, Mark Blount, Ricky Davis, and Marcus Banks join and leave the Celtics roster.

Things have improved for us beantowners since 2007, but the rest of the division remains much of a mystery if not a complete disaster. Report: Wilhite Pulled Groin While Running From Men

September 29, 2009 at 2:20pm in Featured, Patriots
Jeff Christenbury writes
2 comments

The strange situation with Patriots defensive back Jonathan Wilhite got a little clearer Tuesday as police have released the details surrounding the second year player’s whereabouts on Sunday.

According to North Attleboro police, on Sunday morning Wilhite says was confronted by two men in his driveway – one of which was running towards him and shouting – so he ran away from them and went to a local Cumberland Farms and called police. Wilhite says he pulled his groin while running away.

Wilhite was a late scratch for the Patriots on Sunday, though he wasn’t really needed as the Pats cruised to victory. I’m sure we’ll hear more as details come out.

Pryor Takes Full Advantage of Wilfork Injury

September 29, 2009 at 6:30am in Featured, Patriots
Matthew Marcantonio writes
2 comments

Vince Wilfork left a large void to fill in the middle of the Patriots defense, when he went down in the first half of the team’s 26-10 victory over the Atlanta Falcons Sunday.

Last season, if this happened, Mike Wright would have likely filled in for the injured pro-bowler as the team would still have Ty Warren and Richard Seymour on the outside in their 3-4 defense. Well, in 2009, with the Patriots employing a 4-3 defense, the Patriots had two options. Go with Worcester, Ma. native Ron Brace, or fellow rookie Myron Pryor.

Bill Belichick and the coaching staff went with Pryor.

The 2009 sixth-round draft pick from the University of Kentucky may not be a name many Patriots fans recognize, but he has been a pleasant surprise ever since he came to Foxboro in May. Pryor was the 207th player taken in a draft where the Patriots added three defensive lineman. At Kentucky, Pryor was named second team all-SEC after a 29-tackle, 4.5-sack campaign as a senior. Even with an impressive senior season for the Wildcats, Pryor fell to the sixth round as many teams were turned off by his limited size and questionable burst off the line of scrimmage.
