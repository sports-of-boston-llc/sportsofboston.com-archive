Fantasy Wrap-Up: Week 9 (5-26 – 6-1)

June 3, 2008 at 10:04pm in Fantasy, Featured
Pete Rampino writes
2 comments

Two months are in the books, and we are just getting started. The big talk of last week was the call ups of highly-touted prospects Clayton Kershaw (Dodgers) and Jay Bruce (Reds). If you were lucky enough to snag Bruce, then you are a happy owner. As for Kershaw owners, odds are you are demanding a refund. Of course, these are only two of the players in Major League Baseball and you want to know who else made a splash in week 9.

OFFENSE

Jay Bruce (CF), Cincinnati Reds: All hail Bruce Almighty! Reminiscent of Ryan Braun in 2007, Bruce silenced all the critics and lived up to the hype. In his first six games, Bruce is 13 for 22 (.591) with 10 runs, 2 HR, 6 RBI, and 2 SB. I not only think he is this year’s Ryan Braun, I expect him to be this year’s Ryan Braun. Bruce is the real deal and will be an impact for the rest of the season.

Hazel Mae to Leave Nesn

June 3, 2008 at 10:55am in Featured, Now!, Red Sox
Jeff Christenbury writes
One comment

Another one of NESN’s anchors is leaving the network as Hazel Mae has said she will be leaving at the end of the month. Mae has been the host of NESN’s Sportsdesk and other mostly Red Sox oriented programs since joining the network four years ago.

It seems like an amicable separation as NESN has given her their blessing for her future endeavors. As for Mae, she has yet to divulge what her next career move is. This all comes just months after Red Sox reporter Tina Cervasio left in March to work at MSG.

My Take: Personally, I was never really a fan of Hazel Mae, she never really made me want to watch Sportsdesk. It’s been a recurring theme as NESN over the past few years of hiring mostly women to do sideline and studio work for Sportsdesk and Red Sox stuff so perhaps that has something to do with it. I wish her luck in the future of course, but is there anyone out there who will not tune in to NESN because she’s not there?

Ortiz Out With Wrist Injury, Sox Lose

June 3, 2008 at 6:45am in Featured, Red Sox
KC Downey writes
3 comments

Red Sox slugger David Ortiz has been a fixture in the No. 3 spot for much of his Red Sox career. Now, the Sox must move on without Ortiz for at least a month and perhaps the entire season after Sox doctors diagnosed Big Papi with a partially torn tendon in his left wrist, sending him to the disabled list. Ortiz would only beat our for the season if he opted for surgery, though Terry Francona was pretty confident in saying surgery “is very unlikely.”

Perhaps deflated by the bad news, the Red Sox lost the final game of a four-game series in Baltimore 6-3. Hideki Okajima was called to pitch in the eighth inning of a 3-2 game…and proceeded to allow four runs on four hits in just 2/3 of an inning to take the loss. Okie wasted another good start by Tim Wakefield, who went seven innings and allowed just two runs. Manny Ramirez contributed another HR (his 12th) in the loss.

View from the Monster 6-3

June 3, 2008 at 5:46am in Featured, Red Sox
Mike writes
Add a comment

The Red Sox completed their road trip this week, beginning in Seattle with a visit to the Mariners. The Sox took the first game, but dropped the final two, scoring a mere eight runs in the series, capping it by being shut out 1-0 in the finale. They dropped five of six on the West Coast and just could not score runs.

Bartolo Colon led the Red Sox by Seattle 5-3 in the first game, throwing 85 pitches over seven innings, while allowing only one run to earn his second Red Sox victory. Hideki Okajima continues to provide solid relief, pitching a scoreless inning. Jonathan Papelbon, in a non-save situation, made things a little interesting by allowing two ninth inning unearned runs, thanks to his own error. In a balanced hitting attack, David Ortiz added his 12th home run of the season.

Thankfully, Flopping in the NBA Will Be Ending

June 2, 2008 at 5:24am in Celtics, Featured, Opinion, Top Story
Adam writes
4 comments

(This is apart of Sports of Boston’s extensive coverage of the NBA Finals)

Last week, when it was announced that the NBA would begin fining players for flopping, I came quite close to clapping about the story when I first saw it. One of the reasons the NBA has become pretty unwatchable, to me, is the fake theatrics of the game. When it’s a game, it should be about performing well, not acting.

In baseball and football, it is rare that someone acting like they were wronged affects the whole game, and more rare that it occurs more than once a game. Yet, in basketball, guys have been falling all over the place for a few years now, and for a majority of the game. Just ask Vlade Divac or Robert Horry [YouTube videos].
