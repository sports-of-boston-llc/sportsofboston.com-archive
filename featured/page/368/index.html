The Small Market Myth of the 2012 MLB Season

September 19, 2012 at 9:47pm in Featured, Opinion, Red Sox, Top Story
Josh Segal writes
One comment

As Major League Baseball’s 2012 Playoffs approach, a crop of new teams appear poised to contend in October. With perennial doormats such as the Baltimore Orioles, Oakland Athletics, and even Pittsburgh Pirates now in the heat of the competition, a trendy, new train of thought comes with them. The era of big market teams is over and the rise of small market teams has begun. This is what real baseball should look like!

The only problem is that this sentiment is completely false. Luckily for teams like the Red Sox, big market franchises are no less successful than they were last year or in the years before that. They say meat tastes better when it’s local and grass-fed, but it tastes fine when bought from a giant, faceless slaughterhouse too.  So eat up, because in the MLB that’s a good thing. A hotly contested game saw the Patriots receive unexpected hope only to see that hope disappear with one errant kick to seal a 20-18 upset for the Arizona Cardinals. The game was a big defensive battle, with only field goals now and then until the middle of the 3rd quarter. Even when the Cardinals made it a two-possession game, New England stayed in it and got key stops, even forcing a late fumble to control the game’s final before kicking the victory wide.

Fortunately, New England limited Arizona to another field goal.

On the Patriots’ next drive, things continued to go downhill. Two plays in, Julian Edelman caught a pass, but rolled over Aaron Hernandez when he was tackled. Hernandez Leaves Game with Right Ankle Injury

September 16, 2012 at 3:13pm in Featured, Patriots, Top Story
Nick Bohlen writes
Add a comment

New England Patriots tight end Aaron Hernandez left Sunday’s game against the Arizona Cardinals in the first quarter with an apparent injury to his right ankle. His return is questionable.

In the type of play no one likes to see, Julian Edelman was tackled by the Cardinals’ William Gay and the two players rolled up on Hernandez’s right leg as he was trying to block. Hernandez didn’t get up for a number of minutes before being helped off the field and straight to the Patriots locker room to be examined.

Hernandez just signed a lucrative new contract extension before the start of the 2012-13 season for five years, $40 million.

UPDATE: According to a report by the Boston Globe’s Shalise Young (via WEEI), x-rays came back negative, a somewhat positive sign in getting Hernandez back as soon as possible.

Should The Red Sox Owners Sell the Team?

September 13, 2012 at 7:14pm in Featured, Red Sox
Steve Bastek writes
Add a comment

An article surfaced today on foxbusiness.com, discussing the idea of the Red Sox being sold. While the article mentions the ownership has denied the claim that they are shopping the team around, it’s not too crazy to think they are at least gauging the market interest. Nothing seems imminent, or even remotely close of happening, but this is an interesting topic to explore. Should the owners sell the franchise?

Personally I believe it is time for this group to move on and to let someone else take the reigns. When the ownership group took over everything seemed great. The team won a couple championships and were contenders year in and year out. Fenway Park is still in the midst of a record sellout streak, even in the team’s darkest year the park is still filled to capacity. Things have gone real sour in recent years, these past two seasons in particular. With the team dumping most of its high profile contracts, and all indications are manager Bobby Valentine won’t return for a second year, what better time than now than to start over completely with a new ownership group.
